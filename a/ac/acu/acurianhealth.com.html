Information Protected under this Policy

This Policy applies to what we call "Personal Information". We define "Personal Information" as any information that could identify you or could be used to contact or locate you. This may also include information you provide relating to your health. Personal Information includes information that may not identify you directly, but that could be used to identify you if used in conjunction with other information - for example, a user name and password combination. This Policy does NOT apply to information that is collected anonymously (that is, without identifying you in any way) or information about groups, rather than individuals, which we sometimes use to show trends or geographic groupings. (For example, we will not provide your street address to anyone without your permission, but we may display a regional map showing groups of cancer patients in the United States by ZIP Code.)

Information Collected

The only Personal Information about you that we collect is information that you provide. Acurian does not have access to any of your confidential medical records. The only information about your health condition to which we ever have access is information you expressly and voluntarily provide to us or our Data Partners so that we can match you with an appropriate clinical trial, or information we receive from healthcare professionals with your consent, exclusively pertaining to your participation in a trial. At various locations on our site, we ask you for information. It may be optional or mandatory for you to provide the information based on the activity that you are performing. For example, you do not have to give us your real name when you register. We do, however, need to have your real name and contact information if you are seeking to be considered for enrollment in a particular clinical trial, or if you have enrolled and wish us to help you during the trial process. We clearly mark which information is optional throughout our site. If you choose to give us optional information, we will be able to provide additional services to you, and tailor those services more effectively. At any point on our site, though, you will be able to perform a particular activity by providing us with only the minimum information required.

The information we seek to collect at different places on our site allows us to provide you (whether you are a patient, physician, or authorized caregiver) with information that is targeted specifically to you or the individual for whom you are seeking information. In the event that you would like to be considered for enrollment in a clinical trial, this information allows us to contact you and the professionals managing a particular trial regarding your eligibility, and to inform you about specific studies that may meet your eligibility criteria. In the event that you have enrolled in a trial, this information allows us to help keep you informed about it.

We provide information from a variety of reputable sources regarding clinical trials and new medical therapies, and we provide you with the ability to send a link to that content along with explanatory messages written by you to third parties (your friends, relatives, etc.) via e-mail. We do not limit the information you may send in a message. Cookies

A cookie is a piece of data stored on your computer's hard drive containing information about you that helps your Web browser remember specific information that a Web server can later retrieve. For instance, by allowing a cookie to be set for our site, you would not have to log in a password more than once, thereby saving time while on our site. If your Web browser rejects cookies, you may still use our site. The only drawback to this is that you will be limited in some areas of our site. Cookies can also enable us to track and target your areas of interests to enhance your experience on our site.

Security

We take every precaution to protect your Personal Information. When you provide us with Personal Information, it is protected both online and off-line.

  • USER NAME AND PASSWORD: The first line of protection is your encrypted password, selected by you. Your user name is unique to you, and is only effective with your password. No one can access any of the secure areas of our site without a proper user name and password combination. To minimize the chance that someone can guess your password, we require passwords with a minimum length of 8 characters that contain characters with upper and lower case letters and numbers. If a user of our site enters an invalid password a certain number of times, that user will be blocked from entering the site for a period of time thereafter. Since Personal Information will only be displayed or entered in a secure area, the password provides considerable protection.
  • ENCRYPTION: In addition to the password protection, any Personal Information or Personal Health Information you provide is encrypted (using 128-bit encryption) and must be accessed using the SSL (Secure Socket Layer) encryption standard. You can tell that you are on a secure, encrypted page while browsing our site by looking at the lock icon on the bottom of Web browsers such as Netscape Navigator and Microsoft Internet Explorer. If the page is secure, the lock is closed; when the page is unencrypted (as it is during standard surfing), the lock becomes un-locked.
  • FIREWALLS: To protect against hackers entering our server environment, we have erected multiple firewalls. Data contained on our servers is visible only to proprietary middleware applications. Sign-on passwords are required by internal staff and are changed on a periodic basis. All internal activity is logged.
  • OFF-LINE PROTECTION: While we use SSL encryption to protect your Personal Information online, we also work very hard to protect Personal Information off-line. Any information we receive, whether defined as Personal Information or not, is restricted in our offices. Only employees who need the information to perform a specific job (for example, our database administrators) are granted access to your Personal Information. ALL of our employees are kept up-to-date on our security and privacy practices. Every quarter, as well as any time new policies are added, our employees are notified and/or reminded about the importance we place on privacy, and what they can do to ensure our users' Personal Information is protected.
  • PHYSICAL SECURITY: We also protect and secure the physical environment in which data is stored. We store Personal Information on servers kept in a secure environment, within locked, environmentally controlled server rooms.

Please note that no method of transmission over the Internet, or method of electronic storage, is 100% secure. Therefore, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

However, your privacy will be protected since when your Personal Information is added to an aggregated pool of data there is no way to identify you personally.
