Gathering, Use and Disclosure of Information

Users of the Service Generally

"Non-Personally-Identifying Information" is information that, without the aid of additional information, cannot be directly associated with a person.

"Personally-Identifying Information," by contrast, is information such as a name or email address that, without more, can be directly associated with a person.

Like most website operators, we gather from users of the Service Non-Personally-Identifying Information of the sort that Web browsers, depending on their settings, may provide. That information includes the user's Internet Protocol ("IP") address, operating system and browser type, and information about Web pages the user views right before arriving at, while navigating and immediately after leaving the Service.

We analyze Non-Personally-Identifying Information gathered from users of the Service to help us better understand how the Service is being used. By identifying patterns and trends in usage, we can better design the Service to improve users' experiences, both in terms of content and ease of use. From time to time, we may also release the Non-Personally-Identifying Information gathered from Service users in the aggregate, such as by publishing a report on trends in the usage of the Service.

Web Cookies

In providing the Service, Amitree utilizes "cookies". A cookie is a small text file stored on your computer that assists our web servers in remembering settings you make while using the Service.

We may use a combination of “persistent cookies” (cookies that remain on your hard drive for an extended period) and “session ID cookies” (cookies that expire when you close your browser) on the Service to, for example, track overall site usage, track and report on your use and interaction with promoted services and advertisements, and to store an indicator that you have successfully authenticated to the Service, so you do not have to enter your password repeatedly.

You can set your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie so you will have an opportunity to either accept or reject it in each instance. However, note that refusing cookies may have a negative impact on the functionality and usability of the Service.

Clear Gifs

A "Clear Gif" is an object embedded in an email or web page usually invisible to the user and allows website operators to do such things as check on whether a user has viewed a particular web page or opened an email. A Clear Gif is sometimes known as a “Tracking Pixel,” “Web Beacon” or “Web Bug.” Company may use Clear Gifs and similar technologies on the Service or in emails to do things, such as count users who have visited particular pages, to know if an email has been opened by the recipient, and to optimize the Service. You may not decline Clear Gifs if you are using the Service, but you may opt out of emails, discussed below. Third parties may also use Clear Gifs on the Service and we may embed Clear Gifs on the websites of our partners.

Collection, Use and Disclosure of Personally Identifying Information

Service Registration and Use

As defined above, Personally-Identifying Information is information that can be directly associated with a person.

To create a helpful experience for home buyers, their agents, and others, Amitree collects a range of Personally-Identifying Information from and about Service users. Much of the Personally-Identifying Information collected by Company about users is information provided by Service users themselves when they sign up on the Service to Amitree, while interacting with Amitree, or by others who the user has allowed to access their account (more about that below).

We ask this information to deliver to you a more personally relevant experience.

Users of the Service are under no obligation to provide us with Personally-Identifying Information of any kind. It is optional. However, if you do not, it may either prevent you from using the Service, certain Service features, or from really taking advantage of the Amitree services we provide.

Working with Others

Usually, the purchase of a home is a team effort. Home buyers may have co-buyers, work with real estate agents and brokerages, lawyers, contractors, and others. Amitree allows you to bring that team together, online. A home buyer may be invited to become a Amitree user by someone else on their team (usually, their real estate agent), or may sign up to be a user on their own. If a home buyer is a user, they may invite anyone else they want to use Amitree and have access to their account.

The home buyer user controls the Amitree account. The home buyer determines who has access to the Amitree account. You can allow others to view the information, and you can also allow others the ability to add and edit the information relevant to your home purchase, including the ability for them to grant others access to your account. If you have allowed others to access your Amitree account, you are consenting to the use and disclosure of the Personally-Identifying-Information in your Amitree account to those you have invited. If you have granted others the ability to edit the details about your home purchase, you have also consented to them providing or editing that information. You may determine who has access to your Amitree account, and their access privileges, by accessing your Amitree account.

If you provide us with the name and email address of someone else that you want to join Amitree and access your Amitree account – a friend, real estate agent, lawyer, contractor or anyone else -- be assured that we only use that information to contact them about the Service.
