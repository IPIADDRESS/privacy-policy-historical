Privacy Policy
This describes how we collect, use and share personally identifiable information ("PII") we obtain from and about individuals both online and off-line.

General
We respect your privacy. Any personal information that you provide to us through this web site will be used and safeguarded as described below. If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact us.

If you choose to email us and provide personally identifiable information about yourself, we will not use the information for any purpose other than to respond to your inquiry.

If you provide personally identifiable information about yourself to create a profile, we will not use the information for any purpose other than notifying you of your membership status, providing any newsletters you have requested, and notifying you of events and activities we believe you will find of interest. You may remove yourself from future mailings at any time by removing your profile.

We recognize that your privacy is important to you, and, therefore, we employ technical security measures that endeavor to protect information from outside threats. However, America's Most Wanted, its affiliates, and their service providers do not represent, warrant, or guarantee that personal information will be protected against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alterations. We do not accept any liability for personal information submitted, nor for any third parties' use or misuse of personal information.

Please contact us if you feel that this site is not following its stated policy.

Cookies
AMW.COM uses generic cookies that will neither provide nor contain any personal information and are solely for statistical purposes.

Additionally, if you request that the site remember you as a logged-in user on future visits, then a cookie will be set with an identifier that contains no personal information. This identifier allows us to identify you on return visits and automatically log you in for your convenience.

Reporting Tips
You can remain anonymous. If you choose to provide personal identifying information, then America's Most Wanted will take all reasonable steps to protect that personal information, in accordance with its Privacy Policy. America's Most Wanted may forward information that you provide to law enforcement, and by providing such information you consent to sharing it with law enforcement.

Leaving your name or any contact information is strictly VOLUNTARY. However, your tip might provide valuable insight and investigators may want to ask you for more information. Please let us know if law enforcement may get in touch with you regarding your tip, by leaving your name, phone number, email address or other contact information when you submit your tip.

America's Most Wanted respects your right to remain anonymous. Since 1988 we have maintained the trust of our viewers. Respecting the anonymity of those who report tips is a top priority at America's Most Wanted and is critical to our continued success in capturing fugitives and finding missing children.

Information Sharing and Disclosure
All visitors to AMW.COM may remain anonymous. In cases where you choose to provide personal identifying information we will not rent, sell, or share that information about you with outside parties except in instances where we provide products or services you've requested, when we have your permission, or under the following circumstances:
  • We believe it is necessary to share information in order to investigate, prevent, or take action regarding illegal activities, or potential threats to the physical safety of any person, or as otherwise required by law.
  • We transfer information about you if AMW.COM is acquired by or merged with another entity. Should this occur we will notify you before information about you is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy.
  • We may occasionally work with trusted partners who work on behalf of or with us under confidentiality agreements. These companies may join us in communicating with you. However, these partners will not have any independent right to your information.
  • You do not have to provide personal identifying information about yourself There may be instances when America's Most Wanted may disclose PII without providing you with a choice in order to protect the legal rights of America's Most Wanted, other companies within the News America Group or their employees, agents and contractors; to protect the safety and security of visitors to our web sites; to protect against fraud or for risk management purposes; or to comply with the law or legal process. In addition, if America's Most Wanted sells all or part of its business or makes a sale or transfer of assets or is otherwise involved in a merger or business transfer, America's Most Wanted may transfer your PII to a third party as part of that transaction.
