PRIVACY PRINCIPLES

Because your privacy is important to us, Astro operates this Web Site by the following principles:

Principle 1.
Astro explicitly asks when it needs information that personally identifies you ("Profile Information"). Astro asks for your first and last name, state and zip code, gender, year of birth, and occupation. You may update this information at any time from within your Astro web member account by clicking the "Update My Profile" button on the navigation bar, and then fill in the relevant information.

Principle 2.
Your Profile Information is used only for demographic statistics and to display appropriate individualized advertisements. Astro keeps all of your Profile Information private and does not share it with any third parties. Astro will not disclose your Profile Information unless acting under a good faith belief that such action is necessary to:

(1) conform to legal requirements or comply with legal process;
(2) protect and defend the rights or property of Astro;
(3) enforce the Astro Terms of Service; or
(4) act to protect the interests of its members or others.

Principle 3.
Astro will not send you any unsolicited information, including e-mail, except as mentioned below. New users will receive a Astro welcome letter explaining the features provided by the service. Under exigent circumstances, Astro may be required to send you information about the service or your account, but Astro will not send you unsolicited e-mail regarding any commercial offers or advertisements at any time.

Principle 4.
If at any time you believe that Astro has not adhered to these principles, please notify us by e-mail at privacy@Astro.com.my and we will use all commercially reasonable efforts to promptly determine and correct the problem.

Websites that have links on our site may collect personally identifiable information about you. The data protection practices of those websites linked to Astro are not covered by this policy statement. You are advised to check the data protection policies of these third party websites yourself before using those sites.

If you have questions about this policy, please e-mail us at smd_sub@Astro.com.my. For more information about our privacy statement, see the following Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs).

FAQs

(1) Does Astro collect other forms of information about its users?

In addition to the use of Profile Information to operate the site, Astro also gathers anonymous information, which may be used by Astro or shared with its advertisers. This is information which does not personally identify you, but which may be helpful for marketing purposes or for improving the services we offer. Generally, this information is collected through "traffic data" and may entail the use of "Cookies", "IP addresses" or other numeric codes used to identify a computer.

(2) How do I correct or change Profile Information that I have give to Astro?

You may update this information at any time from within your Astro account by clicking the "Update My Profile" button on the navigation bar, and then fill in the relevant information.

(3) Is my Profile Information secure from others using the Astro site?

Profile Information collected by Astro is stored in secure operating environments that are not available to the public.

(4) How else might someone learn of my Profile Information while visiting another site?

In addition to directly providing information to Astro, you might also provide Profile Information through chat sessions, e-mail exchanges or bulletin board use made available to you on other sites, including other sites. Chat sessions and bulletin board or newsgroup use is considered public and you are responsible for your actions in these areas. The Astro Privacy Statement does not cover this type of transfer of your Profile Information. You should always be careful when distributing your Profile Information in this manner.

(5) What are cookies?

Cookies are pieces of information that a Web site transfers to your computer's hard disk for record-keeping purposes. Cookies can make the Web more useful by storing information about your preferences on a particular site. The use of cookies is an industry standard, and many major Web sites use them to provide useful features for their customers. Cookies in and of themselves do not personally identify users, although they do identify a user's computer. Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies. If you'd prefer, you can set yours to refuse cookies. However, you may not be able to take full advantage of a Web site if you do so.
