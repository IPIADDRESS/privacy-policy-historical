Types of Information We Collect

We may collect two types of information from you: Personal Information and Browsing Information.

1. Personal Information. The types of Personal Information that Atlantic Health System collects may include, among other things, the following categories of information (collectively, “Personal Information”):

  • Contact information (e.g., your name, mailing address, telephone number, email address, password, and other login information);
  • Demographic information (e.g., date of birth, marital status);
  • Resume information (e.g., education, work and military history, legal work eligibility status);
  • Personal financial information, or other personal information that you choose to disclose to us.

In general, we do not collect Personal Information unless you choose to provide it. You are generally not required to submit any Personal Information to use our Site; however, you may be asked to provide Personal Information to gain access to some of our content. For example, you may choose to provide your contact information in order to receive information from us regarding our services or to register for an event.

2. Browsing Information. Atlantic Health System may collect browsing and other technical information about you when you visit our Site. This may include, among other things, the following categories of information (collectively, “Browsing Information”):

  • Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, or device ID/UDID, protocol and sequence information;
  • Browser language and type;
  • Domain name system requirements;
  • Browsing history, such as time spent at a domain, time and date of your visit, and number of clicks;
  • http headers, application client and server banners;
  • Operating system and fingerprinting data;
  • Geolocation; and/or
  • Similar behavioral and usage information.

How Information is Collected and Used:

We may collect Personal Information about you from the following sources:

  • Information you provide through correspondence that you and others direct to us, for example email communications; 
  • Information we receive from you, or on your behalf, through information you submit on our Site;
  • Information we receive from your computer or mobile device;
  • Information we receive from our partners or service providers; and
  • Information we receive from other sources, as permitted by applicable laws, rules and regulations.

We may collect Browsing information and use such information as follows:

  • Computer’s IP (Internet Protocol) address or browser type: Our web server automatically collects the IP address of the computers that access our Site. An IP address is a number that is assigned to your computer when you access the Internet. We use this information to gather aggregate demographic information about our visitors, and we may use it to personalize the information you see on our Site and some of the electronic communications you receive from us.

  • Pixel tracking: A tracking pixel is a piece of code embedded on a web page of a website that collects information about a user’s engagement on that page. Tracking pixels may include a command to create cookies that can follow a user across different websites, including non-Atlantic Health System sites.

  • Cookies: A cookie is a small piece of data (i.e., text file) that can be stored on a user’s browser (on any type of device). Cookies may be placed on your device both by us and by third parties with whom we have a relationship, such as web analytic services and advertising network services. We use cookies to track certain information about you based upon your activity on our site or on a third party’s site. This information may include the URL that you just came from or which URL you go to next, what browser you are using, and your IP address. We use this information to better understand and serve visitors to our Site, and to enable us to deliver information tailored to your interests and preferences. We also use cookies in conjunction with certain applications (for example with our web security application) to allow you to easily move through secured pages once you have logged on. If you would prefer not to receive cookies, you may be able to alter your browser’s configuration to refuse cookies.

  • Aggregated Statistics: We may collect statistics about the behavior of users on our Site. For instance, we may monitor the most popular forums on our Site. We may display this information publicly or provide it to others.

  • Web Beacons: Our Site may also contain electronic images known as Web Beacons (sometimes referred to as “clear gifs”). Web Beacons collect only limited information that includes a cookie number, time and date of page view, and a description of the page on which the Web Beacon resides. We may also carry Web Beacons placed by third party parties. These Web Beacons do not carry any personal information and are only used to track usage of our Site and activities associated with our Site, including but not limited to online features, services and/or programs offered.

Google Analytics. Like many websites, we also use Google Analytics to collect information about your use of our Site. This may include frequency of visitation, pages viewed and other sites visited prior to ours. Google sets cookies and may also read preexisting cookies to collect data from your web browser, such as the domain from which you access the Internet, the web address of the website from which you linked to our site, the time and date of your visit, the web pages that you view and click through, and your IP address. This information is stored on Google’s servers. Google uses this information to provide us with reports about traffic to our Site and your visit to our Site. Find additional information on how Google Analytics collects and processes data >

Google’s ability to use and share information collected by Google Analytics is governed by Google’s Privacy Policy. You may prevent Google Analytics from recognizing you on return visits to the Site by disabling cookies on your browser or you may opt-out of our use of Google Analytics by visiting the Google Analytics opt-out page and installing the Google Analytics browser add-on.

Retargeting and Remarketing. We have also enabled additional Google Analytics Advertising Features: Google Display Network Impression Reporting and Remarketing. The Google Display Network Impression Reporting uses the Google Analytics cookies to track our ad impressions and your interactions with those impressions, including the effectiveness of those ads, which generates reports for us to help us analyze our advertisements. Remarketing with Google Analytics uses the Google Analytics and/or a third party cookie to serve advertisements to you across the Internet based on your visit to our Site. We also use the Google Ads service to serve ads on our behalf across the Internet and sometimes on this Site. Google uses cookies (or other similar identifiers) to collect information about your visits to our Site and your interaction with our products and services to generate targeted advertisements to you on other sites that you visit across the Internet. Google’s ability to use and share information collected by Google Analytics about your visits to our Site is restricted by Google’s Privacy Policy. You may opt out of the Google advertising cookies by visiting the Google advertising opt-out page or you may opt out of Google Analytics by visiting the Google Analytics opt-out page. For additional information on Google Advertising, review Google’s personalized advertising policies.

The Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998 (“COPPA”) governs how information is gathered online and how it is used when communication is received from children under the age of 13. Although Atlantic Health System is a non-commercial, nonprofit organization and is not subject to COPPA, we take children’s privacy and safety very seriously.

We encourage parents to get involved with their children’s online usage and to provide guidance and be aware of personal information their children may be providing online. Our patient portal is available for use by children ages 12-17. Parents should be aware that this Privacy Policy will govern our use of Personal Information and Browsing Information, but also that information that is voluntarily given by children – or others – in email exchanges, bulletin boards, or the like may be used by other parties to generate unsolicited communications. We encourage all parents to instruct their children in the safe and responsible use and disclosure of their Personal Information while using the Internet.

If you are under the age of 13, please ask your parents to help you in using those parts of our Site that ask for Personal Information, and ask your parents for permission before you send any information about yourself (e.g., your name, address, email address, etc.) to Atlantic Health System or anyone else over the Internet, and before making any online purchases.
