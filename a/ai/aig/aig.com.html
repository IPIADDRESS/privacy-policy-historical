WHAT IS "PERSONAL INFORMATION"?  
    
The term "personal information" means any information that can be used to identify or contact a specific individual, such as a name, address, telephone number, e-mail address, or policy or claim number. The term "non-personal information" means information that does not allow us to identify or contact a specific individual, such as demographic information, purchase categories (e.g., life insurance), and technical data gathered through a Web browser, such as the particular browser used to access the Website, or through cookies and other similar technologies. The term "IP address" means a number that is automatically assigned to the computer that a visitor to the Website is using by the visitor's Internet Service Provider (ISP).  
    
WHAT TYPES OF PERSONAL INFORMATION DO AIG AND AIG COMPANIES COLLECT ON THE WEBSITE?  
    
We collect various types of personal information from visitors to the Website. For instance, we may offer visitors the opportunity to sign up to become a member of the Website ("Member"), by providing their e-mail address, and responding to other contact and product-related questions we may ask from time to time, in order to receive special e-mail offers and promotions available to Members.
    
AIG and AIG Companies also collect personal information through "contact us" forms, such as first name, last name, e-mail address, telephone number, policy number and any other personal information you include in a question or comment.
    
Depending on your relationship with us (for example, as a consumer policyholder; non-policyholder insured or claimant; witness; commercial broker or appointed representative; or other person relating to our businesses), certain AIG Companies may through other means collect other personal information, including sensitive personal information, such as social security numbers and health information, in the course of their business. Additional privacy notices will be provided by AIG Companies where relevant. Where applicable, we maintain privacy and security policies and procedures that protect social security numbers and other sensitive personal information by maintaining their confidentiality, prohibiting their unlawful disclosure, guarding against unauthorized access, establishing proper disposal procedures for them (such as shredding), and limiting their disclosure to third parties, except where required or permitted by law.
    
WHAT TYPES OF NON-PERSONAL INFORMATION DO AIG AND AIG COMPANIES COLLECT ON THE WEBSITE?  
    
“Cookies” and “Pixel Tags”
    
We constantly seek to maintain the integrity of, and make improvements to, the Website. As part of these efforts, we collect and analyze certain information about how visitors use the Website, as stated below.
    
When you visit our Website, we may collect various types of technical data including (but not limited to) domain name and/or Web browser information. This data may be gathered using cookies and other similar technologies. Cookies are small pieces of information that our server places on a visitor's computer hard drive to enable that person to more easily communicate and interact with the Website and to provide us with important information on how visitors use the Website. This Website uses cookies to allow you to select a preferred location view and default home page view and to let you access recently visited links (such as products, services, and individual AIG Company pages) more easily. Many Web browsers are automatically set to accept cookies. You may change your browser settings to either reject cookies or notify you when a cookie is about to be placed on your computer. Please note, however, that rejecting cookies while visiting the Website may result in certain parts of the Website not operating correctly or as efficiently as if they were allowed. For example, you will not be able to access a preferred location view, a customized default home page view or your recently visited links.
    
In addition, we may use a service that collects data remotely by using "pixel tags”, "web beacons", "clear GIFs", or similar means (collectively, "Pixel Tags") embedded in the Website's content. A Pixel Tag is an electronic image, often a single pixel (1x1) that is ordinarily not visible to Website visitors and may be associated with cookies on the visitors' hard drives. The data we collect includes which operating system and Web browser you use, how you navigate to and through our Website, and how long you stay on our web pages. This information does not include your name, e-mail address or any other personal information.
    
Please note that certain non-personal information may be linked with personal information. Any linked information will be treated as personal information for the purposes of this Policy as long as it is linked. 
    
“IP Addresses”
    
As part of our efforts to maintain and improve the Website, we also collect IP addresses. An IP address is usually identified and logged automatically in our server log files whenever a visitor comes to the Website, along with the time of the visit and the page(s) that were visited. Collecting IP addresses is standard practice on the Internet and is done automatically by many other websites.
