THE SHAGGIES: It’s not a dance, or a hairdo—or some variant on the delightful British slang for sex. It’s a nemesis that descends on the garden when spring has had it, leaving behind nothing but deadheads, underplanted with a whole new palette of emerging weeds–the warm-season kind. The long-reach pruner may prove our best friend (goodbye crispy lilac trusses).
