PRIVACY POLICY

This privacy policy explains what happens to any personal information that you provide to us, or that we collect during your visit. It forms part of our terms and conditions.

Information we collect
In order to run and maintain our website we may collect and process the following information about you:

i. Data about how you use and access our site, including details about your visit such as pages visited, images displayed, search terms used to access our site and information about your downloads. This information includes data about your location, browser, device, screen resolution and other communication data.

ii. Information provided voluntarily by you. For example, your email address and name when you contact us via the contact form.

iii. Information that you provide when you communicate with us by any means.

iv. Information in the exchange form and information in any documents you send along with the exchange form.

v. Any due diligence measures we carry out under the requirements of the Money Laundering Regulations (MLR), including identification documents that we have obtained.

vi. Feedback left by you about our service.

Use of cookies
Cookies are small pieces of data stored in your web browser when you visit a website. Like most sites, we use cookies to store data about your preferences and visit history. Examples for which we use cookies include your preferred payout currency (GBP, EUR, USD). We try to make our website as user friendly as possible, we use cookies to achieve this goal.

We use Google Analytics to collect web analytics data. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect user behaviour data. You can read more about it on this page about Google Analytics cookie usage

The information a cookie holds will not identify you personally. The data is statistical and anonymous. It is possible to change your browser settings to decline any cookies. Read more about controlling cookies on this website: http://www.aboutcookies.org/

Our advertisers may use cookies, over which we have no control. Such cookies may be downloaded once you click on advertisements on our website.

Use of your data
We use the data that we collect from you to provide our services to you. We are legally required, under the Money Laundering Regulations (MLR), to keep a record of all of our customers and to apply customer due diligence measures. In addition to this we may use the data for one or more of the following purposes:

i. To provide information about our services.

ii. To inform you about any changes to our services, website, address, exchange rates or terms and conditions.

iii. To request your opinion (feedback) about our services.

iv. To provide information about the origin of funds to a government, central bank, financial authority or any financial institution who is required to obtain this information under the Money Laundering Regulations (MLR) or a similar foreign legislation.

With the exception of the purposes stated above, we will not send you any unsolicited messages, neither by email, regular mail, text message, fax or telephone.

Storing your personal information
We are legally required, under the Money Laundering Regulations (MLR), to keep our records for five years.

We may keep our records in any of the following formats: originals, photocopies, scanned, computerised or electronic. Electronically stored data may be physically stored, transferred and processed outside of the European Union. By providing your personal data, you agree to this storing, transferring and processing.

We ensure that all reasonable steps are taken to keep your data stored securely. Unfortunately the sending of data via the Internet is inherently not totally secure and on rare occasions such data can be intercepted. We cannot fully guarantee the security of your data, as we do not have control of the Internet. The same applies for any data you send to us via regular mail, fax or telephone.

Disclosure of your data
We will not disclose your personal information to any other party other than in accordance with this Privacy Policy and in the circumstances detailed below:

i. In the event that we sell our business or any part of it: The buyer will receive our records.

ii. In events where we are legally required to disclose your personal data.

iii. To reduce the risk of fraud.

iv. In the event where we need to provide information to a government, central bank, financial authority or any financial institution about the origin of funds.

Third party links
On occasion we include links to third parties on our website. Although we carefully select any external links on our website, where we provide an external link it does not mean that we endorse or approve that site’s Privacy Policy. You should review any external site’s Privacy Policy before sending them any personal data.

Access to information
In accordance with the Data Protection Act 1998 you have the right to access any information that we hold relating to you. Please note that we reserve the right to charge a fee of maximum £10 to cover costs incurred by us in providing you with the requested information. To access your personal data, please send us a request in writing and send it to our address as specified on our website.

Contacting us

info@leftovercurrency.com
