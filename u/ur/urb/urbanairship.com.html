This Privacy Statement applies to information that we collect when you: (i) visit our websites including www.airship.com and www.apptimize.com (the “Sites”); (ii) use our mobile applications, mobile wallet passes, and other software applications made available by us for use on or through computers and mobile devices that link to this Privacy Statement; (iii) visit our branded social media pages; (iv) visit our offices; (v) receive communications from us, including emails, phone calls or texts; (vi) register for, attend and/or otherwise take part in our events, webinars or contests; (vii) submit a job application to us; or (vii) use the Airship Platform as an authorized user (for example, as an employee of one of our customers who provided you with login access to the Airship Platform where we act as a controller of your data (collectively, including the Sites, the “Services”).  “You” refers to any individuals who visits or uses the Services.

In general, with respect to the Personal Information we collect directly from Sites or other Services, Airship is a data controller. This means that as a data controller we determine the purposes for which and the manner in which the Personal Information we collect is to be processed. “Personal Information” is any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

This Privacy Statement does not apply to the extent we process Personal Information in the role of a data processor or service provider on behalf of our business customers, such as information we receive and process through Airship’s technology platforms (including the Airship Customer Engagement Platform, and the Apptimize Testing Platform) and related services (collectively, “Airship Platform”).  For privacy information related to our business customer’s use of the Airship Platform in connection with their mobile application, websites, emails, texts or other digital properties, please reach out to the respective business customer directly.

California residents and Customers with business in the State of California may read our CCPA Privacy Notice here.

In general, we collect information from you based upon our business relationship, your use of our Services, and includes information that may be deemed Personal Information, such as title, name, address, phone number, email address, user name, IP address, and billing and payment information related to payments for Services.

When you use the Services or fill out forms on our Site, we may provide you with opportunities to sign up to receive specific information or Services, download a white paper, or register for an event or webinar, and we may ask for your contact information (e.g., name, title, company name, company address, phone number or email address).  We also automatically collect information as described in Section 5 below.

Registering for and attending an event.  If you register to attend an event, we may ask for your contact information. If you attend an event, we may with your consent scan your attendee badge, which will provide us with your information, such as name, title, company name, address, country, phone number and email address.

Your communications with us. We collect Personal Information from you such as email address, phone number, or mailing address when you request information about our Services, register for our newsletter, request customer or technical support, apply for a job or otherwise communicate with us.

Surveys. We may contact you to participate in surveys. If you decide to participate, you may be asked to provide certain information which may include Personal Information. 

Social media content. We may offer forums, blogs, or social media pages. Any content you provide on these channels will be considered “public” and is not subject to privacy protections.

Registration for sweepstakes or contests. We may run sweepstakes and contests.  Contact information you provide may be used to reach you about the sweepstakes or contest and for other promotional, marketing and business purposes, if permitted by law. In some jurisdictions, we are required to publicly share information of winners. 

Visiting our offices.  If you visit our offices, you may be required to register as a visitor and provide your name, email address, phone number, company name and time and date of arrival.

2.2 Personal Information collected from others.

We may also receive your Personal Information from third parties who provide us with contact information for prospects or leads. When acquiring information from third parties, we will obtain contractual commitments from the third parties that the information was acquired lawfully and with the appropriate level of consent.

Research and development. We may use your Personal Information to create non-identifiable information that we may use alone or in the aggregate with information obtained from other sources, in order to help us to optimally deliver our existing products and Services or develop new products, processes and services.

Deidentified and aggregated data. Deidentified or aggregated information is not Personal Information, and Airship may use such information in a number of ways, including research, internal analysis, analytics and any other legally permissible purposes. Complying with legal obligations.  We may use your Personal Information when cooperating with public and government authorities, courts or regulators in accordance with our legal obligation under applicable laws to protect our rights or is necessary to protect against misuse or abuse of our Sites or Services, pursuing remedies available to us and limiting our damages, complying with judicial proceedings, court orders or legal processes or to respond to lawful requests.

Other Uses. We may use your Personal Information for other purposes disclosed to you at the time you provide Personal Information or with your consent.

5. What device and usage data does Airship process?

We use common information-gathering tools for collecting usage data, cookies, web beacons and similar technologies to automatically collect information that may contain Personal Information from your computer or mobile device as you navigate our Sites or use our Services.

5.1 Cookies, web beacons and other tracking technologies in our Services.   

As you navigate the Sites and use the Services, certain passive information may also be collected through the use of cookies, web beacons and other tracking technologies to obtain certain information about your computer, device, browsing actions, and patterns. This includes IP addresses, cookies IDs, navigational data, contents clicked, the name of the domain and host from which you access the Internet, the browser software you use and your operating system, your browser settings, your device type and similar information, the date and time you access our Services and other device identifiers. This type of information is used for the purposes of gathering data to provide improved administration of our Services and to improve the quality of your experience when interacting with our Services (see Your Privacy Rights section below for more information).

Cookies. A cookie is a small text file that a website saves on your computer or mobile device when you visit the website. Cookies are then sent back to the originating website on each subsequent visit, or to another website that recognizes that cookie, to develop a record of the user’s online activity. When you visit our Sites, we or an authorized third party may place a cookie on your browser and/or device, which collects information, including Personal Information, about your online activities over time and across different sites.  Cookies allow us to track usage, determine your browsing preferences and improve and customize your browsing experience.  

We use both session-based and persistent cookies on our Sites.  Session-based cookies exist only during one session and disappear from your computer when you close your browser or turn off your computer or device.  Persistent cookies remain on your computer or device after you close or turn off. You can control the use of cookies at the individual browser level, but choosing to disable cookies may limit your use of certain features or functions on our Services.  

Types of cookies on our Sites.

  • Essential Cookies. These cookies are necessary for the Sites to function and cannot be switched off. Some examples include: session cookies needed to transmit the website, authentication cookies and security cookies. These cookies also support certain features on the Sites, such as the live chat feature. You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the site will not then work.  
  • Functional Cookies.  These cookies enhance functions, performance and services on the Sites. They allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our Sites. Functional cookies also allow us to improve how our Sites function and to help us provide you with more relevant communications, including marketing communications.  They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around and use the Sites. We may use our own technology or third party technology to track and analyze usage information to provide enhanced interactions and more relevant communications and to enable certain features on the Sites.
  • Targeting Cookies.  These cookies track activity across websites in order to understand a visitor’s interest, and to direct specific marketing to them.  Targeting and advertising cookies may be set through our Site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant adverts on other sites.  

To change your cookie settings and preferences for the Site you are visiting use the Cookie Preference link at the bottom of the webpage.

Web beacons. We also use web beacons (sometimes called “pixel tags” or “tracking pixels”). These are tiny graphics files that contain a unique identifier that is embedded in our Sites and in our emails or other communications that are sent through our Services. This allows us, for example, to monitor the traffic patterns of users from one page within our Site to another, to track access to the communications sent through our services, to understand whether you have come to our Sites from an online advertisement displayed on a Third-Party website or whether you have viewed advertisements on our Sites or through the Services, or to improve our Sites’ performance. For more information about cookies, and how to disable them, please see “Your Privacy Choices” below.

SDKs. We may use third party APIs and software development kits (“SDKs”) as part of the functionality of our Services. APIs and SDKs may allow third parties including analytics and advertising partners to collect your personal information for various purposes including to provide analytics services and content that is more relevant to you.  For more information about our use of APIs and SDKs, please contact us as set forth below
