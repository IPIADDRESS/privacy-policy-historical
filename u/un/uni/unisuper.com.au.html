Website Privacy And Security Policy

1. The UniSuper Website provides a variety of information to the general public and to existing UniSuper members.  It also provides a mechanism for collecting personal and other information about prospective members and existing members. The information about you, which we collect and store, includes the following information:

(a) Information that identifies you, such as your name, date of birth, and address, which is collected when you register and may be updated by you from time to time by using this website;

(b) Tracking information which we collect as you use the site, which is used to improve the site for market research purposes but not used to identify you personally;

2. To access information about your account and to change your personal details you are required to enter a user name and password. The user name and password are used in order to protect your privacy and security.  It is your responsibility to ensure that you do not allow your user name or password to be disclosed to any other person.

3. If you use any link that is offered through this website then the policies of the organisation operating that linked site will apply to any information you provide about yourself once you have entered that link.  UniSuper is not responsible for the privacy or security of any such information.

4. Any information collected by UniSuper on this website may be shared within UniSuper and its related bodies corporate.

5. In order to provide a personalised service we allocate a unique identifier to your browser by using software known as a "cookie".  This software does not pass any personal information to UniSuper or any other organisation and is only used to identify your browser. Cookies store information regarding your preferences and other information specific to you and may be viewed by UniSuper and any other persons who have access to your computer.

6. Any personal information and any information generated through use of Cookies will be used in accordance with this Privacy and Security Statement for the purpose for which we collected it as well as any other purposes related to UniSuper's normal business operations.  Such related purposes include:

(a) Direct marketing of UniSuper products unless you have previously advised that you do not wish to be contacted.  The first time we contact you for marketing purposes we will give you an express opportunity to decline any further contact;

(b) Other purposes connected with the operation, administration, improvement and development of UniSuper;

(c) Where UniSuper suspects that fraudulent or other unlawful activity has occurred;

(d) Where use is necessary to lessen or prevent a serious or imminent threat to life, health or safety or such a threat to public health or safety;

(e) Where such use is otherwise authorised by or under any law;

(f) Use of your email address in order to alert you to any changes or alterations in UniSuper's service that affect you.

7. By enabling you to update your personal information through the "member services" section UniSuper takes reasonable steps to ensure that personal information is accurate, complete and up to date. Where your personal information has not been expressly updated by you, UniSuper cannot be responsible for the accuracy, completeness or currency of the information.

8. We will only disclose personal information;

(a) In accordance with this Privacy and Security Statement; or

(b) Where you have expressly or impliedly consented to such disclosure; or

(c) For the purposes of an investigation into suspected fraud or other unlawful activity; or

(d) Where such disclosure is required or authorised by or under any law; or

(e) When we believe that the disclosure is reasonably necessary to assist a law enforcement agency in the performance of its functions; or

(f) For the preparation or conduct of legal proceedings or implementation of the orders of a court or tribunal.

9. You are entitled to access and view personal information about you stored by UniSuper.  Should you have any queries regarding the information held about you, you may contact UniSuper at the email address, postal address or telephone in the "contact us" section of this website.  You are entitled to access any personal information about you held by UniSuper at any time.  UniSuper will only decline to provide you with such information where;

(a) To do so would pose a serious and imminent threat to life or health;

(b) It would be an unreasonable impact on the privacy of others;

(c) The request is frivolous or vexatious;

(d) There is actual or anticipated legal proceedings over the information;

(e) Providing access would be unlawful.
