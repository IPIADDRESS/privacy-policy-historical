    Voidu’s Website Privacy Policy provides clear and concise information on how visitors' information is collected, processed, and stored. It describes the types of data gathered and how this data is protected, as well as the way communication from third parties to Voidu is treated.  

    We are committed to a lawful, fair, and transparent processing of personal data, adhering to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).  

    Kinds of data and purposes of processing: 

    1. We process the following (categories of) personal data

    Name

    We collect the given names (first name, family name) of customers, and company name, names of job applicants, and names of contacts when they express interest in our services and/or sign up for our newsletter or create a user account on our web portal.

    Email address

    We collect the email address of customers, job applicants, and contacts when they express interest in our services and/or when they sign up for our newsletter, or create a user account on our web portal.

    Telephone number

    We collect the phone numbers of customers, job applicants, and of contacts when they express interest in our services or when they sign up for our newsletter.

    Address

    We collect address details for those users who create user accounts on our portal. This information is key for determining geographical[Office1]  area and regional release information for our content.

    Gender

    We collect gender details with our user account details, as this enables us to offer[Office2]  our users more specific tailored content and to generate accurate statistical data.

    Date of Birth

    We collect date of birth details as an age verification, so that only age-appropriate content is delivered.

    IP Address

    Your IP address is utilized to confirm your geographic region and geolocation. IP addressing is also utilized to determine which products are available to users within specific regional areas.

    2. What (purposes) do we use the data for

    We use the data listed under (1) to offer five services:

    • Exclusive Events Registration and other marketing and promotional programs;
    • Job applications;
    • A newsletter we periodically send to all our subscribers. We also add emails[Office3]  that we obtain from general inquiries to this list. Within each newsletter email, an opt-out link is provided;
    • User Account Management: We manage user accounts on our portal based on the user information provided. This includes Name, email address and physical addressing information, geographic region, and age details. This enables us to provide and track user preferences with regard to content and to provide regional localized content;
    • Financial Data: our payment vendors process your financial data, when you make a purchase through our website.

    3. What is our legal basis for processing this data

    We base our processing on consent of the data subject for the newsletter and user account profiles, the execution of a contract with the data subject for customers, and legitimate interest for job applicants. For job applicants, our legitimate interest is to have a pool of job applicant data that is current and complete.

    4.  Where do we obtain the data from (sources)

    We collect the data through forms submitted via our website, general inquiries by email, contact information provided at business meetings and in business dealings, and information in CVs submitted by job applicants. However, we do not advertise job openings directly on our website.

    5.  How long and where do we store data

    We collect the data through forms submitted via our website, general inquiries by email, contact information provided at business meetings and in business dealings, and information in CVs submitted by job applicants. However, we do not advertise job openings directly on our website.[Office4] [J5] 

    • We store customer information for at least 7 years to be able to meet our obligations to keep accurate records for the tax authorities;

    • We store user account data until the user opts to decommission their account.

    6.  What safeguards have we taken to protect data

    Taking into account the nature, scope, context, and purpose of the processing, we have taken the following technical and organizational measures:

    • Voidu employs an infrastructure of geolocated tenants to ensure that collected data remains within the legal jurisdiction of the region where it was collected;
    • Defense in depth methodologies are employed to ensure that all data storage resides within secured VPNs with strict access controls in place;
    • Encryption technologies are utilized to ensure the confidentiality and integrity of data at rest and in transit;
    • Active security monitoring is employed within our physical infrastructure. We actively monitor the security of our database but may need to rely on our partners and trade affiliates to notify us of any data breaches.

    9. Cookies on Voidu's websites

    Cookies are small pieces of information (small files) that a website leaves on your equipment (e.g. your computer). The website instructs the web browser with which you view websites (e.g. Internet Explorer) to store these cookies on your equipment. On Voidu's websites, three kinds of cookies are used:

    Functional cookies:
    Functional cookies are necessary to make a website function. Voidu uses functional cookies to facilitate navigation on the websites and to store specific user settings or user preferences to optimize your use of the websites.

    Analytical cookies:
    Voidu uses analytical cookies (including pixel tags) in order to measure, by means of the software of third parties like Google Analytics, how many visitors the websites get, which parts of the websites receive the most views, and what percentage of news recipients reads the online mailings. The statistical information collected is used to customize the contents of the websites or online mailings to the needs of visitors or readers in the best possible way.
