Privacy and Policy Document

INTRODUCTION

This website is owned and operated by United Christian Broadcasters Australia Ltd (hereafter referred to as UCB Australia). This Privacy policy protects you as a user of websites administered by UCB Australia. If you have any questions that are not answered in this document please email or contact us as per details below.

OUR COMMITMENT TO YOU

UCB Australia is committed to protecting your privacy. Any information that you provide to us remains private and is only used for the purposes outlined below. The identity of anyone who contacts us is kept confidential. We don't rent, sell or exchange our contact lists with any other organisation.

YOUR CONSENT

By using one of the UCB Australia Web sites, you consent to the collection and use of this information by UCB Australia. If we decide to change our privacy policy, we will post those changes on this page so that you are always aware of what information we collect, how we use it, and under what circumstances we disclose it.

UCB Australia also provides links to affiliated sites. The privacy policies of these linked sites are the responsibility of the linked site and UCB Australia has no control or influence over their policies. Please check the policies of each site you visit for specific information. UCB Australia cannot be held liable for damage or misdoings of other sites linked or otherwise.

What information do we collect? How do we use it?

  • Competitions, Donations and Promotional Features

    • When you donate or enter a contest or other promotional feature, we may ask for your name, address, and e-mail address or other information. This is so that we can administer the donation or contest, and notify winners or contact donors.

  • On-line surveys

    • Our on-line surveys may ask for contact information as well as some demographic information, to find out what we can do to better meet your needs through the various services provided by UCB Australia.

  • Statistical tracking

    • UCB Australia collects statistics on visitor traffic to this web site. All such statistics are anonymous and do not identify visitors in any way.
    For more information about any current technology used by UCB Australia to track visitors to our websites, please contact our Privacy Officer as per the details below.

How does UCB Australia protect my information if I make an online donation with my credit card?
We use a Secure Server for collecting personal and credit card information. The secure sockets layer (SSL) encrypts (scrambles) all of the information you enter before it is transmitted over the internet and sent to us. Furthermore, all of the data we collect is protected against unauthorized access.
Our secure server services certificate is issued by, Equifax Secure Global eBusiness CA-1. When you access the secure section of our site, you will see the identity of Equifax in the page's security settings. This is completely normal.

Our secure server services are administered by our web host, Geo Trust. When you log into the secure section of our site, you will see Geo Trust's identity in the page's security settings. This is completely normal.

What about "cookies"?
"Cookies" are small pieces of information that are stored by your browser on your computer's hard drive. Our cookies do not contain any personally identifying information, but they do enable us to provide download capabilities for software and other files. The Cookies feature of your browser must be turned on in order to remember which aspects of our websites you frequent which may be time or location related.

Will UCB Australia disclose the information it collects to outside parties?
UCB Australia does not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to others. We may provide aggregate statistics about our customers, sales, traffic patterns, and related site information to reputable third-party vendors, but these statistics will not include personally identifying information.

UCB Australia may release account information when we believe, in good faith, that such release is reasonably necessary to (i) comply with law, (ii) enforce or apply the terms of any of our user agreements or (iii) protect the rights, property or safety of UCB Australia, our users, or others. In the future, we may begin partnering with other groups in relaying information that we feel is of interest to our mailing list subscribers. Should we begin doing this, that will be reflected in this privacy policy.
