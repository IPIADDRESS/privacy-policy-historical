Aggregate Information.

When you use our products, we may collect tracking information such as the type of operating system you use, the domain name of your Internet service provider (ISP), your IP address, details about your computer hardware, your browser type, pages you visited on our Sites, and the applications you have installed on your computer, including our and our Partners’ products. None of this information personally identifies you and we do not link aggregate information to Personally Identifiable Information.

When you install our products, we may create a unique anonymous identifier to track installations and enable functionality and features for specific customers who subscribe to our products and services. We do not link the information gathered by this anonymous identifier to your Personally Identifiable Information.

Software Information. Software installed on your computer has unique registry keys. We use this information to determine whether our program offers are compatible with software already present on your PC and to avoid duplication of installed programs. We do not link such gathered information to our users’ Personally Identifiable Information.

Hardware Information. Your computer stores hardware information that we use in order to identify your computer’s hardware configuration, including processor speed and available memory. We use this information to determine system compatibility and we never link this information to your Personally Identifiable Information.

Cookies. A cookie is a small text file sent to your browser which is stored on your computer's hard drive for record keeping purposes. We and our Partners use both session ID cookies and persistent cookies. A session ID cookie terminates when you close your browser whereas a persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period afterwards. To remove persistent cookies, follow the directions provided in your Internet browser’s help file.

Some of the Partner offers on our products may use cookies. We have no control over our Partners’ privacy policies, their cookies, the information collected and its use.

Cookies enable us and our Partners to track visits to our and their sites and stores by collecting information which describes which pages you have seen and measures certain traffic patterns, such as which of our Sites you have visited. We use this information to understand our users' habits so that we can improve each subsequent experience on a Site or better personalize the promotions, content, and banners presented to you. We do not store Personally Identifiable Information in our cookies nor do we link the information in our cookies to our users’ Personally Identifiable Information.

Pixels. Web beacons or pixels from us or third parties (“Pixels”) are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, which track your online Web movement and which may be embedded virtually invisibly on Web pages. We and our Partners employ Pixels to better manage content on our sites, but we do not link the information gathered by Pixels to our users’ Personally Identifiable Information. With respect to third party Pixels, these third parties may also use anonymous information about your visits to websites in order to improve their products and services and provide advertisements about goods and services of potential interest to you. Some of the Partner Offers may also use Pixels. We have no control over our Partners’ Pixels or how they use information collected.

MAC Address. Your computer's MAC address is an alphanumeric identifier that acts as a unique code, assigned to your computer. We may collect your computer's MAC address when you install our products and use your computer's MAC address to track unique installations of our products. We do not combine your computer's MAC address with any of your Personally Identifiable Information.
