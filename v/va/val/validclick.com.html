COOKIES

To enable many of the features available at ValidClick, we may assign "cookies" to your Internet browser. A cookie is a small text file that contains a unique identification number stored on your computer. Cookies by themselves cannot be used to find out the identity of any user. This unique identification number automatically identifies your browser - but not you - to our computers whenever you interact with ValidClick or client site. Only when you agree to provide personally identifiable information to ValidClick or a ValidClick client will we be able to know anything about you other than the random number that our server computers originally assign to your browser.

ValidClick uses "cookie" technology to make your visits more convenient for you. We use cookies to give you the option of saving shopping cart and contact information so you don't have to enter it repeatedly when you are navigating through a ValidClick client site. These are set so we may better customize your experience on ValidClick for your future visits. You are free to reject or "Not Accept" the placement of some or all of our cookies.

There is a lot of confusion about cookies, how they are used, and what they can do. Cookies only contain information that the server computer "knew" in the first place, and can only be read by the serving domain that placed them. For instance, if XYZ.com (a fictitious company) utilizes cookies, ValidClick's servers can't read their contents nor can XYZ.com's servers read ours. Cookies are an essential part of how the Internet works today, and allow server computers to recognize your computer's browser when you return. If you have ever visited a Web page that automatically provides personalized content without you having to "sign in," such as your own stock portfolio, this is almost certainly being accomplished with a cookie. Since cookies are only text files, they cannot "run" on your computer and have no ability to search your computer for other information, or transmit it to anyone.
