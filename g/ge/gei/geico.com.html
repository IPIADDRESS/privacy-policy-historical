Privacy Policy

GEICO respects the fact that your on-line privacy is very important to you. We are continually developing our site with these concerns in mind. You have trusted us with your insurance needs for over 60 years, and it is important to us to maintain a high degree of integrity. Therefore, GEICO does not sell or disclose your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. In addition, we hold our employees to strict guidelines when collecting and/or reviewing your personal information. Below we discuss specific issues that may be of concern to you and your family. If you have additional questions after reviewing this policy, please contact us at the addresses provided at the end of this privacy policy statement.

GEICO collects information in three categories when you visit our Web site. GEICO stores information collected by general browsing, when you request a quotation, and when you request policyholder service transactions in a secure environment behind our firewall. GEICO may use your E-mail address from time to time to communicate new services, special offers, and to confirm completion of transactions.

  When you are browsing the Web site for general information, browsing patterns are stored in our database for review. GEICO reviews customer web browsing patterns to enable us to enhance pages of particular interest to you.
 
  When you complete a request for an on-line auto policy quote, the information is collected to help us offer you proper coverage. Your information is also used to determine if you qualify for any of our many discounts. If you have recently completed an on-line auto policy quotation, your name will remain in our quotation system database for 90 days. If you have shown interest in obtaining a quote in the past you may receive a courtesy reminder when your current policy approaches the renewal date. When you have asked for a rate quotation, GEICO may request credit information in accordance with applicable state law. GEICO employees will not have access to the details of your credit information. When GEICO does request credit information, the inquiry will be reflected in your personal credit file maintained by credit reporting agencies. If you commit to purchase an auto policy with GEICO, you give us permission to confirm your motor vehicle record and claims history.
 
  When you log on to our Policyholder Services site, you will be
asked to fill in information to process your policy change
request. This information will be saved in our database for
quality assurance.

If you are using a security-enabled web browser such as Microsoft Internet Explorer 2.0 or better, or Netscape Navigator 2.0 or better, the information you send to us is encrypted. We use an industry standard Secure Socket Layer to allow for encryption of sensitive personal information such as name, address, credit card or bank account information you have been requested to provide on-line.

GEICO places a small text file called a "cookie", on your hard drive to remember certain information. This information will not be shared with anyone outside of GEICO.
