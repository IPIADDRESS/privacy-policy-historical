How do the Classifieds work?
All members can add any pro audio eBay listings they are currently running to the Classifieds free of charge.

In order to participate in our own private buying and selling system, a one-time $20.00 (USD) fee is payable via PayPal. This will allow you to list items you wish to sell (FS), trade (FT) and post 'want to buy' (WTB) requests for items you’re looking for. Instant access will also allow you to make direct contact for items listed for sale or for trade by our 300k+ membership base.

Please note that the fee MUST be paid via PayPal - no other payment methods are accepted (but you do not need to hold a PayPal account in order to make payment) - access will not be granted under any other circumstances.

To apply for Classifieds access please click here to be redirected to our payment portal.

Can manufacturers, equipment vendors or equipment industry insiders join & post on Gearslutz.com?
Yes... However, they need to be mindful that the site is primarily a gear USERS forum and should NOT post only to promote their products (except in the New Products forum - see below) or to generate sales.
The following behavior is also discouraged by manufacturers, equipment vendors, and equipment industry insiders (post deletion may result if not carefully observed).
1) Posting details of past or ongoing disputes between manufactures, gear vendors or distributors. Please note THE SITE IS NOT TO BE USED FOR AIRING "DIRTY LAUNDRY".
2) 'Trashing' of other manufacturers products or rival dealers product range is unwelcome by dealers & manufacturers. This includes suggestions not to buy, that products are poorly made etc. We also do not approve of 'speculation' on how your competitors' products are made/work/etc.
3) Manufacturers are VERY WELCOME and encouraged to post up a notice / press release about new products in our New Product Alert forum. (Posting the same info in multiple forums however is NOT ALLOWED!)
4) Dealers and manufacturers are welcome to post up notices of 'gear shoot-out' evenings and or special product launch demonstrations at their showrooms or studios hosting their event.
5) It is best to register under your real name or company name so people know who is posting. (please remember - It is STRICTLY FORBIDDEN for anyone to have more than one forum membership (discovery of alternate or bogus identities made for the purposes of covert equipment promotion, trashing of rivals or any other covert activity will result in a BAN from the site.) Putting your company affiliation in your forum signature is strongly recommended.
6) To post info on competitions, giveaways and group buys please email promotions AT gearslutz dot com for the rules. This privilege tends to only be extended to advertisers, so please keep this in mind.


Could it be said there was one rule for some people and another for others?
YES. With thousands of recording engineers registered, the bias must be towards them and not manufacturers or equipment dealers. Thus, in some way, the sites reputation, one of of genuine equipment user opinion, can be preserved. Many equipment vendors are also engineers but in the case of inter company disputes & trashing of rivals, this forum's hammer falls on the side of these individuals being gear dealers. Keep it OUTSIDE, folks!
Jules, the site admin is VERY HAPPY to advise and answer any questions on how manufacturers, gear vendors & distributors can best participate in the forum and deal with situations that may arise. Please do NOT hesitate to contact him at jules @ gearslutz.com at any time. Thank you!

Affilate link disclosure
gearslutz.com has implemented SkimWords technology. It turns certain specific words in posts into links to specific retailers' websites. For example, if someone includes the text 'Apple.com' in their post, the technology might turn it into a link to Apple's website. It doesn't do every possible link, just a few. Note: At present this is disabled for logged in users in most forums. The SkimWords links are obvious since when you mouse over them, the standard tool tip text indicates that it has been created by SkimWords. It is hoped hope that people will appreciate that income from this will go towards maintaining the forums and will not object to links being added into posts and that some of the resulting links will be considered useful. If, however, you find a SkimWords generated link which is not relevant, please report the post to the moderators and we will feed back any issues to the SkimWords tech dept. Thank you

Why is member XXXXXXX deleted or banned?
Members can get banned for any number of reasons. These include, but are not limited to: 1) Persistent rudeness and insults to forum members 2) Persistent use of foul/"locker room" language 3) Persistent complaints about the way the forum is run, it moderators or its administration 4) Persistent posting of off topic threads (political threads, threads that bear no relation to the sub forum guidelines, etc) 5) Persistent wasting of moderators time by indulging in flame wars & bickering We don't issue statements on the circumstances of events that lead to people being banned nor can we comment or give any info out on any user account status other than your own.
