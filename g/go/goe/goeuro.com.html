I. Information we collect

  1. Information collected via Technology

    In an effort to improve the quality of the Site and the Service, we track information provided to us by your browser when you view the Site or use the Service, such as your Internet service provider, the website you came from (known as the “referring URL”), the type of browser you use, your operating system, the device from which you connected to the Service, the time and date of access, the Site pages viewed, time spent at our Site, the sites visited just before and just after our Site, and other information. We track this information using cookies, or small text files which include an anonymous unique identifier. Cookies are a feature of web browser (Internet Explorer, Firefox, etc.) software that allows web servers to recognize the computer used to access a site. They are small pieces of data that are stored by a user's web browser on visiting a site to simplify subsequent interactions with that site by the same user or to use the information to streamline the user's transaction on related web pages. This makes it easier for a user to move from site to site and to complete transactions over the Internet. Cookies should make your online experience easier and more personalized. You can find more information about cookies and how to manage them at http://www.allaboutcookies.org/.

    Cookies are sent to a user’s browser from our servers and are stored on the user’s computer hard drive. Sending a cookie to a user’s browser enables us to collect information about that user and keep a record of the user’s preferences when utilizing our services, both on an individual and aggregate basis. For example, GoEuro may use the following types of cookies:

    If you click through to a third party website from the links provided on the Site or via the Service (to make a reservation or purchase a travel ticket, for example), these other websites may also place a cookie on your browser. We have no control over and are not responsible for cookies set by third party websites or applications, and we encourage you to read the cookies information provided on those other.

    GoEuro may use both persistent and session cookies; persistent cookies remain on your computer after you close your session and until you delete them, while session cookies expire when you close your browser.

    You may disable cookies by changing the settings on your browser. Check the help menu in your browser for further information. Remember that if you disable cookies it is likely to affect how our website works and you may not be able to access or use certain areas of our site or functionality (such as setting up your profile, or saving your preferred travel mode, for example). Unless you have adjusted your browser setting so that it will refuse cookies, our system will place cookies when you log on to our Site.

    • Strictly necessary cookies, which are essential in order to enable you to move around the Site and use its features and without which services you have asked for, search as travel searches and bookings history, cannot be provided.
    • Performance cookies, which collect information about your use the Site, for instance which pages you go to most often, how you use the travel search functions and which travel company websites you visit from the Site. These cookies don’t collect information that identifies you, and are only used to improve how the Site works.
    • Functionality cookies, which allow the Site to remember choices you make (such as your travel mode preferences (e.g. air, rail or bus), your home city and closest train station, bus station and airport, your preferred currency, your user name if you create a profile etc., and provide enhanced, more personal features.
