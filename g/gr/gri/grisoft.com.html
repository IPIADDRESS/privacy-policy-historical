In order to do our job, at times we may need access to certain data on your PC, tablet, mobile phone or other applicable device. We respect your right to privacy.     What Information Does AVG Technologies Collect?

    The type of Personal data and information that we collect and retain depends upon your interaction with our Site and/or our AVG Products. Here is a short overview of the two main types of interactions.

    INFORMATION YOU GIVE US

    We collect the Personal data and information you provide to us on our Site or through our AVG Products, including your name, address, email address, telephone number, and if you pay by credit card, your account information. This happens when you:

    • Create a user account, place an order, register a product
    • Requesting product support or other services
    • Request information about AVG Products
    • Participate in surveys

    Our AVG Products can collect information about your PC, tablet, mobile phone or other applicable device, about files stored or applications installed and sites you have accessed when they identify threats or potential threats. This information, with your consent is transmitted back to AVG. It is possible that on rare occasions this may provide Personal data and information. Be assured we always treat such information in accordance with this Privacy Policy and applicable laws.

    You have the final decision on whether to proceed with any activity that requests Personal data and information.

    INFORMATION COLLECTED AND STORED AUTOMATICALLY

    When you interact with AVG Products or Sites we may maintain a partial or full record about some or all of the following types of information:

    • Your Internet Protocol ("IP") address or other technical information such as a referring web site, if any, and the path you take through AVG Technologies Sites when you visit us online
    • The date and time, the internet address of the website from which you link directly to our website
    • The name of the file or the words you search, and the browser you use to access our website
    • Information about malicious applications, files and other potential threats on your PC, tablet, mobile phone or other applicable device, their behavior and origin
    • Files and the content of files that contain threats or potential threats
    • Information about your device (PC, tablet, mobile phone or other applicable device) like device ID, device name, third party applications, types and number of files and programs on such device, and device settings. Specifically for mobile phones we may also collect serial numbers of SIM cards, serial numbers of the phone, third party apps installed on the device, installation and uninstallation rates, language and manufacturer of the device, screen size and model of the device, the location of the device and the mobile network (mobile carrier) you use but not the phone number

    The information collected and stored automatically by our Site is used to measure the number of visitors to it and to identify system performance or for server issues. We also use this information to help us expand the coverage of our Site and to make it more useful.

    How AVG uses cookies

    We aim to make our services as reliable and easy-to-use as possible. Therefore, when you visit our website, we will place small amounts of information on your device (e.g., computer or mobile phone). These include small text files called “cookies” that are saved in your web browser’s directory. There are different types of cookies but in a nutshell, the main job of cookies is to make our website work more efficiently for you and to enable certain website functionality.

    WHAT DO COOKIES AVG USES DO?

    Cookies help to improve your overall browsing experience, mainly:

    • Help you navigate between different pages on the website efficiently.
    • Remember login and preferences you have given.
    • Save you the hassle of giving us the same information (like your username and password) several times during a single visit.
    • Measure how people are using our services in order to improve our services and browsing experience.
    • Personalize advertising and make the content more relevant for you.

    We cannot know your name or your e-mail address from the cookie, unless you choose to give this information to us in a different manner. You may find further information at the following website www.allaboutcookies.org.

    MANAGE YOUR COOKIES PREFERENCES

    When you visit avg.com website, we show you a notice which tells you how and why we use cookies. If you have not made any changes after having seen this notice, we assume that you are happy with such arrangements and will take this as your agreement. We do not need to seek your agreement for technical cookies which are strictly necessary in order to provide you with our service.

    You may at any time change your cookies preferences. If you decide to cancel your previous agreement with accepting cookies, please click here. In such case, cookies set by avg.com domain will be deleted from your device, with the exception of the technical cookies. You are also free to disable cookies from your web browser at any time, but doing so may prevent you from using specific parts of websites on the Internet, including those at Avg.com. Some cookies are even essential in order for you to use Avg.com. You may find further information about the cookies we use and their functions in the section below 'Which types of cookies do we use' .

    WHICH TYPE OF COOKIES DO WE USE?

    The following table lists all of the cookies used at Avg.com and describes their purpose:

    Cookie Group Purpose
    Technical cookies These cookies are necessary for proper functionality in certain areas of our website, such as enabling the e-commerce part of our site, offering you product and pricing combination relevant for your region, allowing us to balance the load on our servers, recording user preference for cookies usage, etc. Cookies in this category comprise both session as well as persistent cookies. The site or its parts would not work well or at all without these cookies, therefore these cookies are always used, regardless of user preferences. Cookies in this category are always set by the Avg.com domain.
    Analytics cookies These cookies are used to collect information about how visitors use the AVG website. We use the information to compile reports and to help us improve our site, make it more user-friendly and monitor that it is performing correctly. The cookies collect information in an anonymous form about visitor’s activity on our website, as well as about from where visitors have come to the site and the pages they have visited. Cookies in this category are set by Avg.com or third party domains.
    3rd party tracking cookies These cookies are used to collect information about how visitors use our website and what keywords they used which led them to our site, which websites they have visited, as well as traffic sources where they come from in case of our marketing campaigns. We use this information to compile reports and to help us improve our site. The cookies collect information in an anonymous form. These cookies are set either by the Avg.com or third party domains.
    3rd party integration cookies These cookies are used to integrate 3rd party functionality in our website, such as YouTube videos, feedback forms or social media icons that allow visitors to share our content. Cookies in this category can be set by the following domains: YouTube.com, Twitter.com, Linkedin.com, Facebook.com.

    California Online Privacy Protection Act Statement

    In response to the recently enacted law A.B. 370, amending the California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA), AVG states the following:

    (1) Because AVG does not “track” the people who visit its website, AVG.com does not respond to “do not track” signals sent from web browsers.

    (2) When people visit AVG.com, AVG does not allow third parties to collect personally identifiable information (PII) about an individual’s online activities undertaken over time and/or across different websites.

    What does this mean for you?

    As “The Online Security Company”, AVG Technologies knows that you care about how your PII is used and shared. AVG is in the security business to protect you—and keep you educated as well. The following background information will help you learn some more about the key terms around the DNT concept and how AVG continues to respect your privacy.

    What is Do Not Track?

    DNT is a concept introduced by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) for the Internet industry. It is designed to help the industry develop and implement a mechanism that would provide you with a choice. Specifically, DNT is a way for you to choose whether you want to allow websites to collect information about your Internet activity when browsing, which could be principally used to deliver targeted advertisements to your devices.

    What is a DNT signal?

    A DNT signal carries information about your tracking preferences, which is sent to the online service operator, such as a website (here, AVG). Technically speaking, the usual means of delivering this signal is through a DNT header set in your browser.

    How does AVG respond to the signal?

    While there is no single set of guidance on how to address the CalOPPA requirements, we can faithfully disclose how AVG responds to the DNT signals it receives.

    Being a security and privacy company, our practices as such are privacy aware.

    Currently, AVG does not respond to the DNT signals because we do not “track” our users. In other words, AVG’s practice does not meet the definitions of “tracking” as outlined in the above mentioned opinions. AVG does not provide people’s PII to third parties (unless these parties supply AVG with outsourced services, which then makes them subject to AVG’s policies). Additionally, AVG never combines your collected PII with tracking data collected on other web sites.

    Does AVG allow third parties to collect PII about your online activities over time and across different Web sites when you use our web site?

    No. From our site you may be able to access other sites, such as when you access Facebook or Twitter via a link that you click. This action is subject to the policies and practices of those companies.
