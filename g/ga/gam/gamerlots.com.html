4. Use of Information and Information Sharing

4.1 Cookies

A "cookie" is a small file that contains a line of characters that is sent to your computer when you visit a website. When you visit the website again, the cookie allows that site to recognize your browser. However, we do not use cookies to store personal information such as your name. You can always disable cookies or set your browser to alert you when cookies are being sent to your computer.

Thus, Google, as a third party supplier, uses cookies to serve ads on our site. Even though this type of cookies make it possible to serve targeted advertisements in your browser (based on your search interests and previous browser history, for example) they do not any “personally identifiable information” like name, physical address, phone number, social securityor bank account number. You may opt out of the use of the DART cookie by visiting http://www.networkadvertising.org/managing/opt_out.asp.
