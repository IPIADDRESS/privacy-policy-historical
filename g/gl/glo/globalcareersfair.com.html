We do not collect personal information unless you choose to provide it to us. 1.1.2 Demographic Information: information that is not unique to you in the sense that it refers to selected population characteristics, such as your ZIP code or postal code, age, preferences, gender, race or ethnicity, occupation, career history, interests and favorites. 1.1.3 Behavioural Information: information relating to how you use the Platform, what services you access, and information about your computer hardware and software including your IP address, geolocation, browser and operating system type, domain names, access times and referring Web site addresses. This information is necessary for analysing the use of resources, troubleshooting problems, preventing fraud, and improving our services. 1.2 Information you post in public areas of the site or placed in the searchable CV database will be accessed, used, and stored by others around the world, including those in countries that might not have legislation that guarantees adequate protection of personal information as defined by your country of residence. COOKIES 6.1 What are Cookies? 6.1.1 Cookies are small pieces of text saved on your computer. The text is saved on to your browser and acts as a memory, allowing websites that you use to remember your preferences and behave accordingly. Cookies do not contain viruses. 6.2 GCF’s use of Cookies 6.2.1 GCF use Cookies to differentiate users of the Global Careers Fair from one another and recognise you when you log on to the Platform from the same computer. They help us to make sure that, when a user logs on to the Platform, they can see their information. The Cookies also allow information to be shared with another user on request. For example, when a user applies for a job on the Platform, we need to provide their information to the employer and use Cookies to do so. 6.2.2 GCF use Code Managed Cookies on the Platform and store the following information: (a) First name; (b) Last name; (c) User ID; (d) User type ID; (e) Producer event ID; (f) Email address; and (g) City. 6.2.3 GCF does not use Cookies to provide any information unless it is required to help the users get the benefit of using the Platform. 6.2.4 All Cookies used on the Platform are set to expire automatically after the user’s session has been idle for two hours. 6.3 Your choices 6.3.1 You can change your browser settings to have your computer warn you every time a Cookie is being sent, or you can turn off all Cookies. 6.3.2 Turning off Cookies will prevent proper use of the Platform and the Services provided by GCF. Cookies must remain on if you are to use the Platform. The Platform will still function and Services can still be provided if you change your browser settings to receive a warning every time a Cookie is sent. 6.3.3 Each different browser will have a different way of changing the settings. To view and to change your browser settings, you should use the Help menu on your browser and follow the instructions. 7.
