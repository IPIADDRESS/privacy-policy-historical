Blog Usage

It is possible for you to visit the Blog and receive information regarding KMC and related news without providing us with any of your personal information.  When you use the Blog, our servers automatically record information that your browser sends whenever you visit a website.  These server logs may include information such as your web request or visit, your prior web site address, your next web site address, Internet Protocol address, browser type, browser language, the date and time of your visit or posting and one or more cookies that may uniquely identify your browser.

KMC takes appropriate security measures to protect against unauthorized access to or unauthorized alteration, disclosure or destruction of data.  These include internal reviews of our data collection, storage and processing practices and security measures, as well as physical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to systems where we store personal data.

Use of Cookies

Kia-BUZZ may collect and store information about you on your computer through the form of an internet “cookie.”  A “cookie” is a small file of characters that is stored in your computer after your first visit, which uniquely identifies your browser.  For example, in order to leave a comment, you are required to enter your name, email address and as a result, it will be filled automatically through the use of a “cookie.”  “Cookies” are used to provide you with a more personal experience on Kia-BUZZ.  Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies, but you can reset your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent.  Some Blog features and services may not function properly if your cookies are disabled or deleted.

If you have any comments or questions regarding our Policy, or KMC’s treatment of personal information, please contact Kia-BUZZ editor. When we receive written complaints, it is our policy to contact the complaining user regarding his or her concerns.  We will cooperate with the appropriate regulatory authorities, including local data protection authorities, to resolve any complaints regarding the transfer of personal data that cannot be resolved between KMC and an individual.
