Use of Cookies

We may collect information about your general internet usage such as your IP address, geographical location, browser type, referral source, length of visit and number of pageviews by using a cookie file.

What is a cookie file? It's a text file sent by a web server to a web browser to be stored. The text file is sent back to the server every time the browser requests a page from the server. Thus, cookies enable the web server to identify and track the web browser.

We use cookies to improve the website's usability and to personalize your online experience. Most browsers automatically accept cookies, however, you are allowed to refuse or decline cookies. This will, unfortunately, render some website features non-functional, which will deny you to experience the services to its fullest. Some cookies are essential for the site to operate, such as the cookies used to identify users when they log in.

When you sign up on Keeko and sign in with your Twitter, Tumblr, Google plus, LinkedIn account, you are already giving permission to the use of cookies. However, we have no control over our advertisers that also use cookies.

Keeko secures your personal information and takes reasonable precautions to prevent the loss, misuse, alteration of your data as well as unauthorized access, use or disclosure from outside parties.

However, with the inherently unsecure data transmission on the internet, Keeko cannot guarantee security of data sent over the web. In addition, you are responsible for keeping your access (username and password) confidential.
