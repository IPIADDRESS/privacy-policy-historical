    This Privacy Policy applies both to personal information collected on web sites provided and hosted by Schoolwires for your school, school district or other organization (the “school”), and also to personal information which may be collected when individuals visit the Schoolwires corporate web site. Many of the provisions of this Policy apply equally to the personal information collected on Schoolwires-hosted sites as well as on the corporate web site, while other provisions are more specific to our practices regarding the collection, use and sharing of personal information when Schoolwires is serving in a particular role.

    When hosting a web site for the school, Schoolwires, Inc. is proud to power your Internet site. We are dedicated to providing easy-to-use, powerful and affordable Internet sites. When visiting our corporate web site, we are proud of the leading role we play in providing community-based website solutions to the k-12 education market and encourage you to learn more about the valuable solutions that we offer.

    At Schoolwires, we are equally committed to protecting your privacy. This Policy was developed to give you a full understanding of terms and conditions relating to information collected by Schoolwires as the provider of the school's web site and in connection with your visit to our corporate web site.

    For web sites powered and hosted by Schoolwires, this Privacy Policy pertains only to the personal information that Schoolwires collects from you or that is provided to us by the school. Personal information, also referred to at times as personally identifiable information, is information that identifies a particular individual, such as a person’s first and last name, postal address, e-mail address and phone number.

    In connection with the school’s Schoolwires-hosted site, this Privacy Policy applies only to Schoolwires’ own practices regarding personal information which is collected on the ASP-Delivered site hosted by Schoolwires and NOT to sites hosted by the school or a Third-Party Hosting Facility. Schoolwires has been retained by the school to provide and host the website and community management platform on which the school has added customized content to build its website. The school administers and oversees the website and, accordingly, has control over who can register for use of various features of the site and who can access the personal information posted or maintained on the site. The school can also add content and information directly onto the site. The school may have its own policies addressing collection, use and sharing of personal information. For information about whether this Privacy Statement applies to you and about the school's own policy, please contact the school’s webmaster.

  • Privacy of Children; Privacy of Students

    Schoolwires is dedicated to the privacy of children under 13 years of age. We do not collect or entice (by use of contests, games, prizes, or other enticements) from children more information than is needed to access services. Before Schoolwires collects personal information from children under 13, the school must first obtain a signed parental consent form. We will not share any personal information about children with any third parties other than as specified in this Policy.
     
    As a parent or legal guardian for children under 13, you may review your child's personal information. After your child's parental consent form is submitted, an individual at the school will supply you with your child's Sign-In and Password so that you may access and review the types of personal information collected from your child. If you would like to refuse to allow any further collection or use of your child's information, please contact the school’s webmaster. You may also raise questions or concerns with Schoolwires via the Contact Information provided below.
     
    Schoolwires is also dedicated to maintain the privacy of all students, whether under 13 or older, in accordance with applicable laws. In this regard, Schoolwires will maintain any and all personally identifiable information in education records which come into the possession of Schoolwires in accordance with the requirements of the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (“FERPA”), and will not disclose such information to third parties except with the consent of a parent, the school or an eligible student (as applicable), unless otherwise permitted under FERPA and its associated regulations. One instance in which personally identifiable information in education records may be disclosed to third parties under FERPA is if the information is classified as “directory information” by your educational institution. Please check with the school’s webmaster if you have any questions as to which information they have classified in this manner.

  • Terms which Apply to all Personal Information Collected by Schoolwires

    Modifications to Personal Information
    There are three ways your personal information can be potentially modified, depending on your role:
    1. For users of a site hosted by Schoolwires for your school, as well as users of our corporate website, if your personally identifiable information changes (such as ZIP code, phone, email or postal address), or if you no longer desire full access to Schoolwires services, you may update or delete your personally identifiable information. This can be done by Signing In and clicking on the Access My Info button at the top of the school's page.
     
    2. For Schoolwires-hosted websites for schools, Site and Subsite Directors have access and the ability to update personal information. These Editors only have access to their school's user information. For children under 13 years of age, the school is responsible for first validating receipt of the parental consent form.
     
    3. For Schoolwires-hosted websites for schools, Schoolwires may gather information from Site Directors in the school which would allow Schoolwires to conduct a mass user import to update user information. For children under 13 years of age, the school is responsible for first validating receipt of the parental consent form. 
     
    Links
    The school's site, and/or our corporate website, may contain links to other sites. Schoolwires is not responsible for the privacy practices of other such sites. Schoolwires privacy statement applies solely to information collected by Schoolwires.
     
    Surveys and Feedback Forms
    From time to time, our site may request information from you through surveys or feedback forms. Participation in these surveys or feedback forms is voluntary. If personal information is requested, Schoolwires will fully disclose this to you prior to submission. Information collected is used for the purposes of improving our product and services and understanding our audience and no personal information collected in this manner will be used or disclosed except in compliance with this Policy.
     
    Security
    Schoolwires takes security seriously and employs reasonable security measures and procedures designed to protect your information from unauthorized access and improper use. Only employees, trusted contractors supporting the operation of the website and other third party partners and contractors expressly identified in this Policy, who are all bound to protect the information appropriately have access to personally identifiable information.
     
    For Schoolwires-hosted sites, as well as our corporate web site, the servers that store personally identifiable information are maintained in a physical environment that utilizes industry-standard security measures. Personal information is stored in password-controlled servers with limited access. Please be aware though that, despite our diligent efforts and implementation of reasonable security measures, no security measures are perfect or impenetrable and no data transmissions over the Internet can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. If you have any questions about the security of the school's web site, please send a message to security@schoolwires.com.
    If the school hosts your site or chose to host with a Third-Party Hosting Facility, the school or the Third-Party Hosting Facility is responsible for ensuring security. In addition, as noted above, the school has the ability to control the collection, use and dissemination of personal information maintained or processed through the website. Contact the school’s webmaster for information regarding its security provisions. 
     
    Schoolwires Privacy Policy Statement Changes
    If we change our privacy policy, we will post the revised policy to www.schoolwires.com/policies and other places we deem reasonable and appropriate to keep you informed. When we believe material changes affecting users under 13 years of age are made to this privacy policy in connection with websites we host for schools, Schoolwires will notify the school and request that the school obtain verifiable parental consent to the extent required, and in accordance, with applicable law. No notice, other than a change to the “Effective” date of this policy, will be provided for any non-material changes to this policy. Material changes will be effective thirty (30) days from when we provide notice to schools or post a notice on our website, as applicable.

If you do not agree to this Privacy Statement, please do not use Schoolwires services. Similarly, with respect to personal information which you provide voluntarily (such as name, address and telephone number in order to register with us), please do not submit such information if you do not want us to collect and maintain it.

  • Information We Collect When Hosting a School’s Website

    You may visit the school's site as a guest without registering. Registration, however, will give you access to certain features as well as the ability to personalize certain features. Schoolwires collects personal information via registration. Registration occurs in two ways:

     
    1. Self-Registration
    If you choose to become a registered guest, on behalf of the school, we will ask for personal information from you through the school's site powered by Schoolwires. After we collect this information from you, it may be verified by an official at the school. So that the registration process is compliant with federal law under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), you are first asked for your birth date. Birth date information is collected only to validate your age and is not store.
     
    Children under 13 years of age are not allowed to register themselves with the school's Schoolwires site. Before a child under 13 may access features that require registration, the school must first obtain a signed parental consent form. After consent is received by the school, the school is responsible for providing parent access to the child's profile so the parent may, at any time, review, modify, or delete the child's personal information. A parent also has the right to refuse to further collection of the child's information. If you have questions regarding your profile, please contact the school’s webmaster.
     
    If you are over 13 years of age, you are required to supply your First and Last Name, Email Address, Sign-In Name and Password. These fields are labeled to indicate that they are required. The following fields are labeled as optional: Title, Address, City, State, ZIP Code, Phone and Fax. After registration, the Site Director for the school may decide to assign to you a role or roles (viewing privileges). Based upon your designated role(s), you may have access to additional information or services. If you have questions concerning your role(s), please contact the school’s webmaster. 
     
    2. Pre-Registration
    You may be pre-registered by the school in one of two ways. Either way, the school may not share personal information for children under 13 years of age with Schoolwires without first receiving parental consent.
     
    2.1. Your registration information may be shared with Schoolwires via a school-provided list. Schoolwires would use this list to conduct a batch import of information.
     
    2.2. A school-designated individual may input registration information for you. 
    Information collected for self-registration or pre-registration may also be used to (1) provide you with important information about the school's site, such as updates or notices, (2) improve our product and services, (3) improve customer service, (4) create a profile for you, and/or (5) in aggregate, guide our internal marketing efforts. No personally identifiable information will be shared with third parties except to the extent expressly described in this Privacy Policy and otherwise in accordance with applicable law.
     
    Additional Information Which You Provide to Us
    Additional information may be requested of you in other situations, for example, if you submit a customer support request or a content submission request. In such instances, the goal of collecting additional information is to serve you more effectively and efficiently.
    In addition, students, members of the school, and other users of the website may choose to post or download/upload various pieces of content and/or information on or through the use of the site, and may use certain “social media”, collaboration, interaction and other capabilities of the site to exchange various categories of data, materials and information. While we cannot know in advance all types of such information, one example is that teachers or administrators may post blogs, announcements and other information and content directly onto the site. Another example is that students may chat with each other, or send instant messages or e-mail, via technology which may now or in the future be made available over the site and may share information, photographs, images or recordings with each other or with the community. Information such as an individual’s name and e-mail address can also be exchanged or made available in messages, chats and other social media and collaborative communications facilitated over the website. Schoolwires has no practical ability to restrict the information, conduct, communications, or content which might be posted on or exchanged through the use of its technology, nor can Schoolwires ensure prompt removal of any such communications, information or content after transmission or posting. If you or your child provide personal information when using any social interaction, collaboration or messaging capabilities or features of the site (such as chat rooms, blogs, message boards, profile pages, etc.), that personal information may be copied, publicly posted and otherwise disclosed without limitation as to its use by third parties. If you are concerned about any information or content that may be posted, transmitted or accessed on the site, please contact the school’s webmaster.
     
    In connection with the use of “PassKey Manager™” or similar solution, you or the school may provide us with your (or your child’s) login credentials for certain software applications and web services in order to facilitate single sign-on access to these applications and services. These login credentials may include the username, password and any other required information which you selected or were supplied in order to access certain applications and web services.

  • How We Use Information Collected When Hosting a School’s Website

    Profile
    We do not associate information that we collect through log files or third parties with your personal profile. The information is provided voluntarily by you when you register for the site. A profile is stored information that gives you access to certain features as well as the ability to personalize certain features. Your profile can be used only by you, Schoolwires and the school.
     
    Cookies and Web Beacons
    A cookie is a piece of data stored on your computer tied to information about you. The only personal information a cookie can contain is information you supply. Schoolwires uses session or dynamic cookies to let us know when a user is signed in. Once you close your browser, the cookie simply terminates. We use web beacons (single-pixel gifs) to count visitors.
    When a user is signed in and depending on the user's assigned role(s), additional services become available through our menu system. Again, depending on your assigned role(s), cookies may offer the following benefits after Sign-In:
     
    1.A personalized salutation greeting
    2.Content tailored to your role(s)
    3.Use of services that require Sign-In
    4.Session time-out, a security feature
    You can disable cookies in your browser's preferences. If you disable cookies in your browser, you may still use the school's site. Services, however, that require Sign-In will not work if cookies are disabled.
     
    Communications from the Site
     
    Service Announcements
    From time to time, it may be necessary to send out a strictly service-related announcement. For instance, if our service is temporarily suspended for maintenance, we might send you a notification email. Users cannot opt-out of these communications though they can deactivate their account. (For more information, see the Modifications to Personal Information section.) However, these communications are not promotional in nature.
     
    Data Retention
    Once you have deleted your account, your information is permanently removed from our servers. We do not retain your data except as necessary to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes and enforce our agreements, all in compliance with applicable laws.
     
    E-Alerts and Subscriptions
    E-Alerts and Subscriptions is an optional feature that allows registered users to subscribe to content on a site. If the school purchased the E-Alerts software option, you can use two different kinds of E-Alerts:
     
    1. Broadcast E-Alerts: These are accessed by Site and Subsite Directors to send an email message to a specific group or multiple groups of users (i.e., users assigned the same viewing permissions [roles]).
    2. Content E-Alerts: These provide for automatic email notification to users whenever significant changes are made to one or more homepages or sections to which they subscribe. Please note that the content editors decide whether to send an E-Alert to subscribers. The email message that users receive contains a link to the actual homepage or section, allowing them immediate and direct access.
    Users can remove Content E-Alerts through options in their Account Settings. 
     
    Customer Service
    As necessary, Schoolwires may communicate with you via email, phone or online to provide requested services and in regards to account-related issues.

    Sharing of Information Collected When Hosting a School’s Website

    Though we make every effort to preserve your privacy, we may need to disclose personal information when required by law wherein we have a good-faith belief that such action is necessary to comply with a current judicial proceeding, a court order or legal process served on the school's site.

    Users who have been assigned by the school to editorial positions of Site or Subsite Directors have access to all registered user information strictly and solely on their school's site. Contact the school's webmaster for more information regarding who has been assigned these positions and how this position-assigning process works for the school.

You will be notified by a prominent notice on our corporate website prior to a change of ownership or control of your personal information. For more information, see the Schoolwires Privacy Policy Statement Changes section.
     
    Service Providers
    From time to time, we may retain trusted businesses and contractors to provide certain services which support our provision and hosting of the website or otherwise support the operation of the associated online services. We may also contract with trusted third parties to provide complimentary products or services which may be integrated with, linked to (either via a website link or otherwise) or accessible from (such as through single sign on functionality), the website. These business and contractors who have access to personally identifiable information are required to protect this information in a manner that is consistent with this privacy policy, for example, by not using the information for any purpose other than to carry out the services they are performing for us or to facilitate access to an integrated service offering. We will not disclose your personal information to any third party to the extent prohibited by law.
     
    Compliance with Laws
    Schoolwires will, of course, comply with the requirements of COPPA and FERPA to the extent that these laws apply to the information collected and maintained by Schoolwires. Accordingly, notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Policy, Schoolwires will not collect, use or disclose personal information covered by COPPA and FERPA except in compliance with the respective requirements of each of these statutes and their associated regulations. Please see the discussion above for more details regarding compliance with these laws. Schoolwires will also comply with all other applicable laws which govern the information which it maintains.
