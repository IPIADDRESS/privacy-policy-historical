Partners Privacy Policy

Partners Federal Credit Union (also known as "Partners") is committed to safeguarding and protecting the confidentiality of both financial and personal information. In this disclosure, we will explain the information we gather, how we safeguard the information, and how we use it.

Security Procedures

We restrict access to non-public personal information about you to those employees who need to know that information to provide products and services to you. We maintain physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards that comply with the law to guard your non-public personal information.

Information Collected by Partners

The Credit Union collects non-public personal information about you from the following sources:

  • Information, such as your name, address, e-mail address, birth date, income, social security number, we receive from you on applications or other forms;
  • Information, such as your account balances, credit card usage, payment history or parties to the transaction, about your transactions with us, our affiliates, and others;
  • Information, such as your creditworthiness and credit history, we receive from a consumer reporting agency;
  • Information, such as your payment history, your transactions and income, that we obtain when verifying the information you provide on an application or other forms (this may be obtained from your current or past employers, or from other institutions where you conduct financial transactions);
  • Information, such as your income, home ownership and marital status obtained from census data providers, marketing researchers and public records.

Information Shared with Third Parties

We do not disclose any non-public personal information about our members and former members to anyone, except as permitted by law. The following are some examples of when we may disclose information:

  • To companies that assist us in administering and bringing you our products and services. The information shared with these companies typically includes information to process transactions on your behalf, conduct our operations, follow your instructions as you authorize, or protect the security of our financial records. These companies may include check and statement printing companies, and data processing vendors.
  • To businesses with whom we have a written joint marketing agreement to offer a broader array of products and services. Joint marketing agreements include confidentiality provisions to protect member information. These businesses may include financial service providers, such as insurance companies.
  • To companies that perform marketing or other business or professional services on our behalf such as businesses that perform mailing services. We have written agreements with these companies that include confidentiality provisions to protect Member information.
  • To consumer and credit reporting companies.
  • To comply with a judicial proceeding, court order or legal process served on us.

Former Members

If you decide to terminate your membership or become an inactive member, we will adhere to the privacy policies and practices as described in this notice.
