Pagenation Privacy Policy

At Pagenation we are strong believers in everyone's inalienable right to be left alone, to be allowed to live and make choices without the world studying every move, and we pledge to honor your privacy in every way we can. We request that children always ask their parents for permission before sending any information about themselves to us or anyone else over the internet.

Pagenation and its related content and service sites, believes that electronic privacy is crucial for the ongoing success of the Internet as a service, commercial, and entertainment medium. On occasion, Pagenation will make available to potential advertisers, research firms, and business partners aggregated results from research it has conducted on its Pagenation readership base. Pagenation will only provide such demographic information on an aggregated basis and does not disclose individual reader information.

E-mail - Any/all e-mail addresses sent to us as the result of correspondence with the consumers of Pagenation will not be used or collected for the purposes of reselling to a third party and will not be added to a bulk e-mailing list.

Cookies - The use of advertising cookies sent by third-party servers is standard in the Internet industry. You may occasionally get cookies from our advertisers. Pagenation cannot and does not control these cookies.

Traffic Data - Each time a visitor comes to a Pagenation website, information is collected to improve the overall quality of Pagenation sites. Pagenation collects the visitor's domain name/IP Address (e.g., whether the user is logged on from netscape.com or yale.edu or 255.255.255.0), referral data (e.g., we record the address of the last URL a user visited prior to clicking through to an Pagenation site), and browser/platform type (e.g., a Netscape browser on a Macintosh platform). In addition to collecting this type of information, Pagenation's advertisement delivery system collects aggregate search terms for internal reporting and targeted advertising. Pagenation's advertisement delivery system collects visitor information for purposes of product monitoring, product improvement, and targeted advertising. Pagenation works with third parties that serve ads for this site.

Pagenation also counts, tracks, and aggregates the visitor's activity into our analysis of general traffic flows at Pagenation sites (e.g., tracking where traffic comes from and how traffic flows within the Pagenation sites). Pagenation may merge this information into group data, which may then be shared on an aggregated basis with potential advertisers, research firms, and business partners. Pagenation will not disclose your individual identity or personal data. When Pagenation does present aggregated information to potential advertisers, research firms and business partners, no one will be able to identify you or contact you.
