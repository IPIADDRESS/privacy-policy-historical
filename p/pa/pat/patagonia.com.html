Information we collect




We do not share information about you with other companies. We automatically collect your IP address, but not your email address when you visit our site. We use your IP address to help diagnose problems with our server, and to administer our Web site.

Through the order forms on our site, we collect information necessary for billing and shipping. We collect your contact information (like email address) and financial information (like credit card numbers). Contact information from the order form is used to send orders and information about our company to you. You may opt-out of receiving future mailings; see the How to remove your name from our mailing or email list section below. Financial information that is collected is used to bill you for products and services.


About cookies and how we use them


Back to Top

A cookie is a small file that a Web site puts on your hard drive so that it can remember something about you at a later time. Cookies make it easier for you to use our site.

We use cookies for two reasons. First, a cookie is generated that allows you to retain contents in your shopping bag while you are checking out other areas on our site. When you are done, the gear in your bag is still there for you to buy. We also use cookies to collect aggregate data about what areas of our site are the most popular and least popular. This allows us to give you more of what you want and less of what you don't want. In keeping with our commitment to your privacy, we do not analyze this data on the personal level.

We contract with Coremetrics, a service partner, to provide data collection and reporting services. When you browse or shop at Patagonia.com, Coremetrics may use cookies on our behalf and may receive information about your browsing and buying activity on this Web site. We do not allow Coremetrics to use this information in any manner, other than to help us provide you with the products and services available on our site. You can deactivate Coremetrics' ability to analyze your Web browsing and purchasing behavior. You also have the option to continue to benefit from the improved shopping experience enabled by this service, but stay anonymous. For further information, including how to opt out, consult Coremetrics' privacy policies.

Secure shopping at Patagonia



Back to Top

Ordering online at patagonia.com is safe and easy! Protecting your order information is a priority, so we use VeriSign's Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) technology. SSL encrypts ALL of your personal information including credit card number name and address to avoid the decoding of that information by anyone other than Patagonia. If you are using a Netscape browser, check the security of your connection by looking at the lower left-hand corner of your browser window after accessing the server. For Internet Explorer, check the little boxes near the lower right-hand corner. If you see an unbroken key or a closed lock (depending upon your browser), then SSL is active. You can also double-check by looking at the URL line of your browser. When accessing a secure server, the first characters of the site address will change from "http" to "https".

We have implemented the strongest security available for Patagonia.com from VeriSign. VeriSign software enables the use of digital certificates which confirm that your browser in communicating securely with Patagonia.com. You may click on the VeriSign logo to the right to verify that your browser is communicating securely with Patagonia.com.

Some versions of browsers and some firewalls don't permit communication through secure servers. If you are unable to connect to a secure server or would rather place your order by phone, please call us at 1-800-638-6464
