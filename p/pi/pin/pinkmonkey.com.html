Children's Online Privacy Protection Act

PinkMonkey.com is in compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), which became effective April 21, 2000.

In compliance with the Childrens Online Protection Act, 15 U.S.C. section 6501 - 6506 and 16 C.F.R.312.1-312.12, this site does not permit children under 13 years of age to become users. If you are under the age of 13, you must ask your parent or guardian to access the site.

PinkMonkey.com is also in compliance with the initiative and spirit of the guidelines set forth by the Children's Advertising Review Unit (CARU). We have made appropriate changes to our site to ensure compliance even though this is a voluntary action. We have considered it a primary goal to remain a "G" rated site. We have made every attempt to exclude content that is adult in language or content or contains sexual innuendo. Other teen and student targeted sites view this type of material as an attraction and we do not want to be included in that group. If you find objectionable material on this site or on a site for which we have provided an external link, please let us know support@pinkmonkey.com.

Access to the Internet:

The PinkMonkey.com website is only available to persons with access to the Internet. To reach the site, you must have the proper equipment and connecting devices. The overall Internet contains websites containing material which is not appropriate for all viewers, particularly minors. PinkMonkey recommends that each parent take responsibility for the activity of their children to install effective filtering/blocking mechanisms on each computer a child may access to protect your children from inappropriate content. If you install an effective means of filtering/blocking on each computer, you greatly reduce the ability of your children to access inappropriate content. By simply installing a quality filtering/blocking mechanism on each machine, you greatly reduce the chance that your children will be exposed to inappropriate content. Regardless of what service they may use to access the Internet. Please act responsibly.

Cookies:

A cookie is a small amount of data, which often includes an anonymous unique identifier, that is sent to your browser from a web site's computers and stored on your computer's hard drive. Each web site can send its own cookie to your browser if your browser's preferences allow it, but (to protect your privacy) your browser only permits a web site to access the cookies it has already sent to you, not the cookies sent to you by other sites.

Choices about Cookies: You can configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is set. (Each browser is different, so check the "Help" menu of your browser to learn how to change your cookie preferences.) If you reject all cookies, you may not have access to certain aspects of the website.
