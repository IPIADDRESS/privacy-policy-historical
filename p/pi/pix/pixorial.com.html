Pixorial Privacy Policy

Effective June 1, 2010 

Pixorial, Inc. (“Pixorial”, “we”, or “us”) is dedicated to protecting your privacy online. This privacy policy (“Privacy Policy”) describes the ways we collect, store, use, and protect the personal information you share with us in connection with your use of the services we provide, including, without limitation, our website located at www.pixorial.com (the “Site”) and all of our services accessed through third party web sites (together referred to as the “Services”). By using our Services, you consent to the collection and use of information in the manner described below. This Privacy Policy is incorporated into and is part of our Terms of Use located at http://www.pixorial.com/pixorial/terms_of_use, and together these documents are the overall agreement between you and us. This Privacy Policy may change from time to time so please check back periodically.



Information We Collect Automatically

When you visit the Site, we automatically collect non-personal information including your IP address and standard web log information, such as your browser type and operating system, the date and time you access the Site, where you linked from, and the pages you visit. We use this information to help us make the Site more useful to visitors. For example, the information helps us determine the number of visitors to the Site and the types of technology our visitors use. We may use your IP address for additional purposes, including, without limitation, to diagnose or service technology problems reported by our users or engineers that are associated with the IP addresses controlled by a specific web company or internet service provider, to tailor advertising based on geographic area or other information derived from your IP address or to provide aggregate information derived from IP addresses to advertisers.

While you are browsing, we do not collect any personal information about you, such as your name, address, telephone number, or e-mail address, unless you provide it to us voluntarily, for example, by sending us an e-mail message or requesting to be placed on our e-mail list.



Information We Collect From Registered Users

When you open an account with us, we will ask you for your name and email address. If you are a registered user and you sign up for paid services, you may be asked to provide additional personal information, including, for example:

  • Your phone number
  • Your billing and mailing addresses
  • Your credit card information

We may obtain additional information about you from personal or third party websites, databases, publications, or other publicly available sources. It is your choice to post Content and make it publicly available. You are solely responsible for the Content you post. We also collect information about your transactions with us and with some of our business partners, and we store your Content, associated labels and editing meta-data. In addition, if you contact us, we may keep a record of that correspondence. If you give us permission in your privacy and application settings, located at http://www.pixorial.com/account, to share your Content with your friends, we may ask you for the names and e-mail addresses of such friends. You can choose not to provide any of the information we request, but then you may not be able to use all of our Services. For purposes of this Privacy Policy, “Content” includes, without limitation, data, text, information, screen names, graphics, photos, profiles, audio and video clips, and links to third-party content that you submit to us in connection with the Services.



Information We Do Not Collect

Pixorial does not knowingly collect or maintain any personal information from children under the age of 13. No part of the Site is intended or designed for the purpose of attracting any person under the age of 13. If you are under the age of 13, do not use the Site. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under age 13, we will delete that information as quickly as possible. If you believe that we might have any information from a child under age 13, please contact us at privacy[at]pixorial.com.



Information We Temporarily Place on Your Computer's Hard Drive

A cookie is a small data file that is sent to your browser from a Web server and stored on your computer's hard drive. Pixorial uses temporary cookies in limited ways. When you log into your account, for example, we send a “session cookie” to your computer. This type of cookie helps us to recognize you if you visit multiple pages on the Site during the same session, so we do not need to keep asking you for your password. Once you log out or close your browser, the temporary cookies used by Pixorial expire, with the exception of a “remember me” cookie. Pixorial cookies do not generate personal data, do not read personal data from your computer, and are not linked to any information that could be used to identify you.

You can always choose not to accept Pixorial's temporary cookies. Your browser instructions contain the information you need to modify your choices about cookies. By modifying your browser preferences, you can accept all cookies, be notified when a cookie is set, or reject all cookies. If you choose to reject all cookies, however, you may be unable to use all of our Services.



How We Protect Your Information

Pixorial takes commercially reasonable steps to protect the privacy and security of your personally identifiable information.

  • Pixorial keeps the personal information you provide on servers that are located in physically secure facilities and protected by firewalls and other technological means.
  • Your address and credit card number are encrypted once they are transmitted to us and stored in encrypted form.
  • Our employees are trained to support these security measures and only have access to your information on a need-to-know basis.

In addition, your customer account is password-protected. You can and should significantly increase the security of your personal information by changing your password three or four times a year and keeping it confidential. You are solely responsible for maintaining the secrecy of your unique password and account information at all times. While we strive to protect your information, we do not represent, warrant, or guarantee that your personal information will be protected against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alterations, and do not accept any liability for the security of the personal information submitted to us nor for your or third parties' use or misuse of personal information.

These third-parties may include other websites, including social-networking providers such as Facebook, Linked-In, MySpace, etc., as well as individual subscribers to these third-party services.

  • We may transfer or assign the information to another company or organization as part of a merger or acquisition, the sale or transfer of all or a portion of our business or assets or other change of control of our company.

We do not sell or rent your personal information. However, we may sell or transfer non-individualized information to third parties, including summary or aggregated anonymous information about all or sub-groups of our users.
