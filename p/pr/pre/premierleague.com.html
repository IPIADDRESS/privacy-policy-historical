Privacy Policy

The privacy of your personal data is important to the FAPL. We will comply with our obligations under the Data Protection Act 1998 and related legislation (the "Act"). The following sets out what we do and what we do not do with the information we collect using this Website.

1. The FAPL may use cookies to enable the FAPL to identify whether you are registered on the Website and to shortcut your access to the Website. The FAPL will only read cookies from your cookie file placed there through your web browser's interaction with the Website.

2. If you chose to register with the Website the personal data you enter in the registration sections will be used to identify and send targeted materials to you from time to time relating to the FAPL and related matters, unless you use the tick box on the registration page to choose not to receive materials.

3. We may also disclose the personal data you provide to carefully selected third parties who may send materials to you, unless you use the tick box on the registration page to indicate that you do not wish your details to be passed on to such third parties.

4. Following registration, if you want to stop receiving materials from the FAPL or you wish us to stop supplying your details to third parties at any time please email remove_list@fapl.co.uk and we will delete your details from our distribution list as soon as reasonably practicable.

5. The Website is hosted on a server in the UK.

We may need to amend this Privacy Policy from time to time where it is necessary to do so for legal, technical or commercial reasons and any such amendments shall be effective as party on the Website. Please check these terms regularly to see if there have been any changes.

If you would like access to the information we hold about you please contact us. We may require you to confirm your identity and pay a small administrative fee in accordance with the Act. For more information about data protection law, contact the office of the Information Commissioner.
