Collection of Usage Information. When users use the Digital Service (including by accessing its associated Website and/or Mobile Applications), we automatically collect “Usage Information” which include among other things, which pages of our Website or sections of our Mobile Applications were visited; the order in which they were visited; when they were visited; which hyperlinks were “clicked”; your activity on the Digital Service (such as title selections, watch history and search queries); and details regarding your interactions with customer service (such as the date, time and reason for contacting us, transcripts of any chat conversations, and if you call us, your phone number). Collecting such information may involve logging the IP address or other unique identifier (“Device Identifier”) for your computer, mobile phone, tablet, gaming system, smart TV, set top box, or other device (any, a “Device”) used to access the Website, Mobile Applications or other components of the Digital Service, operating system and browser software used by each user of the Digital Service. Technology Used on the Digital Service.  We also use or may use cookies, web beacons and/or embedded scripts to help us determine and identify repeat visitors, the type of content and sites to which a user of our Digital Service links, the length of time each user spends at any particular area of our Digital Service, and the specific functionalities that users choose to use. Essentially, cookies are a user’s identification card for the LDN servers. Web beacons are small graphic files linked to our servers that allow us to track your use of our Digital Service and related functionalities. An embedded script is programming code that is designed to collect information about your interactions with the Digital Service, such as the links you click on. The code is temporarily downloaded onto your Device from our web server or a third party service provider, is active only while you are connected to the Digital Service, and is deactivated or deleted thereafter.

Cookies, web beacons and embedded script allow LDN to serve you better and more efficiently, and to personalize your experience at our Digital Service. Cookies, web beacons and embedded script are not used to retain Personally Identifiable Information. You should be able to control how and whether cookies will be accepted by your web browser. Most browsers offer instructions on how to reset the browser to reject cookies in the “Help” section of the toolbar. If you reject our cookies, certain of the functions and conveniences of the Digital Service may not work properly, but you do not have to accept our cookies in order to use many functionalities of the Digital Service.

California Business & Professions Code Section 22575(b)(as amended effective January 1, 2014) provides that California residents are entitled to know how LDN responds to “Do Not Track” browser settings. LDN does not currently take actions to respond to Do Not Track signals because a uniform technological standard has not yet been developed. We continue to review new technologies and may adopt a standard once one is created.
