Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy of Procore Technologies, Inc. (“Procore”) is designed to explain to you how your information is collected, used, and protected. By using any of Procore’s websites or services you signify your consent to our collection and use of personal information as described in this Privacy Policy. This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Procore will post the most recent version of this Privacy Policy on the Procore.com website (“Base Domain”).

What Information Do We Collect?


When visiting any website maintained by Procore you may provide Procore with two types of information (i) personal information you knowingly choose to disclose that is collected on an individual basis and (ii) website use information collected on an aggregate basis as you browse a Procore maintained website.

Registration Information


You may provide Procore with information about you, your firm or company, and your practices when you register to be a user of Procore, register for certain services, or register for email alerts.

Email Information


If you choose to correspond with Procore through email, we may retain the content of your email messages along with your email address and Procore’s responses.

Comments and Interactive Forms

Procore may collect information if you provide it in feedback or comments or otherwise post information in or to any interactive forms found in any Procore website.  Please do not post any personal information in interactive forms that you expect or wish to keep private.

Web Site Use Information

Similar to other commercial websites, websites maintained by Procore utilize a standard technology called “cookies” and web server logs to collect information about how such website is used.  Information gathered through cookies and web server logs may include, but is not limited to, your IP address, the date and time of visits, the pages viewed,  and the amount of time spent at a website.

Information Storage

All information Procore collects is stored in Procore’s databases and may be kept indefinitely.  Although Procore makes all reasonable efforts to store collected information in a secure operating environment that is not available to the public, Procore cannot and does not guarantee the security of collected information or that the public will not be able to access such information.

Privacy Protection


If you create an account or register with Procore, your account information may be password protected. Please create a strong password and keep in a safe place. If you have forgotten your password, Procore will only send the password to the registered holder of the account.

Procore does not knowingly collect any information from persons under the age of 13.

Procore, or users of our websites who post on our websites in interactive forms, may provide links to third party websites, which may have different privacy policies. Procore does not have any control over the privacy policies of those third party websites, and we encourage all users to read the privacy policies of each and every website visited.

Sharing Information with Third Parties

As Procore develops its business, Procore may buy or sell assets or business offerings. Customers, emails, and website visitor information is generally one of the transferred business assets in these types of transactions. Procore may also transfer such information in the course of corporate divestitures, mergers, or any dissolution. Procore does not provide or sell customer, email and website visitor information to third parties who would use such information for marketing or solicitation purposes.

International Users


By visiting a Procore website and providing Procore with information, you acknowledge and consent that Procore may use the information collected for the reasons outlined in this Privacy Policy or in Procore’s other communications with you, including the transmission of information outside of your resident jurisdiction. Such information is stored on servers located in the United States. By providing Procore with your information, either passively or actively, you acknowledge and consent to the transfer of such information under the terms herein and to this locale.

Certain Disclosures

Procore may disclose your personal information if required to do so by law or subpoena or if Procore believes that such action is necessary to (i) conform to the law or comply with legal process served on Procore or its affiliated parties or (ii) protect and defend Procore’s rights and property, Procore’s websites, the users of Procore’s websites, or Procore’s affiliated parties.
