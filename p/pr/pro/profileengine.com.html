Profile Technology Ltd privacy policy 2011

PRIVACY POLICY

Profile Technology Limited create and develop a range of websites and social network applications. Within this document the term 'Sites' shall refer to any and all of our applications, websites and any other services we provide. Where we collect personally identifiable information from you ("Personal Information") we will deal with that information in accordance with our Terms of Service and the Privacy Policy set out below.


Privacy Policy

The Privacy Policy sets out the basis on which we will deal with any Personal Information we collect from you, or that you provide to us. We have adopted our Privacy Policy so that our collection, storage, use and disclosure of your Personal Information complies with the Privacy Act 1993. By using our Sites, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

Solely for the purpose of this Privacy Policy "we", "us", or "our" means Profile Technology Limited and/or MrMr C L aydon.

Information we may collect from you

We may collect and process the following information about you:

  • Information that you provide by filling in forms on our Sites. This includes information provided at the time of registering to use Sites, subscribing to our service, posting material or requesting further services. We may also ask you for information when you report a problem with any of our Sites.

  • If you contact us, we may keep a record of that correspondence.

  • We may ask you to complete surveys that we use for research purposes, although you do not have to respond to them.

  • Details of your use of our Sites including, but not limited to, traffic data, location data and other communication data and logs of the resources that you access.

  • Any other information which you submit to othe social networks and which is made available by those social networks for use by Platform Developers in accordance with their policies. 'API data'

  • Any parts of your profile on another social network which that social network has allowed our search engine to download and store at a time when your privacy settings permitted search engines to index those parts of your profile. Note that such information is not obtained via the "Platform APIs" and therefore our use of this information is not controlled by the developer policies of the social network Developer Policy. Information collected in this way is treated by us as "Public information". Note: We are not notified if you revoke the permission for search engines to index your profile and doing so will not remove your profile from our database. Revoking that permission only prevents search engines from accessing changes make to your profile after you revoked the permission.

IP addresses and cookies

We may collect information about your computer, including where available your IP address, operating system and browser type. Please note that we may use geolocation software which may identify your IP address as belonging to a particular area. This is not 100% accurate but may lead to the disclosure of the region from which you access our Sites. By accessing our Sites you agree to the public disclosure of this information.

We may obtain information about your general internet usage by using a cookie file which is stored on the hard drive of your computer. Cookies contain information that is transferred to your computer's hard drive. They help us to improve our Sites and to deliver a better and more personalised service. They enable us:

  • To log you in to the Sites and keep your login session active.

  • To estimate our audience size and usage pattern.

  • To store information about your preferences, and so allow us to customise Sites according to your individual interests.

  • To speed up your searches.

  • To recognise you when you return to Sites.

You may refuse to accept cookies by activating the setting on your browser which allows you to refuse the setting of cookies. However, if you select this setting you may be unable to access certain parts of our Sites or they may not work correctly. Unless you have adjusted your browser setting so that it will refuse cookies, our system may issue cookies whenever you use our Sites.

Where we store your personal data

The information that we collect from you may be transferred to, and stored at, a destination within or outside New Zealand. It may be processed by staff who work for us or for one of our suppliers or contractors anywhere in the world. Such staff may be engaged in, among other things, the fulfilment of your order, the processing of your payment details and the provision of technical or support services. By submitting your personal information, you agree to this transfer, storing and processing.

All information you provide to us may be stored on our servers. Any payment transactions will be dealt with by PayPal or another payment processor of our choice in accordance with their terms. Where we have given you (or where you have chosen) a password or other secure login method which enables you to access certain parts of our Sites, you are responsible for keeping this password or secure login method confidential. We ask you not to share a password with anyone.

Unfortunately, the transmission of information via the internet is not necessarily secure. Although we will do our best to protect your personal data, we cannot guarantee the security of information about you when it is transmitted to and from our Sites; any transmission is at your own risk. Once we have received your information, we will use strict procedures and security features to try to prevent unauthorised access.

We regularly backup information and data that we hold to improve the integrity of our Sites in the event of a system failure. Please note that these backups may be stored indefinitely and will hold a snapshot of the information we held about you at that time. Therefore, any changes that you make to the information you supplied to us after the date of the backup will not change the information contained in that backup.

Where it is necessary to restore data or build a new database from a backup copy, recent changes may be lost and old or deleted content may reappear on the system. We may attempt to alert you to this fact where it is deemed necessary and you have provided contact information but we are under no obligation to do so. It is up to you to check regularly and ensure your details are up to date.

We may use your Personal Information in the following ways:

  • To be used in conjunction with any of our Sites.

  • To enable other users of our Sites to find you, identify you, learn about you and contact you.

  • To ensure that content from our Sites is presented in the most effective manner for you and for your computer.

If you do not want us to use your information in this way, or to pass your details on to third parties for these purposes, please do not tick the relevant box or do not provide your email address or other personal information on the form on which we collect your information (the “Registration Form”).

We may also use your information to help advertisers reach the kind of audience they want to target. We may make use of the Personal Information we collect from you to enable us to comply with our advertisers' wishes by displaying their advertisement to you.
