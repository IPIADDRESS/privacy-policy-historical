Privacy Statement for Zimbra, Inc.

Scope of This Notice

This Privacy Statement is intended to describe Zimbra's privacy practices and provide information about the choices you have regarding the ways in which information collected by Zimbra is used and disclosed.

Our Commitment to Privacy

At Zimbra, your privacy is important to us. To better protect your privacy, we have provided this Statement explaining our information practices and the choices you can make about the way your personal information is collected, used and disclosed. To make this Statement easy to find, we have made it available on every page of our site.

The Information We Collect

This Privacy Statement applies to all information collected by or submitted to Zimbra, including personal information. "Personal information" is data that reasonably can be used to identify or describe an individual.

Zimbra collects personal information when:

  • you create a user account;
  • you request information or materials (e.g., whitepapers);
  • you participate in surveys and evaluations;
  • you participate in promotions, contests or giveaways;
  • you submit questions or comments to us.

Zimbra also collects personal information from individuals (with their consent) at conventions, trade shows and expositions.

The types of personal information collected may include (but are not limited to):

  • your first and last name;
  • your title and your company's name;
  • your home, billing, or other physical address (including street name, name of a city or town, state/province, country);
  • your e-mail address;
  • your telephone number;
  • any other identifier that permits Zimbra to make physical or online contact with you;
  • any information that Zimbra collects online from you and maintains in association with your account, such as:
  • your Zimbra display name,
  • your Zimbra password.

Using Your Personal Information

Zimbra uses the personal information you provide to:

  • create and maintain your accounts;
  • answer your questions;
  • register you in programs (per your request);
  • send you information;
  • send you surveys.

We also use this personal information to provide you with information related to your account and the products or services you downloaded from us, to better understand your needs and interests, to improve our service, and to personalize communications.

Sharing Your Personal Information

Unless you assent, Zimbra will never share the personal information you provide to us except as described below.

Zimbra may disclose your personal information to third parties under any of the following circumstances:

  • As required by law (such as responding to a valid subpoena, warrant, audit, or agency action, or to prevent fraud). For research activities, including the production of statistical reports (such aggregated information is used to describe our services to current and prospective partners and other third parties, and is not used to contact the subjects of the report).
  • In the context of a business transaction, such as a merger, acquisition, consolidation, or divestiture (pursuant to a pledge of confidentiality under which the recipient agrees to use the information for no purpose other than carrying out the transaction).

Your Choices About Receiving E-Mail

Zimbra makes every effort to provide you with the ability to opt-in and opt-out of receiving communications from Zimbra.

Zimbra may send you email according to the registrations you have entered on our alerts page, or in response to your questions.

Zimbra will provide you with the opportunity to opt-out of any of our direct marketing communications at any time. All promotional e-mail sent from Zimbra will contain a provision allowing you the option to decline receiving future promotional e-mail. You may unsubscribe to an e-mail communication by contacting Zimbra via the unsubscribe data provided in those emails or at www.zimbra.com.

For your protection, Zimbra may contact you in the event that we find an issue that requires your immediate attention.

Cookies and Other Browser Information

Our website automatically captures IP addresses. We use IP addresses to help diagnose problems with our server, to administer our website, and to help ensure the security of your interaction with the website. Your IP address is used to help identify you.

As part of offering and providing customizable and personalized services, Zimbra uses cookies to store and sometimes track information about you. A cookie is a small amount of data that is sent to your browser from a Web server and stored on your computer's hard drive. All sections of www.zimbra.com where you are prompted to log in or that are customizable may require your browser to accept cookies.

If you do not want your personal information to be stored by cookies, you can configure your browser so that it always rejects these cookies or asks you each time if you accept them or not. However, you must understand that the use of cookies may be necessary to provide certain products or services, and choosing to reject cookies will reduce the performance and functionality of the site. Your browser documentation includes precise instructions explaining how to define the acceptance of cookies.

Our Commitment to Data Security

Zimbra trains its employees on our privacy policy guidelines and makes our privacy policy available to vendors and partners.

Public Forums Reminder

This site may make chat rooms, forums, message boards, and/or news groups available to its users. Please remember that any information that is disclosed in these areas becomes public information. Exercise caution when deciding to disclose your personal information. Although we value individual ideas and encourage free expression, Zimbra reserves the right to take necessary action to preserve the forum's integrity, such as removing any posting that is vulgar or inappropriate.

Our Commitment to Children's Online Privacy

Out of special concern for children's privacy, Zimbra does not knowingly accept online personal information from children under the age of 13. Zimbra does not knowingly allow children under the age of 13 to become registered members of our sites. Zimbra does not knowingly collect or solicit personal information about children under 13.

In the event that Zimbra ever decides to expand its intended site audience to include children under the age of 13, those specific web pages will, in accordance with the requirements of the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), be clearly identified and provide an explicit privacy notice addressed to children under 13. In addition, Zimbra will provide an appropriate mechanism to obtain parental approval, allow parents to subsequently make changes to or request removal of their children's personal information, and provide access to any other information as required by law.

About Links to Other Sites

This site contains links to other sites. Zimbra does not control the information collection of sites that can be reached through links from www.zimbra.com. If you have questions about the data collection procedures of linked sites, please contact those companies directly.

How to Access, Modify or Update Your Information

Zimbra gives you the ability to access, modify or update your information at any time. You may log in and make changes to your login information (change your password), your contact information, your general preferences and your personalization settings.

How to Contact Us

If you have any questions about any of these practices or Zimbra's use of your personal information, please see our forums for more info.

Zimbra will work with you to resolve any concerns you may have about this Statement.

Changes to this Privacy Statement

Zimbra reserves the right to change this policy from time to time. If we do make changes, the revised Privacy Policy will be posted on this site. A notice will be posted on our homepage for 30 days whenever this privacy statement is changed in a material way.
