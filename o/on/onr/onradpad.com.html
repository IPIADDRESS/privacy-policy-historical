Cookies

Like many websites, we use “cookies” to collect information. A cookie is a small data file that we transfer to your computer's hard disk for record-keeping purposes. We use cookies for two purposes. First, we utilize persistent cookies to save your login information for future logins to the Site and Application. Second, we utilize session ID cookies to enable certain features of the Site and Application, to better understand how you interact with the Site and Application and to monitor aggregate usage by RadPad Users and web traffic routing on the Site and Application. Unlike persistent cookies, session cookies are deleted from your computer when you log off from the Site, Application and Service and then close your browser. Third party advertisers on the Site and Application may also place or read cookies on your browser. You can instruct your browser, by changing its options, to stop accepting cookies or to prompt you before accepting a cookie from the websites you visit. If you do not accept cookies, however, you may not be able to use all portions of the Site or Application or all functionality of the Service.

Paying Rent With Your Credit Card Can Make Sense

You use a credit card to pay for gas, groceries and even the clothes you're wearing, but does it really make financial sense to put your rent on your credit card? It might sound risky, but according to this article by USA Today, it can make a lot of sense if you're using the right credit card and have financial discipline.

So when does it make sense?

  1. If you're using your credit card to quickly improve your credit score.

    Paying your rent with a credit card can help you build or repair your credit score very quickly. Your payment history makes up 35% of your credit score and if you pay your rent off every month, you can quickly see the positive impact on your credit score.

  2. If you're using a credit card that gives you cash back or other perks like airline points.

    For example, the Barclaycard Arrival Plus World Elite MasterCard has a 2.2% rewards rate on every purchase. Paying $1,000 a month in rent on your card would net you $22 a month in rewards.

  3. If you have a new credit card with a good introductory sign-up bonus.

    To redeem some of these sign-up bonuses, you are required to spend a certain amount of money in a short period of time.

    For example, the Barclaycard is offering 40,000 bonus miles worth $400 for travel when you spend $3,000 or more on purchases within the first 90 days from account opening. If you normally wouldn't hit the $3,000 mark, it might make sense for you to pay rent on this card to get there as the rewards will more than offset any processing fees you'll pay.

  4. If it’s the only way you can pay your rent on time.

    It’s not uncommon to run in to situations where you don’t have the cash in your account to cover your upcoming rent. Maybe you don’t get paid until after your rent is due. Using a credit card to ensure your rent is paid on time almost always costs less than the late fee’s your landlord will charge you or a returned check fee.

  5. Finally, it's important to pay off your rent every month.

    Failing to pay off your credit card every month can leave you hanging with interest. If you leave a balance of $1,000 on your card at 12.99% APR, you'll end up paying $10.75 the first month in interest, which can offset the value of gaining cash back or airline miles.
