Privacy Statement

Privacy statement:

All personal information collected by National Prescribing Service Limited will be kept strictly confidential and stored securely. All and any information collected at this web site ­ including the content of application forms, and e-mail addresses ­ will be used only for the intended purposes and will not be sold, re-used, rented or loaned for other purposes.

Data elements recorded for the purposes of updating the mailing list are:

* name
* e-mail address
* postcode
* category of occupation e.g. general practitioner, pharmacist

Such information is used only for the distribution of National Prescribing Service Limited and its related publications, and for communication with readers.

Readers should advise National Prescribing Service Limited of any change of address so that emails are not sent to a discontinued email address. Contact details can be removed from the National Prescribing Service Limited mailing list at any time on request.

Any personal information provided to National Prescribing Service Limited by site users will be treated with the utmost care, and will not knowingly be used in ways not explicitly consented to by the user who supplied the information and to whom the information refers. All staff who handle such data are bound by confidentiality agreements to protect the use and security of, and access to, the information.

When users visit this web site, the National Prescribing Service Limited site server makes a record of their visit and records the following
information: server address, top-level domain name (e.g. .com, .gov, .au, .edu, etc.), date and time, geographic location and duration of the visit, the pages accessed and the documents downloaded, the type of browser and operating system used, and the site visited before the National Prescribing Service Limited web site. National Prescribing Service Limited staff analyse these records to monitor use of the site.

National Prescribing Service Limited does not collect personal medical information, nor does it provide personal medical advice.

Our use of cookies:

A cookie is a small amount of record-keeping data that a website transfers to your computer. It is sent to your browser from a web site's computer and stored on your computer's hard drive. Most Web browsers are set to accept cookies.

Each web site can send its own cookie to your browser if your browser's preferences allow it. But, to protect your privacy, your browser only permits a web site to access the cookies it has already sent to you, not the cookies sent to you by other sites. However, if you do not wish to receive any cookies you may set your browser to refuse them.

Cookies in and of themselves do not personally identify users, although they do identify a user's browser. The cookies transferred by the National Prescribing Service Limited website simply contain a unique anonymous identifier which helps us keep track of the number of times a specific advertisement has been seen by an individual computer. This enables National Prescribing Service Limited to limit the number of times a particular ad is shown to each user (e.g. 3 times).
