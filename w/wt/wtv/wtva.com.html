By using the sites, you signify your agreement to this Privacy Policy.  If you do not agree to this Privacy Policy, please do not use the sites.  This Privacy Policy may change from time to time, so please check back periodically to ensure that you are aware of any changes.  Your continued use of the sites will signify your acceptance of these changes.

Information Collection And Use

WTVA does not condition access to any part of the sites on the provision of PII except as described in this Policy.  Each time you visit our sites, our Internet service providers will record your domain name, IP address, browser, platform, date and time of your visit, and the titles of the pages you view.  It is standard practice among Internet service providers to have their servers collect this type of information.

Whenever you visit our sites, WTVA also receives and records information on our server logs from your browser, including your IP address, WTVA cookie information, and the pages you request, and relates it to the PII you provide.  WTVA uses this information for the following general purposes: to improve our products and services, to contact you, to conduct research, to evaluate traffic patterns, to plan adjustments to our sites, and to provide anonymous reporting for tracking usage of our sites.  Our Internet service providers may use the information to make certain their servers are performing optimally.

Once you have registered to post information to or to receive information from our sites, we reserve the right to send you certain communications relating to WTVA's services, such as announcements, administrative messages, contest promotions, and messages from our contest sponsors, that are considered part of your WTVA account.

You will be offered the opportunity to opt-out of receiving future messages and announcements and to remove your name from WTVA mailing lists.  The opt-out provisions do not apply to information collected by cookies or used internally to recognize you and/or facilitate your visits to the sites, or information we may retain to comply with legal requirements.

Our sites use a software technology called "cookies."  Cookies are small text files that we place in a visitor's computer to store preferences and for identification purposes.  A cookie file can contain information such as a user ID to track the pages visited, but the only personal information a cookie can contain is information you set your browser to allow.  These files are used for site registration and customization the next time you visit us.  Some parts of the sites may also use cookies to track user traffic patterns.  Our sites do this in order to determine the usefulness of our site information to our users and to see how effective our navigational structure is in helping users reach that information.  Third party advertisers on our sites may also place cookies on your computer for their own use, and WTVA has no control and cannot accept any liability for cookies placed by third parties.

Please note that cookies cannot read data from your hard drive.  Your Web browser may allow you to be notified when you are receiving a cookie, giving you the choice to accept it or not.  If you prefer not to receive cookies while browsing our sites, you can set your browser to warn you before accepting cookies and refuse the cookie when your browser alerts you to its presence.  You can also refuse all cookies by turning them off in your browser.  By not accepting cookies, some pages may not fully function and you may not be able to access certain information on our sites.

"Web beacons" are small pieces of code placed on a Web page to monitor the behavior and collect data about the visitors viewing a Web page.  For example, Web beacons can be used to count the users who visit a Web page or to deliver a cookie to the computer of a visitor viewing that page.  We may use Web beacons on our sites from time to time for this purpose.

We have taken steps to assure that all information collected will remain secure and in its original form, i.e., free from any alteration.  As such, access to all PII is strictly controlled.  We have put in place appropriate physical, electronic, and managerial procedures in an effort to safeguard and help prevent unauthorized access, maintain data security, and correctly use the information we collect online.
