What is WindowsMedia.com?
WindowsMedia.com consists of a family of websites and web based services. The websites consist of the WindowsMedia.com home page and all pages that are distributed from that domain.

The WindowsMedia.com services include the following:

  • Metadata Retrieval. Returns general and detailed metadata for CD's, DVD's, and media files to enhance the playback experience. General data might include album art, track names, or lyrics. Detailed data might include a bio on the artist.
  • Metadata Submission. Allows user's to submit additional general and detailed metadata for CD's, DVD's, and media files to WindowsMedia.com in cases where the metadata provided by Windowsmedia.com is incomplete or inaccurate. This service allows users to help improve the quality of the Metadata Retrieval service for other users.
  • Media Guide. An immersive set of web pages focused on finding available media to play in Windows Media format.
  • Radio Tuner. An immersive set of web pages focused on finding available online radio stations.
  • Codec Download. A service that allows users to acquire certain Codecs automatically during playback if they are not resident on the user's system.
  • Player Update. A service that allows users to detect and acquire updated Windows Media Player components.
  • Newsletter Signup. The Media Guide provides a link to the MSN Newsletter service to allow user's to signup for the Windowsmedia.com newsletter.
  • Downloadable Skins. A web page focused on additional skins users can download and install.
  • Downloadable Visualizations. A web page focused on additional visualizations users can download and install.

The WindowsMedia.com services are utilized by Microsoft Windows software such as Microsoft Windows Media Player and Microsoft Internet Explorer.

What Information Does WindowsMedia.com Gather?
In general, WindowsMedia.com does not collect personally identifiable information, such as name, address, or telephone number. WindowsMedia.com does ask for your e-mail address which is a piece of personally identifiable information, if you sign-up for the WindowsMedia.com newsletter. WindowsMedia.com uses the MSN Newsletter Service to deliver you the WindowsMedia.com newsletter. If you wish to remove yourself from the email distribution list you may unsubscribe at any time by clicking on the unsubscribe link at the bottom of the newsletter.

To create a more personal experience, WindowsMedia.com may prompt you for anonymous demographic information, such as your zip code. Your zip code is used to provide you location based services such as local radio stations. Providing anonymous demographic information is optional.

WindowsMedia.com keeps track of the websites and pages you visit within WindowsMedia.com, in order to determine what WindowsMedia.com websites and services are the most popular. This data may be used in an aggregate form by WindowsMedia.com to deliver content that customers may be interested in.

WindowsMedia.com automatically collects certain standard information about your computer hardware and software when you visit its site and stores this information in a log. This standard information can include: your IP address, browser type, domain names, access times and referring website addresses. The log also includes any data WindowsMedia.com receives when processing service requests. WindowsMedia.com retains its daily logs for 12 months.

As described in greater detail below, WindowsMedia.com collects information through the use of cookies and web beacons, and uses that information to operate WindowsMedia.com websites and deliver the services you have requested.

The Use of Cookies by WindowsMedia.com
A cookie is a text file that is placed on your hard disk by a Web page server. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. Cookies are uniquely assigned to you, and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie to you.

A cookie will be sent to WindowsMedia.com whenever its services are accessed. The cookie allows WindowsMedia.com to personalize your WindowsMedia.com experience such as storing your Radio Tuner presets. The cookie also contains a unique identifier which allows WindowsMedia.com to generate anonymous visitor statistics.

You have the ability to accept or decline cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer. If you choose to decline cookies, you may not be able to fully experience the interactive features of WindowsMedia.com or other websites you visit. For example if you decline cookies, most WindowsMedia.com features will continue to be available, but some features, such as the Radio Tuner presets, will not function correctly.

The Use of Web Beacons
WindowsMedia.com web pages may contain electronic images known as Web beacons - sometimes called single-pixel gifs - that allow WindowsMedia.com to count users who have visited those pages and to deliver co-branded services. WindowsMedia.com may include web beacons in WindowsMedia.com Newsletters in order to count how many messages have been opened.

Web beacons collect only a limited set of information including a cookie number, time and date of a page view, and a description of the page on which the Web beacon resides. WindowsMedia.com web pages may also contain Web beacons placed there by an advertising partner, to help WindowsMedia.com determine the effectiveness of our Internet advertising campaigns.

Web beacons are not used to access your personally identifiable information while visiting WindowsMedia.com websites or using its services; they are an industry standard technique we use to compile aggregated statistics about WindowsMedia.com website usage.

Communication with Streaming Media Servers
One of the primary functions of the WindowsMedia.com is to allow access to streaming audio, video, and radio stations. The streaming media servers that provide this content are typically operated by non-Microsoft content providers, and are not under Microsoft's control. During playback of streaming media, Windows Media Player will send the streaming media server a log. It is the responsibility of the content provider to disclose to you whether the logs are shared with third parties and how the logs are used. For more information about your privacy and the playback of streaming media, see the privacy statement for your Windows Media Player.
