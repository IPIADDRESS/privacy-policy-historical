World Press Review Privacy Statement


World Press Review takes the privacy of all its users and customers very seriously, and takes great care with user and customer information.

We do not collect any information about you except what you choose to tell us via our fully secure SSL-encrypted server.

Unless you have voluntarily given us the information we will always ask for your explicit consent if we need to process any personal data about you.

How do we use your information?

We may use your personal information to communicate with you, for record keeping purposes, or to improve the usefulness and content of our website. If you subscribe to the magazine, and only if you subscribe to the magazine, we may also sell your name and home address to carefully-screened direct mail companies and publications so they may contact you. Under no circumstances will we sell your e-mail address to anyone.

Your choice:

We give you the opportunity to opt out of these additional uses of your personal information. To stop receiving offers from our affiliates, kindly contact customer service.

Security:

We take every precaution to protect your personal information. When we ask you to submit sensitive personal financial information, such as your credit card number, we use industry standard Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) encryption technology, making these pages secure.

Our website may contain links to other websites, and you should be aware that we are clearly not responsible for the privacy practices on other sites. We recommend that you individually check the privacy statements of each and every other website that you visit.
