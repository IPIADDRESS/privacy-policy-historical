Dear Friends,

Some food for thought for our readers who are parents of school-age kids:

Just the other day my husband and I found ourselves having lunch in a local pizza place. As I looked around, I realized that other than the manager of the shop, we were the only adults in the place—it was packed with junior high school students who had just gotten out of school.

A few tables away, a group of girls hovered around a girl who was crying. Boy troubles, girlfriend troubles, I thought to myself, remembering how cruel those pre-teen years could be and how I’d never want to go back there again. Those of you who have been there know just what I mean.

As we sat there eating our slices, I found myself listening to the girls’ conversation, wondering what was wrong with the girl who was crying.
