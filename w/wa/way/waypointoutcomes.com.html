WHAT TYPE OF INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT, AND HOW IS IT USED?

When you visit the Site or use the Services, we may collect passive information about your visit. Passive information is any form of aggregate data, navigational data, tracking data, click-stream data, or historical data, which does not on its own personally identify you.

For example, when you visit the Site our servers may collect some basic technical information, including your IP address, the domain name of your ISP (e.g., whether you logged on from aol.com or whitehouse.gov), referral data (e.g., the address of the last URL a user visited prior to clicking through to our Site), and browser and platform type (e.g., a Firefox browser on a Macintosh platform). Your IP address may be collected to determine your geographical location; we may share this information with publishers of textbooks available on the Site as part of our compliance with the publishers’ security requirements. We will not provide to the publishers any information in connection with your IP address that may allow a publisher to identify you.

The Site also uses electronic files such as cookies, web beacons, or log file information to: (1) identify unique browsers that visit the Site; (2) track usage throughout the Site (e.g., tracking where traffic comes from, how traffic flows within the Site, etc.); (3) store information so that you will not have to re-enter it during your visit or the next time you visit the Site; and (4) monitor aggregate metrics such as total number of visitors, pages viewed, etc.

We may share aggregate statistical information with business partners, publishers, or other third parties, but none of them will receive personally identifiable information. For example, we may tell a publisher that X number of trial members accessed a certain area of the Site or viewed a certain textbook, but we will not disclose anything that uniquely identifies those individuals.

From time to time, we may establish a business relationship with other businesses to perform services on our behalf, with whom we may share your personal information (“Service Providers”). For example, we may contract with Service Providers to provide certain services, such as hosting and maintenance, customer relationship management, data storage and management, and direct marketing campaigns. We provide our Service Providers with only the information necessary for them to perform these services on our behalf. Each Service Provider must agree to use reasonable security procedures and practices, appropriate to the nature of the information involved, in order to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

In addition to the above, we also reserve the right to disclose your personally identifiable information as required by law and when we believe that disclosure is necessary to comply with the law or protect the rights, property, or safety of the Site or Services, our users, publishers, or others. Due to the existing regulatory environment, we cannot ensure that all of your personal information will never be disclosed in ways not otherwise described in this Privacy Policy.

If you believe we have received information from a child under 13, please notify us by emailing us at privacy@bridgepointeducation.com.
