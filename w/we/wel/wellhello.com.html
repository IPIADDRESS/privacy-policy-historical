What Are Cookies?

A “cookie” is a small piece of data that a Web site stores in a designated file on your computer. Cookies are commonly used to store user names and/or passwords to facilitate access to the site. Cookies can also be used to store user preferences, login dates and times, and content viewing history. When you visit a website, that site can access that information which it stored in a cookie. Cookies make your online experience easier and more personalized. We use cookies and other technologies to recognize you and personalize your experience, as well as to help us understand how our site is used. Users must have cookies enabled in order for the site to function properly. Cookies themselves do not tell WellHello any personal information about the user. If you do not accept our use of cookies, you may be limited or restricted in your use of the Site.

What Is Server Log and Clickstream Data?

Our site’s servers automatically identify your computer by its Internet Protocol (IP) address (a unique string of numbers that is assigned to your computer by your Internet Service Provider) each time you access the site, and maintain logs of the specific content on the site that you access. Our servers also record various information pertaining to your computer configuration, including the Web browser software you use, the resolution settings of your monitor, and the website from which you were referred to us, if any. We use this information anonymously to help us determine what is the most beneficial for our users and how we can create a better overall user experience.
