WrestleZone.com Privacy Information
  • Does WrestleZone.com collect information about me?
    Sometimes, various sections of our website may collect information about you, but we do not collect any information without telling you. The only information we collect is contact information, through our free email program, contests, and any other area where you might be asked to submit information. You are not required by any means to submit ANYTHING to us, this information is only necessary if you wish to participate in a WrestleZone contest or WrestleZone.com's free webmail service. WrestleZone reserves the right to save, utilize, any contact information, unique identifying information, or dempgraphic information you CHOOSE to submit to us. At this time WrestleZone.com does not collect any medical or financial information.

  • Do companies affiliated with WrestleZone.com collect information about me?
    At this time the only other companies that have content on WrestleZone.com are Burst Media, Inc, and Peel Advertising. Their respective privacy policys can be found at Peel Privacy Policy & Burst Media Privacy Policy Once you click through a link on WrestleZone.com that takes you off the WrestleZone.com server (ie: advertisements, links at the bottom of news articles), the WrestleZone.com privacy policy will no longer apply.

  • Does WrestleZone.com put any cookies on my computer?
    There is only one place on all of WrestleZone.com that will put a cookie on your computer. This section is WrestleZone.com's Discussion Forums. All of WrestleZone's forums will put a small cookie on your computer, which is used STRICTLY for security of ours forums, and to prevent and prosecute spamming in our forums. The cookies that our dicussion forums will put on your computer does not collect ANY information about you.

  • What can I do to protect my privacy online?
    • Junkbusters is an excellent source of information about a wide variety of privacy issues including unsolicited commerical e-mail (a.k.a "spam") and paper junk mail, web ads, and telemarketing.
    • The Electronic Privacy Information Center (EPIC) is a source of information regarding civil liberties issues and protecting privacy, the First Amendment and constitutional values.
    • The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) is also a good source for information about privacy issues as well as freedom of speech rights in cyberspace.
