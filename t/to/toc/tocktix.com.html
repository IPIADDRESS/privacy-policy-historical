What about cookies and other technologies?

Tock also uses automatic data collection technologies to collect information about your equipment, browsing actions and patterns as you navigate through the Site. Information collected by Tock through automatic data collection technologies includes, but is not limited to, details of your visits to the Site (e.g., traffic data, location data, logs and other communication data and the resources that you access and use on the Site) and information about your computer and internet connection (e.g., your Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, operating system and browser type). The technologies we use for this automatic data collection include, but are not limited to, cookies, local storage, tracking pixels, and IP address logging.

Cookies are small amounts of data downloaded to your computer to track movements and behavior within web sites. We may use cookies to deliver content specific to your interests and for other purposes. We may collect information on the frequency that certain digital activities are accessed, how much time is spent engaging certain informational content and which links are clicked. We may link cookie information to personal information.

Local storage is similar to cookies in that it is stored on your computer and can be used to store certain information about your activities and preferences.

Tracking pixels (a.k.a. beacons or tags) are small graphic images placed on a web page, web-based document or in an e-mail message. Tracking pixels are invisible to the user because they are typically very small (only 1-by-1 pixel) and the same color as the background of the web page, web-based document or e-mail message. We do not use tracking pixels to collect personal information about you. However, we may use tracking pixels to capture statistical usage information for the Site or features or other elements on the Site. We may link tracking pixel information to personal information to personalize user experience and for statistical analysis of user experiences on the Site.

Tock also maintains log files which contain IP addresses. An IP address is a numeric address that is assigned to your computer by your Internet Service Provider. In general, we only use log files to monitor traffic on the Site, to report aggregate information to our third-party providers, to troubleshoot technical problems and for statistical analyses and fraud and abuse prevention. In the event of user abuse of the Site, we may block certain IP addresses. IP addresses may be used to personally identify you in order to enforce our Terms of Use.
