Definitions

To help you better understand what you're reading, below are definitions of key terms used in this Privacy Policy and in our Terms of Use (unless otherwise defined); should you have any questions please contact us.

Affiliates

In online retail, affiliation is common in marketing and selling. Basically it means someone, say Amazon, has a website where they sell products, and they pay commission to other entities that send them buyers. So when you click an Amazon link from our site and buy something there, we get paid commission (usually around 4%).

Algorithm

A process or set of rules followed by a computer in performing problem-solving operations.

Browser Web Storage

With the help of a technology called HTML 5, this allows websites to capture and store data in a browser on a device. When used in "local storage" mode, it enables data to be stored across sessions. This allows for data to be retrieved even after you close and re-open your browser. No doubt you've come across this in the "Restore Tabs" feature of browsers.

Cookies

A cookie is a small file containing a string of characters that is sent to your computer when you visit a website. When you visit the site again, the cookie allows that site to recognize your browser. Not only recognize it, cookies may even store your preferences and other info. Some people argue that cookies help for a more smooth browsing experience.

If you're not one of those people, you can configure your browser to refuse all cookies, or to let you know when a cookie is being sent. Keep in mind that some web features or services may not function properly without cookies.

Data Controller means the natural or legal person who (either alone or jointly or in common with other persons) determines the purposes for which and the manner in which any personal information are, or are to be, processed. Data Processor (or Service Provider) means any natural or legal person who processes the data on behalf of the Data Controller. We may use the services of various Service Providers in order to process your data more effectively.

Data Subject (or User)

This would be you. Defined as any living individual who is using our Service and is the subject of Personal Data.

Device

A device is a computer that can be used to access out services. Examples include desktop computers, smartphones, smart speakers, and tablets are all considered devices.

IP address

This is your devices digital address so to speak. Every device connected to the Internet is assigned a number known as an Internet protocol (IP) address. It is often used to identify the location from which a device is accessing the internet.

Non-Personally Identifiable Information (Non-PII)

This is information that is recorded about users so that it no longer reflects or references an individually-identifiable user.

Most of the information we collect falls under this category. With the exception of email addresses, and unless you leave any personally identifiable information in comments; or unless you're a contributor who agreed to share their bio, we typically can't tell anything about you.

Personal Information

This is information that you provide to us which personally identifies you, such as your name, email address, or other data that you leave on our site that can reasonably be used to identify you.

Referrer URL

A Uniform Resource Locator, a.k.a a Referrer URL, is information transmitted to a destination webpage by a web browser, typically when you click a link to that page. The Referrer URL contains the URL of the last webpage the browser visited.

Sensitive Personal Information

This relates to topics such as confidential medical facts, racial or ethnic origins, political or religious beliefs, or sexuality.

Server Logs

As is the case with most websites you visit on a daily basis, our serve automatically records the page requests made when you visited our sites. These “server logs” typically include the date and time of your request, your web request, IP address, you browser type, your browser language, and one or more cookies that may uniquely identify your browser.

Unique Identifiers

This is a string of characters that can be used to uniquely identify a browser, app, or device. Different ones vary in how permanent they are, whether they can be reset by users, and how they can be accessed. They're used for various purposes, from security and fraud to remembering your preferences and providing personalized ads.

For example, unique identifiers stored in cookies help sites display content in your browser in your preferred language.
