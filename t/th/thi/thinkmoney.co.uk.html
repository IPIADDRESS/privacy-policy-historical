Privacy Policy

How your personal information is used by Think Money Group

The information contained in this privacy notice tells you how your personal information is collected and used by Think Money Limited and the Think Money Group companies.

Your information will be held by Think Money Limited which is part of the Think Money Group of companies (collectively referred to as ‘we’, ‘us’, ‘our’ or the ‘Group’). The companies within the Group are set out in section 12 of this Privacy Policy.

Your information will be securely held in the Think Money Group systems, and any third party systems where required, to enable us and the companies within the Group that you may have dealings with, either now or in the future, to be able to administer any products or services that they provide to you.

Last update : 21/08/2017

1. What information we may collect from you

The information we collect from you will depend on the product or service you apply for, or the service that we provide to you. This will likely include the collection of:

  • your personal details (e.g. name, date of birth)
  • address details
  • contact details (e.g. phone number, email)
  • sensitive data* (e.g. medical information)
  • financial information
  • employment information
  • information on how you use our website(s) and products and services

You may not have to provide all the information requested but doing so will help us to ensure we have a fuller understanding about you to allow us to provide services to you.

Sensitive Data

*The Data Protection Act 1998 (the Act) defines some personal information as “sensitive data” (physical or mental health, sexual life, criminal proceedings or offences, religious beliefs, race or ethnic origin, political opinions or trade union membership). This information may be necessary to collect, for example, when you apply for an insurance quote. Where we need to collect and process sensitive data about you, by providing this information to us you give your explicit consent for us and any other third party to process this sensitive data as set out in this Privacy Policy.

2. Joint accounts

If you provide personal information about someone else, for example when a joint application is made, you must do so with the consent of the other person. If you enter into a product jointly with another person, your personal data and any information about the product or service provided to you will be shared with the other person.

The personal information about a joint applicant may be used in the ways as described in this privacy policy.

3. How and where we collect information

We will collect information from you in various ways, including:

  • when you make an application or enquiry within the Group;
  • information received from a third party, for example when we are required to run checks with a third party, such as a credit reference agency or fraud prevention agency, or where you have previously consented for your information to be shared with us;
  • when you provide information to the Group to help them provide and/or administer any product and/or service that is provided to you by the Group;
  • when you participate in market research, competitions and promotions provided by, or on behalf of the Group;
  • when we may need to obtain up to date information about you to meet our legal and regulatory obligations;
  • where you have provided your consent; or
  • where you use a website controlled by the Group.
4. How we may use your information

The personal information you provide may be used in a number of ways, for example;

  • responding to your requests, enquires or complaints;
  • to provide our product or services and to notify you of any changes or developments with the products, services or platforms we provide;
  • statistical analysis;
  • profiling purposes;
  • helping us and the other Group companies to understand and develop the Group’s respective businesses, including new and innovative products and services;
  • to meet our legal and regulatory obligations and for the prevention of fraud, money laundering and terrorist financing;
  • advising you of promotions, competitions and offers;
  • using information gathered from market research or evaluating the effectiveness of marketing and training;
  • undertaking financial reviews; or
  • where we have your consent.

We will keep your information for as long as we reasonably require it, or where we are required to do so under a legal obligation.

5. How we may share your information

We may share your personal information with companies or organisations outside of the Group where:

  • we need to do so to provide you with products or services, for example credit reference agencies;
  • we have entered into an arrangement for a third party to provide us with services, for example IT platforms and services, communication services or market research;
  • where we’re required to do so under a legal or regulatory obligation or court order; or
  • where we have your consent to do so.
6. Safeguarding your personal information

We take the protection of personal data very seriously and we will maintain appropriate safeguards to ensure the security, integrity and privacy of the information you have provided.

7. Credit Applications

If you submit a credit card, loan or mortgage application, we may share your information with one or more of the lender(s) whose relevant products we offer (either directly or via a credit broker). We may also make one or more credit searches for each applicant when making our recommendation or selecting a lender. The chosen lender may also search your record to verify your credit worthiness and identity. The credit reference agency will add details of any searches to your record which can be seen by other organisations that make searches and they may use it to help make credit decisions about you, to prevent fraud and money laundering and to trace debtors. Where there is a joint application the outcome of any credit search may be shared with both persons who make an application.

8. Insurance Applications

If you make an enquiry for an insurance quote or purchase an insurance policy online or over the phone your information will be shared with insurance providers or administrators to provide you with a quote and/or to administer and provide the insurance policy. More information about how your information may be used by the insurer and/or administrator can be found in the policy documents you will receive.

9. Using fraud prevention agencies

The personal information we have collected from you will be shared with fraud prevention agencies who will use it to prevent fraud and money-laundering and to verify your identity. If fraud is detected, you could be refused certain services, finance or employment. Further details of how your information will be used by us and these fraud prevention agencies, and your data protection rights, can be found by contacting us or email us at contactus@thinkmoney.co.uk

10. Sharing your information outside of the EEA

Although we do not routinely transfer personal information outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we may be required to transfer your personal data to staff or to one of our suppliers who are based outside of the EEA. By submitting your personal data, you agree to this transfer, storing or processing. We will take all steps reasonably necessary to ensure that your data is treated securely and in accordance with this privacy policy.

11. How we may contact you about products and services we provide to you

To help us keep you up to date about the products and services that we provide to you and to ensure that you’re kept fully informed we may contact you by letter, telephone, email, text message or may send you messages by any online customer platforms. If you don’t want to be contacted in a particular way then you can request this at any time.

It is important that you keep us up to date when you change your contact details to ensure that we use your up to date contact information.

17. Cookies

When using this website, some information may be collected automatically using 'cookies'. These are small text files that facilitate the processing of your data and enable us to analyse how the website is being used. Cookies can be temporary or permanent.

Temporary cookies form part of the security process while you are using the website; permanent cookies identify the link you used to find our website, check your browser so that we can make sure that our website and services work well with your computer and to help us monitor traffic on our website.

Why are cookies used?

They help visitors. Cookies allow sites to do things like provide personalised content and remember their log-in details and settings. You can turn them off - this won't stop a website from working, but it might mean it won't work as well as it could, or that you have to do the same thing more than once.

They help website owners. Cookies tell website owners things like: what search engine a visitor used to find the website, how often they've visited it, how long they've spent on it, and so on.

Disabling/Enabling Cookies

You have the ability to accept or decline cookies by modifying the settings on your browser and by clicking on the disable button below. Please remember though that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

None of the features of our website will be affected - giving you the full experience.

Disable

If you are using Microsoft Windows Explorer:

  • Open 'Windows Explorer'
  • Click on the 'Search' button on the tool bar
  • Type 'cookie' into the search box for 'Folders and Files'
  • Select 'My computer' in the 'Look In' box
  • Click 'Search Now'
  • Double click on the folders that are found
  • 'Select' any cookie file
  • Use the 'Delete' button on your keyboard

If you are not using Microsoft Windows Explorer, then you should select 'cookies' in the 'Help' function for information on where to find your cookie folder. We do not pass on, or sell cookies to any other companies. Further information on the use of and managing cookies can be found at http://www.allaboutcookies.org.
