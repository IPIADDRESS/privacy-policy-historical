Cookies

A cookie is a small-size text file transferred from a server to web browser and stored on your computer’s hard drive or mobile device. It allows a website to “remember” who you are and simplify the next log in without the need of entering the credentials. It does not include any personal information about you. In most cases, it stores only the session ID.

Most browsers accept them automatically unless it is configured to block them. How to disable cookies in your browser you can find in help documentation on the internet. We recommend leaving them turned on.

Our website thinkmobiles.com uses session cookies (temporary cookies) to verify your identity when you log in, to keep track of which pages you visit, to personalize our service to you. Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser (i.e. the software program you use for searching and viewing various kinds of Internet resources such as information on a website).

No cookies on our site are served or used by third parties.

Web beacons:

Some of our web pages contain electronic images known as web beacons (as well known as web bugs, clear gifs, or trackers) that allow us to count the number of users who have visited these pages. They don’t allow us to identify any particular individual. Web beacons collect only limited information which includes a cookie number, time and date of a page view, and a description of the page on which the web beacon resides.

Web beacons behave the same way as any other content request included on a web page, it is not possible for you to refuse them.
