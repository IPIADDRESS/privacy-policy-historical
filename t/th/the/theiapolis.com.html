Privacy Policy

Theiapolis respects privacy of Internet Users and does not collect personally identifying information without the Internet User's permission.

However you should know that third parties may be placing and reading cookies on your browser, or using web beacons to collect information, in the course of ads being served.

This policy applies to the web site http://www.theiapolis.com/ ("Theiapolis") and all its sub-domains. The full text of our privacy is available on the Web at: http://www.theiapolis.com/privacy.html.

We invite you to contact us if you have questions about this policy.


Collection of Data from Visitors

Theiapolis does not collect Internet User identity or contact information from Internet User's visits to theiapolis.com. For each visit to theiapolis.com a given amount of information is collected and stored on our servers. No more information is collected than is typically collected in server logs by web sites in general.

Click-stream data:

  • URI of requested resource:
    The full URI requested by the user. This mean the full address of the page being requested. This information is only used for statistics purpose to determine which parts of our site are the most visited. This information is never linked to an individually identified person.

  • Request timestamp:
    Time that the request was processed by the server. We use this information to watch the evolution of visits along days, weeks and months. For example this helps us to schedule maintenance operations. This information is never linked to an individually identified person.

  • Client's IP address:
    The IP (Internet Protocol) address of the user's computer. This data is anonymous. It is simply a number in the form xxxx.xxxx.xxxx.xxxx. In theory it is possible to find exactly who you are, or more precisely, where you are, thanks to this number. However, in practice this data can only be used to record the number of unique visitors and their country or origin. That is all. As with all the other information we collect, it is never linked to any individually identified person. We only use this information in a global way to know how many visitors are from the USA, Canada, France, UK, and so on. This helps us to determine the kind of audience of our site and thus to customize as much as possible and to match visitors' interests.

  • Data bytes in response:
    Data bytes returned by the server in response. This is the size of the files served. This information helps us to manage our bandwidth.

  • Response status code:
    HTTP status code generated by the server for the response. Status codes allows tracking possible missing files or broken links (well known 404 error).

HTTP protocol elements:

  • User agent information:
    The client description string sent by most Web browsers, giving the type, version, and platform type of the user's Web browser. This is sent by clients as the User-Agent: HTTP header. This information is used for statistics to know how many visitors are using Internet Explorer, Firefox, Opera, etc. We also use this information to customize HTML pages. It happens that sometimes Web browsers do not interpret an HTML code exactly the same way. So with this information we can serve pages that are compatible with visitor's Web browser.

  • Last URI requested by the user:
    This is sent by clients as the Referer: HTTP header. This data is not linked to an individually identified person and is only used to know from where visitors are coming and how they browse our site.

The above data are marked as non-identifiable. This means that there is no reasonable way for the site to identify the individual person this data was collected from.

Cookies

HTTP cookies, or more commonly referred to as Web cookies, tracking cookies or just cookies, are a technology which can be used to provide you with tailored information from a Web site. A cookie is an element of data that a Web site can send to your browser, which may then store it on your system.

Theiapolis makes use of cookies to store a given number of information such as session tracking for logged members.

We use cookies to ensure that we give you the best experience on our website. If you continue without changing your settings, we'll assume that you are happy to receive all cookies from this website. If you would like to change your preferences you may do so by following the instructions here

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are common questions and answers regarding our privacy policy.

What information does Theiapolis collect from you?

  • You can visit Theiapolis and use many of our services without having to tell us who you are or reveal any personally identifiable information to us. It is only when using certain Theiapolis services, or participating in the Theiapolis Community, that you will be asked to provide us with personally identifiable information (e-mail address, date of birth, etc.)

  • Theiapolis may set and access cookies on your computer. Cookies are used to store your preferences/options and other kinds of information in order to customize your use of Theiapolis, as well as to identify you when you log on to your account.

  • Whenever you visit Theiapolis our web server logs automatically receive and record anonymous information from your web browser including your IP Address, Theiapolis cookie information, the Theiapolis page(s) you request, and any search terms you query.

  • When using some particular services of Theiapolis you may be requested to supply personal information, such as your email address. For example, to participate in the Theiapolis Community you have to provide a user name (which can be a nickname), a valid email address and your Date of Birth.

Do any third parties receive or collect your information through Theiapolis?

  • Theiapolis does not and will not share personally identifiable information with other companies or individuals.

  • Theiapolis allows other companies that are presenting advertisements on some of our pages to set and access their cookies on your computer or use web beacons to collect information, in the course of ads being served. Other companies' use of their cookies is subject to their own privacy policies, not this one. Advertisers or other companies do not have access to Theiapolis' cookies or Members' data.

What are you doing with e-mail addresses?

  • When you participate in the Theiapolis Community by creating an account, posting messages, uploading images, etc., you have to provide a valid e-mail address. However this e-mail will be kept confidential. Theiapolis will never share, communicate or sell these e-mail addresses with other companies or individuals.

  • Theiapolis will only use your e-mail address for communication purpose. For example, we may address to you, from time to time, newsletters announcing changes to Theiapolis.

  • When you participate in the Theiapolis Community, this e-mail address can be used to send you notification each time a topic you are monitoring is updated.
