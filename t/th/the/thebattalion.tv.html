(4) Collection of Non-Personal Data

GIFs. A clear gif [Graphics Interchange Format] (also known as pixel tags, web beacons or web bugs) is a tiny line of code only 1 by 1 pixel in size that is programmed to collect certain Data about a visit to a particular website, such as how many hits a site link receives or to retrieve track visitor preferences. These devices automatically collect data, including your Internet server and browser identification, Internet service provider, click-through information, the web page from which a view enters our site, web pages visited, usage patterns, duration of site visit and domain type. We may use these devices, and the general information gathered from these devices, to gauge the effectiveness of our website and site features; to tailor our website to specific interests or participation; to simplify site navigation; or to deliver a more personalized website service by recognizing certain choices you have made on our site. We may also use these devices to conduct research, for auditing purposes, or to estimate our audience size. We do not use cookies or gifs to independently collect personal information. However, the aggregate "hits" data collected by these tracking devices may electronically link to some personal information that you have voluntarily provided over our website. We do not disclose this combined information (personal information linked to our cookie and gif collections) to nonaffiliated third parties. To prevent the automatic recording of information through cookies, you can configure your browser to either reject all cookies or notify you when a cookie is sent. If you disable cookie tracking, however, you may find that some areas on the website will be slower or may not function at all.
