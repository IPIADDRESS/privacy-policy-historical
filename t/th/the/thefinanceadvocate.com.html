Types of Information Collected

The only information we collect from you is anonymous data. We automatically track and collect the following types of information: 1) IP addresses; 2) domain servers; 3) types of computers accessing the Site; and 4) types of web browsers used to access the Site. Third party service providers also collect information from you when you use our site, including information about your use of our Site (for example, pages displayed and items clicked) and information that that is useful for displaying customized advertising to you.

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your computer when you visit certain web pages. A4D collects information through cookies and web beacons about your web browsing activities such as the address of the page you are visiting, the address of the referrer page you previously visited, the time you are viewing the page, your browsing environment and your display settings. We place a cookie on your computer to: analyze web traffic patterns on the Site and other non-A4D websites; understand how you use and interact with the Site; optimize your experience on our Site; obtain anonymous individual or aggregate auditing, research, modeling and reporting information; provide you relevant advertising and content; and properly manage our Site.

Please note that cookies and web beacons are also placed by third party service providers who perform some of these functions for us. Other companies’ use of their cookies and web beacons is subject to their own privacy policies, not this one. If you are concerned about cookies, most browsers permit individuals to decline cookies. A user refusing cookies can still fully navigate our Site. Similarly, code on our site will cause your browser to communicate with our 3rd-party service providers’ computers to help us analyze your use of our Site.

You will see these ads on our Site and when you visit other websites and online services. These third parties use various techniques (including cookies, JavaScript, device identifiers, location data, and web beacons) to record the pages and usage patterns of you, your browser, and your device for the purpose of customizing the advertising you see and increasing its effectiveness. To opt out of the Doubleclick cookie, visit the Google advertising opt out page. To opt out of Google Analytics, visit the Google Analytics opt out page.
