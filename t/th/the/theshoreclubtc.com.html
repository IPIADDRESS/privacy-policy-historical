The Shore Club on Long Bay ("The Shore Club") is committed to protecting your privacy and developing technology that gives you the most powerful and safe online experience. This Statement of Privacy applies to the web site of The Shore Club, www.ShoreClubTC.com, and governs data collection and usage. By using the web site of The Shore Club, you consent to the data practices described in this statement.

Collection of your Personal Information

The Shore Club collects personally identifiable information, such as your e-mail address, name, home or work address or telephone number. The Shore Club also collects anonymous demographic information, which is not unique to you, which may or many not include your ZIP code, age, gender, preferences, interests and favorites.

There is also information about your computer hardware and software that is automatically collected by The Shore Club, through Google Analytics or other traffic log analysis software for example. This information can include: your IP address, browser type, domain names, access times and referring Web site addresses. This information may be used by The Shore Club for the operation of its service, to maintain quality of its service, and to provide general statistics regarding use of the web site of The Shore Club.

Please keep in mind that if you directly disclose personally identifiable information or personally sensitive data through The Shore Club public message boards, this information may be collected and used by others. Note: The Shore Club does not read any of your private online communications.

The Shore Club encourages you to review the privacy statements of Web sites you choose to link to from the web site of The Shore Club, so that you can understand how those Web sites collect, use and share your information. The Shore Club is not responsible for the privacy statements or other content on Web sites outside of the web site(s) of The Shore Club.

Use of your Personal Information

The Shore Club collects and uses your personal information to operate the web site of The Shore Club and deliver the services you have requested. The Shore Club also uses your personally identifiable information to inform you of other products or services available from The Shore Club and potentially its affiliates. The Shore Club may also contact you via surveys to conduct research about your opinion of current services or of potential new services that may be offered.

The Shore Club does not sell, rent or lease its customer lists to third parties. The Shore Club may, from time to time, contact you on behalf of external business partners about a particular offering that may be of interest to you. In those cases, your unique personally identifiable information (e-mail, name, address, telephone number) is not transferred to the third party. In addition, The Shore Club may share data with trusted partners to help us perform statistical analysis, send you email or postal mail, provide customer support, or arrange for deliveries. All such third parties are prohibited from using your personal information except to provide these services to The Shore Club, and they are required to maintain the confidentiality of your information.

The Shore Club does not use or disclose sensitive personal information, such as race, religion, or political affiliations, without your explicit consent.

The Shore Club will disclose your personal information, without notice, only if required to do so by law or in the good faith belief that such action is necessary to: (a) conform to the edicts of the law or comply with legal process served on The Shore Club or its web site; (b) protect and defend the rights or property of The Shore Club; and, (c) act under exigent circumstances to protect the personal safety of users of the web site of The Shore Club, or the public.

Use of Cookies

The web site of The Shore Club may use "cookies" to help you personalize your online experience. A cookie is a text file that is placed on your hard disk by a web page server. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. Cookies are uniquely assigned to you, and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie to you.

One of the primary purposes of cookies is to provide a convenience feature to save you time. The purpose of a cookie is to tell the web server that you have returned to a specific page. For example, if you personalize page on the web site of The Shore Club, or register with The Shore Club web site for services, a cookie helps The Shore Club to recall your specific information on subsequent visits. This simplifies the process of recording your personal information, such as billing addresses, shipping addresses, and so on. When you return to the web site of The Shore Club, the information you previously provided can be retrieved, so you can easily use the features that you customized.

You have the ability to accept or decline cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer. If you choose to decline cookies, you may not be able to fully experience the interactive features of the web site of The Shore Club or other web sites you visit.

Security of your Personal Information

The Shore Club secures your personal information from unauthorized access, use or disclosure. The Shore Club secures the personally identifiable information you provide on computer servers in a controlled, secure environment, protected from unauthorized access, use or disclosure. When personal information (such as a credit card number) is transmitted to other web sites, it is protected through the use of encryption, such as the Secure Socket Layer (SSL) protocol.

Changes to this Statement

The Shore Club will occasionally update this Statement of Privacy to reflect company and customer feedback. The Shore Club encourages you to periodically review this Statement to be informed of how The Shore Club is protecting your information.
