We do not collect any personal information from users who browse our website. Users browse anonymously. We do analyze our website logs to collect aggregate data—such as the number of hits per page—and use this data to improve the value of the materials available on our website. If you decide to join the community, you accept responsibility for your profile information.

Our website logs are not personally identifiable, and we make no attempt to link them with the individuals that actually browse the site. We also track content usage and traffic on the site by using “cookies,” a feature of your browser. There is no personal data collected by the cookies. Instead, these software tags record information such as a user’s browser type and the specific sections that a user visited while on our site. Cookies don't tell us who you are, your email address, or anything personal. You can choose, with your browser, not to accept cookies. If you choose not to accept cookies, you may not be able to view all areas of our website.

Some areas of our website may be password-protected. In other select areas, which may include free downloads, newsletter, or other offers, we may request that you provide us with basic information before granting you access to the area. If you choose not to register and provide this information, you can still use most of the site. However, you will not be able to access areas that require registration.

Please note that anything you post on our blog at byronkatie.com is not protected. In addition, this site may contain links to other sites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or the content of such websites.

Privacy of our e-mail lists
We maintain several email lists to keep interested parties informed about Byron Katie, her events, products, and more. Individuals must voluntarily request to join our mailing lists by signing up through a form or other means on our website or providing us with their email address at an event, seminar, direct-mail response, or other channel. We do not sell, rent, loan, trade, or lease the addresses on our list to anyone.

You can request to no longer receive email from us. To opt out of our email list, reply to one of our emails and request to be removed or send an email to webmaster@thework.com.
