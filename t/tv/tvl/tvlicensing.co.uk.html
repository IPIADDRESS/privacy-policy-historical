Privacy policy
This website is operated by the British Broadcasting Corporation in its role as Statutory Licensing Authority and its agents, trading as "TV Licensing" of 100 Temple Street, Bristol, BS1 6AB. ("We").

We aim to be as clear as possible about how and why we use information about you on this website. If your questions are not fully answered by the information below, please contact us. We'll be happy to help.

Our guiding privacy principles
Compliance with the Data Protection Act 1998 underlies all of our TV Licensing personal information privacy practices. We take all reasonable care to safeguard your information through security policies and secure business processes.

Why we collect information about you
To administer television licence applications, to enable us to collect television licence fees and to enforce television licensing policy. The information we hold may be used for any of TV Licensing's functions.

To ensure that correspondence with you is accurate and legally compliant.

To keep your information secure. To minimise the risk of unauthorised access to your information, we use some of your information to verify your identity when you use the website and our customer service helpdesk.

To help understand and improve the effectiveness of the site for our users.

How we receive information about you
We receive information from you in four ways:

Directly from you. When you apply for a television licence we ask for information about yourself to allow us to correctly process your payment, to send your licence and to contact you by mail, telephone, email or other methods in the future on matters relating to your licence. See the detailed statements on individual web pages describing how your information will be used.

From third parties. We may ask for information about you from third parties, for example when we get authorisation for a payment you make using a credit or debit card.

From the site. The data that we collect does not identify you as an individual. We use 'first party session cookies'. Please click on the link for more information on how we use cookies

From our advertising. We may use cookies to help measure and improve the effectiveness of our banners and emails in order to make sure that our publicity spend is allocated wisely.

We know that your privacy is very important to you and that you take the security of your information seriously. Cookies
Cookies are small text files placed on your computer when you browse a website. In common with many other websites, TV Licensing use cookies for a variety of purposes.

TV Licensing uses first party 'session cookies' to retain information from page-to-page on our online forms. So if you're completing a form and you decide to go back to a previous page, you don't need to fill it in all over again. We also use 'first party persistent cookies' in a general way, to gauge use of our website and measure response to our publicity and site usage.

TV Licensing may use the same cookie for more than one purpose. For example, to understand the effectiveness of a campaign a cookie may be used to measure media responsiveness and site navigation.

You cannot be individually identified from the information stored in, or obtained from, a cookie.

Most browsers are set to automatically accept cookies and if you're using Microsoft's Internet Explorer or Netscape Navigator you should be able to configure your browser to restrict or block cookies, or you may wish to delete them.

For example, you may decide to delete or block the tracking cookie(s). The main one is set by the domain reporting.tvlicensing.co.uk and is managed by our agent Red Eye International Ltd. For your information, this particular cookie cannot be used or accessed by any company besides TV Licensing.

For more information, including browser-specific instructions on how to restrict, block or delete cookies, visit: http://www.allaboutcookies.org/manage-cookies/index.html.

Please note that should you block all cookies, you will not be able to use the forms on our site. If you block our tracking cookie only, your use of our site during a session should not be affected.
