Cookies

A cookie is a small data file that is written to your computer's hard drive by your web browser. A cookie file can contain and/or automatically collect information, such as a user identification code or IP address, that a website will use to track the pages and number of times you have visited. The data read from these cookies may be linked to personally identifying (PII) and non-personally identifying (non-PII) information.

We and the third-party companies we employ use cookies and similar technologies on the Sites to personalize and optimize your browsing experience and the Sites by:

providing you tailored ads and content,
enabling social media features,
safeguarding against spam and malware,
analyzing trends, traffic, and user behavior,
administering the website,
gathering demographic data about our user base as a whole,
tracking web and advertising analytics throughout the Sites
performing location-related functionalities and analytics, and
educating ourselves about how we can continue to improve the Sites and its various elements.
If you would like more information about the above practices and to know your choices about not having this information used by our partner companies, please visit the Network Advertising Initiative's tool for opting out of interest-based advertising.
