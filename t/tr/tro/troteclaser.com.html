Privacy Policy & Data Protection

1. General information

Dealing sensitively with customer data  is of greatest importance for Trotec Laser GmbH (“Trotec”, “we”, “us”). Thus, personal data is collected, stored and used in accordance with the data protection regulations, particularly the Austrian Data Protection Act in its current version ("DSG") and the EU General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR") for fulfilling contractual obligations. Any use over and above this, which is not justified on the basis of overriding legitimate interests in accordance with the applicable Data Protection Act, is permissible only after your consent following prior informatoin. You can revoke your consent at any time (e.g. via an e-mail to trotec@troteclaser.com). This does, however, not affect the right to data processing for the performance of a contract. More details about the information that we collect, process and use when you visit and use our website www.troteclaser.com ("website") have been provided below.

2. By using our website, you are consenting to this Privacy Policy.  Please read it carefully. [1]

Managing Your Information Preferences

You can review, correct, update, or change your personal data or opt out of receiving certain e-mails by e-mailing us at trotec@troteclaser.com.  You are able to opt out of receiving marketing e-mails from us.  If you have questions or concerns regarding this Privacy Policy, please e-mail us at the same address.

3. Which of your data does Trotec process?

Contact and enquiry data: When you actively establish contact with us (e.g. via e-mail or via our contact form), the data and details (name, address, company name, question, telephone number, e-mail and content of the enquiry) are processed.

Registration data: If you register for seminars and workshops, we record the registration data provided (name, company name, address, e-mail, telephone, booked events).

Location data: If you are searching for a Trotec location near you, we process the location (address) specified by you for displaying the hits. We delete this information after your visit to the website.

Technical data: We record the IP address of your device, the Internet browser used and your OS, your clicking behaviour on the website and the website from which you visit us. This personal data can also be collected by using cookies (see below).

4. For which purposes does Trotec process your personal data?

Trotec collects, stores and uses your personal data, (i) to allow you to access special information, (ii) to contact you within the scope of the business relationship (iii) to improve the services provided by Trotec and the website in accordance with Art 6 Para 1 lit b GDPR.

Contact you for advertising purposes, for

  • sending marketing and product information in relation to goods and services from the (new) product range of Trotec (via e-mail, text message, fax, social media messenger services)
  • satisfaction surveys regarding service and consulting
  • requirements analyses
  • event invitations, training opportunities, vouchers and discount campaigns
  • reminders, if you have added products or services to the shopping basket, but have not completed the buying process
  • in relation to regional customs and traditions, such as sending Christmas or greeting cards

is done only after obtaining your consent in accordance with Art 6 Para 1 lit a GDPR. Within the scope of the consent for receipt of the newsletter, you also consent to your data being sent within the Group to affiliated companies of Trotec Laser GmbH, namely Trodat GmbH and Trodat Produktions GmbH, each at Linzer Straße 156, 4600 Wels, so that they can also contact you, to the extent of your consent. You may revoke this consent at any time for individual or all purposes by contacting Trotec.

Insofar as it is necessary for contract fulfilment, your personal data will be disclosed to companies of the Trotec Group or their sales and service partners in the countries relevant to you so that they can take action on behalf of Trotec Laser GmbH. We will therefore send your data to third parties only for the fulfilment of our obligations.

5. How long does Trotec store your personal data?

Your personal data will be stored by us for as long as required to fulfil our obligations towards users. However, due to our retention obligations, we store your personal data in relation to orders and bookings for three or a maximum of seven years after contract fulfilment. If you have registered to receive newsletters or be contacted for marketing purposes and are not one of our customers, we store your data until you revoke your consent and for a maximum of three years after your last contact. 

6. Cookies and Google Analytics

Like most websites, we use cookies. Cookies are small text files which a website or your service provider places on the hard disk of your device via the web browser so that the website can be more conveniently designed and your user behaviour on the website can be understood better.

Trotec mainly uses (i) functional cookies, which are necessary for operating our website, and (ii) cookies, which collect statistical data about the use of the website, so that we can improve our services. Trotec occasionally also uses trustworthy cookies from third-party providers who collect this information for us.

This website also uses Google Analytics, a web analysis service of Google Inc. (“Google”). Trotec uses data from Google Analytics to improve the website for the visitors. This data shortens the loading times and makes the content that is are generated more interesting for the visitors.

The following information is collected by Google Analytics: browser, device type, device model, location, country, city, service provider, monitor resolution, dwell time, language, operating system, visited pages on the website. The IP addresses are anonymised in advance and a personal reference cannot be established with the collection of data.

The information created by the Google Analytics cookie concerning your use of this website is generally transmitted to Google’s servers outside Europe (e.g. in the USA) and stored there. Google will use this information to evaluate your use of the website, to produce reports concerning the website activities for the website operator and to produce additional services associated with the use of Internet and the website. Google may also transfer this information to third parties, if this is legally permitted or if third parties process this data on behalf of Google.  For more information about how to opt out of having your information used by Google Analytics, visit tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout/.

You can prevent the cookies from being installed by means of a relevant setting in your browser software; however, we must point out that in such a case, all functions of this website will not be available to you in their full capacity. Unless you opt out, the cookies are stored on your end device for a duration of 26 months.

7. Online Tracking

Our systems do not recognize browser “Do Not Track” signals, but several of our service providers who utilize these cookies on our website enable you to opt out of targeted advertising practices.  To learn more about these advertising practices or to opt out of this type of advertising, you can visit www.networkadvertising.org or www.aboutads.info/choices/.  We also provide you with additional tools to opt out of marketing from us.  You can learn about this in the “Managing Your Information Preferences” section of this Privacy Policy.[2]

9. How does Trotec protect your data?

We take appropriate technical and organisational security measures within the scope of Art. 5 Para 1 lit f or Art. 32 GDPR to protect your personal data from unintentional or unlawful deletion, modification, damage or loss as well as against unauthorised disclosure or unauthorised access. Furthermore, we and our employees undertake to comply with data secrecy.  However, no data transmission over the Internet or other network can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.  As a result, while we strive to protect information transmitted on or through the website, we cannot and do not guarantee the security of any information you transmit on or through the website, and you do so at your own risk.[3]

Our website may contain links to other websites or allow others to send you such links.  A link to a third party’s website does not mean that we endorse it or that we are affiliated with it.  We do not exercise control over third-party websites.  You access such third-party websites or content at your own risk.  You should always read the privacy policy of a third-party website before providing any information to the website.[4]

Please be aware that your personal data and communications may be transferred to and maintained on servers or databases located outside your state, province, or country including the United States.  The laws in those countries may not provide the same level of data protection compared to the country in which you initially provided your personal data. However, please rest assured that we implement all the necessary and appropriate legal, organizational and technical measures to protect your personal data. Furthermore, as regards the transfer of personal information to countries outside the EU, we have entered so-called “Standard Contractual Clauses” approved by the Commission of the European Union for international transfer of personal information to controllers or processors established in third countries as the case may be.

12. Compliance with Laws and Law Enforcement

We cooperate with government and law enforcement officials and private parties to enforce and comply with the law.  We may disclose personal data and any other information about you to government or law enforcement officials or private parties if, in our discretion, we believe it is necessary or appropriate in order to respond to legal requests (including court orders and subpoenas), to protect the safety, property, or rights of our company or of any third party, to prevent or stop any illegal, unethical, or legally actionable activity, or to comply with the law.[6]

13. Which rights do you have?

You have a right to access personal data that we process (Art. 15 GDPR). In addition, you have the right of rectification of inaccurate data and erasure in accordance with Art. 16 and 17 GDPR). Furthermore, you have a right to restriction of the processing (Art. 18 GDPR) and the right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR). Furthermore, you may revoke your consent to processing of personal data for the future if the processing is based on your consent. If you no longer want to receive our newsletter or our advertising e-mails, please click on the “unsubscribe” link, which is contained in all of the e-mails sent by Trotec. In addition to this, it is also possible to revoke by calling or e-mail.

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Austrian Data Protection Authority (Wickenburggasse 8, 1080 Vienna). If you have any questions about your rights or the data processing, you can also contact us:
