Privacy Policy

As with many websites, we collect the e-mail addresses of those who communicate with us via e-mail, aggregate information on what pages consumers access or visit, and information volunteered by the user. The information we collect is used to improve the content of our web pages and the quality of our service and is never shared with or sold to other organizations for commercial purposes, except to provide products or services you've requested, when we have your permission, or if it is necessary to share information in order to investigate, prevent, or take action regarding illegal activities, suspected fraud, situations involving potential threats to the physical safety of any person, violations of our Terms of Service, or as otherwise required by law.

Information Gathering and Usage

  • When you register on Ghost.org we ask for information such as your name, email address, billing address, credit card information. Members who sign up for the free account are not required to enter a credit card.
  • Ghost Foundation uses collected information for the following general purposes: products and services provision, billing, identification and authentication, services improvement, contact, and research.

Cookies

  • A cookie is a small amount of data, which often includes an anonymous unique identifier, that is sent to your browser from a web site's computers and stored on your computer's hard drive.
  • Cookies are required to use the Ghost.org service.
  • We use cookies to record current session information, but do not use permanent cookies. You are required to re-login to your Ghost.org account after a certain period of time has elapsed to protect you against others accidentally accessing your account contents.

Data Storage

Ghost Foundation uses third party vendors and hosting partners to provide the necessary hardware, software, networking, storage, and related technology required to run Ghost.org. Although Ghost Foundation owns the code, databases, and all rights to the Ghost.org website, you retain all rights to your data.

Disclosure

Ghost Foundation may disclose personally identifiable information under special circumstances, such as to comply with subpoenas or when your actions violate the Terms of Service.

Data Location

If you choose to provide Ghost.org with your information, you consent to the transfer and storage of that information on our servers located in the United Kingdom.

Changes

We may periodically update this policy. We will notify you about significant changes in the way we treat personal information by sending a notice to the primary email address specified in your Ghost.org primary account holder information or by placing a prominent notice on our site.

Questions

Any questions about this Privacy Policy should be addressed to info [at] ghost.org.

Made for Love, Not for Profit

The Ghost Foundation is a legally registered not-for-profit organisation. Why should you care? It means that no one owns shares of monetary value in the project. We're building Ghost because we want online publishing to be better. We want to make decisions focused on that goal, not on building a startup that we can sell to Facebook for $1billion.

Any money Ghost makes can only ever be used to re-invest in making Ghost better. Not making shareholders rich.
