TraitPerception, Inc. (“TraitPerception” or “we”) operates the Traity services to help you reach new opportunities based on who you really are in terms of your personality strengths. We provide you with services so that you can learn more about yourself, provide and receive feedback from others, search for a job, source leads for recruiting or sales, create and maintain a professional proﬁle, and build your network of professional connections.

We provide this Privacy Policy to inform you of our policies regarding the collection, use, disclosure and protection of information. 2. Traity Community Basics

Through the Traity Services, we provide you with the tools to instantly discover your strengths and weaknesses as perceived by yourself and by your contacts, and start a professional network based on your existing Facebook proﬁle and comprised of your friends and their friends and that you can expand as you connect with other Traity members and friends. You also have the opportunity for potential employers, business partners, Traity customers, professional contacts and friends (the “Traity Community”) to ﬁnd you. We allow our members to communicate and collaborate with each other, learn about business opportunities and jobs and obtain Introductions. Additionally, Traity users may celebrate success stories, engage in activities and acknowledge our community’s pool of talent. You may also have the opportunity to post certain of your Traity activity to Facebook or other third party forums or to have us do so on your behalf (although we don´t request this permission by default).

Below we summarize what it means to be part of the Traity community and how your professional proﬁle and activity will be displayed among the Traity Community, on Facebook, and publicly.

Your Traity Proﬁle is your extended resume on the Internet, including your personality strengths and weaknesses, so that you can ﬁnd you the best companies for “Cultural Fit” . Your “Traity Proﬁle” consists of your information and activities on Traity, including your work history, education, personal and professional work and accomplishments, your connections, your self-evaluation personality, the feedback you receive from other connections, anything you post about yourself or within your proﬁle and actions you take within Traity. Your Traity Proﬁle may be publicly visible to everyone on the Internet. Traity allows you to control whether certain information is public (but note the default may be that certain information in your Traity Proﬁle is designated “everyone” and will be publicly available). You can visit your Account Settings to change this.

Your Traity Proﬁle can be accessed through the Traity Services (e.g., by searching for your name on Traity or by clicking on your name listed on a Connection’s proﬁle) or directly through a simple URL (http://traity.com/namesurname), which you can share or display as you choose (for example, on your email signature or blog).

There are several types of users on Traity, and you may interact with these users in different manners, including through public posts and private messaging features within Traity:

• Traity User. A Traity User is a user who has registered to join Traity by installing any of our mobile or web applications or who has otherwise registered for Traity.• Extended Network Member. An Extended Network Member is a person who has not installed any Traity application but is connected to a Traity User through other social networks (e.g., a Facebook friend or second-degree Facebook friend).
• Connections. A user’s Connections consist of (1) that user’s connections on Traity to Traity Users and (2) Extended Network Members. Additionally, when you join Traity you are automatically connected on Traity to your Facebook friends who are Traity Users and Extended Network Members. You may, however, remove such connections from Traity.
• Enterprise Users. Enterprise Users are corporate customers who use Traity for networking, recruiting and business development purposes. Notwithstanding your privacy settings, Enterprise Users can view any information in your Traity Proﬁle that is designated for “Everyone” or for “Connections Only.”

If you are a Traity User, we may provide functionality such that when you view another Traity user’s proﬁle, that user will be able to view certain information about you (such as your proﬁle photo and your name, company, title, and city).You may be able to view Traity proﬁles without disclosing that information by changing your account settings.

You may restrict the visibility of information displayed on your Traity Proﬁle using our different privacy settings: “Strengths” (will show only your personality strengths), “My personality” (will show your full personality information) and “No Personality” (will not show any of your personality information). To change your privacy preferences visit Account Settings. You can also set your proﬁle as “Public” or “Private” if you don´t want to be searchable.

Public Proﬁle Searchability: If your BranchOut Proﬁle is public, BranchOut makes it available for indexing on search engines, subject to the search engines’ own indexing schedules. Thus, there will likely be a delay before your Traity Proﬁle initially appears in a search engine’s search results and before any update you make to your Traity Proﬁle is reﬂected on a search engine.

Default Post Visibility: When you join Traity, by default your Personality Reports with the answers you provide and the feedback you receive will be visible to Everyone. You can change your default post visibility in Account Settings.
