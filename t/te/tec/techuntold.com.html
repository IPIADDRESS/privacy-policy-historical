What Information Do We Collect & Why Do We Collect This Data?

We collect your personal information when you subscribe to any of our newsletters or contact us using the contact form.

You may have to enter name and Email address when subscribing to our newsletters, contacting us or leaving a comment.

Email Subscription

Your name and email is collected after your consent (by checking the checkbox) for the purpose of sending you updates from our site directly to your email address.

You can always unsubscribe anytime using the Unsubscribe option which is available in every email we send.

Contact Forms

We receive your name and Email address after your consent (by checking the checkbox) when you contact us using our contact form. We keep this data for customer service purposes, but you do not use the information submitted to them for marketing purposes.

Comments

When visitors leave comments on the site we collect the data shown in the comments form after your consent, and also the visitor’s IP address and browser user agent string to help spam detection.

An anonymized string created from your email address (also called a hash) may be provided to the Gravatar service to see if you are using it. The Gravatar service privacy policy is available here: https://automattic.com/privacy/. After approval of your comment, your profile picture is visible to the public in the context of your comment.

Name and email address are used for contacting our users or sending them latest updates on our blog.

Cookies

TechUntold.com does collect cookies automatically when you visit our site on your browser. Please note that cookie is a non-personal information that is not personally identifiable to you and allows TechUntold.com to recognize you each time you visit or login to our site. TechUntold.com doesn’t use cookies to store visitor’s information. 

Third party services such as Google Adsense, Readwhere and Google Analytics which we use on our site store cookies to track visitors and for better ad management. These third-party ad servers or ad networks use technology to the advertisements and links that appear on www.techuntold.com send directly to your browsers. They automatically receive your IP address when this occurs. Other technologies ( such as cookies, JavaScript, or Web Beacons ) may also be used by the third-party ad networks to measure the effectiveness of their advertisements and/or to personalize the advertising content that you see.

TechUntold.com has no access to or control over these cookies that are used by third-party advertisers.

For any concern with a third party, please contact them directly.

External Links

TechUnold has links to other websites on its pages that are not under our direct control. Once you have left TechUntold.com and landed on another site, then it’s their privacy policy that comes into action. We won’t be able to control a user’s privacy on another site. We cannot also be held liable/accountable for any loss/damage occurring as a result of activity on another website.

However, we only recommend relevant products that have high ratings and positive reviews. Apart from that, we are also affiliate partners with other quality products like mSpy and Stellar Data Recovery.

How We Protect Your Data

We use SSL certificate and password and API keys that are regularly cycled for security purposes .

In addition to this, the following precautions have been made:

  • Database access is strictly limited to those requiring it
  • Server access is strictly limited to those requiring it
  • Administrator roles are designed such that those performing routine administrative tasks do not gain access to data that is not relevant to their role

What Data Breach Procedures We Have In Place

In the unlikely case of a data breach, the following steps will be followed:

  • Site, server and database access will temporarily be disabled
  • All of our passwords and security certificates will be changed
  • Our users will be emailed and notified of the breach
  • An internal investigation will be started to ascertain as much as we can about the breach
  • The TechUntold.com site, server and databases will be re-enabled once they’ve been proven to be secure
  • Changes will be made, and documented, to prevent a similar breach in the future
  • A follow up an email will be sent to all of our users, detailing what we learned from the investigation, along with the steps to prevent future breaches
