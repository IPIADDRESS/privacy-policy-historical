privacy policy

Textbooks.com respects your privacy and we will do our best to earn and keep your trust. This Privacy Policy identifies how we collect, use and protect the Personal Information you provide to us when you visit this site.

What Personal Information Do We Collect and How Is It Used?

  • Information you actively submit to Textbooks.com. You may choose to provide certain personal information to Textbooks.com through this site. This “Personal Information” may include your name, email address, billing address, shipping address(es), telephone number, and credit card information. You may be asked to provide this information when you register to create an account with Textbooks.com, complete an order with Textbooks.com (purchase or buyback), subscribe to our Newsletters or Alerts, or contact Textbooks.com with a question or comment. You can browse the Textbooks.com site and take as much time as you want to review our books and other products, services and reviews without having to submit such Personal Information. Even when you use our shopping cart as you browse, there is no need to submit Personal Information. You will need to provide Personal Information in the following instances:

    • Creating a Textbooks.com account. You will need to create a new password-protected Textbooks.com account to complete an order online. To create an account, we require that you provide us with the following Personal Information: your email address, and your first and last names.

    • Completing an order. To complete an order, we require you to access your password protected Textbooks.com account. You will access stored Personal Information or be asked to submit additional Personal Information such as your credit card number and billing address. If you do not feel comfortable sending your credit card information to us over the Internet, you still can shop with us. We welcome you to call us to complete your purchase by phone. Please go to our Help link and see the Payment Options section for details.

    • Receiving newsletters and alerts. When you complete an order, you will be given the opportunity to sign up to receive our promotional newsletter and alerts. The option will be selected for you; however, you can opt-out from the receipt of such communications. You also can opt-out from receipt of such communications by logging onto My Account and following the instructions under “email preferences,” or by selecting the “unsubscribe” link at the bottom of any newsletter or alert.

    • Contacting Textbooks.com. To contact our Customer Service Team online with any questions, you will need to provide your name and email address. You can also contact us by telephone.

    We use the Personal Information described above to fulfill your orders for books and related merchandise, provide customer support, and communicate with you. In addition, Textbooks.com may use the information to market its products and services to you. You can opt-out from us using your information for this purpose at any time by logging onto My Account and following the instructions under Preferences and Surveys.

    Except as described below, Textbooks.com will not disclose your Personal Information to third parties who are not our affiliates without your consent. We do not sell or exchange customer lists or customer information for marketing purposes with unaffiliated third parties, and will not do so without your express permission. We may share anonymous, aggregate information concerning the demographic makeup of our customers to unrelated third parties.

    We may share Personal Information we collect for business purposes with companies and individuals who provide servicing, administrative, marketing, and other services for us, or on our behalf. Companies we hire to provide such services are only permitted, by contract, to use your information for the performance of the specific services we have requested of them.

    Textbooks.com may disclose Personal Information to government and regulatory authorities as permitted or required by applicable law, to enforce or apply the terms of any of our policies or user agreements, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Textbooks.com, our users, or others.

    Textbooks.com is not responsible for the privacy practices or the content of other websites that may be linked to this site. When you click on links and banners on our site that take you to third-party web sites, you will be subject to the third parties' privacy policies. you are linking from our site or browsing on your own.

  • Automatically collected information. Textbooks.com’s web servers automatically collect anonymous information about a visitor’s IP address, browser type and referrer by reading this information from your browser. This information is collected in a database and is used to help us recognize you each time you return to our site. This information is automatically logged by most websites.

  • Information collected using cookies. Textbooks.com may use various kinds of software devices, such as “cookies” to collect information about Internet use. These small files identify your browser and save information such as passwords so that Web sites can recognize you. You can set your browser to disable cookies, but some portions of this site (and other sites) may not work properly if you do this.

  • Information collected using pixel tags or clear GIFs. Textbooks.com may use "message format" and "message open" sensing technologies to help us understand the effectiveness of certain of our email marketing efforts. Both technologies require the use of pixel tags or clear GIFs (also called web beacons). The "message format" sensing technology allows us to recognize whether you have enabled your email program to receive HTML emails; if so, this information is then associated with your email address so that subsequent messages can be sent to you in HTML format. The "message open" sensing technology allows us to recognize whether you have opened our email message. We can only detect this if your have enabled your email program to receive HTML emails.

How Is Your Personal Information Protected?

Textbooks.com maintains reasonable security procedures and practices appropriate to the nature of the information, to protect the Personal Information from unauthorized access, destruction, use, modification or disclosure. Nonaffiliated third parties are contractually obligated to maintain strict confidentiality, and are not permitted to use your Personal Information for marketing purposes, or for any purpose other than that we have requested of them. To further secure your Personal Information:

  • We use Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) encryption technology to prevent Personal Information from being intercepted and read as it is transmitted over the Internet. The encrypted data goes to a secure site where your information is stored on restricted-access computers located at restricted-access sites.

  • We cross check an email address with the submitted password to validate access to any Textbooks.com customer account.

  • We do not allow any email address to access more than one Textbooks.com account. When a new Textbooks.com account is created, we require you to submit an email address that is not associated with an existing Textbooks.com account.

  • We encourage you to use a password that is not easily determinable (e.g., don't use your name or street name). Keep your password secure; do not share it with anyone. The password is the "key" to your account; anyone who gets this "key" can, along with your email address, gain access to your account.

    • If you forget your password, go to the My Account log in page and click on the “Forgot your password?” link, and then type in the email address of the account you would like to open. A message will be sent to your email address with instructions on how to access your account and change your password.

    • If you want to change your password or other account information, simply log onto your account and follow the instructions.

  • We encourage you to remember to sign out. To further prevent unauthorized access to your account, remember that after you sign in, you should sign out once you have (i) completed your transaction, (ii) completed managing your account, or (iii) decided to step away from your computer. Anyone who accesses your computer after you have signed in but before you have either signed out or your session has timed-out can access your account.

How Can You Access Your Personal Information?

To access, correct or delete your Personal Information stored by Textbooks.com, please log in to the My Account page and update your information.

How Will You Know If We Change Our Privacy Policy?

Textbooks.com may, but is not required to, update this policy from time to time. Accordingly, you should periodically check this web site for any changes.
