The Tealium Web Site may contain links to other websites. The privacy statements of such other websites govern the content and use of information of those websites. Tealium encourages you to review the privacy statements of such other websites to understand their information practices. Please read the following carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your personal information and how we will treat it. Tealium collects individually identifiable information (“Personal Information”) from: (i) individuals who visit the Tealium Web Site (“Site Visitors”); and (ii) individuals who use the Services (“Customers”).

Site Visitors
Tealium may collect information from your computer or mobile device as you navigate the Tealium Web Site through the use of commonly used web site information-gathering tools, such as cookies and web beacons (“Web Collection Information”). The Web Collection Information includes information from your web browser (such as browser type and browser language), your Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, and the actions you take on the Tealium Web Site (such as the web pages viewed and the links clicked). Read more detailed information about how we use web beacons and cookies and the purpose for which we use them.

Customers
Tealium may ask you to provide Tealium with personal contact information, such as name, company name, address, phone number, and email address (“Contact Information”) if you request additional information about the Services or register to use the Services. If you purchase the Services, Tealium may require you to provide financial and billing information, such as billing name and address, company bank account number, credit card number, and the number of employees within the organization that will be using the Services (“Financial Information”). Tealium may also ask you to provide additional information, such as number of website visitors or number of employees (“Other Customer Information”). Contact Information, Billing Information, and Other Customer Information about Customers are referred to collectively as “Customer Information”.

User Generated Content
Some areas of our website allow you to provide content to us directly, for example, where comments are left on a bulletin board, blog or chat room on our website (a “Public Forum”). In using any Public Forum on our website you give your consent that any personal data or information you submit about yourself or any third party may be read, collected, or otherwise used by anyone who reads that public forum. We will not be responsible for any personal data or information that is posted on a publicly viewable public forum. To request removal of any personal data or information from our blog or community forum, contact us at privacy@tealium.com. In some cases, we may not be able to remove your personal data or information, in which case we will let you know if we are unable to do so and why.

Tealium uses Customer Information to perform the Services requested. To read more about Tealium’s use of Web Collection Information, please click here.

Tealium may use Customer Information and Web Collection Information to enforce Tealium’s web site policies, or to protect Tealium’s intellectual property and other rights or the rights of others using the Tealium Web Sites or the Services.

3. Web Collection Information

This section describes the types of Web Collection Information used on the Tealium Web Site and in connection with the Services, and how this information may be used.

Cookies
Tealium uses cookies to make interactions with the Tealium Web Site easy and meaningful. When you visit the Tealium Web Site, Tealium’s servers send a cookie to your computer. Client-side tracking objects (commonly called cookies or JavaScript files) are small text files located in your browser directory that may be placed on your device. Each cookie will typically contain the name of the domain from which the cookie has come, the “lifetime” of the cookie, and a value (usually a unique number). For a more thorough explanation of what cookies are and how they operate, please visit aboutcookies.org or allaboutcookies.org. On their own, cookies do not personally identify you; they recognize your Web browser. Unless you choose to identify yourself to Tealium, either by responding to a promotional offer, opening an account, or filling out a web form (such as a “Contact Me”), you remain anonymous to Tealium. You have the ability to manage the use of cookies on your computer using controls in your browser. To learn more about how to manage cookies, visit allaboutcookies.org.

Tealium uses cookies that are session-based and persistent-based. Session cookies exist only during one session, i.e., only while you are reading or navigating the Tealium Web Sites. They disappear from your computer when you close your browser software or turn off your computer. Persistent cookies remain on your computer after you close your browser or turn off your computer. Using your browser, you may disable the use of cookies, delete cookies, or individually reject cookies at any time. You may also disable JavaScript at any time. However some features of our website may not function properly unless cookies and JavaScript are enabled and some areas of our website may not function at all. In addition, you may not be able to successfully use the Services.
