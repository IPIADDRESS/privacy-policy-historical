Tealium Inc. Website Privacy Statement

Privacy Statement Effective as of  September 29, 2016

Tealium Inc. (“Tealium”) is committed to protecting the privacy of individuals who visit Tealium’s website (http://tealium.com) (“Tealium Website”) and individuals who use the Services as defined below. This Tealium Website Privacy Statement describes Tealium’s privacy practices in relation to the use of the Tealium Website and the related services offered by Tealium, which services are accessible via the Tealium Website (the “Services”). The Tealium Website may contain links to other websites. The privacy statements of such other websites govern the content and use of information of those websites. Tealium encourages you to review the privacy statements of such other websites to understand their information practices. Please read the following carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your personal information and how we will treat it. Tealium will take all steps reasonably necessary to ensure that your information is treated securely and in accordance with this Tealium Website Privacy Statement.

1. Information We May Collect From You

Tealium collects individually identifiable information (“Personal Information”) from: (i) individuals who visit the Tealium Website (“Site Visitors”) and (ii) individuals who use the Services (“Customers”).

SITE VISITORS
Tealium may collect information from your computer or mobile device as you navigate the Tealium Website through the use of commonly used website information-gathering tools, such as cookies and web beacons (“Web Collection Information”). The Web Collection Information includes information from your web browser (such as browser type and browser language), your Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, and the actions you take on the Tealium Website (such as the web pages viewed and the links clicked). You can read more detailed information about how we use web beacons and cookies and the purpose for which we use them here.

CUSTOMERS
Tealium may ask you to provide Tealium with personal contact information, such as name, company name, address, phone number, and email address (“Contact Information”) if you request additional information about the Services or register to use the Services. If you purchase the Services, Tealium may require you to provide financial and billing information, such as billing name and address, company bank account number, credit card number, and the number of employees within the organization that will be using the Services (“Financial Information”). Tealium may also ask you to provide additional information, such as number of website visitors or number of employees (“Other Customer Information”). Contact Information, Financial Information, and Other Customer Information are referred to collectively as “Customer Information”.

USER GENERATED CONTENT
Some areas of our website allow you to provide content to us directly, for example, where comments are left on a bulletin board, blog or chat room on our website (a “Public Forum”). In using any Public Forum on the Tealium Website you give your consent that any Personal Information you submit about yourself or any third party may be read, collected, or otherwise used by anyone who reads that Public Forum. We will not be responsible for any Personal Information that is posted on a publicly viewable Public Forum. To request removal of any Personal Information from any Public Forum, contact us at privacy@tealium.com. In some cases, we may not be able to remove your Personal Information, in which case we will let you know if we are unable to do so and why.

Tealium may also use Web Collection Information alone or in combination with Customer Information to provide personalized information about Tealium and the Services. To read more about Tealium’s use of Web Collection Information, please click here.

Cookies
Tealium uses cookies to make your interactions with the Tealium Website easy and helpful. When you visit the Tealium Website, Tealium’s servers send a cookie to your device. Client-side tracking objects (commonly called cookies or JavaScript files) are small text files located in your browser directory that may be placed on your device. Each cookie will typically contain the name of the domain from which the cookie has come, the “lifetime” of the cookie, and a value (usually a unique number). For a more thorough explanation of what cookies are and how they operate, please visit aboutcookies.org or allaboutcookies.org. On their own, cookies do not personally identify you; they recognize your browser. Unless you choose to identify yourself to Tealium, either by responding to a promotional offer, opening an account, or filling out a web form (such as “Contact Me”), you remain anonymous to Tealium. You have the ability to manage the use of cookies on your computer using controls in your browser. To learn more about how to manage cookies, visit allaboutcookies.org.

Tealium uses cookies that are session-based and persistent-based. Session cookies exist only during one session, i.e., only while you are reading or navigating the Tealium Website. They disappear from your computer when you close your browser or turn off your computer. Persistent cookies remain on your computer after you close your browser or turn off your computer. Using your browser, you may disable the use of cookies, delete cookies, or individually reject cookies at any time. You may also disable JavaScript at any time. However some features of our website may not function properly unless cookies and JavaScript are enabled and some areas of our website may not function at all. In addition, you may not be able to successfully use the Services.

Wherever we transfer, store or process your Personal Information, we will take reasonable steps to safeguard the privacy of your Personal Information. For European Site Visitors and Customers, your Personal Information may be transferred outside of the European Union in order to provide the information or Services you request from us. By visiting the Tealium Website or using the Services, you agree to Tealium transferring your Personal Information outside of the European Union.

Tealium participates in and has certified its compliance with the EU-U.S. Privacy Shield Framework (Privacy Shield) and Swiss Safe Harbor. To learn more about the Swiss Safe Harbor and view Tealium’ s certification the Swiss Safe Harbor, visit https://safeharbor.export.gov/swisslist.aspx.

Tealium is responsible for the processing of Personal Information it receives, under the Privacy Shield, and subsequently transfers to a third party acting as an agent on its behalf. Tealium complies with the Privacy Shield Principles for all onward transfers of Personal Information from the European Union, including the onward transfer liability provisions.

With respect to Personal Information received or transferred pursuant to the Privacy Shield, Tealium is subject to the regulatory enforcement powers of the U.S. Federal Trade Commission. In certain situations, Tealium may be required to disclose Personal Information in response to lawful requests by public authorities, including to meet national security or law enforcement requirements.
