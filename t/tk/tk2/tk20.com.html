Watermark shall not be responsible for such practices that take place once you leave the Site.

GDPR

What is GDPR?
The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a Regulation in the making by which the European Commission intends to strengthen and unify data protection for individuals within the European Union (EU). It also addresses export of personal data outside the EU. The regulation became effective on May 25, 2018.

Watermark’s Commitment
Watermark is committed to high standards of information security, privacy and transparency. We place a high priority on protecting and managing data in accordance with accepted standards including ISO 27001 and PCI-DSS. The company is complying with applicable GDPR regulations, most importantly as a data processor, while also working closely with our customers and partners to help them meet their contractual obligations as data controllers.

Watermark’s approach to preparing for GDPR is overseen by an internal cross-functional team. As a Company that always considered data security as one of our highest priorities, we will continue the policies that have been place for years. For example:

  1. Continuously improving on our on existing security and business continuity management systems.
  2. Continuing to encrypt all data in transit and at rest.
  3. Watermark will inform any and all client organizations promptly in the event of any incidents involving your users’ data.
  4. Not collecting non-essential data from our clients within our products other than that necessary for us to provide our services.

Going forward, Watermark is committed to the following additional items consistent with GDPR Compliance:

  1. We will be reviewing our existing contracts with data controllers, the use of sub-contractors and any data export arrangements and adding a Data Processing Addendum where deemed appropriate.
  2. Offering enhanced client services to help our client organizations meet their obligations as Data Controllers.
