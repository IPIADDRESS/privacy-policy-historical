    Yes! Insurance is required by law!

    Driving without insurance or other proof of financial responsibility is illegal in almost all states. Penalties for driving without insurance will include a large fine, license and/or registration suspension, and even possibly jail time.

    Furthermore, insurance protects you from major financial burdens in the event of an accident. It also covers damages caused to your and other people's property and injuries. Auto insurance is a social responsibility; driving without insurance puts you and others at financial risk!

    Coverage types

    A typical auto insurance policy offers the following 4 basic types of coverage:
    1. Bodily injury and property damage (injury and damage to others)
    2. Personal injury protection (medical costs)
    3. Uninsured motorist (accident with uninsured motorist)
    4. Collision and comprehensive (damage to your car)
    Click here to learn more about different coverage types.

    Required coverage levels

    Liability auto insurance coverage is required in almost all states, each with its own minimum requirements. Regardless of your state's minimum, experts recommend that all drivers have liability coverage that is no less than $100,000/$300,000/$50,000 (bodily injury per person/bodily injury per accident/property damage).

    Some states also require PIP and/or Uninsured motorist coverage. For complete state liability limits and a list of states that require non-liability coverage such as PIP, click here

    What affects my insurance rates?

    Typically, having higher limits and a low deductible will be more expensive. Your driving history, driving patterns, and the type of car you own are also important factors to how much you'll pay for auto insurance. Other major factors include age, gender, marital status, geography, and credit score.

    Certain professions, students with good grades, and safe drivers are all eligible for discounts.
