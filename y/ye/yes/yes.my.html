What is "personal information" as used in this Privacy Policy?
"Personal information" means information that identifies you or could be used to identify you and which is submitted to and/or collected by YTL Communications Web Sites. Examples of personal information include your name, your postal address, your e-mail address and your telephone number.

How does YTL Communications collect information online?
YTL Communications collects information in two ways:
  1. Personal Information You Submit:
    YTL Communications collects personal information that you enter into data fields on YTL Communications Web Sites. For example, you may submit your name, postal address, e-mail address, and/or other information in order to receive information about various subjects, register for YTL Communications programmes, contact YTL Communications customer service, or respond to YTL Communications surveys. To protect your privacy, you should not provide YTL Communications with any information that is not specifically requested.
  2. Passive Collection of Unidentifiable Information:
    YTL Communications Web Sites may collect information about your visits to YTL Communications Web Sites without you actively submitting such information. Unidentifiable information may be collected using various technologies, such as cookies, and web beacons. Cookies are small text files that are transferred to your computer's hard disk by a website. Web beacons (also referred to as GIF files, pixels or Internet tags) help YTL Communications recognise a unique cookie on your browser. Your Internet browser automatically transmits to YTL Communications Web Sites some of this unidentifiable information, such as the URL of the web site you just visited and the browser version your computer is operating. Passive information collection technologies can make your use of YTL Communications Web Sites easier by allowing YTL Communications to provide better service, customise YTL Communications Web Sites based on consumer preferences, compile statistics, analyse trends, and otherwise administer and improve YTL Communications Web Sites. Certain features of YTL Communications Web Sites may not work without use of passive information collection technologies. Information collected by these technologies cannot be used to identify you without additional identifiable information and YTL Communications will not connect additional identifiable information with information collected through the use of such tracking technologies. With respect to limiting or disabling tracking technology please see below "What choices do I have about how YTL Communications collects and uses personal information about me?"

Other parts of YTL Communications Web Sites may ask whether you wish to opt into our contact lists for offers, promotions and additional services that may be of interest to you.

You may also be provided with preference questions or preference boxes allowing you to indicate that you do not want YTL Communications Web Sites to use tracking technologies, such as cookies, to "remember" your personal information, such as mailing addresses, on return visits. However, YTL Communications Web Sites that use tracking technologies to collect unidentifiable information do not generally provide you with the ability to opt out of the tracking technologies. Some Internet browsers allow you to limit or disable the use of tracking technologies that collect unidentifiable information.
