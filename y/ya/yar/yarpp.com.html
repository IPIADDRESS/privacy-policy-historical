YARPP.COM PRIVACY POLICY

Effective January 1, 2014

This Policy may change from time to time. Please check back frequently for updates.

Adknowledge EN, Inc., and its YARPP.com business operations ("YARPP", "we", or "us") respect and protect the privacy of consumers. This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes what information we collect, how we use that information, and some of your privacy rights regarding that information.

Collection of Information and Your Privacy Rights

What information we collect and how we collect it.

1.1 Subscription or Survey Information. YARPP collects information from you when you voluntarily provide it to YARPP or by others means, such as registration pages operated by third party publisher networks (collectively, a "User Form"). In a User Form, YARPP or a third party such as a blog or website (each such third party a “Publisher”) may ask you for your name, your company’s name, email address, street address, zip code, telephone numbers, and job title (together, "User Information"). Completing the User Form is voluntary and you are under no obligation to provide User Information.

1.2 User Behaviors Collected with Cookies, Web Beacons, and Other Information. YARPP uses cookie and web beacon technology to associate certain Internet-related information about you, such as Internet Protocol address and what Web browser you are using, with certain of your online behaviors, such as opening or clicking on a particular online advertisement. Additionally, YARPP may use other new and evolving sources of information and information gathering in the future (together, "Technology Information").

(a) Cookies. A cookie is a small amount of data stored on the hard drive of your computer that allows YARPP and/or its partners to identify your IP address with corresponding information in YARPP's database. YARPP and/or its partners may use cookies to track individual responses or views of advertisements, and use this information to help predict what other advertisements may be of interest to you and enable you to receive customized advertisements or content. These cookies contain no personally identifiable information. The cookies may reflect de-identified demographic or other data linked to data you voluntarily have submitted to us, such as your email address, that we may share with any third party, solely in hashed, non-human readable form. To learn about how you can opt-out of the receipt of cookies, please visit http://www.aboutads.info/choices. To learn about how you can remove cookies placed on your computer or prevent the placement of cookies on your computer, please see Section 4 below. To learn more about cookies in general, please visit http://www.cookiecentral.com.

(b) Web beacons. A web beacon is programming code used to display an image on a web page and can also transfer an individual's or Publisher’s user identification to a database and associate the individual or Publisher with previously acquired information about them. Web beacons enable YARPP to track certain Web sites visited by consumers or advertisements you view online. Web beacons help determine products or services in which consumers may be interested and help track online behaviors for marketing purposes. More information about web beacons is available at http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/W/Web_beacon.html.

(c) Where Technology Information is Collected. Technology Information is collected directly from YARPP advertisements and Publishers that YARPP contracts with to display advertisements. Such advertisements will usually be displayed within one of the following advertising vehicles: Websites. YARPP may set a cookie on a consumer’s computer when the consumer view advertisements delivered on Publisher websites. After a cookie is set, YARPP may track how consumers interact with the advertisement and the Publisher website (for example, registering, purchasing, browsing, etc.).

1.3 Email Databases. YARPP and/or its partners may track Publisher and advertiser interaction with email solicitations sent to Publishers and advertisers (for example, to sign up for YARPP Pro).

1.4 Outside Information. YARPP may lawfully receive information about you from third parties, such as information located in public databases ("Outside Information").

1.5 No Information Collected from Minors. YARPP does not knowing collect personally-identifiable information about minors under the age of 18. MINORS UNDER THE AGE OF 18 SHOULD NOT PROVIDE ANY PERSONAL INFORMATION, INCLUDING THEIR EMAIL ADDRESS, TO YARPP. If YARPP is notified it has personal information about a minor under the age of 18, that information will be promptly deleted from our database.

1.6 Individual Information. As used herein, Individual Information means User Information, Technology Information, Outside Information, and any other information YARPP lawfully obtains about you.

Use of Information and User Data

How we use and share the information we collect.

2.1 Discretion to Use Information. Information voluntarily provided to YARPP or lawfully obtained by YARPP is the property of YARPP and may be used by YARPP for any lawful purpose, including the sale, lease or transfer of the information to Publishers, advertisers and data aggregators for marketing purposes. If you wish to close your account or remove your email address with us, you may follow the instructions at the end of each email message to unsubscribe or follow the instructions detailed at Section 4 below.

2.2 Targeted Advertising and Information. YARPP may use Individual Information to target advertising and other information, such as business, weather, entertainment, sports and general news alerts and similar information, more effectively. When an individual is online, YARPP may use Technology Information to associate an individual with their Individual Information and YARPP attempts to show information and advertising for products and services to which the person may respond. YARPP may use data collected from a response to one type of advertising or information for targeting advertisements or other information through a different venue. For example, YARPP may notice that you responded to a shampoo advertisement and use that response to deliver a shampoo-related advertisement and other information to you.

2.3 Legal Process. YARPP may disclose Individual Information to respond to subpoenas, court orders and other legal process as required by law.

2.4 Summary Data. YARPP may sell or transfer non-individualized information, such as summary or aggregated anonymous information about all persons or sub-groups of persons.

2.5 Business Transition. YARPP may transfer all or part of your Individual Information as part of a business transition such as a merger, acquisition, or asset sale. How we store and protect the information we receive.

2.6 Storage of Individual Information. YARPP stores Individual Information in a database on YARPP computers; the information is maintained and stored in encrypted form. In addition, YARPP's computers have security measures, such as a firewall, to protect against the loss, misuse, and alteration of the information under YARPP's control. YARPP also maintains physical and procedural safeguards to protect Individual Information. Notwithstanding such measures, YARPP cannot guarantee that its security measures will prevent these computers from being illegally accessed and the Individual Information on them stolen or altered.

Privacy Practices of Third Parties

Third Party privacy policies and use of cookies.

3.1 Advertiser cookies and web beacons. Publishers who place advertisements on the Internet may use their own cookies, web beacons and other technology to collect information about you as a consumer. YARPP does not control Publishers' use of such technology. YARPP may place cookies of its Publishers or advertisers who are under contract with YARPP, on your computer. These cookies may be placed at the time you open and/or click within email sent by YARPP or a Publisher under contract with YARPP, and may collection information about you over time across different websites. This Policy does not apply to the practices of Publishers or other companies that YARPP does not own or control or to people that YARPP does not employ or manage.

3.2 Links. The Publishers' Website and email messages transmitted by YARPP on its own behalf or on behalf of Publishers may contain hypertext links to the Websites of third parties. YARPP is not responsible for the privacy practices or the content of such other Websites.

3.3 Affiliated Companies that are not Publishers. YARPP may disclose or transfer Individual Information to wholly owned or controlled YARPP entities or entities which own or control YARPP (such as Adknowledge EN, Inc.), in YARPP's discretion.

How You May Exercise Your Privacy Rights

4.1 Websites. To prevent the display of Publisher or YARPP advertising on a Website, do not visit the Website. To only prevent YARPP from tracking your behavior on a Website, turn off cookies from that Website and do not click on or interact with any advertisements. Please note that YARPP does not have a mechanism or process to respond to any “do not track” signals which a web browser may send or receive, other than as described in this section.

4.2 Email Databases. To unsubscribe from YARPP or Publisher email lists, click the link included in the footer of any message delivered by YARPP or a Publisher and follow the instructions provided.

4.3 Widget or Application Uninstalls. To deactivate or uninstall the YARPP Word Press plug-in from your computer, click the deactivate button in your Word Press admin settings. For additional assistance on how to do this, you may visit our FAQ located at the following url: http://wordpress.org/plugins/yet-another-related-posts-plugin/faq/. For any non-Word Press websites, simply remove the widget’s java script from your website.

4.4 Cookies. Check instructions about your web browser to learn how to prevent placement of, or how to remove, cookies placed on your computer by YARPP.
