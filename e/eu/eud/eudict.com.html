Privacy policy


EUdict take your privacy concerns seriously. We do not collect any personal information from users browsing this website. A cookie is a small piece of data that is sent to your browser from a Web server and stored on your computer's hard drive. Cookie is used only for keeping your preferred language, last searches and for remembering the status of "Disable Autocomplete", "Translate upon paste" and "Select term in search box" options. If you want you can reset your browser to refuse all cookies but then this web site will lose the options set out above. We agree that this information will not be made available to any third party without the explicit consent of users. EUdict uses analytic software to collect data about web browsers and computers but this information contains no personal information about any visitor to our site; that is, no names, addresses, telephone numbers, e-mail addresses, and the like. Although we took all the preventive measures to make this site safe, we are not responsible for any damage or loss related to use of the content found on this website. We cannot be held responsible if some offensive words from the search cloud end up indexed by the search engines. In web browsers supporting speech input (Google Chrome, Chromium) recorded word is transferred to Google servers. The mentioned changes take effect on publication on this web site.

These companies may place or recognize a unique cookie on your browser and use information (not including your name, address, email address or telephone number) about your visits to this and other Web sites in order to provide advertisements about goods and services that may be of interest to you.
