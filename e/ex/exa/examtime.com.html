COOKIES

Our website uses "Cookies" to distinguish you from other users of our website and to anonymously track visiting and browsing patterns. A Cookie is a small text file implanted by us and most other website operators (or an advertiser on this site) on the hard disk of your computer when you visit the site. Cookies can collect information about users of our site like you, such as your names, address, e-mail details, passwords and your user preferences. While cookies and the information they obtain may not identify a living individual on their own, they may be able to do so in combination with other information held by us or a third party. This helps us to provide you with a good experience when you browse our website and also allows us to improve our site. The website uses session cookies for site navigation purposes. These cookies expire and are deleted at the end of a browsing session. We also use Google Analytics for tracking and data capture purposes. Data collected through cookies is always anonymous and aggregated.

PRIVACY OF CHILDREN

Our services and products are not targeted at children under thirteen (13) years of age. If you are under 13 years old you will not be allowed to open an account on our site or subscribe for any or our services. If we discover that we hold or have inadvertently disclosed any personally identifiable data for any user or customer under 13 years of age, it will be deleted from our system to ensure the privacy of the child.

SAFEGUARDING CHILDREN’S PERSONAL DATA

We also have a number of protections dealing with the personal data of persons under 18 years of age. These are that a guardian or parent has the right to review the personal data the site has collected from their child, have their child’s personal data deleted from the site and also request us to cease obtaining further personal data from the child going forward. To do so if you are the parent or guardian of a child either post or send by fax to us an affidavit, along with a copy of your driver’s license or passport, to the address or fax number below:

  • Examtime Limited,First Floor, 8/9 Hanover Street East, Dublin 2, Ireland.
  • Ireland: + 353 1 679 5047

The affidavit must be signed by and in the presence of a notary public who is a person qualified to have documents sworn under oaths. Your affidavit must confirm:

  • You are the parent or guardian;
  • Your child is under the age of eighteen years;
  • Whether you would like to have the right to review the information the site has collected from your child, have your child’s personal data deleted from the site and/or request us to stop collecting further personal data from your child in the future.
  • Your child’s username or account name;
  • Your e-mail address.

When we receive the affidavit we will be able to carry out your instructions. If you are submitting an affidavit in relation to a child under 13 years of age, then please do not include any of their personal data. If you do include any personal data of the child, your request is your instruction for us to receive and store the personal data as reasonably necessary to fulfil your request.
