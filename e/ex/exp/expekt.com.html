9. Cookies

What is a cookie?

A cookie is a short text file sent through your browser, registered on your device when visiting a website. Your web browser then sends these cookies back to the originating website on each subsequent visit, or to another website that recognises these cookies. We use cookies for navigation needs on our website, and to optimize and personalize our services, like allowing pages to load faster because certain content is stored on your browser or by helping us authenticate you to deliver personalized content. You can find out more about cookies and how to manage them at sites such as www.allaboutcookies.org.

Different types of cookies are used on our website and each has it specific target.

Cookies deemed as strictly necessary

These cookies allow you to use the main functionalities of our website and access to secured areas of the website (example: access to your account). Without these cookies, our website cannot run normally. These cookies also ensure security of the website for Expekt and its users. For example, we use Session Identification Cookies to monitor the time of your connection. That’s why you are requested to re-connect after a certain time lapse. Somehow, we also use a permanent Identification Cookie that registers your Username (but not your password) to ease you next connections by filling in advance the « Username » field.

Analytic Cookies

Those cookies enable us to measure the website frequentation, to know how it is used and how users react to proposed products. Thus, the collected data enables us to improve our website and your navigation experience. The collected data remains strictly anonymous and is only subject to a purely statistical process. With this respect, we use a third-party service: Google Analytics. If you do not wish that Google Analytics collects or uses your information, you can directly unregister through your browser on the following Google page (https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout).

Functional Cookies

Those cookies allow you to personalize your experience on our website (for example to remind you to make a first deposit, or to direct you to your favourite universe). Thanks to those cookies, you may also be informed of Specific Promotional Offers depending on your place of connection. They may also provide you with functionalities you have personally requested. We may also use a cookie to help us route traffic between servers and thus measure loading times. Sometimes, we also store data on your browser or your device so that functionalities you usually use are automatically uploaded and thus respond quicker.

Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to display advertisement related to your centre of interests during your navigation on the web. They are also used amongst other to limit the number of times you are exposed to see a single advertisement and allow to measure their efficiency. We work with web editors, advertisers, and service providers to promote Expekt on other websites. Expekt may then use your navigation data through cookies managed by a partner. The collected data remains strictly anonymous and is only subject to a purely statistical processing. Displayed banners are thus personalized.

Cookies and Device Identifiers for Transactions Safety

We inform you that when making a deposit on the website, Expekt or service providers acting on their behalf, may use cookies or collect data related to your device, notably necessary to recognise the equipment used (technical features of the computer, pad, phone…) to identify and secure transactions on it (Prevention of the risk of fraud). Cookies or Device Identifiers are used with this respect. You are then informed by a banner when arriving on the web page. When pursuing your navigation on our website you accept data collection and creation of the identifier.

How to manage your cookies

At the moment, it is not technically possible to adjust your cookie settings at our individual websites, but you can manage your cookies through your browser. We recommend that you do not block or delete your cookies as it may limit the experience of some of the websites you visit. The Cookies can usually be changed on the “Settings” section of your browser.
