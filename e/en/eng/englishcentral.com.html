INFORMATION WE COLLECT

User Provided Information. EnglishCentral collects personal information from you when you register on the WebSite, update your profile, and use various features of the WebSite. To register with EnglishCentral, you must provide a user name, password, native language, countryand email address and other information.. Once you are a registered user of EnglishCentral you will be given the option to add additional personal information about you to your profile. By enabling the invisible mode you can limit what information will be viewed by others. In invisible mode you will be able to send messages to other users, chat with other users, and view and post comments and messages, but other users will only be able to view your user name and country flag. They will not be able to view other information in your profile. However, while in invisible mode you cannot invite other users to join your circle of friends, and they cannot invite you to join theirs. You can enable or disable the invisible mode at any time. To do this, just click on the "My EnglishCentral" link listed throughout the WebSite and then click on "My Account" to select or unselect the preference for "invisible mode". However, enabling invisible mode will not affect information that had been previously posted. Any previously posted information may still be visible to other users. Also, disabling invisible mode will make information about your previous activity on the WebSite, even while you were in invisible mode, available to other users. Be aware that whether you are in invisible mode or not, if you choose to make public posts, submit a comment, or chat, then any personal information you actively include in such submissions, and certain basic profile data that cannot be hidden, such as your username and your country flag, will still be accessible to all registered users and any visitors to the WebSite. By posting a profile or disclosing personal information in our online forums, directly to users in chat rooms or through messages, or otherwise, your information is made public and you should not expect any privacy in such an environment. We may also collect information regarding your interaction with other users and your use of the WebSite, including content you view linked from the WebSite and EnglishCentral points that you have earned, which will also be displayed on the WebSite. From time to time we may ask you to respond to surveys or other requests for information, and we collect the information you provide. We may collect information from you when you participate in sweepstakes and promotions offered on the WebSite. We may also collect information about you through other methods, including research surveys or information we obtain from third parties, including verification services, data services, as well as public sources. We use this information to continually offer you the most value as a user of the WebSite.

Tracking Technology. EnglishCentral logs IP addresses (a unique number assigned to every computer on the Internet) in order to improve security, analyze trends and administer the WebSite. We may track your movement within the WebSite, the third-party webWebSite pages from which you were referred, your use of the media player, access times and browser types. We use this information to gauge the effectiveness of our WebSite, track information for statistical purposes and to improve the products and services we provide. We store information that we collect through cookies, log files and other tracking technology such as clear gifs. We use cookies on the WebSite. A cookie is a small text file that is stored on a user's computer for record-keeping purposes. We use session ID cookies and persistent cookies. We use session cookies to make it easier for you to navigate our WebSite. A session ID cookie expires when you close your browser. A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time. You can remove persistent cookies by following directions provided in your Internet browser's "help" file. Persistent cookies are used to differentiate visitors of the WebSite from registered users, to customize the content of certain sections of the WebSite and to enable us to track and cater to the interests of users, in order to enhance their experience on the WebSite. If you reject cookies, you may still use the WebSite, but your ability to use some areas of the WebSite may be limited and you may not be able to use certain features of the WebSite.
