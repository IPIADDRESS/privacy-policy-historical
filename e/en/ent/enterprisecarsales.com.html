Non-Personal Information

As part of the standard operation of our Sites, certain non-personal, anonymous information may be automatically provided to our Web servers by your computer or mobile device. In addition, we may collect your browser type and operating system. Except as otherwise explained in this policy, we may combine the non-personal information we collect from you (as described above and as described below, under “Cookies & Pixel Tracking”) with your personal information.

We also collect Mobile Device brand and model, mobile operation system and carrier to determine whether users view our .com or mobile version of the Sites.

Cookies & Pixel Tracking

Cookies are short strings of text sent from a Web server to a Web browser when the browser accesses a Web page. In simplest terms, when the browser requests a page from the Web server that originally sent it the cookie, the browser sends a copy of the cookie back to that Web server. A cookie typically contains, among other things, the name of the cookie, a unique identification number, an expiration date and domain name information. Cookies may be “session-only” or “persistent.” Persistent cookies last for more than one visit and are typically used to allow a visitor to our Sites to personalize their experience.

By adjusting the settings in your browser, you can refuse to accept cookies or give yourself the opportunity to accept or reject cookies, or receive notice of cookies. Software programs are available that block cookies. Blocking certain cookies will also block the use of certain pixel tags. Refusing cookies may impinge or block altogether your user experience by eliminating personalization or other features.

We use cookies to analyze the traffic on our Sites (such as total visitors and pages viewed), to personalize features and content, to save you the trouble of retyping your name or other information when you come back to our Sites, and to make improvements to the Sites based on the data we collect. We do not save passwords or sensitive information in cookies. The use of cookies has become a standard in the Internet industry, especially at Web Sites that provide any kind of personalized service.

We also use pixel tags (also known as “clear gifs,” “Web beacons” or “1-by-1gifs”) on our Sites and in our advertisements. Pixel tags consist of a few lines of computer coding and are not visible to the site user. Pixel tags help us analyze our visitors’ behavior on our Sites and with respect to our advertisements (for example, whether or not you have clicked on one of our ads). We may work with our Affiliates (companies related to us) and our service providers to help us track, collect and analyze this information. Pixel tags may be used to collect a variety of information about your visit to our Sites, including the pages you view, the features you use, the links you click and other actions you take, all of which may be combined with other information we have collected from you. We use this information to provide personalized and relevant content and advertising to you, to measure the effectiveness of our marketing and advertisements, to enhance the effectiveness of our Sites, to compile aggregate usage statistics, to determine if errors are being encountered on the Sites and for other internal business purposes. To opt-out of tracking for our rental sites please click here. To opt-out of tracking for all of our other business units, please click here. We work with a service provider that helps us to optimize our search engine marketing. It does this by associating pixel tags with the terms that you use when searching online for a rental car (for example, SUV or a certain destination). If you select Enterprise from among your search results, our service provider will analyze your search terms to present you with the most appropriate landing page (for example, SUVs available in Florida). Our service provider does not tie the search terms or any other information to your personal information; it may tie search term information to your IP address. Please go to the section below, CHOICES YOU CAN MAKE ABOUT YOUR INFORMATION, for information about how you can block cookies and certain pixel tags.

We do not control these cookies. The use of cookies by advertisers has become standard practice in the Internet industry.
