Cookies

Cookies are files with small amount of data, which may include an anonymous unique identifier. Cookies are sent to your browser from a website and stored on the hard drive of your computer or mobile device if you agree.

We use "cookies" to distinguish you from other users and to improve our Service. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, though you do not have to. We do not control the use of cookies by third parties. You can instruct your browser to refuse cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent by readjusting your browser settings. However, if you do not accept cookies, you may not be able to use some portions of our Service.

For further information about how we use cookies (including how to disable them), please refer to our Use of Cookies at https://earnably.com/cookies.
