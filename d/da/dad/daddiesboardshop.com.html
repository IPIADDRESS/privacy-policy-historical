 This privacy policy was last updated on 10/18/2006.  Protecting your privacy is a top priority to us.  We are in the business of selling skateboard and snowboard gear, not names or personal information. Daddiesboardshop.com has created this privacy policy in order to disclose our information gathering and dissemination practices for this web site. 

What we collect:
  • We may collect your name, address and phone number.  We will need this information to complete and process your order.  This information is viewed only by the owners and employees of daddiesboardshop.com if they are involved in the completion, delivery or customer support of your order.   How this information is used:  The order is processed and kept in our confidential files for record-keeping purposes. All paper data is destroyed via docu-shred on a bi-monthly basis. 
  • Credit Card information.  Your credit card is not charged until we view your order and pull your products. We do not use automatic credit card processing. This allows us to verify the validity of every order placed at daddiesboardshop.com. Your credit card numbers are NEVER stored on the Internet.  How this information is used:  Once the order is processed, the credit card information is deleted and must be reinserted when you purchase again.
  • Email address.  We may request your e-mail address to contact you if we have a question regarding your order.  This information may be disclosed to trusted persons working together with daddiesboardshop.com if they are involved in the completion, delivery or customer support of your order.  How this information is used:  Customers that open an account may receive notifications of new arrivals, discounts and updated information from our web site.  You may "Opt Out" of receiving these emails by using the "Unsubscribe Me" link at the bottom of every e-mail.
  • Cookies.   Cookies are a message that a web server transmit to a web browser to personalize the user's experience on our web site.  Cookies can help store personal information about you so that when you return to the site you have a more personalized experience. Have you ever returned to a site and have seen your name mysteriously appear on the screen?  It is because on a previous visit you gave your name to the site and it was stored in a cookie so that when you returned you would be greeted with a personal message.  Cookies do not act maliciously on computer systems. They are merely text files that you have the option of deleting at any time.  Both Microsoft Internet Explorer and Netscape can be set to reject cookies if the user prefers to use the Internet without enabling cookies to be stored.  Please note that you create a customer account you must enable Cookies on your browser.  In Netscape, follow the Edit/Preferences/Advanced menu and in Internet Explorer, follow the Tools/Internet Options/Security menu to set Cookie Preferences.
