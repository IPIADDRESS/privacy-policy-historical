Diglo.com abides by a very serious policy in relation to your privacy which is highly regarded. Please read the following to learn more about Diglo.com’s Privacy Policy ("Policy"). This Policy details how Diglo.com receives, collects and uses information in connection with their website located at diglo.com(the "Site").

Use of Information

Diglo.com does not automatically collect any personally identifiable information, such as names, e-mail, age, gender, address, telephone numbers, login names and passwords or other self-identifying information ("Personal Information"). Diglo.com may request that a user provide an e-mail address if such user wishes to be contacted regarding any offers, updates, newsletters and service related announcements. If a user provides an e-mail address to Diglo.com, such user authorizes Diglo.com to send user communications.

Diglo.com may use cookies on the Site. Cookies are pieces of information that a website transfers to an individual's hard drive for record-keeping purposes. Cookies make web-surfing easier by saving preferences and browsing patterns while you are at Diglo.com’s Site. Cookies may be used to analyze Site usage, improve content, customize the Site's content and layout and for other customer service purposes. Diglo.com’s use of cookies is in no way linked to any Personal Information.

Diglo.com may automatically receive and record certain non-personally identifiable information on its server logs from your browser, including your IP address, browser type, internet service provider (ISP), cookie information, and the webpage that a user visits. Diglo.com collects non-personally identifiable information for general purposes, including but not limited to analyzing trends, administering the site, tracking user movements, conducting research, and providing anonymous reporting to internal and external clients. Diglo.com will not link any Personal Information, including e-mail addresses, with aggregate data of its users.

Disclosure and Third Parties

Diglo.com does not rent, sell, or share any information about the user with any third-parties. Diglo.com may disclose your Personal Information if Diglo.com believes such action is necessary to: (a) comply with the law or legal process served on Diglo.com; (b) protect and defend the rights or property of Diglo.com (including the enforcement of our agreements); or (c) act in urgent circumstances to protect the personal safety of users of Diglo.com services or members of the public.

Security

Diglo.com takes reasonable precaution to protect Personal Information from misuse, loss and unauthorized access. Although Diglo.com cannot guarantee that Personal Information will not be subject to unauthorized access, Diglo.com has physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards to protect the Personal Information. Personal Information is protected by secured networks and access is limited to a few authorized employees and personnel. However, no method of transmission over the Internet, or method of electronic storage, is 100% secure, however.

Mergers/Acquisitions

In the event that Diglo.com goes through a business transaction, such as a merger, acquisition by another company, or sale of all or a portion of its assets, Personal Information will likely be among the assets transferred. Diglo.com will post a notice on this website of any such change in ownership or control of the Personal Information Diglo.com may have prior to such transfer.

Modifications

From time to time, Diglo.com may revise this Policy. Diglo.com will post the revised Policy on the Site, so that users can always be aware of what information Diglo.com collects, how the information is used and under what circumstances such information may be disclosed.

Change or Removal of Information

If a user's e-mail address changes, a user may correct such information by sending an e-mail to privacy@diglo.com.

Contact Information

Please direct all questions in connection with this Policy via e-mail to privacy@diglo.com.
