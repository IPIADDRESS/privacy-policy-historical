General Statement

Distil, Inc., a Delaware corporation (the “Company”), maintains this website (“Website”), among other reasons, to provide you access to technology that monitors your website in order to prevent hackers, spammers, bots, scrapers and others using automated methods from infringing your proprietary rights and disrupting the optimal operation of your website (collectively, the “Services”), subject to the Terms and Conditions of Use you accept upon registration (the “Terms”) and this privacy policy (the “Privacy Policy”).

This Privacy Policy only applies to this Website and describes the information we collect, how we use it, with whom we share it, and other matters related to such information. By using this Website, you signify your agreement to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If changes are made to this Privacy Policy, you will signify your agreement with the new policy by continuing to use this Website after the changes have been posted.

The Company controls, owns, and manages the information collected on the Website and may use the information solely for its own purposes.

If you have any questions or concerns about our Privacy Policy, contact our privacy team at: info@distilnetworks.com.

The Information We Collect

  1. Information You Provide.

    1. (a) When you sign up at the Website for the Services you provide us with your contact information and payment information. In some cases we may ask for additional information for security reasons or to provide specific services to you. We may also supplement the information you provide with information we receive from third parties.
    2. (b) When you send email or other communications to us, we may retain those communications in order to process your inquiries, respond to your requests and improve the Services.

  2. Site Information.

    When you use the Services, we automatically receive, record and cache the content of your site, including, without limitation, documents, browsers, your IP address, cookie information, software and hardware attributes, usage information and other site properties.

  3. Transactional Information.

    We may retain the details of transactions or payments you make on the Website. If you do not want us to store your payment source account information, please contact our privacy team to have it removed.

  4. Cookie Information.

    We use “cookies” (small pieces of data we store for an extended period of time on your computer, mobile phone, or other device) to make the Website easier to use, to make our advertising better, and to protect both you and the Company. For example, we use cookies to store your login ID (but never your password) to make it easier for you to login whenever you come back to the Website. We also use cookies to confirm that you are logged into the Website, and to know when you are interacting with the Website and our advertisements. You can remove or block cookies by using the settings in your browser, but in some cases that may impact your ability to use the Website.

  5. Clear GIFS.

    We also employ a software technology called “clear gifs” (also known as “web beacons” or “web bugs”) that helps us better manage content on the Website by providing us feedback as to what content is effective. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on your computer’s hard drive, clear gifs are embedded invisibly on a website page, web-based document or email message and are about the size of the period at the end of this sentence. We use clear gifs in our HTML-based emails to confirm receipt of, and response to, our emails, including those that you forward to other recipients. This allows us to gauge the effectiveness of certain communications and the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. If you would like to opt-out of these emails, please see the “Opt-out” section below.

We use the information we collect to try to provide a safe, efficient, and customized experience. Here are some of the details on how we do that:

  1. To Manage the Services.

    We use the information we collect to provide our services and features to you, to measure and improve those services and features, and to provide you with customer support. We use the information to enforce our Terms. We also use a variety of technological systems to detect and address anomalous activity.

  2. To Contact You.

    We may contact you with service-related announcements from time to time. You may opt out of all communications except essential updates as described in the “Opt Out” section below. We may include content you see on the Website in the emails we send to you.

  3. Email Communications.

    If you do not want to receive commercial email or other mail from us, please indicate your preference during the registration process or by opting out as described below. Please note that if you do not want to receive legal notices from us, such as notices regarding this Privacy Policy, those legal notices will still govern your use of the Website, and you are responsible for reviewing such legal notices for changes. We may receive a confirmation when you open an email from us if your computer supports this type of program.

  4. To Serve Advertisements.

    We occasionally pair advertisements we serve with relevant information we have about you to make advertisements more interesting and more tailored to you.

  5. To Advertise Our Services.

    We may ask third party advertisers to display advertisements promoting our services. We may ask them to deliver those advertisements based upon the presence of a cookie, but in doing so we will not share any other information with the advertiser.

  6. To Offer Joint Services.

    We may provide services jointly with other companies. If you use these services, we may share your information to facilitate that service.

  7. Association with Personally Identifiable Information.

    We may associate your online navigational information and transactional information gathered by cookies and clear gifs to your personally identifiable information. We associate this information only for our purposes, to deliver services and products to you, to improve our business and to market our products and services through email, mobile advertising, direct mail and other media. The association of navigational information, cookies, clear gifs and personally identifiable information results in a more personalized experience on the Website that is tailored to your interests.

  8. Data Retention.

    We retain your site content for only as long as necessary for its analysis, then delete it once the analysis has been completed. We do not store your site content.

How You Can Change or Remove Information

  1. Editing your information.

    You are responsible for providing accurate information when registering with any portion of the Website that requires registration, or performing other functions that might require you to submit personally identifiable information to the Website. If you wish to correct any personally identifiable information you have provided to us, you may do so by contacting our privacy team at the address and email set forth above.

  2. Deactivating or deleting your account.

    If you want to stop using your account you may deactivate it or delete it. The account can only be deleted by providing notice to us through the Website. When you deactivate an account, no user will be able to see it, but it will not be deleted. We save your profile information in case you later decide to reactivate your account.
