Privacy
Privacy Statement:

Your email address
While there is no obligation to register and create a user account with DriverSide, there are a number of reasons why you may decide to do so.  To create an account, you will be asked for to input a valid email address and a password.  Registration is free and doing so enables us to send you important updates on your vehicles.  You always have the ability to change the email address that we store for you and can also request that we delete your email address (and your entire account) anytime by sending such request to info@driverside.com or by mailing a request to DriverSide Accounts, 110 Sutter St., Suite 300, San Francisco, CA 94104.

To the extent that you do maintain an active email address in our system, we will use commercially recognized and reasonable care to maintain the security of that email address.  We will not share, sell or otherwise make your email address available to any third party unless you request that we do so (described below).  With a registered account on DriverSide, you will be able to request certain email notifications for various content related to your vehicle and/or your experience on DriverSide.   You will also have the ability to list your vehicle(s) for sale and if your choose to do so, you will receive email notifications from DriverSide that there is or are parties interested in the purchase of your vehicle.   You will always have the ability to change or delete any notification requests anytime.

In certain locations on DriverSide in which we obtain personally identifiable information from you, we may include "check boxes" for the purpose of soliciting your consent to receive special offers and/or other information from us and other entities selected by us. By leaving these check boxes "checked," you are choosing to receive commercial communications from us and/or those other entities. A feature included in your voluntary DriverSide account is the ability to establish and customize individual profiles for any number of vehicles.  Each of the profiles that you create can contain whatever information you choose to include.  Those profiles can be made public on the DriverSide site, and in such case, the profiles, including any information that decide to add the profiles, will be accessible by other visitors to DriverSide.  DriverSide will not publish any personally identifiable information on these profiles, and in fact, has no mechanism to do unless you decide to add such information at your sole discretion.  When setting up your vehicle profile of DriverSide, you will be given the opportunity to decide whether the entire profile, certain parts of it, or none of it are made public.  Such choices can be modified by you at any time through your account settings, by sending a request to info@driverside.com or by sending us a request by mail at DriverSide Accounts, 110 Sutter St., Suite 300, San Francisco, CA 94104.

Your Zip Code
We request your Zip Code in a number of locations on the DriverSide site.  Providing us your Zip Code enables us to display to you various content and tools relevant to your specific location.  We provide this information for Zip Codes that are within one of the 50 states or the District of Columbia, but not those in other U.S. territories or possessions or for the postal codes of Canada or other countries.

When you first provide your Zip Code to us in response to a direct request by us that you provide your Zip Code, we serve a "cookie" to you that includes your Zip Code. ("Cookies" are described below.) That cookie permits us to recognize your Zip Code when you use other applications, or upon your return to DriverSide without our having to ask you again for your Zip Code.

You have the opportunity to change your Zip Code at appropriate times during your visit to DriverSide  if you want to get information for a different Zip Code.  Changing your Zip Code in any part of the DriverSide site automatically updates your Zip Code cookie.

Other Cookies
"Cookies" are small data files that many Web sites write to your hard drive when you visit them. A cookie file contains information such as a user ID that a site may use to track the pages you've visited. However, the only personal information a cookie can contain is information you supply yourself. Cookies served by us don't permit us to read data from your hard drive or to read cookie files created by other Web sites.

We use cookies in many ways, including the following:

First Visits: When you first visited the DriverSide, we served a cookie to you. This cookie resides permanently on your hard drive (unless you chose to delete it) and enables us to track visitor activity across our Web sites.

Features: Whenever you use certain features on the DriverSide site, such as Dealer Locator, we may serve a "session cookie" to you, which is a cookie that is only used by us during your use of that application. In addition, for some applications we may serve a cookie to you that has a longer expiration date. These cookies help us manage our delivery to you of feature information and related content.

Lead Forms: In certain other features, you may be presented with a form that requests, among other items, your name and contact information. When this form is submitted by you the information you provide is stored on our computers, so that we have a record of what we have sent to our partners. We also serve a cookie to you telling us where that information is maintained, so that we can "pre-fill" your name and contact information for you when in the future you call up a similar form.

Please note that we do not link the information we store in cookies to any personally identifiable information you submit while on our the DriverSide site.
