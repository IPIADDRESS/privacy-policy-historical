Use of Cookies

Cookies are commonly used throughout the web and are used on the DriverSleuth.com website. A cookie is a small piece of text data placed on your hard drive by DriverSleuth.com's servers to better serve our visitors and customers. Use of cookies have no way to determine the identity of the visitor. Cookies are harmless and cannot access your computer or place malicious software on your PC. Most browsers enable you to allow or disallow cookies when you visit a website. Driversleuth.com uses getclicky.com standard analytics packages which gathers limited information on visitors to our website automatically, and stores this anonymous information in server log files. This information may include IP addresses, browser type, basic IP information, referring pages, operating system used and date/time stamps. This information does not identify individual users; it is used only for site administration, as well as for analysis of our website visitors and trends. We also use conversion tracking package from google adwords to track downloads/leads/sales from our visitors with a 30 day tracking cookie. We use both session ID cookies and persistent cookies. A session cookie expires when your browser is closed, and a persistent cookie may stay on your hard drive for an extended period. Session cookies make it easier for you to navigate our site; persistent cookies can perform such activities as storing your password for forums or blogs so you do not have to sign in every time you visit those areas, and/or may be used to enable us to track and target the interests of our users so as to enhance the overall experience of using our site. We have no access to or administration abilities over any cookies that are used by any advertisers on our site.
