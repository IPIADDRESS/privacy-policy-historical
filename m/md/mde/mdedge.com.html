At MDEdge, we understand that privacy is an important and necessary part of the medical profession. We want you to know that we take your privacy just as seriously as you would take the privacy of your patients. The MDEdge Web site is a physician-only community, and MDEdge is committed to honoring your privacy and earning your trust. To that end, we have adopted a set of standards and special protections for dealing with your personal information. This document is our privacy policy; it outlines why we collect and how we handle your personal information.

Coverage
This privacy policy relates to our Web site, (www.mdedge.com). "MDEdge" is also the name used to refer to our company as a whole.

When we refer to ourselves as "we" or "MDEdge," we mean our entire company, including any company that we control (for example, a subsidiary that we own). We may share information among those companies that we own or control, but that information is always protected under the terms of this privacy policy.

You should read the privacy policy of any Web site that you visit after you leave our site. We are not responsible for how other Web sites treat your information, which they may collect, or privacy.

Information Collection
MDEdge is the sole owner of the information collected on this site. We will not sell, share, or rent this information to others in ways different from what is disclosed in this statement. MDEdge collects information from our users at several different points on our Web site.

Information Collected from Unregistered Visitors
Visitors to the MDEdge Web site can access the Web site's log in page, and browse some areas of the site, without disclosing any personal information. As a visitor, we do track information provided to us by your browser, including the Web site you came from (the "referring URL"), the type of Web browser you use, when you accessed our Web site, and other information that does not identify you personally.

Registration
In order to use this Web site, a physician-user must first complete a registration process. When you register to use MDEdge, we ask you to provide us with certain personal and other information, including, but not limited to, your name, address, e-mail address, and DEA number. This identifying information is required for registration. We use this information to positively identify you as an individual and to verify that you are a physician.

During the registration process, you will also generate a personal profile, including information about your medical specialty, content preferences, and notification preferences. We use this information as the basis for the highly defined personalization provided by MDEdge. It helps us to determine the types of information and services that are most likely to interest you and to direct this information to you. You may change your registration information at any time by accessing the "My Profile" area from your personal home page on the Web site.

Identifying Information from Other Sources
We may also collect information about physicians who register on our Web site from other sources in order to verify their licensure status and identity. In some cases, we will also collect identifying information from a third party in order to offer special content or opportunities to a specified individual or group of users. All user information received from other sources is handled by MDEdge in accordance with this privacy policy.

Other Information Collected
Revenue Opportunities: MDEdge offers unique revenue opportunities to physician-users such as consulting engagements and market research studies. In most cases, these opportunities include an honorarium payable to participants. Some of the opportunities are offered and compensated directly by us, and some opportunities are offered through our Web site by affiliates or partner companies. If you decide to participate in revenue opportunities offered through MDEdge, you may be asked to confirm existing personal information or to provide additional information, such as your Social Security number or Federal Tax ID number, in order to receive your honorarium. Once you participate in a revenue opportunity, the information you provide will be saved by MDEdge. Please note, as well, that if a revenue opportunity which you select is offered by another company, MDEdge may provide necessary identifying or payment information to that company to facilitate your participation and eventual payment.

Discussion Boards and Commentary: MDEdge provides opportunities for feedback and interaction by physician-users in the form of discussion boards and commentary submissions within the Web site. When you post a message on our Web site, that message will be accompanied by identifying information that will become public, and that all registered users will see. MDEdge reserves the right to determine what identifying information will be displayed along with a message, and this information may include your initials, geographical location, specialty, or other such information. Please remember, when you are posting publicly, any user of our Web site can see your message. You should not post any information you want to keep private to any public forum on our Web site.

Additional Forms and E-mails: We may also ask you to provide additional information via e-mail or Web-based forms in order to obtain additional services or information or to resolve complaints or concerns. This additional information will be subject to the terms of this privacy policy.

Cookies
Cookies are a technology used by MDEdge to identify a user as the user moves through our Web site. A cookie is a piece of data stored on a user's hard drive containing information about the user. We use cookies to personalize our Web site and to track your usage of the site. Cookies can save you time and improve your experience on MDEdge. For instance, by setting a cookie on our site, you would not have to log in a password more than once while you are on the site. Cookies can also enable us to track and target your interests to enhance your experience on our site. Many consider the use of cookies to be an industry standard.

Your Web browser can be set to allow you to control whether you will accept cookies, reject cookies, or to notify you each time a cookie is sent to you. If your browser is set to reject cookies, our Web site will not recognize you when you return, and significant functionality may be lost. The Help section of your browser will tell you how to prevent your browser from accepting cookies. We strongly recommend that you set your browser to allow cookies if you plan to use our Web site.

Although cookies do not normally contain personally identifiable information, if you are a registered user we may associate your registration information with cookies our Web site places on your computer's hard drive. Associating a cookie with your registration data allows us to offer increased personalization and functionality. For example, you can activate the "auto log in" feature of the Web site, and it will remember your user name and password in order to bypass the sign-in process when you return to the site. Without cookies, this functionality would not be possible. For more information about the "auto log in" feature, we strongly recommend that you read the auto log in help file found on the log in page and in the registration process.

Log Files
We also gather information about you that is automatically collected by our Web server, such as your IP address and domain name. We use this information to analyze trends, administer the site, track user's movements, and gather broad demographic information for aggregate use.

Policy on Children's Privacy
The MDEdge Web site is designed and intended for use by adults, and is not intended to be - and may not be - used by children under the age of 18. Minors under the age of 18 may not register for our Web site, and we do not collect personally identifiable information from any person we know is under the age of 18.

When we share information with third parties, we ask that they agree in writing to abide by our privacy policy. Security and Protection of Information
MDEdge has taken a number of security measures to protect information that we have collected about you.

When you submit sensitive information via the Web site, your information is protected both online and off-line. When you submit information to MDEdge via the Web site, that information is encrypted and is protected with an Internet security protocol know as Secure Sockets Layer - SSL. While on a secure page, the lock icon on the bottom of Web browsers displays as locked, as opposed to un-locked, or open, when you are just 'surfing the Web'.

While we use SSL encryption to protect sensitive information online, we also take reasonable steps that we determine are important to protect user information off-line. There is restricted access to all of our users' information. Only employees who need the information to perform a specific job are granted access to personally identifiable information. Employees must enter their password to gain access to your information. Furthermore, all employees are kept up-to-date on our security and privacy practices. Our employees are notified and/or reminded frequently about the importance we place on privacy, and what they can do to ensure our customers' information is protected. Finally, the servers that we store personally identifiable information on are kept in a secure Web-hosting environment, in a locked cage.

Your Access to Information and Notification Choices
Here we share with you how to access and correct information we have about you, and how to select communication preferences for this Web site.

Correction of Information
You can access your personal information at any time by clicking on the 'My Profile' link from your home page on the MDEdge Web site. If you believe that information collected by our Web site is in error, you may edit your personal profile any time that you like.

You may also ask that the personal information you have given to us be deleted from our Web site. Requests for deletion of your information will result in termination of your access to the MDEdge Web site. In these situations, we may keep certain non-personally identifiable demographic information about you for internal purposes. You may contact MDEdge customer support (support@mdedge.com) at any time to make changes to your personal information or to remove your personal information from the Web site.

Getting a Written Copy of Your Profile
You can get a copy of your personal profile information by printing a copy of the page containing the information from your Web browser.

Notification Choices
MDEdge conducts five different types of e-mail communication to its users based upon the e-mail address you provide during registration.

Service Updates: We send users service announcement updates via e-mail. Members are not able to un-subscribe from service announcements, which contain important information about the Web site.

Newsletter: The MDEdge newsletter is an opt-in service which is provided up to twice per month, which means that you must request to receive newsletter mailings from us.

Breaking News: Breaking news is also an opt-in service which is provided irregularly (only when such news is available). You must request to receive breaking news mailings from us.

Content Updates: This opt-in service will provide you e-mail communication up to once per week to notify you of content added to the Web site that matches your personal profile. You must opt-in to receive content update mailings from us.

Opportunity Updates: This service is also opt-in and provides information regarding new or ongoing opportunities available on the MDEdge Web site that match your user profile. Please remember that such opportunities will be filled by qualified physician users on a first come, first served basis.

You can change your notification preferences at any time to control what e-mail communications you receive from MDEdge. Notification preferences can be changed by accessing the 'My Profile' link from your personal home page on the MDEdge Web site or by e-mailing support@mdedge.com.
