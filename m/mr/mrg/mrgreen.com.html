    Collection and Use of Other Information; Use of Cookies
    You may have heard of "cookies," which are small files or pieces of information that are stored by your browser on your computer's hard drive when you visit a web site. The use of cookies is an industry standard and you will find them at most web sites. Pepsi may use cookies to collect other information during your visit to our web sites, such as the particular site areas you visit and the activities in which you participate at our web sites. Pepsi does not automatically collect any Personal Information, but we may combine non-personal information collected automatically (through cookies) with voluntarily submitted Personal Information. Such link may occur only if you provide Pepsi with Personal Information on our sites. This practice allows Pepsi to keep track of items such as sweepstakes participation, email and advertising interaction levels, survey responses, online coupon redemption and other web activity in order to better tailor our web sites and our products to your interests and needs. Cookies may also be used to help speed up your future activities at our web sites -- the computer can remember that you have already provided Personal Information to us and not request that you provide any a second time.

    Anonymous information about your computer, such as your IP address (a number assigned to your computer whenever you surf the Internet), the type of Internet browser you are using, and the type of computer operating system you are using may also be collected by us using cookies. Web servers, the computers that "serve up" web pages, automatically identify your computer by its IP address. When you request a page from this web site, our servers log your IP address.

    Most browsers are initially set to accept cookies. If you'd prefer, you can set yours to refuse cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent. Refusal of cookies at the site you entered may result in an inability to visit certain areas of the web sites or to receive personalized information when you visit the web sites.

    Protection of Children's Personal Information
    Pepsi is committed to protecting the online privacy of children and making the Internet safe for our users. It is our policy to prohibit users from registering on our sites or otherwise submitting Personal Information to us except with express permission from their parent/guardian. We encourage parents/guardians of children under 13 to regularly check and monitor their children's use of email and other activities on line.

    Please note that everything in this section and elsewhere in this notice about Pepsi's information collection and usage practices regarding children under 13 applies only to Personal Information about a child under 13, which is specifically the information we collect and can trace back to a specific child. If the information we collect is anonymous or combined as aggregate information, so that we cannot "match up" the child to their Personal Information, then this children's policy does not apply. You should assume, however, that all Personal Information we collect is tracked on an individual basis unless you are informed otherwise on the website.
    • Types of Personal Information Collected from Children. In order for a user to register on our site, we initially require the following information:
      • Password
      • Email address
      • Birth date

      If the Birth date entry reveals the child is under the age of 13, we freeze the information the child has provided and we request the email address information of the child's parent/guardian so that we can obtain consent from the parent/guardian. The information the child provides will not be used in any way until we receive authorization from the parent/guardian. After the parent/guardian grants permission for the child's registration, this information will then be used in accordance with this Privacy Policy.


    • Permission Process. As stated above, children under the age of 13 will be requested to provide the parent/guardian's email address. At that time, an email will be sent by Pepsi to the parent/guardian explaining that the child is attempting to register on the site. Pepsi will provide the parent/guardian with the site's URL and the child's user name and password in order for the parent/guardian to access the child's information. Once entered into the site, the parent/guardian will have the opportunity to:

      1. Review and submit the child's Personal Information to Pepsi (including child's and parent/guardian's name, email address, postal address and birth date)
      2. Edit the child's Personal Information
      3. Delete the child's Personal Information

      A follow up email message will be sent to the parent/guardian approximately 3 to 5 days after the parent/guardian has submitted the child's registration information to Pepsi. This second email is used by Pepsi to obtain verification from the parent/guardian of their permission to submit the child's Personal Information and register the child on the Pepsi site. In addition to the email, Pepsi will send the parent/guardian a letter or postcard notifying them of the child's registration. The information contained in this letter is similar to the information contained in the email but does not require a response from the parent/guardian. The child's Pepsi registration will be activated once the parent/guardian visits the website and submits or edits the child's information as described above. When a parent/guardian submits the child's registration information, an email is sent to the child notifying them that this has happened and making them eligible to participate in site activities.

    • Use of Children's Information. After the parent/guardian grants permission pursuant to the above process, the child's information will then be used in accordance with this Privacy Policy whenever that child attempts to enter a sweepstakes or engage in other activities on the web site. We will not use the parent's email or postal address for any purpose other than to communicate with the parent in order to obtain permission for their child to register and participate on our web sites, and we will retain the parent's information for our internal records as evidence of our compliance with this Privacy Policy.

    • Manage Your Child's Personal Information. Once the Personal Information of a child is submitted and entered into our database, as the parent/guardian of a child, you are always free to verify or change the Personal Information we have in our database pertaining to that child. Please be aware that for security reasons the parent/guardian must provide Pepsi with the child's user name, password and email address needed to access the child's information. To verify or change the child's Personal Information, please see "Manage Your Personal Information; Unsubscribe".
