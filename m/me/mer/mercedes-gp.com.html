(1) What are Cookies?

MGP uses Cookies to record preferences of visitors. This enables MGP to optimise the design of its website at `{`www.mercedesamgf1.com`}` (“Website”). A cookie is a small file, typically of letters and numbers, downloaded on to a device when the user accesses our Website (“Cookie”). A Cookie is stored on your hard drive.

Cookies are useful, they ease navigation, and allow us to recognise your device. Cookies enable us to identify the most common and popular sections of our Website and so enable us to provide information to you that is appropriate, whilst also allowing us to improve the service that we provide to you. MGP is constantly seeking to provide you with a better service and therefore we use cookies.

By continuing on to this Website, you automatically consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this Policy.

(2) Personal details

Cookies may store personal data such as email addresses and passwords. This will allow the Website to ensure that you don’t have to re-enter log in details and passwords on numerous occasions.

(3) Non-acceptance of Cookies

You do not have to consent to Cookies when you are prompted. You are able to remove Cookies, a step by step guide to removal of Cookies can be found on http://www.aboutcookies.org. This site can also provide further information on Cookies.

Please note, by not accepting Cookies, there may be reduced availability of services on this Website.

MGP is committed to ensuring that your information is secure. In order to prevent any unauthorised disclosure, we collect the above information and store it in a secure database.

(4) Sessions Cookies

Session Cookies allow websites to link the actions of a user during a browsing session (“Sessions Cookies”). Sessions Cookies are essential to enable the user to use the features of a website and allow the access of secure areas of the Website. Services cannot be provided without the acceptance of Sessions Cookies. Sessions Cookies expire after the browsing session and do not need to be stored long term.

(5) Persistent Cookies

Persistent Cookies allow preferences across our Website or other websites to be stored and remembered (“Persistent Cookies”). This will allow advertising to be targeted from information that has been collected. It also allows our Website to information such as language preference. Persistent Cookies will remain on your device until they are deleted.

(6) Third Party Cookies

Third party cookies are placed by organisations other than MGP (“Third Party Cookies”). This may be because there are pages on our site embedded from third party websites. If these pages are from another website we can unfortunately not control these Third Party Cookies. If you would like to change these Third Party Cookies, you should visit their website and check their information on how to manage their Cookies.

Please note that MGP uses all three types of Cookies, Sessions Cookies, Persistent Cookies and Third Party Cookies. Use of the Website indicates your acceptance to this Policy.

Modern Slavery Act Statement

Statement of Mercedes-Benz Grand Prix Limited (trading as Mercedes-AMG Petronas Motorsport) (‘MGP’) regarding the Modern Slavery Act 2015

The Modern Slavery Act 2015 came into effect on 29th October 2015. This law requires large businesses in the UK to disclose information regarding their policies to eradicate slavery and human trafficking from their supply chain and within their business.

MGP understands the importance of these measures and is committed to helping eradicate all forms of slavery and human trafficking around the world.

Supply Chain

MGP strictly prohibits the use of slavery or human trafficking in its direct supply chain. We have implemented a change into our supplier contracts in which our suppliers sign up to a clause which specifically requires them to comply with the Modern Slavery Act 2015. This clause also allows MGP to audit supplier’s compliance with this clause.

MGP will adopt a zero tolerance policy towards violations of the laws banning forced labour, slavery and human trafficking. Our contractual agreements now permit the termination of supplier contracts for that reason.

MGP has compiled a self-assessment questionnaire which it is sending to all new suppliers which will allow MGP to identify any risks in relation to forced labour, slavery and human trafficking.

Our Own Business

Integrity Code

Daimler AG’s (the parent company of MGP) Integrity Code details the rules and procedures by which MGP should treat fellow employees. This includes:

- Transparent and fair behaviour, such as protecting human rights

- Compliance with legislation, including fundamental rights at work - in particular freedom of association and elimination of discrimination throughout employment.

Employee Training

MGP conducts training to emphasise the importance of acting with integrity and in line with its own internal standards. Guidance regarding the Modern Slavery Act 2015 has now been incorporated into our company-wide compliance training sessions, highlighting risk factors for employees to look out for when dealing with suppliers or with fellow employees. The training materials are then made available to all staff via the MGP intranet.
