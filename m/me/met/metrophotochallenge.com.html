Privacy policy

General

Personal information collected on this website will be used to contact you if one of your photos has won a prize and to report that you have won a prize. Metro will not share your personal information with any third parties without your explicit prior consent. Metro and its affiliates will not use your personal information for other then Photo Challenge marketing purposes without obtaining your explicit prior consent.

Using your name, age and city for promotional activity

Metro reserves the right to publish your photos, and your name, and age for all promotional activity related to this competition. For example, Photo of the day – everyday for over 10 days in over 20 countries we will publish a selected photo, with the name of the author, age and city (please read rules and terms & conditions)

What personal information does Metro collect on this website?

Metro will collect your name, surname, age, phone number and e-mail address. We may also require your address. Without submitting this data you cannot take part in competition or prize draw.

Will you receive any mailing after you registered on this website?

After registering online you will receive one email from Metro requiring validation of your email address. The email will contain a link which you must click to validate the email address. You only need to do this once. You will also receive an email in case you win a prize. We will send you other e-mails (photo of the day, e-newslelter, notifications about other metro promotions and projects), only if you give us a prior consent.

Will Metro disclose the information it collects to third parties?

Metro will not sell, trade or rent your personal information to others unless you have given your explicit permission. Metro may also provide aggregate statistics about visitors to its websites for its partners and clients. These statistics will include no personally identifying information.

Why does Metro use "cookies"?

"Cookies" are small pieces of information that are stored by your browser on your computer's hard drive. They make it possible for Metro to identify registered users without needing to ask for registration details every time you vote for an entry or every time you access the site. The website uses cookies to make the user experience easier and speed up some processes.
