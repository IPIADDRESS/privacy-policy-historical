Miniso-au.com Privacy Statement


MINISO has created this privacy statement in order to demonstrate our firm commitment to privacy. We collect certain types of information about the visitors and customers via this website, you should fully understand our policies surrounding the collection and the use of information.

 

Please read our Privacy Policy carefully to get a clear understanding of how we collect, use, protect or otherwise handle your Personally Identifiable Information in accordance with our website.

 

1.       PERSONAL INFORMATION COLLECTED

 

MINISO and Miniso-au.com offers a variety of services that are collectively referred to as the “Service”. MINISO and Miniso-au.com collect information from individuals who visit this website (“visitors”).

 

Some information Visitors provide through optional, voluntary submissions. These are voluntary submissions such as contacting our customer service department via e-mail, participating in our message boards or forums, e-mails to a friend, submissions to receive our electronic newsletters, registering on our website, placing orders and participating in polls, surveys and sweepstakes, we may use your current location to help to find you the nearest store. We may also combine information you give us online with other information wobtain from sources, transactions and communications. This may include, but is not limited to, data from online marketing, mobile marketing and direct mail. We may also combine this information with data that is publicly available and from other data we receive from reputable sources. This may include, but is not limited to, postal address updates and demographic data.  All data combined and stored with the on-line data is protected by this Privacy Policy. Billing and collection functions are handled by PayPal. In performing these services, PayPal collects personal information. Please see PayPal’s privacy statements on its website for details on how PayPal treats this information.

 

(A)    Registration

When registering on our site, as appropriate, you may be asked to enter your email address basically. And you may complete your information in profile fields after registering successfully , the profile fields includes but not limit to your name, country, city, gender, age, au case size, favor colors, birthday, method to buy or other details to help us know you better. We use this data to tailor our visitor’s experience at our site, showing them content that we think they might be interested in, and displaying the content according to their preferences.

 

(B)    Newsletter

Visitors can voluntarily subscribe to our newsletter by entering the email address. We will send the brand update, newest events and the monthly theme email to the subscribers. Visitors may opt-out of receiving future mailings; see the choice/opt-out section below.

 

(C)    Surveys

Sometimes MINISO and Miniso-au.com posts optional surveys in which we ask visitors for demographic information (like their ages, or income level). This allows us to better target our content to our audience. We use contact data from our surveys to send the user information about MINISO. The customer’s contact information is also used to contact the visitor when necessary. Visitors may opt-out of receiving future mailings; see the choice/opt-out section below. You do not have to answer these surveys or all the fields in surveys. If we are asking for optional information, we will mark the required information with an asterisk (*) so you know the unmarked fields are optional and you can skip them if you want. We use the aggregated demographic information to improve the product and service, and as advertisements guideline.

 

(D)    Contest and Sweepstakes

MINISO sometimes holds contest and sweepstakes in which we ask for some contact information like the email address, the demographic information like the age, gender and job, and customized information like preferred colors, preferred au case size. In some case visitors may be asked to fill out a brief survey or upload a photo, which are not limited, to exchange for allowing such visitor to enter the contest or sweepstakes. The customer’s contact information is also used to contact the visitor when necessary and to notify you if you are a winner. Visitors may opt-out of receiving future mailings; see the choice/opt-out section below. Demographic and profile data is also collected at our site. We use this data to tailor our visitor’s experience at our site, showing them content that we think they might be interested in, and displaying the content according to their preferences. Any sweepstakes and other contests will be subject to special rules which will be identified on this website.

 

(E)     Other Information

 

(a)     Website Navigational Information

 

As you navigate the www.Miniso-au.com web site, we may also collect information through the use of commonly used information-gathering tools, such as cookies and web beacons (“Website Navigational Information”). Website Navigational Information includes standard information from your web browser (such as browser type and browser language), your Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, and the actions you take on the website (such as the web pages viewed and the links clicked).

 

(b)     Tracking by Search Terms

MINISO tracks the searching terms which visitors enter in the searching box. The tracking is not associated with individuals, as we do not track how many pages a specific visitor looks through. We use this information to find out which areas of our website visitors like and don’t like based on the amount of traffic to those pages. This provides the basis how we arrange modify the content. 

 

(c)     Third party behavioral tracking

It's important to note that we allow third party behavioral tracking such as Google Analytics. However, we will not use individuals’ information but the aggregate information to improve our website and service, which is stated below.

4.       WEBSITE NAVIGATIONAL INFORMATION

 

As stated above, MINISO and Miniso-au.com uses commonly used information-gathering tools, such as cookies and web beacons, to collect information as you navigate the website (“Website Navigational Information”). This section describes the types of Website Navigational Information MINISO may collect and how MINISO may use this information.

 

(A)    IP Addresses

 

An Internet Protocol (“IP”) address is a number that is automatically given to your computer when you surf the web. We use your IP address to help diagnose problems with our server, and to administer our web site. Your IP address is used to gather broad demographic information.  MINISO and Miniso-au.com collects IP addresses for the purpose of system administration and to provide aggregate data to advertisers about the volume of use on Miniso-au.com. MINISO and Miniso-au.com does not normally link IP addresses to anything personally identifiable, which means that your session will be logged, but you remain anonymous to us. We can and will use IP addresses to identify a user when we feel it is necessary to enforce compliance with our terms and conditions or to protect our service, site, visitors, or others. 

 

(B)     Cookies

 

We may place a text file called a “cookie” in the browser files of your computer. A cookie is a small data file that transfers to your hard drive for record keeping purposes when you visit them. We use cookies for several purposes, which include helping remember and process the items in the shopping cart. Understand and save user's preferences for future visits. Keep track of advertisements. Updating our site based on data such as the total number of visitors and pages viewed. Unless you choose to identify yourself to Miniso-au.com, either by responding to a promotional offer, or filling out your information such as registering with your email address, you remain anonymous to us.

 

You can choose to have your computer warn you each time a cookie is being sent, or you can choose to turn off all cookies. You do this through your browser (like Internet Explorer) settings. Each browser is a little different, so look at your browser's Help menu to learn the correct way to modify your cookies. You do not need to have cookies turned on to use this website.
