Collection and Use of Personally Identifiable Information Generally:

We do not collect personally identifiable information about individuals, e.g., name, street address and zip code, email address, landline and cell phone numbers, gender, birthday and any other information that would allow us to identify you or contact you (collectively "Personally Identifiable Information") on this web site except when you specifically and knowingly provide that information to us. That information will only be used by Lifetime in accordance with the policies set forth below. We never collect your social security number or bank information. Examples of Personally Identifiable Information we may collect from you and use:
  • the e-mail addresses of those who post comments, stories, videos and other content on our web site;
  • the names and e-mail addresses of those who communicate with us by e-mail;
  • the names and e-mail addresses of those who specifically opt in to special offers;
  • information you provide to us in surveys to Members, in connection with contests or sweepstakes which Lifetime sponsors, and/or in response to solicitations for photographs, ideas, or stories for use on Lifetime Online and/or on air.
  • information you provide about yourself when you post to our web site.
Note: You do not have to provide Personally Identifiable Information to us. However, particular services provided through this web site may require that you furnish Personally Identifiable Information, e.g., to become a Member; to enter a Lifetime sweepstakes or contest; to receive one or more of our electronic newsletters; to participate in our online community; and to participate in various promotional opportunities.

Cookies:

We may place a "cookie" in the browser files of your computer. Cookies allow us to track where you travel on our site and what you look at. They also permit us to ensure that visitors are not repeatedly sent the same banner ads. If you are a Member, they allow us to customize the content we send to you. Most web browsers can be set to inform you when a cookie has been sent to you and provide you with an opportunity to refuse that cookie. Refusing a cookie will not interfere with your use of this site, although it is possible some parts of the site will not function properly if you do so. The cookie itself does not contain any Personally Identifiable Information, although it will enable us to relate a visitor's use of our site to information that you have specifically and knowingly provided to us.

Web Beacons:

While you are on the web site, or while you are otherwise viewing the web site content, you may encounter Web beacons, which may come from Lifetime or from third parties, such as advertisers or content providers. A Web beacon, which is sometimes also called a Web bug or "clear gif," is a small graphic image in a Web page, a Web-based document or an e-mail message, which generally allows the party that set the Web beacon to monitor certain information about the viewer of the web page, Web-based document or e-mail message, and may allow access to certain cookies. Web beacons are usually invisible to the user, because they are very small (generally 1-by-1 pixel). However, in general, any electronic image viewed as part of a Web page, including an advertisement, can act as a Web beacon. Web beacons typically collect the URL of the page the Web beacon comes from, the type of browser requesting the Web beacon, the IP address of the computer that the Web beacon is sent to and the time the Web beacon was viewed. We do not have control over Web beacons used by third parties and do not have access to whatever information they may collect.

This web site contains links to third-party web sites. Lifetime is not responsible for the privacy policies and/or practices on those other web sites. When linking to another web site you should read the privacy policy posted on that web site. Our privacy policy only governs information collected on this web site.

We also collect and analyze aggregate demographic information. Further, aggregate information is helpful to us for improving the services we offer and for marketing purposes. We may share aggregate information, which does not personally identify you, with our advertisers, business partners or other companies to provide more relevant services and advertising to you.

Personally Identifiable Information, including financial information that you provide to us, necessarily may be shared with such third parties, but only for the purpose of their providing such services.

Lifetime may use Personally Identifiable Information you provide to us in response to our solicitation for editorial purposes, online and/or on air and we may also use such information for marketing and promotional purposes, as we indicate. Members:

The Membership section of Lifetime’s web site makes special offers available to those users who sign up and provide Lifetime with certain Personally Identifiable Information. Personally Identifiable Information also may be obtained through surveys we send to Members by email (with their consent) or which are posted in the Members section, and are only used for internal review and/or our own market research and/or to improve the content of our web site. If we provide any such information to third parties, we only provide such information in aggregated form.

Member accounts are secured by Member-created usernames and passwords. Lifetime takes precautions to ensure account information is kept private. We use reasonable measures to protect member information that is stored within our database. However, no data transmission over the Internet can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. Accordingly, we cannot ensure or warrant the security of any information you transmit to us, and you do so at your own risk.

Postings and Profiles:

Only Members can post materials to our web site including, without limitation, comments, stories, photos and videos. While only Members can post these materials, Members and non-members alike can see the postings. You should be careful not to disclose sensitive, personal information on our web site. Any information posted can be accessed by the public and may be used by members of the public to send you unsolicited communications. Your username may be displayed when you post material to this web site; therefore, if you associate your name with your username, people may be able to personally identify you and your activities on this web site.

Chats:

From time to time, we will host chats. The terms governing Postings and Profiles also apply to chats.

Newsletters:

If you wish to receive Lifetime electronic newsletter(s), you must be a Member. If you wish to stop receiving a particular newsletter or change your newsletter preferences, please go the Membership section and edit your profile.

If you are a Member, you can sign in on any page of the web site and edit, correct or delete your profile information (i.e., the Personally Identifiable Information you have provided to us). If you receive any other email from us, you may request that we stop sending it to you by mailing us at privacy@lifetimetv.com Please note, however, that if you use more than one name or email address when communicating with us or participating on our web site, you may continue to receive communications from us to any name and/or email address which you have not specified in your request.

Please note: We are not responsible for removing information from third party lists or entities with which we have shared your information pursuant to this policy. If you receive e-mails or other communications from third parties for which you have opted in from Lifetime, you will then be subject to that third party's privacy policy.

Our site is a general audience site, and is not intended for children under 13. Further, we do not knowingly collect information from children under the age of 13. If we receive information from anyone who we later learn is under the age of 13, we destroy the information.
