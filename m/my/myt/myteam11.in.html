Collecting Information/Use of Cookies:

An HTTP cookie (also called web cookie, Internet cookie, browser cookie or simply cookie), is a small piece of data sent from a website and stored in the user's web browser while the user is browsing. Every time the user loads the website, the browser sends the cookie back to the server to notify the user's previous activity.

Cookies or similar electronic tools will be used by us to collect user information. When a user visits the website then a unique number or User Identification (User ID) is assigned so as to understand the User's individual interests. Myteam11.com has no way to know or decide who the User is, even if we assign a cookie to the User's computer. We cannot know until and unless the User voluntarily identifies himself/herself that is through registration process.

A cookie is a piece of data stored on your hard drive that allows us to recognise your computer from one visit and from one page access to the next. This helps us to customise the Site as per your interests to reflect your individual preferences and it also allow us to understand how you and other users use the facilities and play the Fantasy Games so that we can improve our website and also the Amusement Facilities/contests provided by us.

When created, cookies normally don't contain any personal information. They don't scan your computer or do any kind of investigation to find out your personal information. Any personal information they might contain is a result of user’s own input on a website's form that is the information supplied by the User. Myteam11.com will have no control in the case where our advertisers assign their own cookies to the user's browser (which happens if the User clicks on their ad banners).

When any User visits the site then Myteam11.com's web servers will automatically collect limited information about User's computer connection to the Internet, including User's IP address,. (User's IP address is a numerical label assigned to each device participating in a computer network that uses the Internet Protocol for communication. This number allows computers attached to the Internet know where to send data to the User -- such as the web pages viewed by the User. It is similar to that someone needs your mailing address to send you a letter). The User's IP address does not reveal any personal details of user.
