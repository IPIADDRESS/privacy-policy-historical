MyFamily.com Privacy Policy

MyFamily.com recognizes the potential sensitivity of confidential information that may be placed on member family Web sites and is firmly committed to protecting your privacy. The following discloses our information gathering and dissemination practices for this site.

Overview
None of the personal, private information that is uploaded or entered on a MyFamily.com Web site by individual members is viewed by MyFamily.com, or distributed to others outside of your site (except in rare cases involving reported violations of our use policies or legal statutes.) We do collect some data, always with your knowledge and consent, which is used to personalize the site for your convenience and for the benefit of other family members and friends who are included in your site.

A portion of this data, particularly that which reflects membersâ€™ lifestyles and interests, may be used in aggregate statistical form to attract potential sponsors and deliver advertising messages to appropriate users. For instance we might tell a prospective advertiser that 10,000 of our members have indicated an interest in golf, and then distribute an attractive offer exclusively to those users.

In no case is the personal information about individual members sold or otherwise shared with advertisers, sponsors, partners or other 3rd parties. And under no circumstances is this information collected or distributed in any form without your prior knowledge and consent.

Special privacy policy concerning children 13 and younger.
MyFamily.com, Inc. believes that the Internet is an important tool in connecting and strengthening families, but also recognizes that young children need help and protection while using this tool. In that light, and in compliance with the letter and spirit of laws designed to protect children and encourage parental responsibility for children using the Internet, we have adopted this special policy concerning children 13 or under.

  1. We will require children 13 or under to obtain parental approval before registering as a member of a MyFamily site using the MyFamily.com service. During the sign up process to begin, or to become a member of, a MyFamily.com site, each potential member will be required to certify that he or she is over the age of 13, or obtain parental permission to proceed with the registration. In the case of a child registrant, a parent will be asked to provide an e-mail address which is not the same as the address used by the child, and will then receive an e-mail asking for confirmation that the parent approves the child's participation. The child's registration will not be activated until a response from the parent to that email is received.
  2. All children 13 or under who become members of a MyFamily site will be "opted out" of receiving any communication from MyFamily.com at the time of their registration. As members of the site, they will able to "opt in" to site notifications, but not into newsletters or other communications not resulting from a site event.
  3. Parents and site administrators should remember that MyFamily private sites are indeed private, the user provided content in the site feature areas on them is not accessed nor controlled by us. Parents, site administrators and other members of a MyFamily site should be sensitive to the degree that young people participate in a family site and assure that content provided by young people is submitted and used only in an appropriate manner.
  4. Parents will be allowed to request information from MyFamily.com,Inc. about what information their child has provided by their child and is kept by us. We will require positive identification of the parent and their relationship before we release that information to the parent.

How Does MyFamily.com use my personal information?

Our goal is to provide you with the most personalized, relevant, user-friendly Web experience possible. The primary use of all your personal information is to share it with other family members and friends when they visit your site.

MyFamily.com will also use registration and profile information to advise you of new members and new postings to the site, to let you know about new features or other offers of interest from MyFamily.com, or to address customer service needs and requests.

In order to make the site more relevant to you we also direct advertising from our sponsors according to the interests of our users. For instance, if you indicate an interest in cooking in your member profile, you may see an ad from Betty Crocker, while your brother, who likes sports might see an Adidas ad. Ultimately, this makes the service far more valuable to each family member.

In distributing advertiser or sponsor messages, the advertiser never has access to individual account information. MyFamily.com does not sell, rent or otherwise distribute the personal information you provide us. Advertisers simply give us an ad and tell us the type of audience they want to reach (for example, males over 35 years old, or 18-34 year olds interested in outdoor sports). MyFamily.com then displays the ad only to users who meet those criteria.

MyFamily.com works with a third party that serves ads to this site. To find out more about how Flycast manages the privacy of information in conjunction with serving ads on this site, please go to Flycast's privacy policy.

Cookies are tiny files placed onto the hard drive of your computer the first time you visit our Web site that enable our server to recognize or "remember" who you are next time you come back. MyFamily.com does make limited use of cookies to deliver content specific to your interests. For instance they may be used to retain your personal preferences, hold session information for a "shopping basket," or save your password so it doesnâ€™t have to be re-entered each time you login.

These ads may contain cookies used primarily to provide data to the advertiser about where visitors are coming from. While we do use cookies in other parts of our Web site as explained above, cookies received with banner ads may or may not be collected or controlled by our company.

Intel Pentium III Processor Serial Number: MyFamily.com may use the processor serial number function as an additional, optional mechanism by which administrative control or access is granted.
