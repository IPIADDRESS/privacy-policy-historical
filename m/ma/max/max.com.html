PRIVACY POLICY & USER CONSENT

 

This Privacy Policy is designed to help you understand how we 1) collect, record, restore and/or disclose certain User information and communications that you authorize through selecting and enabling a User Profile, and 2) how we collect, use and safeguard the personal information you provide to us.  This Privacy Policy is intended to assist you in making informed decisions about using our site and our services.  In addition, it also constitutes all disclosure necessary for your informed consent.

 

Authorization & Consent to Intercept, Collect, Record & Disclose

Certain Communications & Information

 

By enabling a User Profile, the Primary Administrator is specifically authorizing us to intercept, collect, and/or record certain communications by, and information about, the User of that Profile (collectively “Profile Data”).  Profile Data may include 1) websites visited by the User, 2) information, images or documents downloaded by the User, 3) the User’s e-mail communications through the Service, and 4) the User’s communications sent through the Service by Instant Messenger or similar methods.   The Profile Data is logged and stored by our secure database for thirty (30) days and then automatically deleted.

 

As part of the Service, the Primary Administrator can activate or de-activate this authorization at will, and can also adapt this authorization to desired levels.  The Profile Data is also made available for the Primary Administrator’s review and/or disclosure.  The only other persons with access to the Profile Data are Max’s Vice President of Technical Operations and its Lead Engineer.  Those persons will only access the database and the Profile Data contained therein as required in the ordinary course of its business.  We will not otherwise use or disclose the Profile Data, except as provided herein.  If you have any questions or concerns about effect or use of any User Profile, or the Profile Data collected, recorded or stored by us, please contact management@Max.com.

 

By creating the User Profile, the Primary Administrator consents to the interception, collection, recording, and disclosure of the User’s Profile Data as described herein. In creating and authorizing a User Profile, the Primary Administrator also confirms that he or she is the Custodial Parent or Legal Guardian of any minor for which a User Profile is created.

 

By using the Service, you are also acknowledging and consenting to the interception, collection, recording and disclosure of the Profile Data, as determined by the Primary Administrator in the User Profile.

 

The above-stated consents of the Primary Administrator and/or User shall waive any and all privacy rights that the User might otherwise have, including but not limited to those arising under federal, state or common law, and shall specifically waive any rights created by the Electronic Communications Privacy Act, 18 U.S.C.A. §§ 2510 et seq.     • What Are Cookies?

 

Cookies are a feature of Web browser software that allows Web servers to recognize the computer used to access a Web site. They are small pieces of data that are stored by a user’s Web browser on the user’s hard drive. Cookies can remember what information a user accesses on one Web page to simplify subsequent interactions with that Web site by the same user or to use the information to streamline the user’s transactions on related Web pages. This makes it easier for a user to move from Web page to Web page and to complete commercial transactions over the Internet. Cookies should make your online experience easier and more personalized.

 

    • How Do We Use Information We Collect from Cookies?

 

We use Web site browser software tools such as cookies and Web server logs to gather information about our Service users’ browsing activities in order to constantly improve our Service and better serve our customers. This information assists us to design and arrange our Service in the most user-friendly manner and to continually improve our Service to better meet the needs of our customers and prospective customers.

 

Cookies help us collect important business and technical statistics. The information in the cookies lets us trace the paths followed by users of our Service.  Web server logs allow us to count how many people use our Service and evaluate its capacity.  We do not use these technologies to capture your individual e-mail address or any personally identifying information about you although they do permit us to send focused online banner advertisements or other such responses to you.
