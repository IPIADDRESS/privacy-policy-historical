How Toxic Emotions Are Linked To Cancer
    Society today has taught us that emotions, especially of the negative nature, should be suppressed or held back, thinking that it will lead us to less conflicts or avoid them all together. Yet studies have shown this shouldn’t be the case. In fact, the more you hold them back, the more unnatural and tense you’ll be. The human psyche is made up of so much more than the pursuit of joy, happiness and glee. Negative emotions – like anger, envy, sorrow, disgust, fear, etc. are all natural; they help us define the world we live in, act as buffers warning and safeguarding us from potential or present threats, and if just dealt with wisely, would help us cope and live a more balanced and fulfilling life. Constantly suppressing bad and troublesome thoughts is like running away and not really dealing with your present issues head-on. The bottled up emotions will then turn sour and ultimately toxic. Anger could turn into rage and hatred. Sorrow could turn into depression. Fears could turn into panic, or worse yet, phobias. You get the idea.       Recent studies have shown that suppressing toxic emotions could lead to cancer – particularly anger, hate, resentment and grief. Scientists have discovered that the rise of stress hormones like cortisol directly suppresses our immune systems to function properly. It’s like our bodies’ security system goes out of whack, and with our literal “bodyguards” not up to order, the normal cells will mutate and turn into cancer cells.     The studies have noted that most of the cancer patients had low anger scores, which means they either suppressed (consciously held back), repressed (unconsciously put in the back of their minds) and restrained themselves from expressing their anger. Follow-up studies, on the other hand, showed that survival rates of people who openly expressed their anger have doubled. If you’d like to read more about this in detail, I highly recommend you go to Alternative-Cancer-Care’s website.       Cancer, by definition, is the uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells in our bodies that will then spread and invade healthy nearby tissues. In essence, they’re cells that don’t belong to your body but will try to destroy you. I guess what nature is trying to teach us is that: Toxic emotions are never meant to be part of us. The human soul, as enigmatic as it could be, has never been designed to harbor emotional toxins, and it is our responsibility to be free from them. Mental hygiene is just as important and vital as physical hygiene.  
7 Reasons Why Laughing Each Day (Not Just an Apple) Keeps the Doctor Away
    The old proverb, “Laughter is the best medicine”, originally came from the Bible. Proverbs 17:22 says that, “A merry heart doeth good like a medicine: but a broken spirit drieth the bones.” In literature, we can find numerous writers magnifying the powers of laughter. To name a few, E. E. Cummings wrote that, “The most wasted of all days is one without laughter.” An unknown joker wrote, “Take two jokes and call me in the morning.” A worthwhile and free prescription, if you ask me.     It’s no wonder; even modern day science has come to attest this phenomenon. Studies have been steadily on the rise in backing it up. Here are a few reasons why you should never let a day pass by without having a few minutes of a good old chuckle:   1.    Laughter is good for the heart. Maybe not for a specific ailment, but laughter does have healing properties, according to studies. A good belly laugh can cause your brain to release endorphins, our bodies’ “happy pill”. Endorphins are pleasure-inducing neurotransmitters that help increase blood flow and reduce cholesterol build-up. The Biblical proverb, “A merry heart doeth good like a medicine”, really does hold true, even in a medical sense. 2.    Laughter has numerous physical benefits. Research has found these powerful benefits in laughter therapy: •    Improves oxygen intake •    Steadies blood pressure •    Unwinds muscles •    Improves sleep •    Eases digestion or calms stomach aches •    Improve overall mood and attitude   3.    Laughter is a powerful cure to stress. A good laugh can do wonders in bringing balance to your mind and body. It helps lighten burdens, reduce pain, ease up pressure and as a whole, supports both physical and emotional health. 4.    Laughter is contagious and helps you build connections. You noticed how, when a person takes a picture of you, you’re not the only one smiling at the camera? Even the photographer is smiling with you. This isn’t just a way to bond or encourage you to smile, but is actually caused by “mirror neurons”. When you’re down on your lumps, sticking around with jokers would be a great way to lift that frown off your face. Not only that, when there’s relational tension and conflicts, laughter can help you reconnect, build rapport, and even release anger and aid you in forgiving more easily. 5.    Laughter makes you tough. People who persevere and resilient have been known to be happy people. People who know how to laugh at themselves tend to accept their mistakes more easily, thereby enabling them to acknowledge these mistakes, recognize what went wrong, and overall, learn to overcome them. And with the ability to endure, success will be within reach. 6.    Laughter can help burn calories. OK, so maybe not a whole afternoon of gym’s workout worth of calories. But one study noted that laughing for a good 10-15 minutes is the equivalent of 40 calories. Also, your body will be more in sync and filled with happy neurons that could help make going to the gym an overall better experience. It’s a win-win situation, really. 7.    Laughter is good therapy for patients. Laughter therapy is now being explored by researchers on its effect especially in cancer patients. With its ability to decrease stress-related hormones, it can distract them from the patients from pain and boost their immune system. I know, cancer is no laughing matter, but as hard as it may seem to be, laughing may induce physical changes in the body. It may not be the next cure to cancer but would be a tremendous help in the overall healing process.

Mount Mayon
    The Mount Mayon is a volcano that has an exact symmetrical cone. It is called the "perfect cone". The location of the volcano is at the province of Albay in Luzon. The perfect cone has an elevation of 8,081 ft with a prominence of 8,028 ft. As an active volcano its last eruption was on the year 2010. Mayon can be found at the tip or within the boundary of Legazpi City. In the year 2000 it was developed into a national park. It is also nicknamed as Bulkang Magayon after the legend of "Daragang Mayon".     The perfect cone of the volcano was formed by layers of lava flows and eruptions that it has gone through in the past years. It is included in the Pacific Ring of Fire like other volcanoes in the Pacific Ocean. The volcano is situated in the east side of Luzon with a position close to the Philippine Trench or the boundary wherein the Sea Plate is placed right under the Mobile Belt. When these plates collide with the oceanic plate it is driven down towards the Earth’s mantle. Magma enters and finds a way through exit points such as the Mount Mayon through those gaps in the crust created by the collision of plates.     For over 400 years Mayon Volcano has erupted 48 times. It is the most active volcano in the Philippines. Its most vicious eruption ever recorded was on February 1, 1814 when the volcano spitted black ashes continuously until it buried Cagsawa town. It is the most fatal explosion as its casualties rose to 2,200 Albay residents. There are theories that this explosion triggered the eruption in Mount Tambora the following year which led to the “Year Without Summer” of 1816.     As for the longest eruption without any interruption the Mayon volcano spitted fire for seven days starting on June 23, 1897. It erupted eastward so that Bacacay Village was buried 49ft under the lava. This eruption took 100 lives in Libon and another 400 from the other surrounding villages.     Fortunately in the 1984 eruption an estimated number of more than 70,000 people were evacuated from danger zones before the volcano erupted. There were low casualties also in the 1993 eruption compared to past eruptions as only 77 were killed most of which were farmers.     It was the same with the 2006 eruption which has no record of any loss of life. It is reported though that the said eruption was now weaker than the past. But some scientists still believe that the rumblings and activities that Mount Mayon is going through constantly from 2006 to 2011 are all part of a possibly more destructive eruption somewhere in the future.     Legends were offered by the people in an attempt to provide answers to the mysterious origin of Mayon. How Toxic Emotions Are Linked To Cancer
    Society today has taught us that emotions, especially of the negative nature, should be suppressed or held back, thinking that it will lead us to less conflicts or avoid them all together. Yet studies have shown this shouldn’t be the case. In fact, the more you hold them back, the more unnatural and tense you’ll be. The human psyche is made up of so much more than the pursuit of joy, happiness and glee.
