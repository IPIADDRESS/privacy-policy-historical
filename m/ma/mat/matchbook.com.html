Information collection

We collect two types of information from online activity at Matchbook: general tracking data and user-specific data.

General tracking information is statistical and other analytical information collected on an aggregated basis. It does not identify you as an individual, and is used only to enable us to better understand user requirements.

General tracking data includes information on web traffic, such as a user's browsing activity; IP address; PC operating system; type of browser, and similar details.

User-specific information is particular to each individual user who chooses to provide that information.

User-specific information includes your name and address, date of birth and e-mail address. Additional information, such as payment card details, bank account details or online e-wallet account information, may also be provided in the Cashier section.

Cookies

What are cookies?

Cookies are pieces of information in the form of very small text files that a website stores in a user's browser and can retrieve whilst a user is browsing the website in order to identify the user and understand the user's activity. Matchbook uses cookies on our website to improve its performance and your user experience.

What type of cookies does Matchbook use?

When you use the Matchbook website the following types of cookies can be set on your device:

  1. Session Cookies: these are stored on a user's device and remain there until the termination of the session of a given browser. Stored information is then permanently removed from the device memory. Session Cookies Mechanism does not allow Matchbook to access any personal data or private information from a user's device.
  2. Persistent Cookies: these are stored on a user's device and remain there until deleted. Termination of the session of a given browser does not cause their removal from the user's device. Persistent Cookies Mechanism do allow Matchbook to access personal data or private information from a user's device.

What does Matchbook use cookies for?

Session management

Cookies are used for authenticating a user in the website, securing the user's session in the website and facilitating the correct configuration of the user’s preferred website functions.

Example: Identifying you as being signed in to Matchbook.com and keeping you logged in throughout your visit.

Personalisation

These cookies allow the website to remember choices a user makes and provide enhanced experience and more personalised features. They allow the user to personalise the content of the website according to user preferences, recognition of the user's device and its location and displaying the web pages accordingly, remembering user's settings and personalising user's interface.

Example: Remembering your preference for type of odds type, language or currency.

Tracking 

Matchbook uses Google Analytics that set their own cookies. These cookies are used to collect statistical information about visitors of the website and the pages they view. These cookies do not collect information that identifies a visitor. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and used anonymously.

Example: Understanding what content is popular to helps us to improve our website.

 

Third party cookies

Some cookies that have been set on our websites are not related to Matchbook.com. When you visit a page with content embedded from, for example, Twitter or YouTube, these service providers may set their own cookies on your web browser. We do not control the use of these cookies and cannot access them, as cookies can only be accessed by the party who originally set them. Please inspect the cookie policy of the third party for further details.

More information about cookies and details of how to manage or disable them can be found on www.aboutcookies.org.

PLEASE NOTE: We strongly recommend that you permit the use of cookies; if you choose to disable cookies by modifying the settings in your browser then you may find that certain sections of the Website do not work as they should, and we do not accept any liability to you for any losses you incur as a result of this.
