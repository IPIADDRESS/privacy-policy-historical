Information About All marketday.com,seasonsofsharing Visitors
In general, our service automatically gathers certain usage information like the numbers and frequency of visitors to marketday.com and seasonsofsharing.com and each of its areas, very much like television ratings that tell the networks how many people tuned in to a program. We only use such data in the aggregate. This collective data helps us determine how much our customers use parts of a site, so we can improve that site to ensure it is as appealing as we can make it for as many of you as possible. For example, marketday.com and seasonsofsharing.com use a technology nicknamed "cookies" that tell us how and when pages in our sites are visited, and by how many people. Market Day and Seasons of Sharing cookies do not collect personally identifiable information and we do not combine information collected through cookies with other personally identifiable information to tell us who you are or even what your screen name or e-mail address is.

Information About You
We may specifically ask for information about you when you sign up to use a service or when you order a product. We need certain information such as your name, billing address and credit card number in order to provide that service or product to you. We do not collect that information for any purpose other than to process your Market Day or Seasons of Sharing order.

E-mail Addresses
When you register with marketday.com or seasonsofsharing.com, we ask for your email address. This information is used to contact you with questions that we may have about your order. In addition, you may also 'opt-in' and elect to receive promotional information via e-mail from Market Day or Seasons of Sharing. Your e-mail address will also be provided to the Market Day Chairperson at your school, for the express purpose of enabling the chairperson to contact you regarding your order or to promote the Market Day sale or Seasons of Sharing program at your school.

E-mail addresses may also be collected at times other than registration; for example, in connection with promotions. Market Day and Seasons of Sharing may from time to time send promotional information to these e-mail addresses. If you wish to 'opt-out' from receiving such material, please log-in at www.marketday.com and choose ‘personal settings’ from the menu at the page bottom. Then select ‘no’ to receive e-mail newsletters.

These e-mail addresses are to be used solely for the promotion of the Market Day sale or Seasons of Sharing program at your school or to provide customer service for issues relating to the Seasons of Sharing program or the Market Day sale or products. Any other use is prohibited.

Disclosure
We collect the domain names of visitors to our web site and the e-mail addresses of those who communicate with us via e-mail, however, we do not disclose information about your individual marketday.com or seasonsofsharing.com visits or information that you may give us, such as your name, address, email address or telephone number, to any outside companies for their personal use.

Special Attention to Kids
Market Day Corporation takes special care to protect the safety and privacy of young people visiting our web sites. We do not specifically collect information about children and believe that children should get their parents’ consent before giving out any personal information. We encourage you to participate in your child’s experience in cyberspace and to follow these simple safety tips before your child explores the Internet.

  1. Don’t give your Internet password to anyone, even your best friend.
  2. Never tell someone your home address, telephone number, school name or any personal information.
  3. If someone says something that makes you feel unsafe or funny, take charge! Don’t respond. Tell your parents immediately.
  4. Never say you’ll meet someone in person.
  5. Always tell a parent about any threatening or bad language you see online.
  6. Don’t accept things from strangers (web links, e-mail, files).

Market Day Corporation recommends that parents use web filtering technology to supervise their children’s access to the web.
