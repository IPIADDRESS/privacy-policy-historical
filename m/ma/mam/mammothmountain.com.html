Trail Report - Last Updated: Apr 7, 2007 12:13 PM

Even though it has been a light snowfall year, trail crews have a lot of work to do to get the mountain ready for biking.

The Mammoth Mountain Bike Park will open June 23, 2007. See you then!

Fishing Report Details

Hot Creek  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Fish has been steady with a Beatis hatch coming off around midday and caddis poping off in the later afternoon, 2-5pm. The flows have come up but still clear at this time runoff will not be a problem. Plenty of weed grow for the fish to hide in. Overall the conditions are great for this time of year, Nymphing with should give you some good result in the mid morning as things warm up and then you should see fish rise for the emergering Mayflies and Caddis. I would use 6x tippet as the water is clear and the fish have been seeing flies from March 1st. As CADFG changed the regulations allowing winter fishing.

Nymphing Patterns: Surface emergers, scuds, Caddis emerger patterns. San Juan Worms Master nymphs. Small PT,

Dry Fly Patterns: Bwo's,Cdc caddis, Schrodder's Parachute Caddis,Para bwos, Hot creek Caddis, in sizes 18-20s

Fishing Report Details

Upper Owens  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: The Upper Owens river has only been fishing fair. There are some nice fish being hooked but you will have to cover some water. Some spawning Rainbows and Cutthroats present, conditions are clear and low at the present time. There are some bugs hatching Bwos in the Midday and a few caddis. The smaller fish are mostly taking on the surface but keep your eyes peeled as a larger fish or two might come up to the surface. The area below the Benton Crossing bridge to the monument is open.

The Upper Owens River is under special regulations. It is best to look at your CADFG regulation booklet for current regulations.

Nymphing Patterns: Prince Nymphs, Pheasent Tails, Hares Ears, San Juan worms, Are all good flies to start with.

Dry Fly Patterns: BWOs, Orange Stimulators,PMDs and, caddis patterns in size 16-20s

Streamer: Bead Head Black or Olive Wolly Buggers, Muddler Minnows you will need some weight to get them down no dip and strip necessary. Size 8.

Fishing Report Details

Crowley Tributaries  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Conditions on McGee are low and clear. Most of the spawning fish are in the mid to upper reaches. Rainbow's and Cutthroat both present. Use care if wading as most of the spawning fish have started to return to the lake but the future fish are present in the gravel. Spawning fish key on the egg patterns but will take drifted nymphs and if condition are right will come to the dry fly. spawner starting to return to the lake.

Nymphing Patterns: Glow bugs, Prince Nymphs. Micro Mayflies lighten Bugs and Midges all will get result if fish right.

Dry Fly Patterns: Caddis and Mayflies patterns will take fish midday. Try a orange stimulator! size 18.

Streamer: Larger streamers 6-8s.

Fishing Report Details

Lower Owens  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Flows are at 300 cfs up a 100cfs still very fishable Fishing is still good, little tougher to wade in some spots. use Midges early then move to Baetis late morning these will be your most productive patterns for the first half of the day. Try yellow stones in the afternoon and Caddis in the evening. Daytime highs are in the 80s. The Gorge above the Pleasant Reservoir is also a great spot with plenty of opportunities for wild Brown Trout 12-16. You will have to hike to reach this area. It is mostly pocket water and pools. Beware - rattlesnakes may be present.

Nymphing Patterns: Try nymphing in the mornings with WD-40s and Brassies, then go to Bead Head Pheasant Tails, Olive Surface Emergers, Barr's wet emergers or RS Emergers. Sizes 18-20s epoxyback Trico's 18-22s.

Dry Fly Patterns: Small midge patterns,Bwo and caddis pattern 18-20s.

Attractor Patterns: Small Stimulator, Yellow Stone. 16s

Fishing Report Details

East Walker  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: East Walker is fishing well crowds have been a issue in the upper end. If you put the time into fishing below the stock bridge to Murhpys pond. Water levels can change daily as the river is used to water Nevada ranches down stream. At present time the CFS flow is 90 very fishable. Up in the miracle mile area to the dam fishing is still good but the fish have seen a lot of flies. You will need to have a good drift and the right pattern to be productive. Try midge larve and pupa in the early morning then Mayfly nymphs will be the flys of choice. a San Juan worm maybe a good bet also for your top fly. 5x tippet.

Nymphing Patterns: Pheasant Tails, Olive Surface Emergers, Brassies, Red Copper Johns, Bead Head Black and Olive WD-40s, Disco Midges, Barr's Wet Emergers, Zebra Midges, Olive & Natural Birds Nests, Prince Nymphs, and Green Nori's Caddis. San Juan Worm (red) size 14-16.

Dry Fly Patterns: BWO, PMDs, Hemingway Caddis, EC Caddis, Schroeder's Para Caddis and Adams in sizes 16-18, Orange and Yellow stimulators sizes 14-16.

Streamer: Try Muddler Minnows, Wool Head Sculpins, Bunny Leech's, Woolly Buggers and Dave's Chubs. Use size 6s and see what happens.

Fishing Report Details

Crowley Lake  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Conditions on the lake have been great the last few days, as the warm weather continues with light winds, it has brought the water temps up to near perfect temps. The water is in the upper 60s on the surface midday and plenty of midges are hatching. Southwest side of Mcgee bay,Green Banks Sandy point, Six bays, Hilton areas have been producing with both streamers and Midging Tactics. Fish are holding in 10-16 ft. of water and 13 ft. is the good starting point. The water conditions are clear in some places. The little pea dots of alge are presentand starting to make the water a green tint. Look for the next few weeks to be great on Crowley.

Use common sense when driving on the backside of the lake, as there are areas where the ground is very soft, and a tow truck will be costly.

High temps in the 70's and lows in the upper 30's.

Nymphing Patterns: Black or Gray Midges with copper or red and silver wire have been the best, size 16-18s. Callibaetis Nymphs size 16. Brown and Olive leechs getting some good strikes!!

Streamer: Olive leeches and Wolly Buggers, Damsel fly Nymphs. Callibaetis Nymphs all have been working.

Fishing Report Details

Mammoth Lakes Basin  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Road is open. all lakes are ice free. Horseshoe lake is very low, fishing should pick up in the next few weeks with the warm weather. all lakes have been stocked except Horseshoe. stay tuned. try the outlet area of Mary where it flows into Mamie.

Nymphing Patterns: PTs Nymphs, Beadhead Hares Ears, Zebra Midges, sizes 16-20s

Streamer: Black or olive Wolly Buggers. Size 10-12s

San Joaquin  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Road is open. shuttle should start up soon. Over the holiday weekend they were letting you drive down but too many people head down and the Forest Service restricted traffic, fish was good flow were low for this time of year.

Nymphing Patterns: PT, Hares ears,Prince nymphs,San Juan worms

Dry Fly Patterns: Orange Stimulators 14

Streamer: Bea

Fishing Report Details

Bridgeport Reservoir  

Last Updated May 29, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Witnessed fish feeding on the surface from 9am on. Use large Midge patterns, Blood midges hatching. Fish are holding in 12-14' of water best to fish the south end of the lake near Rainbow point and up near East Walker channel. If you're looking to heave it and leave it (Midge fishing as some call it ) use your Crowley tactics,but large flies as the naturals are some of the largest in the world! both Midging and striping will produce.

Nymphing Patterns: Black/copper wire, brown midge, red large 12-14 patterns are working the best. around the 11-16 ft depth.

Dry Fly Patterns: Adult Midge Some Large(red) ones and Calibaetis Patterns to rising fish.

Attractor Patterns:

Streamer: Large brown or Black leech patterns or hornbergs with a Hares Ear dropper.
