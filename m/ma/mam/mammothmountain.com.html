Trail Report - Last Updated: Sep 24, 2007 8:50 AM

Mammoth Mountain Bike Park is now closed. Thanks for making 2007 a season to remember.

Scheduled Opening Day for the 2008 season is Saturday, June 21, 2008.

Fishing Report Details

Hot Creek  

Last Updated Sep 17, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: wind and cooler weather will change the pattern later in the week. crowds have been light as the upper Owens has all the anglers. The action moves from Trico Mayflies Spinners, the female Trico duns in the mornings along with the Caddis and a few PMDs. In the afternoons Caddis and Trico males at Dark. Look for the Baetis to make a beeter showing in the mid-morning. Overall the fishing on the creek is a little tougher due to the thick weeds, and the flows are lower due to the lack of snow pack or rain. Short drag free drifts and light tippet will be in order to hook the phd trout. I would use 6x/7x tippet as the water is clear and the fish have been seeing flies from March 1 (as CADFG changed the regulations allowing winter fishing). Plenty of habitat for the young trout with lots of vegetation to hide under. Fish will be holding in the open pockets and lanes when out feeding. I witnessed Rainbows digging redds on the upper part of Hot Creek this week. The Hot Creek strain spawn this time of year.

Nymphing Patterns: Surface emergers, scuds, Caddis emerger patterns. San Juan Worms Master nymphs. Small PT, Mercer Beadhead micro Stone,

Dry Fly Patterns: Bwo's,Cdc caddis, Schrodder's Parachute Caddis,Para bwos, Hot creek Caddis, Trico spinners in sizes 18-22s, PMD 14-16s

Fishing Report Details

Upper Owens  

Last Updated Sep 17, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Cooler weather on tap along with breeze condition around Weds maybe a little snow for the highcountry later this week. Lots and lots of Planter in the upper, mid and lower areas of the river. Thanks to a deal Doug Smith from June lake worked out with DWP and SCE the river has has lots of water this season. Some brood stock have been planted in the lower end. People wise it's has been crowded during the weekends. Conditions are clear and low at the present time. There are some bugs hatching Trico and Caddis a few Bwos. The smaller fish are mostly taking on the surface but keep your eyes peeled as a larger fish or two might come up to the surface. The area below the Benton Crossing Bridge to the monument is open. Early and late are the best times to fish, with some good Caddis action if the wind lies down before dark! Bad news is the lake is receding fast. Mosquitoes have been a problem in the upper part of the river.

The Upper Owens River is under special regulations. It is best to look at your CADFG regulation booklet for current updates.

Nymphing Patterns: Prince Nymphs, BH Pheasent Tails, Hares Ears, San Juan worms, Are all good flies to start with.

Dry Fly Patterns: BWOs, Orange Stimulators,PMDs and, caddis patterns in size 16-20s Tricos

Streamer: Bead Head Black or Olive Wolly Buggers, Muddler Minnows you will need some weight to get them down no dip and strip necessary. Size 8.

Fishing Report Details

Crowley Tributaries  

Last Updated Sep 10, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Conditions on McGee are very low and clear. Some lake fish have moved into the lower reaches of the creek. The water is clear and fish are spooky! try Hoppers and a dropper.Stealth and a good drift will get you action. The area near the mouth is receding fast and new river channel is exposed every few days. Crowley Lake is well below the fence this year and on a steady march down, making the lake mud flat. Use 6x as the water is very clear.

Nymphing Patterns: ,Bh Pheasant tails. Prince Nymphs. Micro Mayflies lighten Bugs, Bead Head PTs, Zebra midges.

Dry Fly Patterns: Caddis and Mayflies patterns will take fish midday. Try a orange stimulator! size 18. Hoppers

Streamer: Larger streamers as you get near the lower end of creek try woolly buggers and hornbergs and damsels 6,8,10,12 s.

Fishing Report Details

Lower Owens  

Last Updated Sep 17, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Song remains the same but some changes might be on the way as DWP has a crane up at Crowley to lower a work platform into the water. Lower Owens flows are 450 cfs - still fishable but not easy. Fishing is still fair to good, much tougher to wade in a lot of spots. Weather has cooled quite a bit with highs in the 80s. look for overall conditions to improve in the next week or two. Use Tricos/ Caddis early, then move to Baetis late morning. These will be your most productive patterns for the first half of the day. Try Caddis in the late afternoon into the evening along with Tricos. The Gorge above the Pleasant Reservoir is also a great spot with plenty of opportunities for wild Brown Trout 12-16. You will have to hike to reach this area. It is mostly pocket water and pools. Beware - rattlesnakes may be present. Carry plenty of water. It may be best to fish these areas very early as the hot weather is a big problem this time of year.

Nymphing Patterns: Try nymphing in the mornings with WD-40s and Brassies, then go to Bead Head Pheasant Tails, Olive Surface Emergers, Barr's wet emergers or RS Emergers. Sizes 18-20s epoxyback Trico's 18-22s.

Dry Fly Patterns: Small midge patterns,Tricos and Caddis pattern 18-20s.

Attractor Patterns: Small Stimulator, Yellow Stone. 16s

East Walker  

Last Updated Sep 17, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Good news shorter days and much cooler nights helping conditions. Best to fish early and late, weather change may spark alittle more action. Water temps should drop into the 60s from here on out.. Good news is the water is still fairly clear Fishing with dry flies is getting some solid results. Last hour till it's DARK! PMD, CADDIS, 5x tippet. Flows are low at this time - around 90cfs - nothing like last year!

Get a Nevada Fishing license and try down there as overall conditions are better right now.

Fishing Report Details

Crowley Lake  

Last Updated Sep 17, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Over all Crowley has slowed down in the morning but picks up alittle in the afternoons as the lake turns to its Fall mode. Conditions still remain Fair at this time with the catch rate will below mid summer rates. A cold front will past the area later in the week and mix it up again. last weekend the Northend end of the lake was a zoo with the Double Haulers. Great new no more fishing contest on the lake this year. The main focus has been the north end of the lake around the Green Banks. The Fleet heads to the Green Banks area around 8:00am but many areas are fishing well the water is very clear and the wind as been blowing out of the SSW most afternoons. Lake is down about 13-14 ft from the start of the season and still coming down. The draw down maybe coming to a close as DWP has a crane over at Beaver Cove to lower a work platform into the water near the dam.

This time of year it might be best to have a rod loaded with sinking or sink tip to pull streams along with a floating setup for the still fishing method. Fish are keying on both.

The afternoon southwest winds have helped the lake to turn over water conditions have improved and the cooler nights will continue the overall water conditions on the lake. The weeds have almost disappeared. A steady draw down of the lake level is happening this season, the Lake is down about 13-14 ft and still coming down, snow pack and runoff were well below average, and this season was second lowest on record. As far as bugs, lots and lots of Midges on the northend. Perch fry swimming in little schools about 3/4 inch long all over the lake and the fish are keying on them but many of them have been eaten either by the trout or large white birds!!!!!

Flies to try: Black or Gray midge with copper or red wire has been producing try red larve pattern this time of year as the lake moves into the fall pattern. Some days it changes what bead color you have. If you fish the high-pressured areas I would have a good arsenal of sizes and colors. Striping Damsels/ Perch imitations will get action along with Calibaetis nymphs.

Use common sense when driving on the backside of the lake, as there areas where the ground is very soft, and a tow truck will be costly.

Nymphing Patterns: Black or Gray Midges with copper or red and silver wire have been the best, size 16-18s. Red Larve patterns 12-16s. Callibaetis Nymphs size 16. Brown and Olive leechs getting some good strikes!!

Dry Fly Patterns: small black Midge patterns.

Streamer: Olive leeches and Wolly Buggers, Damsel fly Nymphs. Callibaetis Nymphs all have been working perch fry patterns size 12-14s

Fishing Report Details

Mammoth Lakes Basin  

Last Updated Sep 17, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Be prepared as Fall weather will make it first appearance a few days early this season with wind, rain and maybe a little snow midweek. All lakes are well stocked. It may take you a little longer to get there as road work has begun on the way up to lake Mary. The Lakes Basin road may have some delays during the weekdays . All lakes in the Basin have been well planted with one exception (Horseshoe), with CADFG trout along with Alpers trout. Conditions are great right now with the warm weather, just right to put a tube in the water and kick around and stay cool. Try most of the lakes near the inlets and outlets. The fish like to concentrate near those areas. the million dollar trout stock is coming up so the lake basin will be busy over Labor day Weekend but for the mean time enjoy.

Nymphing Patterns: PTs Nymphs, Beadhead Hares Ears, Zebra Midges, sizes 16-20s

Dry Fly Patterns: Adams, light Cahill, Royal Coachmen, Trude, Cal-Mosqitoes, Sierra Bright Dot. 16-20s

Streamer: Black or olive Wolly Buggers damsel flies. Size 10-12s

Fishing Report Details

San Joaquin  

Last Updated Sep 10, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Road is open -. Fishing continues to been Fair to good.Over this season the planted rainbow count in the campground areas is down. Flows are very low for this time of year. Some campgrounds will start to close. Sotcher Lake is producing some Alpers Trout. there is still good dry fly action as water warms up in midday, small Sulfur Duns Hatching, Try Orange Stimulators. Light Cahill's, and Brown sedges. Best fishing is just before Dark. If you are camping use extreme caution as fire danger right now is EXTREMELY HIGH and restrictions are in place. If you want to target the wild fish - small Rainbows, Browns and hybrid Goldens, Brook trout. Hike away from the campground areas as they are hit the hardest. A trail runs most of the river from lower Rainbow Falls up to Shadow Lake. Use a dropper off your dry fly to maximize your efforts!

Nymphing Patterns: PT, Hares ears,Prince nymphs,San Juan worms, zebra midges 16-20s

Dry Fly Patterns: Orange Stimulators 14 Para Adams, PMDs,Cream colored Mayfly Patterns!! 14-16s,Elk Hair Caddis, Hot Butt's Caddis. 16s Light Cahills

Fishing Report Details

Bridgeport Reservoir  

Last Updated Sep 10, 2007 by Harry Blackburn: Lake is low and launching your boat will need to be done at the bath tub near the Dam. Morning lows are getting in the upper 30s cooling the water. Fishing should pick up. Try both large an Small Midge patterns, Blood midges hatching fish feeding on the adults. Fish are holding in 5 to 12 feet of water. It's best to fish the south end of the lake near Rainbow point and up near East Walker channel. some of the Crowley regulars have pulled out and head up. If you're looking to heave it and leave it (Midge fishing, as some call it) use your Crowley tactics, but large flies as the naturals are some of the largest in the world! Both Midging and striping will produce. Try fishing a dry fly to sipping fish with a Blood Midge adult Pattern and Hang ON! They are size #12.

Nymphing Patterns: Black/copper wire, brown midge, red large 12-14 patterns are working the best. around the 11-16 ft depth.

Dry Fly Patterns: Adult Midge Some Large(red) ones and Calibaetis Patterns to rising fish.

Attractor Patterns:

Streamer: Large brown or Black leech patterns or hornbergs with a Hares Ear dropper. Damsel fly Patterns.
