Privacy Policy

We will keep your personal information secure and safe and undetake not to provide it to third parties unless it is necessary for us to do so to perform our service to you, e.g. if you enquire about a product and we need to send your details to the seller in order for them to respond. We use email addresses for functional purposes only, such as to send registration information, receipts, reminders, etc, for offers related to the nature of our website and for services which you have opted into. If we conduct market research for third parties, we only provide aggregate results to such parties and promise never to release any personal details about users or individual responses unless we are compelled to by law.
 
We use third-party advertising companies to serve ads when you visit our website. These companies may use aggregated information (not including your name, address, email address or telephone number) about your visits to this and other Web sites in order to provide advertisements about goods and services of interest to you. If you would like more information about this practice and to know your choices about not having this information used by these companies, please see: http://www.networkadvertising.org/managing/opt_out.asp
 
Here is a list of cookies currently used on the MummyPages website:
 
PHPSESSID – cookie which identifies user session. It allows user to provide relevant content by his browsing experience. Also, it is required for login. Disabling it will make some functions not to work properly, and login will not work.

home_tab_active – cookie, which remembers, which tab in title page (tools, popular baby names, etc.) user has clicked. Next time user will come to title page, last viewed tab will be shown. Disabling it will always display default tab.

user_info – cookie, which stores unique identifier for user. It is required to ensure security of logged-in user, and to allow automatic login next time user visits website (if user wants to do so). Cookie is unique for user’s computer, so malicious users will not be able to simulate . Disabling it will make login impossible.

remember_me – cookie, which remembers if user has requested automatic login next time he visits website. Disabling it will make automatic login impossible.

already_show – flashbox cookie, stores if flashbox was already shown.

mummypages_count – required for flashbox
