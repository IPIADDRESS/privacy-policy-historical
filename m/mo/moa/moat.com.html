At Moat, we are committed to protecting the privacy of your personal information. It’s yours, it matters to you and we get that. This Privacy Policy is subject to the Terms of Service posted on the Sites. By using our Sites or Services, you expressly consent to Moat’s collection, storage, use and disclosure of your information as described in this Privacy Policy. Please note that if you disagree with anything in this Privacy Policy, you may not use the Sites or the Services.

Information Collected From Our Sites and Services

  1. Automatic Collection: Like most web businesses, we use automated mechanisms such as cookies to keep track of users interactions with the Sites and the Services. This may include your Internet protocol address, referring website address, browser type and access times. We refer to this type of data as "anonymous" user information. A glossary that explains each of these italicized terms is included at the end of this Privacy Policy. We also use third party analytics such as Google Analytics to gather and analyze anonymous user information.
  2. Volunteered: If you open an account with us, purchase products or services, send us an email, or share personal information with us in any other way, we collect the information you share. This obviously depends on what information you choose to provide but it may include your name, email address or billing information. You do not have to share this information with us, but without it you may not be able to access certain content or features or use certain Services.
  3. Posted: If you post information on public areas of the Sites (or elsewhere on the Internet) that information may be collected and used by anyone, including Moat. (We strongly recommend that you do not post any information that allows strangers to identify or locate you. Posting such information may result in unsolicited and unwanted contact from others, to say the least.)

Client Services

We offer Services that help our clients understand how users interact with online advertising. In connection with online ads, we may use automated mechanisms to gather anonymous user information ourselves, we may analyze and share that information with our clients and their business partners, and we may facilitate our clients' direct gathering of such information. The short answer is that we generally use this information to help Moat provide you with better service and to help our advertisers and clients provide you with more relevant offers, which in turn helps cover the cost of providing our Sites and our Services and third party websites and services. Among other things, the information we collect can enable Moat to:

  • provide you with additional information we think is of interest to you

Glossary

  • Cookie: a cookie is essentially a reminder – it tells the cookie owner when a website or ad is being viewed by a user who has been there before. (Technically speaking, a cookie is a small electronic file containing a string of characters that a server places on your computer when you visit a site or view an ad.) When you visit the Sites or use our online products or services, Moat’s servers send a cookie to your computer. Cookies by themselves do not identify you personally. Unless you choose to identify yourself to us (eg, by opening an account), you will remain anonymous to us. Most web browsers accept cookies by default but you can change that setting (instructions should be provided within your browser itself). However, if you disable cookies, certain features of the Sites and Services may not work. Moat, its business partners, its clients and their business partners may use other technologies to collect anonymous user information in addition to cookies (eg, web beacons, tracking pixels and Flash technology) but we thought it would not be very useful to define them all here. You can find a great deal of information about these mechanisms online and if you really need help, ask us and we will point you to some good resources.
  • Internet protocol address: an individual "name" that is assigned to each computer on the Internet, so that each computer can be told apart.
  • Referring website address: the domain name or URL of the webpage you were visiting right before you came to the Sites.
  • Browser: is the software application on your computer that lets you "see" websites. Common browsers are Microsoft Internet Explorer, Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome, Opera and Apple Safari.
