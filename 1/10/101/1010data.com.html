Privacy Policy

Updated February 22, 2019.

This policy covers how 1010data, Inc. and its affiliates (“1010data”, “we”, “us” or “our”) treats data and information collected and received through www.1010data.com, www.1010reveal.com, and www.1010equities.com (“Site”), including its big data solution currently called 1010discover (“1010discover”) and the services provided by use of 1010discover (“Services”).

Scope of this Policy

This policy applies only to information collected and received by us through access and use of 1010discover and/or the Site and does not apply to any other information collected by us through other means. If you have other agreements with 1010data related to its other products and services, those agreements control with regard to the subject matter of those agreements. Further, this policy does not cover the activities of third parties.

It is your responsibility to read and understand this policy. By using 1010discover and/or visiting the Site, you are agreeing and consenting to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree to all of this policy and do not wish to be bound by it, you should not use 1010discover or visit the Site.

This policy may change from time to time so please check back here frequently.

What personal information we collect and receive

Personal information is generally understood to include information that can be used to identify, distinguish or trace a natural person’s identity, either alone or when combined with other information.  Personal information may include the name, address, zip code, e-mail address, telephone number(s), Internet Protocol (IP) address, and other information collected from or about a natural person or the device he/she uses, such as usage, location or contacts data or statistics and other aggregated information that can be linked back to a specific person. Personal information may also include more sensitive information such as driver’s license number, other government issued id, credit, debit, bank or payment card number or account information, other financial account information, and social security number (“Sensitive Information”). Other than to fulfil your payment obligations to us, we do not seek or collect Sensitive Information and advise you to use caution in choosing what information you upload to 1010discover, especially Sensitive Information.

The personal information we collect and receive may include the following:

  • Personal information you provide when you register with 1010discover (including for trial access), request information from 1010discover and when we perform Services for you.
    • This may include:  your name, industry, organization name, job, contact information, such as e-mail address, phone number, and shipping address, payment information, such as a credit or debit card number, and password for your account access.
  • Personal information that you upload into 1010discover.
  • Information related to transactions requested or completed through the Site.
  • Communications with us (whether in writing, by email, or by telephone).

 

Whenever you visit the Site or use 1010discover, we also receive and record information on our server logs from your browser, including your computer or mobile device's operating system type and version, browser type and language, your Internet Protocol (IP) address, and geographic areas derived from your IP address, 1010discover cookie information, file information, time stamped logs regarding access times and duration of visits, the websites you visited before coming to the Site, and other usage data relating to your activities on our Site, including the pages you request. We may relate this information to the personal information you provide for purposes described in this policy.

Certain third parties, including analytics companies, advertisers and ad networks, may also automatically collect information about you through our Sites, using cookies, Web beacons, device identifiers, and similar technologies, including personally identifiable information about your online activities over time and across different websites, devices, online channels and applications.

How we use Personal Information

We use personal information collected through our Site or through use of 1010discover for purposes described in this policy and the Terms of Use  you enter into with us prior to using 1010discover. For example, we may use your information to:

  • Provide and deliver you access to 1010discover after you’ve registered, paid for, or requested such access.
  • Provide software updates and communicate with you about 1010discover, related products services and updates;
  • Operate and improve our internal operations, systems, products, and services;

  • Link or combine information about you with other personal information we get from third parties, to help understand our users’ needs and provide our users with better and more personalized service;
  • Enforce our terms and conditions or protect our business, third party service providers, or users; and
  • Protect against, investigate, and deter fraudulent, unauthorized, or illegal activity.

 

We may also contact you about your registration details, to manage any account or subscription you have with us, or to provide customer service, even if you have opted-out of or declined to consent to receiving promotional emails.  We may also contact you to inform you of revisions to this policy.

We also may use location data, IP address or device identification number, and other information from and about devices, browsers, operating systems, manners of connecting to the internet, internet service providers or wireless carriers, or other information related to use of 1010discover, the Services or the Site in de-personalized form to improve our products and services and occasionally for other internal purposes.

How we disclose your information, including personal information

We do not rent, sell, or share the information that you upload to 1010discover or the Site with non-affiliated third-parties, except that information may be disclosed under the following circumstances:

  • When we have your permission including when you choose to share information on any public portions of the Site such as our online forums.

  • We may disclose personal information to comply with laws or in response to legal process, for example, in response to a court order or a subpoena. We also may disclose personal information  in response to a law enforcement agency's request, or where we believe it is necessary to investigate, verify, prevent, enforce compliance with, or take action regarding: illegal or suspected illegal activities; suspected fraud; situations involving potential threats to the physical safety of any person; protection of the rights and property of 1010data, 1010discover, our agents, customers, or others; or violations or suspected violations of our agreements and policies or as otherwise required or permitted by law or consistent with legal requirements.

How we secure and store your information

We have put in place reasonable and appropriate physical, electronic, and organizational procedures to help safeguard information collected or received through 1010discover and the Site and to protect your information from unauthorized or unlawful use, as well as the accidental loss, destruction or damage while under our control.

We are a global company with affiliates, varied business processes, management structures and technical systems that cross borders. Information collected by 1010discover and on the Site, may be stored on your computers, on your mobile devices, or on servers controlled by us, and this information may be transferred to, accessed from, or stored and processed in, the United States or other countries which may not have data privacy laws that provide protections equivalent to those provided in your home country. However, we will protect all personal information we obtain in accordance with this policy and take reasonable steps to ensure that it is treated lawfully. We will only retain personal information for as long as needed to fulfill legitimate business purposes, including the purposes outlined in this policy, or for a period of time specifically required or allowed by applicable regulations or laws. Note that upon termination of your 1010discover account, we will maintain the information uploaded to 1010discover for six (6) months, to enable a prompt and efficient re-engagement of your account, should you decide to do so.

While we take reasonable steps described above to secure your information, because no data transmission is completely secure, and no system of physical or electronic security is impenetrable, we cannot guarantee the security of the information that you send to us or the security of our servers, networks or databases, and by using 1010discover and/or our Site you agree to assume all risk in connection with your personal information and the information you upload thereto. We are not responsible for any loss of such information. To help protect your personal information and the information you upload to 1010discover, please use a strong password, do not use the same passwords to access your 1010discover accounts that you use with other accounts or services, and protect your user names and passwords to help prevent others from accessing your accounts and services.

In the unlikely event that we believe the security of your information in our possession or control may have been compromised, we may seek to notify you. If notification is appropriate, we may notify you by e-mail, push notification, or otherwise.

In addition, should you wish to stop receiving marketing communications directly from us, you will be able to unsubscribe or opt-out, or you can contact us as described at the end of this policy and we will make the changes for you.  Please remember to include your full name and email address in order that we may make the changes that you request. Cookies and “do not track”

“Cookies” are pieces of information that an online service transfers to your hard drive to store and sometimes track information about your device. “First Party Cookies” are cookies that we set to collect information through the Site or within 1010discover for our use. “Third Party Cookies” are cookies that are set by third parties to collect information through the Site on our behalf; such third parties may collect information for their own purposes too.  We do not necessarily control or influence the specific types of information that third parties may collect through the Site.

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but if you prefer, you can change your browser to prevent that. You are not obliged to accept cookies and you can adjust your browser’s setting to prevent it from accepting cookies on your device – go to www.allaboutcookies.org for instructions on how to disable cookies. However, you may not be able to take full advantage of 1010discover or the Site if you do so.

First Party Cookies

  • We may collect information about your device using first party cookies, including, where available, your IP address, operating system and browser type for system administration.
  • 1010discover and the Site may also use first party cookies, for example, to:
    • Gather customer journey information to fulfil your requests;
    • Provide privacy protection when you visit 1010discover and the Site;
    • Store details of your marketing preferences to improve and enhance your use of 1010discover and visits to the Site;
    • Evaluate our advertising and promotional effectiveness;

Third Party Cookies

Ad network providers, advertisers, sponsors, traffic measurement services, and other third parties may set cookies through the Platform and Site and access your device if you choose to have cookies enabled in your browser or they may otherwise have access to information about your device that does not directly identify you. These third party cookies are set to, among other things, help deliver advertisements that you might be interested in, to prevent you from seeing the same advertisements too many times and to conduct research regarding the usefulness of certain advertisements to you. Note that any images (or any other parts of a web page) served by third parties in association with third party cookies may serve as web beacons, which enable third parties to carry out the previously described activities. Third party cookies and web beacons are governed by each third party’s specific privacy policy, not this one. If you do not want the services that third party cookies provide, you may be able to opt-out by visiting http://youronlinechoices.eu, http://www.aboutads.info, and/or http://www.networkadvertising.org.

As discussed above, you may opt out of third party cookies at any time. However, we do not currently support any browser based Do Not Track (DNT) settings or participate in any DNT frameworks, and we do not assign any meaning to any potential DNT track signals you may send or alter any of our data collection or use practices in response to such signals.

California privacy rights

California’s “Shine the Light” law, Civil Code section 1798.83, requires certain businesses to respond to requests from California customers asking about the business’ practices related to disclosing personal information to third parties for the third parties’ direct marketing purposes. Alternately, such businesses may have in place a policy not to disclose personal information of customers to third parties for the third parties’ direct marketing purposes if the customer has exercised an option to opt-out of such information-sharing. We have such a policy in place. To make such a request, please contact us as described and the end of this policy.
