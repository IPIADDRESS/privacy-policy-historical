    Information Collection Disclosure

    Registration

    During registration you are required to give contact information such as first and last name, email address, university (if applicable), zip code, gender as well as a protected password. We ask that you provide demographic information (such as your university, gender and level) to us so we can provide you a more personalized experience on our site.

    Chegg also provides users with the option of entering school/class information which is used to help customers find the correct textbooks for their classes. School information is displayed in the public/private section, however your profile information will only be provided as authorized by you in the private section of Chegg.com. Once a user, you may provide additional information in the profile section describing your contact information, privacy filters and student information and billing and shipping address changes. Providing additional information beyond what is required at registration is entirely optional, but enables you to better identify yourself and helps Chegg find the textbooks you want.

Chegg will also send you informational emails regarding your order, returns, extensions, purchases, gift certificate purchases, coupon purchases, cancellations, website updates, and for any user inquiries or problems.     Cookies & Log Files

    Like most web sites, Chegg uses cookies and web log files to track site usage. A cookie is a tiny data file which resides on your computer which allows Chegg to recognize you as a user when you return to our site using the same computer and web browser. Unfortunately, if your browser settings do not allow cookies, you will not be able to use our website. Like the information you enter at registration or in your Profile, cookie and log file data is used to customize your experience on the web site.

    One type of cookie, known as a "persistent" cookie, is set once you've logged in to your Chegg account. The next time you visit the Chegg web site, the persistent cookie will allow us to recognize you as an existing user so you will not need to log in before using the service. Additionally, if you log out of the Chegg site you will need to re-enter your password the next time you visit in order to log in to your account. This cookie also functions as a "session" cookie, is used to identify a particular visit to the Chegg site. Session cookies sections expire after a short time or when you close your web browser.

    Due to the communications standards on the Internet, when you visit the Chegg web site we automatically receive the URL of the site from which you came and the site to which you are going when you leave Chegg. We also receive the Internet protocol (IP) address of your computer (or the proxy server you use to access the World Wide Web), your computer operating system and type of web browser you are using, as well as the name of your ISP. This information is used to analyze overall trends to help us improve the Chegg service. The linkage between your IP address and your personally identifiable information is never shared with third-parties without your permission or except when required by law.


    Web Beacons

    We employ as well as our affiliate program partner Commission Junction a software technology called clear gifs (a.k.a. Web Beacons/Web Bugs), that help us better manage content on our site by informing us what content is effective. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of Web users for affiliate program. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user.s computer hard drive, clear gifs are embedded invisibly on Web pages and are about the size of the period at the end of this sentence. We do not tie the information gathered by clear gifs to our customers. personally identifiable information. This information is used to help improve the overall quality and experience of our website. To learn more about our advertising company.s use of clear gifs, please go to www.cj.com


    Ad Networks

    We allow other companies, called ad networks, to serve advertisements to Chegg public or private users. Ad networks include third party ad servers, ad agencies, ad technology vendors and research firms. Chegg may "target" some ads to users that fit a certain general profile - for example, "engineering students at Stanford University." Chegg does NOT use personally identifiable information to target ads.

    To deliver these ads properly, Chegg may include a file, called a web beacon, from these ad networks within pages served by Chegg so the networks may provide anonymous, aggregated auditing, research and reporting for advertisers as well as to target ads to you while you visit other websites.

    Because your web browser must request these advertisements and web beacons from the ad network's servers, these companies can view, edit or set their own cookies, just as if you had requested a web page from their site. These general targets may also be used by ad networks to display targeted ads on other websites as a substitute for random, irrelevant ads. You will never see additional ads as a result of being a Chegg user and Chegg never provides personally identifiable information to any advertiser nor any third party.
