CAST Privacy Statement

CAST recognizes the importance of protecting your personal information and is committed to safeguarding your privacy online.

CAST has instituted policies intended to ensure that your personal information is handled in a safe and responsible manner. We also post this privacy policy so that you know what kind of information we collect, how it is handled, and with whom it may be shared.

As we continue to develop our Web site and take advantage of technologies to improve the service we provide, this policy may change. We therefore encourage you to refer to this policy on an ongoing basis so that you understand our current privacy policy.

By accessing or using the CAST Web site, you agree to the terms of this CAST Privacy Statement, as outlined below. If you do not agree to these terms, please do not access or use this site.

Collection of Personal Information
When you engage in certain optional activities on this site, such as downloading software or registering for seminars, CAST may ask you to provide your name, mailing address, e-mail address, and other personal information by filling out and submitting an online form.

The information is used to deliver better customer service in general, improve your experience on our Web site, and inform you of new product features and commercial offers.

When you provide personal information to CAST, you understand and agree that CAST and its subsidiaries and affiliates may transfer, store, and process your customer profile in any of the countries in which CAST and its affiliates maintain offices. The information provided by visitors is kept private and confidential. The information will not be sold to outside parties. It is stored on a secured server that is not accessible through the Interne.

Use of Cookies
When you visit the CAST Web site, you can surf the site anonymously and access important information without revealing your identity. We use "cookies" to make your visit more efficient. A cookie is small amount of data that is transferred to your browser by a Web server and can only be read by the server that gave it to you. It functions as your identification card, recording your preferences.

Most browsers are initially set to accept cookies. You can set your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie, giving you the chance to decide whether or not to accept it.

While CAST uses cookies to track your visit to the CAST Web site, this information does not identify you personally and you remain anonymous unless you have otherwise provided CAST with personal information.

Updating your Personal Information
Please contact us to modify the information previously provided to us by sending an email to webmaster@castsoftware.com with your updated contact information.

Contact CAST
If you have any questions about this privacy statement, you can contact CAST by email: webmaster@castsoftware.com or by mail CAST, 3 rue Marcel Allégot, 92190 Meudon France.
