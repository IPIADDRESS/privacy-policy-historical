Policy:

Any person upon application may receive any administrative information about the University held by the University, subject to the following conditions:

    • the information is not already available to the public in some other way;
    • the information provided does not include any material which is privileged, private to individual students or employees or confidential as defined by these regulations;
    • the applicant pays the required fee.

Purpose:

To provide for and regulate the access to administrative information held by the University and to protect the privacy of personal information held by the University.

Scope:

This policy is applies to all administrative information at Carleton University.

See the full policy below.

ACCESS TO INFORMATION

 

INTRODUCTION

 

 

I POLICY

Any person upon application may receive any administrative information about the University held by the University, subject to the following conditions:

    • the information is not already available to the public in some other way;
    • the information provided does not include any material which is privileged, private to individual students or employees or confidential as defined by these regulations;
    • the applicant pays the required fee.


II PRINCIPLES

This policy is an adaptation of the Province of Ontario's Freedom of Information legislation to Carleton Universityâ€™s operations so that the University will meet the public policy objectives of that legislation. The need for the adaptation is the product of the fact that the organizational form and function of government, the context for the design of the legislation, is fundamentally different from that of the University but the principles underlying the adaptation are the same. These principles are basically three in number. They are:

    • A democratic society requires an informed population and to achieve that condition information about what the government and the public institutions are doing and plan to do, needs to be known.
    • To achieve that end a broad access to information about the government and the public institutions needs to be created for the public. This broad access needs to be balanced by certain operational requirements of privacy, confidentiality and legal privilege.
    • To arbitrate conflicts between the requirements of broad access and these operational requirements of privacy, confidentiality, and legal privilege needs an independent referee and so independent commissioners are appointed to perform this function.

While the University is not a government agency and neither its structure nor its function mirrors the function or structure of government, it is an institution which is tax-supported and performs a public function. It has been the wish of successive governments that the Universities in this province adopt policies akin to the legislation so that information about the operations of the Universities will be known to their several communities.

 

REGULATIONS

 

I. DEFINITIONS

1. ADMINISTRATIVE INFORMATION

Is that information held by the University which describes the policy, plans and procedures of the University's operations and without limiting the generality of the foregoing includes:

(a) Agenda, briefing papers, minutes, appendices and correspondence of all committees and subcommittees of the University except where the University has designated the work of the committee or subcommittee, as the case may be, to be confidential;

(b) The records of all administrative offices of the Univeristy including those of the Departments, Institues, Schools and Faculties of the University save and except where the record or some part of it is classified as privileged, private or confidential pursuant to these regulations.


2. PRIVILEGED INFORMATION

Is that information or record which is subject to solicitor-client privilege or that has been prepared by or for counsel employed by or retained by the University for use in giving legal advice or in contemplation of, or for use in, litigation.


3. PRIVATE INFORMATION

Is personal information pertaining to an individual student or employee and is subject to the provisions included in collective agreements as may be amended from time to time by the parties thereto or to Section V (PROTECTION OF PRIVACY) of these regulations and includes without limiting the generality of the foregoing:

(a) information about individuals as patients obtained by the University's Health Services during their treatment of such patients;

(b) information related to the race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, or marital or family status of an individual;

(c) information relating to the educational, medical, psychiatric, psychological, criminal or employment history of the individual or about financial transaction involving the individual;

(d) any identifying number, symbol or other identifier assigned to the individual;

(e) the residence address and telephone number of the individual;

(f) the salary and employment benefits and beneficiary of an employee;

(g) the grades or marks of an individual student;

(h) the personal opinions or views of the individual except where they relate to another individual;

(i) correspondence received from an individual that is implicitly or explicitly confidential and replies to the correspondence that would reveal the contents of the original communications;

(j) the views or opinions of another individual about the individual;

(k) the individual's name where it appears with other personal information relating to the individual or where the disclosure of the name would reveal the personal information about the individual; or,

(l) the library circulation record of an individual.

4. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Is information which may not be disclosed for the following reasons:

(a) where disclosure could be reasonably expected to seriously threaten the safety or health of an individual;

(b) where disclosure could be reasonably expected to interfere with a law enforcement matter or disciplinary proceeding or conflict resolution process of some kind within the University;

(c) where disclosure of records or information would reveal information supplied in confidence implicitly or explicitly where the disclosure would result in any undue prejudice, loss or gain to any person, group of persons, community, organization or financial institution or agency or could result in similar information no longer being supplied to the University;

(d) where disclosure of records would undermine the effectiveness or fairness of an auditing procedure or of an examination testing procedure or other means of evaluation of student performance;

(e) where, consistent with the University's research guidelines, disclosure of information concerning the past, present or proposed research activities of members of the University community, would be contrary to the public interest or would undermine the professional or personal interests of the researchers involved in the project;

(f) where the records contain:

(i) trade secrets or financial, commercial, scientific or technical information that belongs to the University or one of its constituent units or members and has potential monetary value;

(ii) information where the disclosure could reasonably be expected to prejudice the economic or financial interest or the competitive position of the University;

(iii) positions, plans, procedures, criteria or instructions to be applied to any negotiations carried on by or on behalf of the University or one of its constituent units;

(iv) plans relating to the management of personnel or the administration of the University or one of its constituent units that have not yet been put into operation or made public;

(v) information including the proposed plans, policies or projects of the University or one of its constituent units where the disclosure could reasonably be expected to result in premature disclosure of a pending policy decision or in undue financial benefit or loss to a person.

(g) because it is opinions, advice or recommendations obtained, prepared or recorded, or consultations or deliberations that have taken place, in the course of or for the purposes of, the deliberative processes of the University where such disclosure would undermine the effectiveness of those processes;

(h) where disclosure could reasonably be expected to:

(i) prejudice the conduct of relations between the University and federal or provincial governmental authorities or other agencies providing funding to the University or any of its constituent units;

(ii) reveal information received in confidence from federal, provincial or foreign governmental authorities;

(iii) reveal information received in confidence from other universities, colleges and similar institutions and from organizations formed for the purpose of representing the interests of such organizations or various constituencies within them;

(i) where disclosure would reveal the substance of the deliberations of the Senior Management Committee or the Committee of Deans, or a subcommittee of one of these bodies or any committee which reports to the President through one of these bodies;

(j) where the information or record has been designated confidential by the Board of Governors or the Senate pursuant to their regulations.

5. RECORDS

Includes all non-published documents, regardless of their physical form or characteristics, created or received by Carleton University and its employees, in pursuance of and in connection with their administrative functions, and containing information on the organization, function, procedures, policies, and activities of the University or other information of past, present, or future value to the University.

6. ARCHIVAL RECORDS

Are those records possessing sufficient historical, administrative, or legal significance to warrant their being preserved permanently by the University Archives.

7. RECORDS MANAGEMENT

Is the application of systematic control of the recorded information that is required in the administration and operation of University activities. The services provided through a records management program include files management, the design and application of records retention and disposal schedules, disaster planning, vital records programs, and semi-active records storage.

II. OPERATING PRINCIPLES

1. GENERAL

(a) To achieve a condition of broad access to administrative information of the University some changes to practices and procedures of the University are needed. The first of these is the establishment of a uniform system for the management of this information in an organization which is highly decentralized and where each unit has its own filing system and method of keeping records. To make access to information practicable there will be a need for some uniformity of record management and to this end units will have to adjust their systems over time to fit within these rules.

(b) The fact that there have been no consistent or uniform systems in place over time means that even within units the filing systems reflect differences due to changing records retention policies. Because the University is in the process of setting up an archives there is a large number of records, documents and files which are no longer in the custody of the originating departments but held in some form of storage with a minimum of cataloguing. All reasonable attempts will be made to retrieve such documents, records or files if asked to do so but the University reserves the right to refuse access to such records, documents or files if the amount of time required to locate them, edit them for privileged, private or confidential information and reproduce them is excessive in terms of the available resources. Any decision to deny access to information in storage will be made by the University Officer and is subject to the complaint procedure described in these regulations.

(c) Access to information held in the Archives shall be subject to the rules of the Archives.

2. OPERATING PRINCIPLES TO BE USED IN THE APPLICATION OF THESE REGULATIONS

(a) These regulations are not intended to replace or restrict existing procedures and practices within the University community relating to access to information that is not private information about individuals.

(b) These regulations apply to all records within the custody and control of the University administration.

(c) The research and study notes, reports, manuscripts, publications and personal communications, whether in manuscript or electronic form, of individual faculty members, staff, and students are not subject to these regulations unless prepared under contract for the University or in the context of University administrative work.

(d) The right of access conferred by these regulations shall normally be implemented by making available, upon request, a photocopy of the record in question. The University may make access conditional upon the payment of an application fee and reasonable charges for the cost of photocopying the original copy and may charge, when retrieval is difficult, a fee for retrieval.

(e) Where a request for access pertains to a record containing material which is exempt from the general rules governing access together with material which is not exempt, the University shall make reasonable efforts to sever and disclose the non-exempt material.

(f) Where a written policy has been adopted under the authority of the University's governing statute, its explicit provisions shall in the event of conflict take precedence over these regulations.

(g) Financial information such as the operating budget, the Audited Financial Statements and other financial information which has been made available publicly for some time shall continue to be made available in

accordance with past practices and therefore is not subject to these regulations. Other financial information may be requested and its availability is subject to these regulations.

(h) Regulations governing the evaluation of students are available in the University Calendars and such regulations shall provide the conditions upon which students shall have access to information about their evaluation and such regulations shall as minimum require each instructor to provide written notice to each class before the last day for late registration of the elements that will contribute to the final grade and their weighting and this information and any amendments to it shall be retained by the instructor for a period of one year and shall be available from the instructor without charge upon application to any student registered in the class during the period of time the information is retained.

III. ORGANIZATION
  1. APPOINTMENT OF OFFICERS

For the purposes of this policy:

(a) in each of the Faculties, the Office of the President and the Offices of the Vice Presidents an officer shall be appointed as the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Officer for that Faculty or Office, hereinafter referred to as the 'Officer';

(b) The President shall appoint a University Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Officer, hereinafter referred to as the 'University Officer';

(c) the Board shall enact a bylaw creating the office of University Commissioner for Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy, setting the process of appointment, terms and conditions of the office and its duties and authority, hereinafter referred to as the 'Commissioner'.

 

2. DUTIES

(a) It shall be the responsibility of the Officer to:

(i) ensure that the units within his/her jurisdiction are complying with these regulations;

(ii) receive and process requests for information, determine the fee to be set, and rule in the first instance as to whether or not the information requested is accessible pursuant to these regulations;

(iii) receive requests to correct personal data held by any unit within the Officer's jurisdiction and determine in the first instance what action should be undertaken in response to such requests;

(iv) refer to the University Officer any matters requiring interpretation or appeal.

(b) The University Officer shall:

(i) oversee and ensure the adoption of record-keeping and disclosure practices consistent with these regulations;

(ii) receive requests for access to information or for the correction of personal data either upon appeal or upon referral from Officers who themselves have been requested to supply information or correct personal data in circumstances which raise a question concerning the proper application of these regulations;

(iii) make a decision concerning a request or appeal received under the paragraph above and communicate the decision in writing to the person making the request;

(iv) cooperate with the Commissioner in the investigation of complaints and pursuant to these regulations comply with such instructions as may be given by the Commissioner to resolve such complaints.

(c) The Commissioner shall:

(i) be appointed by the Board upon retainer and shall be a person experienced in the administration and adjudication of Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy legislation and shall not in any other way be employed by the University;

(ii) receive in writing complaints from an individual who is aggrieved by a decision of the University Officer with respect to a request for access to information or the correction of personal data or fees to be charged in association therewith, and investigate and report on such complaints;

(iii) have full authority to determine the manner in which a complaint shall be investigated and the procedures to be followed in any hearing, interview, or proceeding that the Commissioner may consider appropriate in order to effect a proper disposition of the complaint;

(iv) if, at the conclusion of an investigation, the Commissioner finds that the complaint has merit he/she shall so inform the President and the University Officer and shall recommend to the University Officer what action the Commissioner deems appropriate in response to the complaint; and

(v) if the President instructs the University Officer not to comply with the recommendation of the Commissioner, the President shall so inform the Commissioner and the Board of Governors in writing of the reasons for setting aside the Commissioner's recommendation.

(d) The Commissioner shall have the following additional responsibilities and powers:

(i) to review and make recommendations with respect to the amendment of these regulations and others which pertain to Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy;

(ii) where, in the opinion of the Commissioner, a collection of records containing personal information in the custody or under the control of the University is of such a nature that it creates significant risks of privacy invasion, to require the appropriate administrative officer or officers of the University to prepare a written policy statement concerning that collection pursuant to section V (Protection of Privacy);

(iii) to prepare and present to the governing bodies of the University an annual report concerning the implementation of these regulations.

IV. PROCEDURES AND FEES

1. APPLICATION FOR INFORMATION

(a) Any person may apply to the appropriate Officer for a copy of any administrative information held by units within the jurisdiction of the Officer. Upon receipt the Officer shall determine whether the information can be released and the time and cost of finding and reproducing it and shall so inform the applicant. If the request is for information which is privileged, private or confidential and it is not possible to sever such information from the public information requested the Officer shall so inform the applicant and the University Officer in writing giving reasons for the denial.

(b) In the case that the information is available it shall be reproduced and made available to the applicant upon the presentation of a receipt from the Business Office.

(c) In the case that the application has been denied and the applicant appeals the denial, the University Officer shall examine the reasons given by the Officer and within five working days of receipt of the appeal of the denial the University Officer shall confirm, amend or overturn that decision and shall so inform the applicant and the Officer in writing giving the reasons for the decision.

(d) In the case that the applicant is dissatisfied with the decision of the University Officer he/she may complain in writing to the Commissioner for a review of the decision.

2. PROCEDURE FOR COMPLAINTS

Upon the receipt of a complaint by an applicant the Commissioner shall determine what method of investigation is required to determine the facts of the conflict and shall give notice to the University Officer and the applicant of how the Commissioner plans to proceed. It shall be at the sole discretion of the Commissioner to determine what procedures shall be followed in any hearing, interview, or proceeding in order to effect a proper disposition of the complaint.

3. FEES

(a) The University may require the applicant to pay,

(i) a search charge for every hour of manual search required in excess of two hours to locate a record;

(ii) the costs of preparing the record for disclosure;

(iii) reproduction, computer and other costs incurred in locating retrieving, processing and copying a record;

(iv) shipping or mailing costs; and

(v) where the applicant is not a student of the University or a member of its faculty, staff or alumni, an application fee.

(b) Before any search is undertaken for which a preliminary estimate indicates that the retrieval and reproduction cost would be greater than $25.00 the applicant shall first be given an estimate of the cost of the search and if the applicant accepts in writing the estimate the University shall find and reproduce the information requested and the applicant shall be charged the actual cost of the search or the estimate, whichever is less. In the circumstances where the estimate is greater than $100.00 the University may require a deposit before commencing the search for the information.

(c) In the case of an individual asking to examine his/her student or employee file such request shall be governed by the rules flowing from the specific legal relationship the applicant has with the University and it will be necessary to provide the information in accordance with the regulations governing that relationship. In any event, in such cases there shall not be any fees levied for the retrieval of the information except in the case of a transcript where the prescribed fee shall apply and only such fees for copying the information shall be charged as are permitted by the contract which flows from the legal relationship the applicant has with the University. In the event that such contract is silent on the matter the standard page cost shall be charged for a copy of any information copied and provided to the applicant.

(d) The University Officer may waive the payment of all or any part of an amount required to be paid under these regulations where, in the opinion of the University Officer, it is fair and equitable to do so after considering:

(i) whether the payment will cause a financial hardship for the applicant;

(ii) whether dissemination of the record will benefit public health or safety; and/or

(iii) any other relevant consideration.

V. PROTECTION OF PRIVACY

1. Except where specific agreements have been reached in collective bargaining, the regulations below shall govern the collection, use, disclosure, retention and disposal, access and correction rights, and exemption from the access right of personal or private information.

2. The University shall collect and record only such personal information as is either reasonably necessary to the proper administration of the University and its academic and/or other programs or is required by virtue of data collection or reporting requirements lawfully imposed upon the University by the authority of the federal or provincial government.

3. The University shall not use personal information in its custody or under its control except:

(a) where the person to whom the information relates has identified that information in particular and has consented to its use;

(b) for the purpose for which it was obtained or compiled or for a consistent purpose;

(c) for statistical analyses; or

(d) in other circumstances, where the use of the information is necessary and proper in the discharge of the University's functions and responsibilities.

4. The University shall not disclose personal information in its custody or under its control except:

(a) under the access to information regulations;

(b) where the person to whom the information relates has identified that information in particular and has consented to its disclosure;

(c) for the purpose for which it was obtained or compiled or for a consistent purpose;

(d) to an officer or employee of the University who needs the record in the performance of his or her duty;

(e) for the purpose of complying with a requirement to provide information lawfully imposed upon the University by the authority of the provincial or federal government;

(f) where disclosure is to an institution or a law enforcement agency in Canada to aid in an investigation undertaken with a view to a law enforcement proceeding or from which a law enforcement proceeding is likely to result;

(g) where disclosure is necessary to aid in the investigation of allegations that individuals have made false statements or engaged in other misleading conduct concerning their attendance or performance or status within or completion of an academic program of the University;

(h) where disclosure is made to another educational institution or to a professional licensing authority or board of certification or similar institution, provided that the University has made reasonable efforts to inform affected individuals of the existence of a practice of making such disclosures;

(i) in compelling circumstances affecting the health or safety of an individual if upon disclosure notification thereof is mailed to the last known address of the individual to whom the information relates;

(j) in compassionate circumstances, to facilitate contact with the next of kin or a friend of an individual who is injured, ill or deceased; or

(k) to a person who has been authorized by the individual to whom the information relates to make an enquiry on that individual's behalf, or where that individual is incapacitated or deceased, has been authorized by the next of kin or legal representative of that individual.

5. The restrictions on disclosure set out in this section on Protection of Privacy do not apply to the fact that an individual is or is not employed by the University or is or is not registered in a particular academic unit, program or session or to the fact that an individual has successfully graduated or completed a program on a particular date or has or has not received particular academic or other University honour or distinctions.

6. The University shall take reasonable precautions to protect the security of records containing personal information, shall retain such information only for reasonable periods of time and shall make reasonable arrangements for the disposal or destruction of such records when that reasonable period has expired.

7. Every individual who is an employee or student of the University shall be granted access to records containing personal information concerning the individual with respect to which the individual is able to provide sufficiently specific information to render the record reasonably retrievable by the University.

8. Every individual who is given access under these regulations to information concerning her/himself is entitled to:

(a) request correction of the personal information where the individual believes there is an error or omission;

(b) require that a statement of disagreement be attached to the information reflecting any correction that was requested but not made.

9. The University may refuse to disclose to the individual to whom the information relates personal information:

(a) where the information is privileged, private or confidential as defined by these regulations;

(b) where disclosure would constitute an unjustified invasion of another individual's personal privacy;

(c) if it is evaluative or opinion material compiled solely:

(i) for the purpose of determining suitability, eligibility, or qualification for employment or for the awarding of a contract with the University;

(ii) for the purpose of determining eligibility or admission to an academic program of the University;

(iii) for the purpose of determining eligibility or suitability for the awarding of a scholarship, bursary or other form of financial assistance, or

(iv) for the purpose of peer review processes related to determinations concerning eligibility or suitability for the granting of tenure or promotion, research grants or other benefits to members of the faculty or for similar purposes;

where disclosure could reveal the identity of a source of furnished information in circumstance where it may reasonably have been assumed that the identity of the source would be held in confidence;

(d) that is medical information where disclosure could reasonably be expected to prejudice the mental and/or physical health of the individual; or

(e) that is a research or statistical record.
