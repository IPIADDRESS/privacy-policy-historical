Definition of “Personal Information”

“Personal information” means information about an identified or identifiable individual. Examples of personal information include your name, your email address, and your photograph.

Definition of “Author,” “Visitor” and “User”

When you use our Service to create a CaringBridge Site, you are a “Primary Author.” Primary Authors maintain (add, edit and delete content) their CaringBridge Site and can also permit other individuals to do the same. These individuals are “Co-Authors” and Co-Authors can assist Primary Authors in maintaining their CaringBridge Site, however only Primary Authors may deactivate their CaringBridge Site and add and remove Co-Authors. The Primary Author and any Co-Authors may be referred to collectively as “Authors.” All Authors are individually responsible for moderating the content of their CaringBridge Site as well as their own conduct with respect to the CaringBridge Site. Visitors do not have the ability to edit or erase their own comments. The term “User(s)” may be used to refer to an Author, a Visitor or both.

Definition of Submission

In this Privacy Policy, we refer to the text, photographs, images, illustrations, graphics, sound recordings, video and audio–video clips, links, and other materials Authors and Visitors post on a CaringBridge Site as “Submission(s).” All Submissions must comply with our Terms of Use.

What Personal Information Do We Collect Through Our Service?

Information You Volunteer. CaringBridge collects the personal information you and others knowingly and voluntarily provide when you use our Service, for example, the personal information you provide when you create, update or visit a CaringBridge Site, complete a survey, make a donation, or use our “tell a friend” feature.

Email Communications from the Site. We will occasionally send you information about products, services, newsletters and promotions on our Site. You can sign up for these emails from us on our registration page. Out of respect for your privacy, we present the option not to receive these types of communications. If you no longer wish to receive our newsletter and promotional communications, you may opt-out of receiving them by following the instructions included in each newsletter or communication or by emailing us at customercare@caringbridge.org. We offer an opportunity to opt-out of certain communications in your account, or you may contact us at customercare@caringbridge.org or CaringBridge 1715 Yankee Doodle Rd. Suite 301, Eagan, MN 55121 to opt-out.

Live Chat. We use live chat to assist you with any questions or customer care questions you may have. If our live chat is offline we will collect your name and email address so that we may reply to your request.

Credit Card Information. When you provide credit card information through our Service, the information you provide is never stored on our server. Instead, when you click on the “submit” button, it is forwarded immediately to a third–party service provider for processing. We use industry standard Secure Sockets Layering (SSL) software to encrypt all of your credit card information as it travels over the Internet to our third–party service provider. We do not retain a copy of your credit card information. Our service providers use your credit card information only to process your transaction (and for fraud prevention purposes) and are bound by contract to keep your credit card information confidential.

Information Sent to Us by Your Web Browser. We collect information that is sent to us automatically by your Web browser. This information typically includes your IP address, the identity of your Internet service provider, the name and version of your operating system, the name and version of your browser, the date and time of your visit, and the pages you visit. Please check your browser if you want to learn what information your browser sends or how to change your settings. Generally, we do not link the information provided by your browser to information that identifies you by name.

More About IP Addresses. An “IP address” is a unique number that is automatically assigned to your computer when you connect to the Internet. It is used to identify your computer’s “location” in cyberspace, so that the information you request can be delivered to you. If you use a dialup connection or a connection that assigns dynamic IP addresses, your computer will be assigned a new IP address each time you connect to the Internet. If, however, your computer is permanently connected to the Internet using a static IP address, the IP address assigned to your computer will generally be the same each time you use your computer.

IP addresses do not include your name, email address or other information that identifies you personally, but in some cases, they can be used to identify you. For example, if you have registered as an Author or a Visitor, we may link your IP address to account information that identifies you personally. We may also provide your IP address to our third–party service provider who can use it to identify your state and zip code. This gives us valuable demographic information about the individuals who use our Service. Our service provider is bound by contract to use the IP addresses we provide only for this purpose. In addition, if, for example, we suspect fraud or a threat to the security of our Service, we may share our server logs – which contain Visitors’ IP addresses — with the appropriate investigative authorities, who could use that information to trace and identify individuals.

Cookies and Similar Technologies. We use “cookies” and other Web technologies to collect information and support certain features of our Service. For example, we may use these technologies to

  • collect information about the ways Visitors use our Service — which pages they visit, which links they use, and how long they stay on each page;
  • support the features and functionality of our Service — for example, to save you the trouble of reentering information already in our database or to prompt the settings you established on previous visits; and
  • personalize your experience when you use our Service.

Generally, the information we collect using these Web technologies does not identify you personally. If, however, you have created a user identity, for example, by registering as an Author or a User, we may link the information we collect using these Web technologies to other information that identifies you personally.

If you do not wish to receive cookies, you may set your browser to reject cookies or to alert you when a cookie is placed on your computer. You may also delete our cookies as soon as you leave our Service. Although you are not required to accept our cookies when you visit our Service, if you set your browser to reject cookies, you will not be able to use all of the features and functionality of our Service.

How Do We Use Survey Information?

From time to time, we may post a survey on our Service or, if we have your contact information and your consent, we may send you a questionnaire or survey. Your participation in any survey is always voluntary. We compile statistical information from the responses to our surveys. These statistics do not identify you personally. How Do We Use Journal Entries, Guestbook Entries, Tribute Messages and Photographs?

CaringBridge Sites, by their very nature, contain a great deal of personal information about the Author of the CaringBridge Site, Visitors to the CaringBridge Site, and other individuals. CaringBridge Sites, including journal entries, guestbook entries, tribute messages and photographs, may be viewed by anyone who has access to the CaringBridge Site. For this reason, we offer password protection and let the Author(s) of the CaringBridge Site control the level of privacy that will apply to their CaringBridge Site. In general, CaringBridge does not use the personal information contained in journal entries, guestbook entries, tribute messages or photographs, except to provide our Service to you, your friends and your family.

We do not have the resources to monitor every CaringBridge Site and do not have the obligation to do so. We do, however, reserve the right to review the content of any CaringBridge Site, for example, if we receive a complaint about misuse of our Service or as part of a random spot check. We reserve the right to delete any journal entry, guestbook entry, tribute message or or photograph that violates our Terms of Use. We also reserve the right to shut down any CaringBridge Site that violates our Terms of Use. Finally, in some cases, we may take legal action against individuals who violate our Terms of Use.

What Steps does CaringBridge Take to Safeguard your Personal Information?

In General. We maintain reasonable administrative, physical and technological measures to protect the confidentiality and security of your personal information. For example, we use industry standard Secure Sockets Layering (SSL) encryption to protect your Author profile information, site creation information, donation information, and credit card information as it travels over the Internet. We restrict access to our data center and our databases, and we use a secure server dedicated to online donations to protect your personal and financial information. We also have firewall protection in place.

Unfortunately, no website, server or database is 100% secure. Therefore, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. For this reason, you should use care and discretion when you post Submissions on a CaringBridge Site and never post information that can be used for identity theft purposes, such as social security numbers, bank account numbers, or credit card numbers.

Privacy Settings

Our goal is to balance our Users’ need to find each other and communicate information and support against our Users’ desire for a measure of privacy.

We have found that

  • some of our Authors prefer a completely open CaringBridge Site that contains no restrictions regarding who may access it;
  • some wish simply to have all Visitors identify themselves,
  • some wish their CaringBridge Site to be open to most people, but, for any number of reasons, wish to exclude certain specific individuals (“Blocked Visitors”); and
  • some wish to limit access to their CaringBridge Site to family and close friends (“Approved Visitors”).

Because our Authors have different preferences and needs, we let the Author(s) of each CaringBridge Site choose from a menu of privacy settings.

Privacy Settings for CaringBridge Sites Created before June 1, 2005. On June 1, 2005, we upgraded our Service. CaringBridge Sites created on or after June 1, 2005 have different privacy settings than the CaringBridge Sites created before June 1, 2005.

The Author of a CaringBridge Site created on or before June 1, 2005 may choose from the following menu of privacy settings:

  • Level I. Visitors must know the web address (the “URL”) of the CaringBridge Site to view and
  • Level II. Visitors must know the web address (the “URL”) of the CaringBridge Site and the Visitor Username/Password to view and post to the CaringBridge site. The Author of the CaringBridge Site chooses the Visitor Username/Password. Registration is not required.

The privacy level and settings for a CaringBridge Site may be changed at any time by an Author.

Because of the private nature of a CaringBridge Site, CaringBridge does not post a directory of CaringBridge Sites.

Privacy Settings for CaringBridge Sites Created on or After June 1, 2005. The Author(s) of a CaringBridge Site created on or after June 1, 2005 may choose from the following menu of privacy settings:

  • Low:
    • Visitors must know the web address (“URL”) of the CaringBridge Site to visit it. In addition, if an Author chooses to create a Visitor Password, Visitors must know the Visitor Password to visit the CaringBridge Site.
    • Visitors will not be required to register before viewing the CaringBridge Site.
    • Visitors will, however, be required to register before they may post guestbook entries or tribute entries to the CaringBridge Site.
  • Medium:
    • Visitors must know the web address (“URL”) of the CaringBridge Site to visit it. In addition, if an Author chooses to create a Visitor Password, Visitors must know the Visitor Password to visit the CaringBridge Site.
    • Visitors will be required to register before viewing or posting entries to the CaringBridge Site.
    • The Author may create a “Blocked Visitor List” so that Visitors who are registered and on the Blocked Visitor List will not have access to the CaringBridge Site.
  • High:
    • Visitors must know the web address (“URL”) of the CaringBridge Site to visit the CaringBridge Site. In addition, if an Author chooses to create a Visitor Password, Visitors must know the Visitor Password to visit the CaringBridge Site.
    • Visitors will be required to register before viewing or posting entries to the CaringBridge Site.
    • The Author(s) may create an “Approved Visitor List” so that only those Visitors who are registered and on the Approved Visitor List may view or post entries to the CaringBridge Site.

Protecting Children Under the Age of Thirteen

Caring communities may include friends and family of all ages. Special care must be taken to protect children under the age of 13. For this reason, CaringBridge does not permit children to create or maintain their own CaringBridge Sites — or to be an Author — a parent or guardian must register as an Author and take responsibility for the CaringBridge Site. We also do not permit children under the age of 13 to register as Visitors. Instead, their parent or guardian must register as the Visitor, so that the parent or guardian can supervise their child’s activities as necessary. If you believe that a child has posted Submissions on our Service without the consent and supervision of a parent or guardian, please contact us at contentadmin@caringbridge.org so that we can take appropriate action.
