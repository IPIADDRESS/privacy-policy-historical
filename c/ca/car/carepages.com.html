3. Managers

A. Patient Consent Required.

If you are creating a CarePages webpage (“CarePage”) on someone else’s behalf, you must first receive the consent of the patient and provide proof of the patient’s consent if we request it.

B. Levels of Access.

When you create a CarePage, you will select the level of access. There are three levels of access: Community Access, Friends & Family Access and Invitation Only. Community Access will be the default setting.

(1) Invitation Only CarePage: A CarePage with Invitation Only access requires the website Manager to invite and approve all visitors before they can access that CarePage. Access is at the discretion of the CarePage Manager and all new visitors must be approved by the Manager. The Invitation Only access CarePage is not available via the Site search function.

(2) Friends & Family Access CarePage To join a Friends & Family Access CarePage, all registered users must know the exact name of the CarePage and enter it into the Site search function. Site users who are not signed into CarePages.com can find the CarePage listing by typing in the exact name of the CarePage in the Search function at the top of the page. Search results will list the CarePage name, patient name and the facility where the patient is receiving care. Only signed-in members can actually access the CarePage and must have either received an invitation or know the exact CarePage name.

(3) Community Access CarePage Some Managers prefer to open their CarePage to a broader community of support by making their CarePage more accessible to other users of the Site. These Managers share the unique name of their CarePage publicly to invite a larger audience to view their information and provide support. With the Community Access option, a link to your CarePage may display as a search result when a user’s search criteria matches the health condition you indicated for your CarePage or the healthcare organization sponsoring the CarePages website where you created your CarePage. If Community Access is not enabled, members may informally open the access to their CarePage by sending invite emails to members of their extended community. Members who opt for a Community Access CarePage still maintain control over the Guestbook and can block and remove individuals. To protect the privacy of children and infants on the Web, we do not offer the Community Access option for CarePages that are created for children younger than 13 years of age.

Note some CarePages Sites are provided by healthcare sponsors (typically hospitals) (“Healthcare Sponsors”) that choose to offer only the Friends & Family Access and Invitation Only privacy options.

6. Cookies and Targeted Advertising

A. Cookies, Web Beacons and IP Address Information

  • "Cookies" are small pieces of information that a website places on your browser when you visit that website. We may use cookies to provide you with a more personal and interactive experience with the Sites and Services. For example, we may use cookies to control what advertisements you see, the sequence of advertisements and to make sure you don't see the same advertisement too many times. For contests or sweepstakes, we may also use cookies in order to track your progress and the number of entries in some promotions. For polls, we may use cookies to help ensure that an individual can't vote more than once on a particular question or issue. We do not store Personal Information in any cookies on your computer.
  • Cookies can be removed by following your Internet browser's directions within the help tab. In order to use certain Services offered through the Sites, your web browser must accept cookies. If you choose to disable cookies, some aspects of the Sites may not work properly, and you may not be able to access our Services
  • We may also use cookies, pixels, web beacons (which are usually small, transparent graphic images), operating system and device information and navigational data like Uniform Resource Locators (URL) to gather information regarding the date and time of your visit, the features and information for which you searched and viewed, the email you opened, or on which advertisements you clicked. This type of information is collected to make the Site and Services more useful to you and to tailor the experience with us to meet your special interests and needs
  • An "Internet protocol address" or "IP Address" is a number that is automatically assigned to your computer when you use the Internet. We, or our service providers, may use your IP Address when you access the Sites or Services or use other means to assist with delivering geographically targeted advertisements.

B. Targeted Advertising

Companies Everyday Health works with may use cookies for purposes including understanding Web usage patterns of users who see advertisements on the Sites, controlling the sequence of advertisements you see, making sure you don't see the same advertisement too many times, determining your interests and tailoring advertisements and promotions based on those interests. For example, if you read an article about a particular health condition on the Site, we may use cookies from a vendor to later serve you an advertisement for a product related to the viewed article. These third party vendors may connect information about pages you visit on our Site with information about pages you visit on other websites and show you advertising based on this combined information. The advertisement may appear when you are visiting a different section of this Site, another Site or any other website on the Internet if the website also has a relationship with our vendor. You can choose to limit Behavioral Advertising on the Sites by clicking here and opting-out of cookies placed by those vendors listed. After you opt-out, you will still see advertisements but the advertising may not be as relevant to your interests. If you change your computer, change your browser, or delete your cookies, you will need to renew your opt-out.

We adhere to the Self-Regulatory Principles for Online Behavioral Advertising of the Digital Advertising Alliance (for more information click here).
