  1. WHAT INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We may collect information to provide better services to all of our customers and end users. We may collect the following information:

  • Your name and job title

  • Demographic information, such as postcode, preferences and interests
  • Website usage data
  • Information relevant to client enquiries
  • Information pertaining to special offers and surveys
  • Email usage data
  • Customer Enquiries data
  • Webstore order data (in markets where we operate a webstore)

We collect information in the following ways:

Information you give us: Some services enable you to give us information directly. For example, a number of our services enable users to create accounts or profiles. In connection with these services, we may ask you to provide certain information about yourself to set up the account or profile.

Information we get from your use of our services: In addition to the information you provide us, we may collect information about the services that you use and how you use them. This information may be collected automatically through software on your device and other means. This information includes:

Device information: We may collect device specific information (such as your hardware model, operating system version, unique device identifiers, and mobile network information including phone number, IMEI number, IP Address).

Log information: When you use our services or view content, we may collect and store certain information in server logs. This includes: details of how you used our service, such as your search queries, any information stored in cookies.

Location information: When you use our services, we may collect, store and process anonymous information about your device’s location. Data can be collected by using various technologies to determine location, including IP address, GPS and other sensors that may, for example, provide Bullitt with information on nearby devices, Wi-Fi access points and mobile towers.

Unique application numbers: Certain services include a unique application number. This number and information about your installation (for example, the operating system type and application version number) may be sent to a server when you install or uninstall that service or when that service periodically contacts our servers, such as for automatic updates.

Local storage: Data may be collected and stored locally on your device (including personal information) using mechanisms such as browser web storage (including HTML 5) and application data caches.

Cookies and similar technologies: We use various technologies to collect and store information when you visit our Website for any service, and this may include using cookies or similar technologies to identify your browser or device.

Information we receive from other sources: We may receive information about you if you use any of the other services we provide. We are working closely with third parties (including, for example, business partners, payment and delivery services, advertising networks, search information providers) and may receive information about you from them.

Email information:  when you register to receive marketing communications emails about news, offers and updates we will collect and store information about marketing permission, your email preferences and email usage including open and click activity.   Your email preference information can be viewed by looking at MyPreferences which is in the footer of each marketing communications email.

Webstore order data:  Your previous transactional history, including purchase data, voucher codes used, and shipping delivery information.  For more information on Webstore visit Store Terms of Service

Customer Service data:  Customer service ticket history, information about open Customer Support tickets, device information and contact preferences.

Any information we receive will only be provided if you have agreed with third parties that your data can be shared.

  1. Cookies and how we use them

What are Cookies

Cookies are small data files sent by the Website server to a web browser, processor memory or hard drive of your computer or mobile device and stored there. They can be used for a range of different purposes, such as customising a website, helping you navigate a website, improving your website experience, and storing that user’s preferences and login information.

How we use Cookies

We may use cookies to:

  • Analyse our web traffic using analytics package. Aggregated usage data helps us improve the Website, structure, design, content and function.
  • Identify whether you are signed in to our Website. A cookie allows us to check whether you are signed in to the Website.
  • Test content on our website. To ensure our users have a consistent experience on our Website.
  • Store information about your preferences. The Website can then present you with information you will find more relevant and interesting.
  • To recognise when you return to our Website. We may show you relevant content, or provide functionality you used previously.

Cookies do not provide us with access to your computer or any information about you, other than that which you choose to share with us.

Controlling Cookies

You can use your web browser’s cookie setting to determine how our Website uses cookies. You can configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is sent. If you do not want our website to store cookies on your computer or device, you should set your web browser to refuse cookies. Each browser is different, so check the “Help” menu of your browser to learn how to change your cookie preferences. The operating system of your device contains additional controls for cookies.

Please note, however, that some services may be designed to work using cookies and that disabling cookies may affect your ability to use those services, or certain parts of them.

For detailed information on the cookies that we use, please see our Cookie Policy at  https://www.catphones.com/legal/cookie-policy/

You have certain rights in relation to personal information we hold about you. Details of these rights and how to exercise them are set out below. We will require evidence of your identity before we are able to act on your request.

In certain circumstances, you have the right to object to our processing of your Information by contacting us at the address or email address set out above. For example, if we are processing your Information on the basis of our legitimate interests and there are no compelling legitimate grounds for our processing which override your rights and interests. You also have the right to object to use of your Information for direct marketing purposes.

You may also have the right to restrict our use of your Information, such as in circumstances where you have challenged the accuracy of the Information and during the period where we are verifying its accuracy.

  1. e) Right of Data Portability. In certain instances, you have a right to receive any Information that we hold about you in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format. You can ask us to transmit that Information to you or directly to a third party organisation.
