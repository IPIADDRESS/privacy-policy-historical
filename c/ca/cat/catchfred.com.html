Roughhousing. 1

  • Roughhousing.

    Roughhousing is part of the fun of being a dog. Playing is a way for dogs to explore their world and to socialize with other animals and people. It is completely normal, safe, and healthy in most cases, but it can become dangerous if it goes too far. Dogs may play-bite, lunge, swipe, and even bark at you or other dogs during play, but it will normally be done in a gentle, friendly manner. However, playful activities can take a turn for the worse if the dog begins to bite or play in a way that harms people or other animals. Here are some tips about how to prevent rough play.

    Discourage Rough Behavior

    Sometimes people can influence dogs to behave in a rough manner, especially if they themselves are using their hands, arms, or legs while playing with the dog. It is recommended that you behave in a gentle manner with your dog and use toys to play with them instead of your body. Some of this behavior is learned from other dogs that play roughly in general or from dogs that are larger than your dog. If you notice that this behavior may be influenced by another person or dog, then cease the activity between the two parties.

    To prevent a puppy from growing up to be a rough-playing or aggressive dog, you should never play "tug-of-war" or other dominance type games with them — unless you are prepared to win every time. Also, always make it clear that you own all of the toys, and you decide when and which ones your puppy can play with, only keeping one or two toys out at a time. If a dog has too many toys, it may begin to hoard them in a safe place in order to feel more powerful.

    Let the Dog Cool Off

    Playing can often make dogs feel slightly stressed, especially after a rough encounter. One of the best things to do is let the dog cool off with a brief time out of at least five to ten minutes. A firm command to sit or lie down in their bed will cause them to relieve some of the tension and regroup.

Puppy Mills. 2

  • Puppy Mills.

    Essential Facts About Puppy Mills

    If you are a regular catchFred reader, you are likely very concerned about the well-being of your pets. If you are planning to acquire a new puppy or dog, you may be wondering how you go about finding a reputable establishment from where a healthy animal can be purchased. You have probably also heard the term "backyard breeder" or "puppy mill" at some point; however, you may not be exactly sure what such phrases mean. A puppy mill is a term that typically refers to those who breed dogs for a profit, but do not always put the health and welfare of the animals in first place. Rather, money is often the primary focus, with the well-being of the dogs an afterthought. Although not all puppy mill owners mistreat or neglect their animals, the latter do not usually receive the high-quality care that serious breeders give to their dogs. Organizations such as the American Kennel Club state that licensed, responsible breeders focus on producing dogs that are in good physical condition and well-adjusted emotionally. They place significant emphasis on the animals' health and nutrition and ensure that they schedule regular vet visits for checkups and vaccinations.

    How to Identify a Puppy Mill

    Some of the warning signs that indicate an establishment may be a puppy mill include multiple breeds of dogs for sale and large quantities of puppies on the premises. Disreputable puppy farms typically separate the pups from their mothers at a young age and place them in small cages where they are ignored by the owners of the mill. This type of conditioning can lead to psychological problems and health issues in the puppies. Backyard breeders also commonly allow their dogs to mate at too young of an age. Additionally, breeders who produce registration papers from online companies that are not recognized by the American Dog Breeders Association or the A.K.C. are usually not reputable.

Exercising 3

  • Exercising

    Jesse Maria / Editor- catchFred

    Make Losing Weight Fun by Exercising With Your Dog

    The cause of obesity typically stems from the consumption of too many calories, a lack of physical activity, or both. If you are a catchFred reader who wants to lose weight and maintain your dog's health, or help him or her to shed pounds as well, consider exercising as a team. Most dogs enjoy physical activity, making it easy to get into a daily routine. The latter is important, as consistency is key with any exercise program. Therefore, a partnership of this type is a win-win situation for both you and your puppy or dog.

    A Worthwhile Endeavor

    The dangers of obesity are well known to most people in the developed world. Accumulation excess fat puts you at a higher risk for diabetes, heart disease, osteoarthritis and decreased longevity. Therefore, pursuing daily exercise is a worthwhile endeavor. Many experts recommend something called the "buddy system" for dieters. This is because it is a proven fact that exercising with a partner increases your chance for success. Most animal lovers would agree that their dogs make perfect companions: they are nonjudgmental, always eager, and rarely in a negative frame of mind. Enjoying your exercise routine plays a major role in the success of your weight loss plan. For this reason, consider making your dog your primary companion for this activity.

    Nutritional Considerations

    It is also important to consider nutrition when planning an exercise program with your dog. This is because all dogs should have adequate nourishment that will sustain their activities. If you are unsure about your pet's calorie requirements, speak to the vet in charge of his or her primary care in order to clarify this aspect before beginning your dual exercise regimen.

Breeding 4

  • Breeding

    Humans have maintained populations of useful animals around their places of habitat since pre-historic times. They have intentionally fed dogs considered useful, while neglecting or killing others, thereby establishing a relationship between humans and certain types of dog over thousands of years. Over these millennia, domesticated dogs have developed into distinct types, or groups, such as livestock guardian dogs, hunting dogs, and sighthounds.

    To maintain these distinctions, humans have intentionally mated dogs with certain characteristics to encourage those characteristics in the offspring. Through this process, hundreds of dog breeds have been developed. Initially, the ownership of working and, later, purebred dogs, was a privilege of the wealthy. Nowadays, many people can afford to buy a dog. Some breeders chose to breed purebred dogs, while some prefer the birth of a litter of puppies to a dog registry, such as kennel club to record it in stud books such as those kept by the AKC (American Kennel Club).

    Such registries maintain records of dogs' lineage and are usually affiliated with kennel clubs. Maintaining correct data is important for purebred dog breeding. Access to records allows a breeder to analyze the pedigrees and anticipate traits and behaviors. Requirements for the breeding of registered purebreds vary between breeds, countries, kennel clubs and registries.

    Breeders have to abide the rules of the specific organization to participate in its breed maintenance and development programs. The rules may apply to the health of the dogs, such as joint x-rays, hip certifications, and eye examinations; to working qualities, such as passing a special test or achieving at a trial; to general conformation, such as evaluation of a dog by a breed expert. However, many registries, particularly those in North America, are not policing agencies that exclude dogs of poor quality or health. Their main function is simply to register puppies born of parents who are themselves registered.

Caring for a Dog. 5

  • Caring for a Dog.

    Jesse Maria / Editor- catchFred

    Your Dog's Coat and the Role it Plays in General Health

    If you have recently acquired a puppy you will quickly discover that you have a lot to learn about caring for a dog. You may have already visited Catchfred to obtain a variety of helpful tips. This is a good place to start, as you must be concerned with many issues, including the puppy's overall health, keeping him or her fit, and making sure that the animal adjusts to his or her new world with as little fear and discomfort as possible. It is also important to give thought to your dog's coat between regular visits to your vet and groomer.

    The Role of a Dogs' Coat Regarding Health

    It is essential to make sure your pet's coat remains healthy. Bathing your dog should not be considered a task that is done simply for the sake of appearance. This is because the animal's overall health can be evaluated by observing its coat. For example, a dry flaky coat may indicate a skin problem or a nutritional deficiency. Similarly, an oily coat on certain dog breeds is a sign of an imbalanced diet. However, unless the puppy's coat is well cared for, these signs may be masked, making it challenging for you to make the aforementioned observations. For this reason, it is important to use the best shampoo when you bathe your puppy or dog.

    Choosing a Quality Shampoo

    At present, there is a broad range of shampoos for dogs and puppies on today's market, and each company states that they offer the best product. However, as most consumers are aware of, there are always one or two brands that are superior to the rest. Selecting the one that is best for your pet essentially depends on the needs of your puppy and the desired results for which you are looking. This choice must be made wisely, as it will affect your puppy's skin, fur and overall well-being. Selecting the wrong type of pet shampoo or choosing a brand just because it is inexpensive could result in skin irritation or damage to the dog's coat.
