Privacy Policy

Our Commitment to Privacy
Catchafire is committed to protecting your privacy and will make reasonable efforts to provide you a safe and secure user experience. We aim to make our privacy policy easy to read so you that you understand our online data collection practices and usage policies for www.catchafire.org (the "Site" and any "Services"). Please note that your use of the Site and any of our Services is subject to our Terms of Use which you may access at the bottom of our homepage. Our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service are integral to your use of our Site, therefore you must agree to both agreements before using our Site or any of our Services

Scope of the Privacy Policy
The Privacy Policy applies to all information you provide to Catchafire through the Site. If you have submit information to any third parties through our Site and Service, we cannot be responsible for any information collected by those third parties.

What This Means: This Privacy Policy deals with our information collection practices.

What Information We Collect
We generally collect two types of information—personally identifiable information ("PII") and non-personally identifiable information ("Non-PII"). PII is any information that can be used to specifically identify you as an individual. For example, you may submit and we may collect your name, email address, address, phone number, or similar information through the Site. Non-PII consists of information that does not specifically identify you. The following section describes how we collect and use your PII and Non-PII.

What This Means: We collect both personally identifiable and non-personally identifiable information.

How We Collect and Use Information

Cookies and Beacons
Like all websites, we use cookies and other technologies, including web beacons, so that we can enhance your user experience. Web beacons and cookies collect information such as URLs you came from and go to, your browser and OS information, your IP address, your mobile provider, your mobile device, and your ISP. None of the information collected by our cookies or web beacons is PII.

Cookies are bits of data placed on your hard drive when you visit websites. A cookie file contains information that can identify you anonymously and maintain your account's privacy. We use cookies to maintain your identity between sessions so that the Site can be personalized based on your preferences or history. We may also use cookies to track information about which pages you visit within the Site to help us gauge the effectiveness of the Site or our content. We may also store other information such as recent searches in your session. We use cookies to tell us, for example, whether you have visited us before or if you are a new visitor, and to help us understand what areas of the Site are of greatest interest to you. Cookies also may enhance your online experience by saving your preferences and password so you do not have to re-enter this information each time you visit the Site.

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can configure your browser to stop accepting new cookies, to notify you when you a new cookie is received, and to disable existing cookies. If you choose to disable cookies in your browser, however, you may be unable to take full advantage of all the Site’s features.

Log Files
When you are using the Site, we automatically gather basic information from your browser, such as IP address, browser type, ISP, OS, and date/time stamps to analyze trends, administer the site, analyze bugs, and gather broad demographic information. We will not share log files externally.

Information About You
When you register with Catchafire, you may provide a variety of information to help us match you with the best possible volunteer or volunteer experience. We may display some of this information on our site publicly (such as name, location, and profile) and this information can be viewed by all registered users of the Site. We will not display any of your contact information, professional experience, skills, cause interests, or volunteer biography with the general public. However, this information may be shared either with volunteers or organizations looking for volunteers as this is the most reliable way to ensure that volunteers and organizations will be properly match.

Information From Other Web Sites
When associating your Catchafire account with other Internet services, we will collect any information that you make available from your accounts on those websites. We collect this information to help us improve our ability to match you with a volunteer or volunteer opportunity and to improve your overall user experience on the Site.

Email/Telephone
When you register for our services we require an email address that may be used for communications. Email messages may contain code that helps our systems track your usage of these emails, including what links were clicked. If you would rather not receive promotional email from us, please see the "Choice" section below. We reserve the right to send you email regarding service announcements, administrative messages, and Privacy Policy or Terms of Use changes.

We may also contact you by telephone solely in connection with Catchafire services. If you would rather not receive phone calls from us, please remove your phone number from your profile.

What This Means: We collect information through your use of the Site, by email, through cookies, and log files, among other things.

Disclosure of Your Information to Others

Opt-In Requirement
Without your affirmative consent, we will not sell, rent, or otherwise share your PII with other third parties, unless required as described under the Required Disclosures section below. In the case of posting a volunteer opportunity and accepting a volunteering match, we regard this as affirmative consent and will disclose your PII with the matched party.

However, we may use third party suppliers and service providers to facilitate our services, and they may have access to your PII. For example, we may outsource one or more aspects of our Site to a supplier or service provider who performs services according to our requirements. Although we endeavor to select only reputable companies who share our commitment to privacy and will restrict how these suppliers or service providers may use or disclose the PII to which they have access, we are in no way responsible for such third parties or their activities. Therefore, you agree to allow us to give your information to third party suppliers and service providers in order for us to provide service to you.

Due to factors beyond our control, we cannot fully ensure that your information will not be disclosed to third parties. For example, third parties may circumvent our security measures to unlawfully intercept or access your information.

Search Engines and Web Crawlers
Certain PII that you provide through the registration process (such as name, location, and profile image) may be accessible and made public through syndication programs, search engines, crawlers, and other similar programs.

Required Disclosures
We make every reasonable effort to preserve your privacy, but may need to disclose your PII when required by law or if we have a belief that such action is necessary to:

  1. enforce the Privacy Policy or the Terms of Use;
  2. comply with a judicial proceeding, court order, or legal process; or
  3. protect the rights, property, or personal safety of Catchafire, its members, or others.

You authorize us to disclose any information about you to law enforcement or government officials as we, in our sole discretion, believe is necessary or appropriate, in connection with any investigation of illegal activity or that may expose us to liability.

What This Means: We will never sell your information without your consent. However, some of your information may be disclosed through search engines, to third party contractors, or where required.

How to Review and Change Information

You may control the information you provide to us in various ways.

If you would like to change any of your information in our database, you can do so by logging onto your account and following the directions.

To cancel your account, send an email request to help@catchafire.org from the email address you provided us in your registration.

What This Means: You may change your information by accessing your online account.

Age Restrictions
Any person who provides information through the Site represents that they are 13 years of age or older. We do not knowingly collect PII from anyone under the age of 13. Our Site and Service are not designed for users under 13. In compliance with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act, we will purge any information we receive that we believe is from a user under the age 13 upon discovery of such information. If you are a parent or legal guardian of a child under age 13 who you believe has submitted personal information to the Site, please contact us immediately.

What This Means: In order to use Catchafire, you must be 18, or at a minimum 13 with a parent or guardian’s consent.

What This Means: Your cellphone number may be contacted to send you informational text messages from Catchafire.

Choice
You may use the Email Preferences features on the Site to opt-out of notifications, promotional emails and newsletters. You will also have the ability to opt-in in the event that we share your information with other third-party organizations for their marketing or promotional uses or various other programs or features. If you wish to be removed from any communications simply respond to the link provided in the email or email us at help@catchafire.org.

What This Means: You may opt-in or out of emails from us.

Contacting Us
If you have any questions or comments about the Privacy Policy, the practices of the Site, or your experiences with the Site, please contact us at help@catchafire.org.

What This Means: If you have questions contact us.

By using the Site or providing personal information to us, you agree that we can communicate with you electronically regarding security, privacy, and administrative issues relating to your use of the Site.

What This Means: We will attempt to protect your information; however, we cannot make a 100% guarantee that it will be secure.
