Clarifai Privacy Policy

Last Updated: May 21, 2018

The Privacy Shield program, which is administered by the International Trade Administration (ITA) within the U.S. Department of Commerce, enables U.S.-based organizations to join the Privacy Shield Framework in order to benefit from the adequacy determination. Clarifai has joined the Privacy Shield Framework governed by the Department of Commerce (via their website at https://www.privacyshield.gov) and is committed to the principles of notice, choice, accountability for onward transfer, security, data integrity and purpose limitation, access, recourse, enforcement and liability. When a participant’s privacy policy is available online, it must include a link to the Department of Commerce’s Privacy Shield website and a link to the website or complaint submission form of the independent recourse mechanisms that is available to investigate individual complaints. Clarifai has selected the ICDR/AAA as its independent recourse mechanism: http://info.adr.org/safeharbor. Finally, as a last resort and in limited situations, EU individuals may seek redress from the Privacy Shield Panel, a binding arbitration mechanism. For questions or complaints related to our privacy practices, please contact us at privacy@clarifai.com.

How We Collect and Use Information

The purpose of this section is to describe what data we collect, how we collect it and what we use it for. Simply put, we collect data that is publicly available on the internet, from our website, our SDKs, our APIs and our partners or customers to train our algorithms to be faster, smarter and more accurate. You are free to explore our websites without providing any information about yourself. However, if you sign up for the API or any other service, then we will require collection of account creation data, such as your name, email address, password, location and employer and we may collect other personal data as described herein. In addition, if you visit our website and allow tracking mechanisms (such as cookies), we may also collect IP address, browser, geographic information, length of visit and pageviews, referral source, adID and the like. By using our services, you may also send us images or videos with personal data, such as a person’s face, of your users or that may contain other metadata that meets the criteria of personal data.

Sharing Your Information

We use data, sometimes personal data, to train algorithms to recognize what is in images and videos, but there are lots of other things that go into building our products that we can’t do alone. Things like sending you email or figuring out the most popular models in our model gallery. We partner with other businesses to take care of that for us, such as Marketo or HubSpot. Sometimes that means sharing your data with them. However, we value the trust you place in us so we carefully screen our partners and ensure that they can only see personal data that is absolutely necessary for them to get the job done. If you no longer consent to sharing your data with our partners, please contact privacy@clarifai.com.

Information About Children

Clarifai does not knowingly collect or solicit any information from anyone under the age of 13 or knowingly allow such persons to register as users. The Services and content are not directed at children under the age of 13. In the event that we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under age 13 without verification of parental consent, we will delete that information as quickly as possible. If you believe that we might have any information from or about a child under 13, please contact us at privacy@clarifai.com.

Government Requests; Legal Process

Sometimes, we may be required to hand over information to the government or as part of a legal process (like a lawsuit or arbitration).  If we are permitted to do so, we will notify you of any such requirement before we comply. We intend to defend our customer’s’ rights against unlawful search and seizure.  However, we also refuse to support criminal activity.

What Happens if We Sell the Company?

We hope that if we sell the company, it will be to keep the Services running, in which case the buyer would need to assume all your information so they could seamlessly continue providing the Services to you. Sometimes things don’t work out and we have to sell the company in pieces. If that happens, our intent is to provide you with notice so that you can make your choice.
