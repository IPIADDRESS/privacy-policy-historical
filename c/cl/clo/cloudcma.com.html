W+R Studios Privacy Policy

General

This Privacy Policy describes the privacy practices of Woolley Robertson Group, Inc. a California Corporation d/b/a W+R Studios (“W+R Studios,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) with respect to the websites and web applications (the “Sites and Apps”) consisting of the following: Cloud CMA®, Cloud Streams®, Cloud MLX®, and Cloud Attract®, which comprise our Cloud Agent Suite® of products, and any other Internet Sites and Apps operated by us.  We are committed to protecting the privacy or our customers and their clients.  Please read the following information to understand what information we collect and how we use that information.

This Privacy Policy describes our collection, use, disclosure, and protection of personal information collected or provided through the Sites and Apps and how we use personal information in connection with our services offered through the Sites and Apps (“Services”).  “Personal information” is information that identifies an individual and is associated with a specific person.  Personal information does not include information that is anonymous, aggregated, or is no longer identifiable to a specific person.

We are committed to the protection of the privacy of personal information provided to us by our customers and their clients.

Our customers are almost always real estate agents who desire to use our services and subscribe to one or more of our Sites and Apps.  The Sites and Apps are also often involved the collection of personal information from clients of our customers with their consent.  See “Client Information” below.  Our use of the term “customer information” does not include personal information collected by customers from their respective clients.

  • When a customer registers to use our Services, basic registration information is required, such as name, address, telephone number, email address, user name, password, and other registration information.
  • To subscribe for our Services, customers are required to a credit card number, expiration date, and credit card security code.
  • We may collect information about customers’ interaction with our Sites and Apps such as computer browser type, pages visited, average time spent on our Sites and Apps, IP address, unique identifier of the device, operating system version, and app version.
  • We may request or receive other personal information such as feedback, questions, comments, suggestions, or ideas to provide customers with other benefits or to improve upon the Services.  In such instances, customers are given the opportunity to provide or decline that information.

We use customer personal information to:

We may share customer personal information with other third parties with consent or instructions to do so.

Use of Non-Personal Information

When using our services and offerings we may also collect non-personal information − data in a form that does not permit direct association with any specific individual.  Aggregated data is considered non-personal information for the purposes of this Privacy Policy.  If we do combine non-personal information with personal information the combined information will be treated as personal information for as long as it remains combined. 

We may collect, use, transfer, and disclose non-personal information for any purpose.  The following are some examples of non-personal information that we collect and how we may use it:

  • We may collect information such as language, zip code, area code, unique device identifier, location, time, and the time zone where our Sites and Apps is accessed or used so that we can better understand customer behavior and improve our offerings, programs, products, services, and advertising.
  • We also may collect information regarding customer activities from our other programs and offerings.  This information is aggregated and used to help us provide more useful information to our customers and to understand which parts of our programs, offerings, and services are of most interest. 

As our Sites and Apps are navigated, certain information may also be passively collected by us or a third party service provider.  This information includes Internet protocol addresses, browser types, domain names, access times, operating systems, mouse clicks, mouse movements, and scrolling activity.  We also use cookies and navigational data like Uniform Resource Locators (URL) to gather information regarding the date and time of uses or visits.  This type of information is collected to analyze the use of our offerings, programs, and services to make them more useful to customers and to provide customers with relevant offerings.

Protection of Information

We take the security of personal information seriously.  We use technical and administrative security measures such as but not limited to firewalls, encryption techniques, and authentication procedures, among others, to maintain the security of personal information.  However, no complete assurance can be made against all cyber attacks and other security breaches.  To safeguard against unauthorized access, customers are required to login to certain secure areas of our web Sites and Apps using a user name and a password.  Both user name and password are encrypted when sent over the Internet.

Cookies and Tracking

We may aggregate information through cookies, or alphanumeric identifiers that we transfer to a computer's hard drive through the web browser.  This allows us to collect and track information about users and users’ activities online at our websites over time and across third-party websites or online services including, but not limited to, a computer's network address and operating system, the Sites and Apps from which a user linked to our Sites and Apps, the time and date of the visit, how our offerings, programs, services are used and what offerings and services are used.  We may also link this information with other personal information about users and may use this linked information.  This helps us to recognize users, customize, or personalize user’s utilization of our Services and to store such information in between visits to our Sites and Apps.  It also allows us to aggregate demographic and statistical data and compilations of information that may or may not include personal information and provide this information to our business partners, third party vendors, and service providers.  By doing so, we hope to understand our customer’s needs and desires so that we can make new offerings to them or tailor existing offerings to fit their desires.

EU and Swiss Users

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), effective May 25, 2018, creates consistent data protection rules across Europe.  It applies to all companies that process personal data about individuals in the Europe and Switzerland, regardless of where the company is based.

Each of our customers is responsible for complying with all applicable privacy laws with respect to client personal information, including the GDPR.

As part of our Services, we operate as a data processor through our Sites and Apps on behalf of our customers. Where we act as a data processor on a customer’s behalf, we will rely on our customer's legal basis as data controller for our processing of this data.  Where we act as a data processor on the behalf of any European customers, we will ensure that we comply with the specific requirements for data processors.  We have updated our related terms of service to align with the GDPR.  Where we appoint parties to act as data processor on our behalf, we will ensure that we have appropriate terms in place to comply with our requirements under the GDPR and to safeguard personal data.
