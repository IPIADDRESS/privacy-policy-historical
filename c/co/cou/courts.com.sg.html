By placing your Request, you agree that we may store, process, disclose and use data collected from you for the purposes of processing your Request and delivering your Goods.

This Privacy Policy sets out what personal information we collect and how we use it. References in this Policy to COURTS Online include any website owned and operated by or on behalf of COURTS, including the COURTS Online Marketplace.

Without obtaining our customers' consent, COURTS (Singapore) Pte Ltd, will not tamper with customers' browsers or computers.

3   WHAT PERSONAL INFORMATION DOES COURTS COLLECT?


We may collect the following categories of personal information about you:

3.1  Personal information that you provide to us on COURTS Online. This includes any information you provide on COURTS Online (for example when you register as a user or purchase a product on COURTS Online (whether from COURTS or from a third party Seller). This personal information will include your name, your address, your contact information and your payment information (and other kinds of personal information). Sometimes when you provide personal information on COURTS Online, you can choose not to provide certain personal information but this might limit our ability to provide certain features to you. You will be informed what information is required and what information is optional.

3.2   Personal information automatically provided to us. We receive and store certain types of information whenever you visit COURTS Online. For example, like many websites, we use "cookies" and we obtain certain types of information when your web browser accesses COURTS Online or advertisements and other content served on COURTS Online, if applicable.

3.3   Personal information provided from your devices. If you download or use COURTS Online on a mobile device (this could be for example by using the COURTS Online app) and if have requested or consented to location services, we may receive information about your location and your mobile device, including a unique identifier for your device. Most mobile devices allow you to turn off location services. Please refer to the user manual (or other information provided by manufacturer or software provider) for more information about how to do this.

3.4   Personal information you provide to us via e-mail. We often receive a confirmation when you open e-mail from COURTS if your e-mail provider supports such capabilities. You may also provide us with personal information in the content of e-mails you send to us, as part of your day-to-day interactions with COURTS. You may choose to stop receiving or change the frequency of email, direct mail, phone and mobile marketing communications. You can update and correct your personal information anytime using My Account. Whenever we send you any information via enewletters, we will include instructions on how to unsubscribe and a one click link to do so.

3.5   Personal information from other sources. From time to time we may receive information about you from other sources, for example from other users of COURTS Online including from third party Sellers.

4.1     to handle orders (or to enable third party Sellers or providers of services to COURTS to do so);

4.3    to communicate with you about orders, products, services (or to enable third party Sellers to do so);

4.4    to respond to your requests (or to enable third party Sellers to do so);

4.5     to process payments;

4.8     to display content such as wish lists and customer reviews and recommend merchandise and services that might be of interest to you;

4.10    to prevent or detect fraud or abuses of COURTS Online and to enable third parties to carry out technical, logistical or other functions on our behalf.

5   WHAT ABOUT COOKIES?


We send a "cookie" to your web browser. A "cookie" is a small data text file stored in the hard drive of your computer (if it so permits), which can be retrieved to identify you to COURTS. We do this to: enable our systems to recognise your device and to provide features to you; remember the information that you have previously entered, enabling us to customise COURTS Online to your preferences; and improve navigation and to collect aggregate information. These cookies will not give us any other personal information about you.
Your Internet Service Provider allocates an IP address to your computer or device in order for it to access the Internet. The IP address is considered to be non-personal information and it may be dynamic (changing every time you connect to the Internet) or it may be static (unique to your computer). We collect your IP address. We do this to: diagnose server problems; and report aggregate information and determine the fastest route from your computer to COURTS Online.

6   DOES COURTS SHARE YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION WITH OTHER PEOPLE?

COURTS does not sell or lease your personal information to third parties.
We only use your information for the purposes set out in Section 4 of this Privacy Policy.
We may share your information with the following third parties, where necessary and always in compliance with this Privacy Policy and with applicable laws.
6.1     Third party Sellers. In order for the third party Sellers to provide their services to you when you purchase products or enquire about purchasing products from the third party Sellers.

6.2     Third party service providers. COURTS may need to share your information with certain third parties to perform services on our behalf. Examples include fulfilling orders, delivering packages, sending postal mail and e-mail, removing repetitive information from customer lists, analysing data, providing marketing assistance, providing search results and links (including paid listings and links), processing credit card payments and providing customer service.

6.3     Current or future COURTS entities or subsidiaries. This includes without limitation our business partners and financial institutions, from time to time. These other parts of the COURTS business may be involved in providing certain services to you or supporting the provision of these services.

6.4     Law enforcement agencies. We release account and other personal information when we believe release is appropriate to comply with the law; enforce or apply our rights; or protect the rights, property or safety of COURTS, our users or others including you.

UNLESS OTHERWISE AUTHORISED OR CONSENTED TO BY COURTS, THIRD PARTY SELLERS MUST NOT NOT USE ANY PERSONAL INFORMATION THAT IS ACCESSIBLE FROM COURTS ONLINE, EXCEPT TO ENTER INTO AND COMPLETE TRANSACTIONS CONDUCTED VIA COURTS ONLINE. We shall take reasonable measures to ensure the confidentiality and security of the personal information shared with these third parties and to protect it from loss, misuse, unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration or destruction.
It is important for you to protect against unauthorised access to your password and to your computer. Be sure to sign off when you finish using a shared computer.
