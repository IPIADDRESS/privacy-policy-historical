Cookies, Web Beacons and other Technologies. We collect anonymous information about you using small computer files that are stored on your computer or device, such as log files, cookies, java script, tags and other technologies that track your activities online, etc., such as persistent identifiers, web pixels, pixel tags, flash cookies, user data files, object storage or clear gifs (collectively, "Cookies"). We use cookies to monitor how the Sites are used and to help personalize your online experience. For example, when you register on our Sites, we may store information in a cookie on your computer about your interests and college preferences. The next time you visit one of our Sites from that computer, our servers use the cookie to recognize you. We can then use the information you provided when you registered combined with your activities on our Sites to provide you with services and assistance with your college decisions.

You can accept or decline cookies. Most browsers automatically accept cookies. To learn more about cookies, including how to refuse cookies on your computer, click these links:

  • For Microsoft Internet Explorer: http://www.microsoft.com/info/cookies.htm
  • For Mozilla Firefox: http://support.mozilla.com/en-US/kb/Options+window+-+Privacy+panel
  • Learn about cookies: http://www.allaboutcookies.org/cookies/
  • Learn about Flash cookies: http://www.macromedia.com/support/documentation/en/flashplayer/help/settings_manager.html

Web Beacons. Web beacons are small bits of code embedded in web pages or in emails. We may use web beacons to deliver or communicate with cookies, to count users who used a tool like Badges or visited a web page, and to understand usage patterns. We also may include web beacons in e-mails to learn if messages have been opened, acted on, or forwarded. You can disable the ability of web beacons to capture information in this manner by declining cookies as described above.
