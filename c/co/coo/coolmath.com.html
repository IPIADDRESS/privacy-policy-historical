Global Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 9, 2018

We value your trust and take your privacy and protection of user information seriously. Furthermore, we treat user information as private and confidential. This Privacy Policy describes how we treat user information collected via our website.

What Information We Collect and How We Use User Information

The information we collect will depend on how you use our website. 

We, or third party advertisers we partner with, may collect information about our visitors’ web browsers. This includes certain types of tracking tools like browser cookies and web beacons.

Tracking Information

When users access our website, we record general data about usage, including, but not limited to, IP addresses on a temporary basis, the name of the web pages from which users entered our Website, the pages users visit within our Website, and the amount of time users spend on each page of our Website. We use this tracking information to help administer and analyze our web traffic in order to make improvements to our site.

Cookies

“Cookies” are pieces of data sent from a Web server to a Web browser that helps the Web server to understand usage patterns on its Website.  We use cookies so that we can provide the user with a better experience. We use Google Analytics to help us analyze how visitors use the site.

For example, we will collect the following cookies:

  • first party cookies that are necessary for operating our website
  • first party cookies that tag certain user information (for example, distinguishing between whether the user is a first-time visitor or a returning visitor)
  • third party cookies that help our ad partners display relevant ads

At your discretion, you may configure your web browser not to accept cookies from our website. However, if you do not accept cookies from our website, you may experience decreased performance of the website.

To learn more about these tools and how you can control them, click here .

We will not retain user information  for longer than is necessary for the above purposes, or for legal or regulatory requirements.  When we destroy the information, we will use appropriate organizational and technical safeguards to prevent unauthorized parties from gaining access to the information during the process.

To be certain of your privacy rights, you can contact the appropriate agency in your country that is responsible for overseeing privacy rights of consumers. Certain local laws require us to maintain and report demographic information on the collective activities of our registered users. We may also be required to maintain user information user information for at least seven years in accordance with applicable local laws regarding recordkeeping, reporting and audits.

Furthermore, for data collected in the European Economic Area (EEA), data subjects have the right to:

  • ask for a copy of such information to be sent to a third party;

  • ask for processing of user information to be restricted if possible and required under GDPR;
  • object to processing of user information if possible and required under GDPR;
  • object to automated decision-making where applicable; and
  • contact a supervisory authority in the EEA to lodge a complaint regarding Coolmath’s processing of your personal data.
