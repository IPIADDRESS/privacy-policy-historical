CrowdContent.com is committed to protecting the privacy of visitors to our websites, as well as our members and partners. CrowdContent.com has established this Privacy Policy to explain what information we collect through these websites and how it is used.

In this policy, "CrowdContent.com" refers to staff, board members, cooperating attorneys, interns, volunteers, and consultants of CrowdContent.com, all of whom are bound by law or contract to keep information they receive as part of their work for CrowdContent.com confidential.

Information Gathered by CrowdContent.com Site
For visitors to our website, we may, but generally do not, log standard technical information, such as the numerical Internet protocol (IP) address of the computer you are using; the browser software you use and your operating system; and the Internet address of the website from which you linked directly to our site. Circumstances in which we temporarily log standard technical information include conducting site testing, diagnosis of technical problems, and defending against attacks on the site. We generally do log the date and time users access our site on an anonymous basis. For more information on CrowdContent.com's position on data logging, see the EFF's Best Practices for Online Service Providers.

In order to access the member functionality of CrowdContent.com we use a persistent cookie. We may also use session cookies from time to time. Session cookies expire when you close your browser. You can set your browser to refuse cookies, and use Tor if you wish to keep your IP number and other technical information anonymous.

In addition, CrowdContent.com collects and retains information you submit to us. It is up to you whether to submit information to us, and how much information to provide. If you choose to become a CrowdContent.com member, we ask for your name, email address, city, state/province, postal or zip code, country of residence, and phone number, and invite you to select a password. For online donors and shoppers, we also ask for your credit card number or Paypal login information. We also maintain records of our members' use of the site and may use this data on an anonymous basis periodically. If you use some of the online financial services offered by CrowdContent.com, you are asked to provide personal information, such as a shipping address, necessary to complete your transaction.

We may ask for additional personal information when you provide feedback or comments, or otherwise communicate with us.

From time to time, we may ask for personal information on other portions of the site, such as asking you to sign a petition, participate in a contest, or provide other user generated content to us.

CrowdContent.com's Use of Information
CrowdContent.com sees it as its mission to protect privacy, defend freedom, and protect your rights in the digital world. We are your friends. If you invite another person to join CrowdContent.com, we will ask for that person's name and online contact information. We use this information to contact and, if necessary, remind that person that he or she has been invited to join CrowdContent.com.

If you provide information for publication, such as writing a review or submitting other editorial content, we will publicly credit you for this work.

We may run surveys, contests, or similar activities through this site. Such information will be used for the purposes for which it was collected.

We look at technical information to diagnose problems with our server and to administer the CrowdContent.com site.

If you choose to provide information to us through our online registration, you may be asked to provide your personal information that may include your name, address, telephone number, e-mail address, and other identifying information. We may share the personal information which you provide with our third party processing agents, affiliates and advertisers.

We may use the personal information which you provide to send you information, either online or offline, via email, direct mail, SMS text messaging and telemarketing, regarding products or services offered by third-party advertisers. In addition, we reserve the right to rent, sell or share the personal information which you provide with unaffiliated third-parties; these third-parties may use this information for online and offline marketing purposes.

By signing up on our website and providing your personal information you are expressly consenting to receive communications from us and from anyone that we may provide your personal information. In addition, you expressly consent to be contacted in any manner including via email, direct mail, telephone or SMS text messaging, even if your information is listed on the US National Do Not Call registry or a similar registry. If you do not agree to these terms than do not use our site.

Disclosure of Your Information
While CrowdContent.com endeavors to provide the highest level of protection for your information, we may disclose personally identifiable information about you to third parties in limited circumstance, including: (1) with your consent; or (2) when we have a good faith belief it is required by law, such as pursuant to a subpoena or other judicial or administrative order.

If we make any substantive changes to our policies, we will place notice on CrowdContent.com and post notice of changes on this page.

CrowdContent.com employs industry standard security measures to protect the loss, misuse, and alteration of the information under our control. Although we make good faith efforts to store information collected by CrowdContent.com in a secure operating environment, we cannot guarantee complete security. Information collected by CrowdContent.com will be maintained for a length of time appropriate to our needs. However, we generally do not retain credit card information unless you choose to have us do so.

A Three Step Plan to Getting Out of Debt

Close

Looking for a way to get out of debt and establish a healthier financial lifestyle is a situation more and more people are finding themselves in. Though it is a hard and sometimes long road, it is possible to get out of debt and become debt free. Many are confused and don’t know where to start, but the steps are simple and easy to follow. Following these outlined steps can set all on the path to financial freedom.

Stop adding to current debts. This is the easiest solution to debt and can be accomplished within a day. As obvious as it may seem, this solution is often overlooked by many. Individuals will begin on a journey to reduce the debt that they already have but never stop adding to; thinking of the older debts as the issue and not the fact that it is being consistently added to as an issue as well. Though it can be tough it’s important to take a no non-sense approach and not make excuses.

A simple solution is to stop using all forms of credit, from credit cards to financing. Also remember this step goes beyond these ways of accumulating debt, consider any recurring monthly payments as debts also. From the un-used gym membership to the online-gaming subscription, if it’s not considered a necessity within day to day life its probably a contributing factor to the current financial situation.

Consider establishing an emergency fund. For many individuals the reason why they have acquired so much debt and found themselves in this situation is not always because of frivolous spending, but often times due to crisis. Being able to properly deal with and handle unexpected expenses is a key component to getting out of debt and establishing a debt-free lifestyle. The easiest solution is to save, save, and save. Saving money and using it as a means to handle situations rather than relying on a credit card is a smarter financial decision.

It’s important to be realistic that a nest egg is not going to go from $0 to $10,000 in one day, but rather that it is a gradual process. Once an ideal amount of emergency cash has been saved, somewhere around $1,000, close those credit card accounts that were once relied on. This savings should only to be used in the case of an emergency, not for that new sweater or vacation that you have been planning. Saving and keeping this money apart from other living expenses is a small part of a larger picture to reducing debt.

Consider establishing a snowball plan. The full benefits received from establishing a snowball plan may take some time to be highlighted, but they are definitely worth the time and effort it takes to implement this plan. In a nutshell, a snowball plan requires an individual to pay of their largest debts first and gradually make their way down the list toward the smaller debts. Once an individual has started their journey to get out of debt and has stopped added to their debt is when it is time to start this plan. Establishing and maintaining a snowball plan requires diligence on the part of the payee. Begin by paying the debts with the highest interest rates first. Eliminating those debts that cost the most to own (through interest) will save money in the end and allow the individual to get out of debt faster. It’s important to remember that paying small debts first is not always the most efficient way when trying to repair a failed financial situation.

Choosing a Winning Wedding Cake

Close

One wedding tradition that happily crosses almost all cultural and religious boundaries is the gorgeous wedding cake. As far as the food goes, it is the center of attention. It’s likely to play a large part in your memories of the occasion, too, so it’s important to pick just the right cake for you.

Let’s explore some factors you might want to consider.

The date and location of your wedding might limit your cake choices. For example, if you’re having an outdoor wedding in the heat of summer, you’ll have to avoid ice cream cakes or anything like mousse. You’ll need to choose something appropriate to the season. Your baker can help.

The number of guests expected at your reception is another big factor. You want to have enough cake for all the guests and not too much extra. Again, your baker can help determine the correct size cake once he knows the guest list length.

You could even get fancy with the shape of your wedding cake, but be aware that a traditional round or square design will cut faster and feed more people than more exotic shapes.

You might have a favorite flavor or cater to the differing tastes of your guests by having a cake of many flavors. This is easy with a traditional tiered wedding cake. White cake, chocolate cake, carrot cake, banana cake . . . whatever flavor you like can be made into a wedding cake!

Then, of course, there’s the icing. Although traditionally white or light cream color, today many brides are instead matching the wedding cake icing to the color theme of the wedding.

And finally a wedding cake usually contains decorations. This can range from the old-fashioned and simple wedding couple on top, to an elaborate landscape of flowers, bridges and streams. Your budget and cake size are the only restrictions!

Your wedding cake is a work of art. It will be a centerpiece of your reception, a beautiful picture in your wedding album, and a happy memory of your blissful day. Take some time to make it your own.
