WHAT KINDS OF INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT?

Information which is automatically collected

Certain information about your visit can be collected when you browse websites. When you browse the Website, we, and in some cases our third-party service providers, can collect the following types of information about your visit, including:

  • Domain from which you accessed the Internet
  • IP address (an IP or internet protocol address is a number that is automatically assigned to a device connected to the web)
  • Approximate geographic location based on the IP address of the user’s local system
  • Operating system (which is software that directs a computer’s basic functions such as executing programs and managing storage) for the device that you are using and information about the browser you used when visiting the site
  • Date and time of your visit
  • Pages you visited
  • Address of the website that connected you to the Website (such as google.com or facebook.com)
  • Device type (desktop computer, tablet, or type of mobile device)
  • Screen resolution
  • Browser language
  • Time spent on page
  • Scroll depth – The measure of how much of a web page was viewed
  • User interaction events (e.g. clicking a button or clicking on an ad)

How do we use this information

  • To help make our site more useful to you. For example, based on the history of articles read by you on the website, we determine a more relevant set of articles to recommend to you for further reading, in our recommended articles section, or as part of our infinite scroll functionality, or through browser notifications.
  • To collect aggregated data on site usage. For example, based on the distribution of visitors across different geographies, we tune our marketing outreach in those geographies.
  • To help place more relevant and personalized ads. For example, we use your ad interaction data to cap the frequency of an ad being shown to you, and to show ads that are relevant to your location, or relevant to the content you are browsing, or similar to products that you may have shown interest in, (by clicking) in the past.

Information which you may provide

Newsletters

We collect information including your email address or mobile phone number to deliver alerts or e-newsletters. We use this information to complete the subscription process and provide you with information. You can opt out of these communications at any time by editing your subscription preferences.

Promotions

From time to time, we may offer promotions on the Services. Such promotions may ask for your name and contact information in order to register you for the promotion.

CureJoy Balance Subscription

We may also additionally collect your gender, location (country), age, height, weight, activity level, diet preferences, and other health, wellness and lifestyle information when you sign up for and use CureJoy Balance.

Mobile App

We may also additionally collect your age, gender, location (country), and health preferences when you sign up for and use the CureJoy App.

How do we use this information

We use the above information to send you newsletters, respond to customer service requests, administer your account, respond to your questions and concerns, provide more personalized recommendations and notifications etc.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

CureJoy uses cookies or similar technologies to analyze trends, administer the website, track users’ movements around the website, and to gather demographic information about our user base as a whole. You can control the use of cookies at the individual browser level, but if you choose to disable cookies, it may limit your use of certain features or functions on our website or service. Unless you have adjusted your browser setting so that it will refuse cookies, our system will issue cookies when you direct your browser to our websites. Additionally, some Internet browsers have a “do not track” feature that lets you tell websites that you do not want to have your online activities tracked. These features are not yet uniform, so we are currently not set up to respond to such signals. We use session Cookies that expire at the end of a browser session (from when a user opens the browser window to when they exit the browser) and persistent Cookies which remain on your hard drive until you erase them or they expire. How long a persistent Cookie remains on your browser depends on how long the visited website has programmed the Cookie to last or they can be stored for longer. We use Cookies for different purposes, including tracking your consent for various services, personalizing content, and targeting advertisements.

HOW DO WE USE YOUR INFORMATION?

We use your information: (1) with your consent for the reasons that you provide such information, and (2) for the purposes set forth below:

To provide and enhance the Services

We use the information we have to deliver and enhance our services, including to personalize features and content and make suggestions for you on our Services.

Transactions

We use your Personal Information to process your transactions, send you confirmations, to respond to your requests, and for any related communications.

Marketing

We use the information we have to improve our advertising systems so we can show you relevant ads and measure the effectiveness and reach of ads and services. We may use your Personal Information for other marketing and market research purposes to learn more about our users. We will only contact you for direct marketing purposes in accordance with applicable laws.

Retargeting

We use your information to allow us or third parties to deliver ads based on your interests on our sites, affiliated sites or third party sites. Our retargeting activities abide by the NAI code of conduct.

Analytics

We may store details of your visits to our websites and your electronic interactions with any emails that we send to you (“Usage Data”) under a pseudonym (i.e. not under your name but under an ID number), including the pages you visited and services you used. We use such Usage Data to help us learn more about our users’ preferences, to be able to tailor our websites according to your interests and preferences, to help us address problems with and improve our site design, products and services, to analyze trends and statistics and to present content in the clearest and most friendly manner for you.

Communicate with you

We use your information to send you marketing communications, communicate with you about our Services and let you know about our policies and terms. We also use your information to respond to you when you contact us.

HOW AND WHEN DO WE SHARE OR DISCLOSE YOUR INFORMATION?

To Enhance our Services

We may share your information with third parties who provide services on our behalf to help with our business activities such as ad technology providers, email service providers, data analyzers and business intelligence providers for the purpose of enhancing our products and services. These companies are authorized to use your personal information only as necessary to provide these services to us. These third parties currently may include: Appnexus, Criteo, District M, Facebook, Google, Index Exchange, OpenX, Yieldbot, Insticator, Carambola, SparcMedia.

Third Party Marketing

We provide advertisers with reports about the kinds of people seeing their ads and how their ads are performing, but we don’t share information that personally identifies you (information such as your name or email address that by itself can be used to contact you or identifies who you are) unless you give us permission. We may request to share your information (including email address) with third-party business partners so that they can market their products or services to you. Before sharing your information for this purpose, however, we will disclose the proposed sharing to you and obtain your consent, or opt-in, to such sharing. If you do not opt-in, we will not share your information for this purpose.

Third Party Services

When you interact with a third-party advertiser in our Services, the content creator or advertiser can receive information you share with them, as well as the information needed to complete the transaction, including medical information and contact details.

Asset Sale

We may transfer your Personal Information to a third party in the event of a transfer of all or some of our assets to a third party, provided that the third party agrees to adhere to terms similar to those of this Privacy Policy. Where required by applicable law, we will provide you with an opportunity to reject the transfer of your Personal Information in such cases.

Legal Requirements

We may access, preserve and share your information in response to a legal request if we have a good faith belief that the law requires us to do so. We may also access, preserve and share information when we have a good faith belief it is necessary to: detect, prevent and address fraud and other illegal activity; to protect ourselves, you and others, including as part of investigations; or to prevent death or imminent bodily harm. We may disclose aggregated, non-personal information about website users without restriction.
