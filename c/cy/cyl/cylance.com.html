Privacy Policy

The purpose of this privacy policy (this “Policy”) is to provide you with a description of the types of information Cylance, Inc. (“we,” “our” or the “Company”) collects about you when you visit the Cylance web site located at http://www.cylance.com, and associated sub-domains (the “Site”) and download and install our Cylance V software (“Cylance V”), Cylance PrivateDETECT software (“Cylance PrivateDETECT”) or any installer or other application (collectively, the “Software”), and how we use that information. By visiting or using the Site and/or Software or using our security monitoring and virus protection services (the “Services”), you agree to the provisions of our End User License Agreement http://cylance.com/tools/eula.shtml and this Policy. If you do not agree to the terms of this Policy, please do not use or access the Site or the Software or use the Services.

Information We Collect
When You Download and Register to Use the Software

When you download and register to use the Software, we require that you sign in using a Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn or Google account. You can also choose to link additional social media accounts with the Software so that we can monitor links or attachments sent through those services. When you choose to link one or more of your social media accounts, you grant the Company access to certain information associated with your account, including the primary email address associated with the account and (depending on which type of account you use) your name, photo, headline, current position, who you follow and Tweets from your timeline. We collect the primary email address associated with your account and a random code generated by the social media service (a “token”) that verifies your log in information (your “Account Information”).

When You Visit the Site

In addition, we automatically collect certain aggregate information and analytical data related to your Site visit and Software usage (see “How We Use Cookies” below). Aggregate information is non-personally identifiable or anonymous information about you, including the date and time of your visit, the IP address of your computer, your browser information, the Internet address that you visited prior to and after reaching the Site, the name of the domain and host you used to access the Internet and the features of our Site and Software which you accessed. This data is in no way associated with your personally identifiable information.

While Providing the Services

As an integral part of the Services, the Software will automatically collect and transmit certain data, and hashes including system files, dll files, binary files and/or other executable code, including those which may from time to time be embedded in other file types (“Potentially Suspicious Files”). Potentially Suspicious Files are transmitted to our servers so that we can analyze those files for malicious code, malware or other intrusive programs.

When You Sign Up To Receive Additional Information or Register for a Webinar

At various points on the Site you can sign up to receive additional information or attend a webinar. To receive additional information from us, we ask that you provide your name, company name, email address and phone number. This information is retained by us and trusted third parties affiliated with the Company to provide you with the information, marketing materials or updates for products and services similar or related to the information you have requested. If at any time you do not want to receive further information or materials, you can opt-out by following the instructions in the communication or emailing us at privacy@cylance.com.

How We Use Your Account Information

Your Account Information is stored securely on an encrypted drive with controlled and audited access, and used to inform you of upcoming events, send you information and updates, respond to inquiries for service requests, authenticate your use of the Software and manage the Site, the Software and assess their usage and the usage of the Services.

How We Use Cookies

Cookies are small files that a site or its service provider transfers to your computer’s hard drive through your Web browser (if you allow) that enables the sites or service providers systems to recognize your browser and capture and remember certain information.

We use cookies to compile aggregate data about Site traffic and interaction so that we can offer better Site experiences and tools in the future. We may contract with third-party service providers to assist us in better understanding our Site visitors. These service providers are not permitted to use the information collected on our behalf except to help us conduct and improve the Services. We have no access to or control over these cookies or tracking technologies. This Policy covers the use of cookies on http://www.cylance.com and by us only and does not cover the use of cookies or tracking technologies by any such business partners.

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personally identifiable information to outside parties, except trusted third parties who assist us in operating our Site, conducting our business, or providing you with the Services, who agree to keep your personally identifiable information confidential.

We disclose information that we, in good faith, believe is appropriate to cooperate in investigations of fraud or other illegal activity, or to conduct investigations of violations of our End User License Agreement. We cannot guarantee that we will be able to contact the user(s) in all cases, whether because of a time limit, court order, inability to effectively contact a user, or for any other reason.

We are not limited in our use of non-personal information that does not permit direct association with any specific individual, or non-identifiable aggregate information about our users (such as the number of users of our Services, the geographic distribution of our users, the amount of information located and/or removed, etc.).
