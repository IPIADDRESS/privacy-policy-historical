CyberAlert's Privacy Policy

This privacy statement applies to the use of Cyberalert.com and its allied web sites.

CyberAlert is highly committed to privacy for our clients and visitors.

The following statement reflects our corporate practices to protect the privacy of our clients and prospects.

Registration is not required in order to use or access this website. Some visitors to our site may provide contact information and fill out a brief form to obtain a free trial of our service, information about our services, or educational documents about media monitoring. We use this information to deliver the requested information to the visitor and to send the visitor subsequent marketing or educational information. The information provided by visitors is used solely by CyberAlert. CyberAlert does not sell, barter or give away to unaffiliated third parties any information you provide to us.

CyberAlert.com utilizes industry standard analytics packages which gather limited information on visitors to our website automatically, and store this anonymous information in server log files. This information may include IP addresses, browser type, basic IP information, referring pages, operating system used and date/time stamps. This information does not identify individual users; it is used only for site administration, as well as for analysis of website usage and trends.

Additionally, CyberAlert.com may use cookies to provide easier and faster access to website features such as logins and/or repeat visits. CyberAlert does not link the information we store in cookies to any personally indentifiable information you have submitted while on our site.

CyberAlert collects contact and billing information from our clients. This information is used internally to provide services and to bill clients. In the password-protected subscription area of our site, we track IP address and other data to help ensure compliance by subscribers with our license and access agreement.

CyberAlert follows accepted industry standards to protect any personal information provided to us by visitors or clients against loss, misuse, or alteration. However, no method of electronic storage can ever be 100% secure. Therefore, as with any online service, we do not guarantee the absolute security of contact or client information.

CyberAlert is not responsible for the privacy practices or the content of Web sites to which the CyberAlert site links.
