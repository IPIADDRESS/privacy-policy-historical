Privacy Policy

Thanks for visiting website of High Court of Punjab and Haryana, and reviewing our privacy policy.

We collect no personal information, like names or addresses, when you visit our website. If you choose to provide that information to us, it is only used to fulfil your request for information.

Our website never collects information or creates individual profiles for commercial marketing. While you must provide an e-mail address for a localised response to any incoming questions or comments to us, we recommend that you do NOT include any other personal information.

 

Site Security

 

• For site security purposes IP adresses, time of visit, duration of visit etc are recorded which will enable us to track users in case of a hacking attempt etc.

• Except for authorised law enforcement investigations, no other attempts are made to identify individual users or their usage habits. Raw data logs are used for no other purposes and are scheduled for regular deletion.

• Unauthorised attempts to upload information or change information on this service are strictly prohibited and may be punishable under the Indian IT Act.

 

Latest Important

Judgments

  • CWP-22793-2010

    "Deputy Commissioner, Gurdaspur directed to evict private respondent namely Bhupinder Singh from premises owned by Panchayat Samiti, Batala. Strictures passed against government officials who connived with private respondent to deprive Panchayat Samiti, Batala of valuable property. Direction also issued to initiate departmental and criminal action against delinquent officials and individuals."

  • LPA-1613-2014

    The certificate issued by the Registrar, Births & Deaths, carries a statutory presumption of correctness, but if in the face of such certificate, another date of birth is given, while seeking admission in a school, then principles of estoppel will be applicable. A candidate cannot seek correction of date of birth in the matriculation certificate on the basis of the certificate issued by the Registrar, Birth & Deaths. In any case, no person can seek correction in the date of birth after expiry of 3 years of attaining majority.

  • CWP-20660-2014

    "Finding the writ petition to be an abuse of process of law, it has been held that the petitioner is fighting his political battle in Court rather than in the Constituency. The will of the people cannot be allowed to be tinkered with except in the manner contemplated by law. Petition dismissed with costs of Rs.50,000/-. "

  • CR-3595-2014

    "Civil Trial Procedure- Mandate of Order 18 Rule 3A requiring party to be examined before other witnesses to be strictly followed. The party that does not obtain prior permission from court will loose right to give evidence or if given in breach of law, the evidence will be eschewed.."
