Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy Disclosures

  • Hills Bank and Trust Company privacy policy
  • Trust and Wealth Management privacy policy

Hills Bank and Trust Company has always considered your privacy and the confidentiality of your financial information a high priority. We want you to understand what information we collect and how we use it. In order to provide our customers with a broad range of financial products and services as effectively and conveniently as possible, we use technology to manage and maintain customer information. The following policy serves as a standard for all Hills Bank and Trust Company employees for collection, use, retention, and security of nonpublic personal information.

Other Important Information

We collect the domain name and e-mail address (where possible) of visitors to our web page, the e-mail addresses of those who communicate with us via e-mail, and information volunteered by the consumer, such as survey information and/or site registrations.

The information we collect is used to improve the content of our web page, used to notify consumers about updates to our web site, used by us to contact consumers for marketing purposes, and not shared with other organizations for commercial purposes.

If you supply us with your postal address online you may receive periodic mailings from us with information on new products and services or upcoming events. Persons who supply us with their telephone numbers online may receive telephone contact from us with information regarding new products and services or upcoming events.

If you do not want to receive email from us in the future, please let us know by an email to hillsbank@hillsbank.com or calling us at 1-800-445-5725 (1-800-HILLSBK) or writing to us at Hills Bank and Trust Company, Attn: Marketing., PO Box 160, Hills, IA 52235. Simply state that you do not want to receive email from our company.

As a reminder to those who correspond with us via email, while it is fast and convenient, it is not an entirely secure method of communication. We recommend you take care not to send emails containing any personal, private, or sensitive information you would not want viewed by a third party. So before you take advantage of the convenience of email, we suggest that you do not send an email that includes your Hills Bank account numbers, social security numbers, credit card numbers, Hills Bank Online secure access codes, other PINs you have with the bank, security code, or any passwords.
