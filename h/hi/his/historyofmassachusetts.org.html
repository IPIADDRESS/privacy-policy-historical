When Christmas Was Banned in Boston

When the puritans came over on the Mayflower in 1620, they brought with them their strict ways, their religious views and their distaste for Christmas. Although Christmas was widely celebrated in Europe as a Christian holiday marking the birth of Jesus Christ, puritans saw it as a false holiday with stronger ties to paganism than Christianity. As pious and reserved Christians, puritans also took a dislike to the drinking and dancing associated with the holiday. [read more]
