                      Cookies and other technologies:  

In operating the Services, we or third party services providers use a technology called “cookies.” A cookie is a piece of information that the computer that hosts our Services gives to your browser when you access the Services. Our cookies help provide additional functionality to the Services and help us analyze Services usage more accurately. For instance, our website may set a cookie on your browser that allows you to access the Services without needing to remember and then enter a password more than once during a visit to the website. We allow third party service providers to use cookies or similar technologies to collect information about your browsing activities over time and across different websites following your use of the Services.  We also use local shared objects, also known as Flash cookies, to display content based upon what you view on our site to personalize your visit. Flash cookies are different from browser cookies because of the amount of, type of, and how data is stored.

Google Analytics uses cookies to help us analyze how users use the Site and enhance your experience when you use the Service. In order to collect this information, Google Analytics may set cookies on your browser or mobile device, or read cookies that are already there. Google Analytics may also receive information about you from apps you have downloaded, that partner with Google. How we use Personal information in our Influencer Platform:

 

What information do we collect and where do we collect it from?

 

About influencers: We collect information posted on publicly accessible dot com social media and other publicly accessible websites that are commonly used for self promotion, marketing and publicity purposes. If information is located behind a paywall or a password or have setting that are other than “public”, we will not collect it. We collect:

  • Profile picture
  • Bio
  • Social media public profile information.
    • Facebook: To learn what information is publicly visible on Facebook click the “How is this information shared” section of the Facebook privacy notice here: https://www.facebook.com/about/privacy/previous and  here: https://www.facebook.com/help/203805466323736
    • Instagram:  To learn what information is publicly visible on Instagram see the “How is this information shared” section of the Instagram privacy notice here https://help.instagram.com/519522125107875?helpref=page_content and also here: https://www.facebook.com/help/203805466323736
    • Twitter: To learn what information is publicly visible on Twitter see the “public information” section of the Twitter privacy notice here: https://twitter.com/en/privacy
  • The social media handles and public profile information of the followers for each influencer and for a particular post
  • The social media handles and public profile information of who the influencer is following
  • Location (if this is posted)
  • Email or other contact information (to the extent posted by them on in their name)
  • Website url (if this is posted)
  • Sample of posts posted by the influencer together with
  • Textimage or video url, number and list of handles of the individuals who reacted (like), commented or shared the post and location data on the post (if this is posted)

 

About followers:  We collect the social media handles and public profile information (click to the “public profile information” bullet above).

How do we use the information and what for?

 

We collect and analyze the personal information we collect about an influencer to create a profile about such influencer to better understand the areas or populations in which they are most influential and the types of audiences to whom they appeal.

 

We collect and analyze the personal information we collect about followers of influencers to help determine areas and populations in which influencers are most influential and the types of audiences to whom they appeal.

 

We process this information for our legitimate interest in providing the platform and related services to our customers and for our customers’ legitimate interest in marketing their products.  We implement appropriate safeguards to protect the rights of influencers. For example, influencers can control information we use and share about them as set forth in the [your choices] section of our Privacy Notice, and where our data processing of influencer information is governed by EU data protection laws, influencers also have the rights described in the [EU rights] section of our Privacy Notice.  If you require further information about the balancing test that HYPR has undertaken to justify its reliance on the legitimate interest legal basis under the GDPR, please see [link to contact section above] for further details on how to contact us.

 

Who do we share it with?

 

Influencer information:

 

We share a profile of the influencer created by our system with our clients in order to enable them to reach out to the influencers who would be most effective for a marketing or other campaign that they are considering. The profile contains:

 

  • Basic identifying information of influencer from their public profiles
  • Contact information for the influencer (to the extent listed by them or in their name)
  • Compiled and aggregated information about particular areas and populations with whom the influencer is most influential

 

Follower information:

All follower information is shared only in the aggregate.

 

We also share both follower and influencer information as listed in how we share your information- click

 

How do we maintain the information up-to-date and how long do we keep it?

 

In order to facilitate the most effective campaign opportunities for our clients and the influencers we take measures designed to ensure that the information we collect and the analysis we do are accurate and up to date. To this end:

 

  • We continuously check each influencer’s source websites and update the profiles on our system.
  • We apply consistent quality analysis and testing on samples of posts and followers that we use for our analysis.
  • We employee a team of data scientists that applies algorithms designed to ensure the accuracy of the information.
  • If an influencer contacts us to correct inaccurate information about them, we assess the request and amend the information where appropriate

 

How do we protect the information?

 

Our influencer platform database is stored on AWS, which provides robust technical protections for the data. We apply technical and organizational measures to protect this information. For example, we limit access to our database only to specific IP addresses that are under our control and belong to those employees that need to access this information for the performance of their job duties.

 

What can you do about how we use your information?

 

Influencers and followers whose information is included in our platform have choices in connection with how their information is used as explained in [“your choices”] section of our Privacy Notice.

What are your rights if you reside in the EU?

 

If EU data protection laws apply to you, you are also entitled to the rights listed here section of our Privacy Notice.

 

What about information about children?

 

The influencers and followers whose information we collect are generally over the age of 18. The websites we use to collect information about influencers and followers are generally limited to individuals over the age of 13. Accordingly, we do not proactively collect or share information about children. If we become aware that we have collected personal information children under the age of 16 without parental or guardian consent we will delete this information.

 

If you have reason to believe that a child under the age of 16 has provided personal information to us, please contact us using the information below.

We collect follower and influencer information from dot com websites generally located in the United States and process them in the United States.  To learn about our practices in connection with cross border transfers of information generally see the section above about cross border.
