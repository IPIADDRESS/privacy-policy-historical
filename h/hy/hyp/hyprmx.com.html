Introduction

At Jun Group Productions, LLC and our HyprMX Mobile LLC subsidiary, we connect advertisers and publishers that we call clients to an audience of end users that we call consumers. Consumers are end users of applications and websites that we call properties, and those properties are owned by third parties that we call partners. Our services enable clients to deliver targeted, interactive content that we call engagements to consumers, and in doing so, we collect information about consumers and partners. We also collect information from visitors to our websites, www.jungroup.com and www.hyprmx.com.

This privacy policy describes how we collect, retain, use and disclose information that you make available to us as a user of our services, including as a visitor to our websites, as a consumer end user of our partners' properties, and/or as one of our partners. Information includes personal information that identifies a specific person, such as a full name or email address, and non-personal information, such as your interests, service usage and Internet access device characteristics. Whether or not you have registered with or subscribed for our services, by using our websites or services, you agree to this privacy policy.

Cookies

We refer to browser cookies and other tracking technologies such as pixels, beacons, and Adobe Flash cookies as cookies. Cookies are small bits of data that often include a de-identified or anonymous unique identifier. When you access websites, apps and other services, they may store cookies on your device and use them to recognize you when you make subsequent requests for services from the original service provider or another service provider. We use cookies to collect and store information about your use of our services, including website pages you have visited, partner properties you have accessed, content you have viewed, search queries you have run and ads you have seen. Our services providers and the other third parties that we engage with or link to may also use cookies or other tracking technologies to collect information. In many cases we do not control the use of information by these third parties, and your use of or exposure to their services is governed by their separate privacy policies. If you wish to know more about cookies, you can visit the Network Advertising Initiative.

Most browsers are initially set to accept cookies, but you can change your settings to notify you when a cookie is being set or updated, or to block cookies altogether. Please consult the "Help" section of your browser for more information. By blocking cookies, you may not have access to certain features, content or personalization available through our services. We do not respond to browser "Do Not Track" signals.

Internet-Based Advertising

Interest-based advertising refers to using information collected across non-affiliated, third-party sites and applications over time to provide more relevant advertising to you, based on your interests inferred from that information. The Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) is a coalition of digital advertising companies that has created a self-regulatory code for interest based advertising, and we comply with those self-regulatory principles.

Consumers may opt out of receiving interests-based ads in our services by following these instructions:

1. For iOS devices running iOS 8 or higher, go to Settings > Select Privacy > Select Advertising > Enable the "Limit Ad Tracking" setting. Our services do not support earlier versions of iOS.

2. For Android devices with OS 2.2 or higher and Google Play Services version 4.0 and higher, go to Google Settings App > Ads > Enable "Opt out of interest-based advertising."

For iOS users, additional instructions are available on Apple's support site. If you opt out, you will still see ads and they will not be based on your interests as ascertained from third-party information. Instead, they may be untargeted, target based on first-party information, or related to the content of the property you are using.
