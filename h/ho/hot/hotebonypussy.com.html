Pink Visual Anti-Piracy Policy and Best Practices

Details on Pink Visual's Anti-Piracy and User Safety Policy:

Pink Visual does not permit its content to be distributed by unauthorized third parties including on peer-to-peer (p2p) systems or tube sites. While part of the reason for this policy is to keep our intellectual property from being disseminated by people who are not authorized or licensed to distribute our content, there are other reasons why we discourage the use of peer-to peer-software and tube sites to acquire, trade, or otherwise share adult content.

Why shouldn't you use peer-to-peer software, torrents and/or tube sites to get adult content?

It is illegal to distribute copyrighted content without authorization. It is a violation of federal law to distribute copyrighted materials without authorization. At Pink Visual, we value our customers, and we don't want to see any of you get into legal trouble for distributing our content, or the content of any other rights holder, whether it is a movie studio, a record company, or another adult entertainment studio. If the fact that it is unethical to distribute copyrighted content without authorization isn't enough to persuade you that you shouldn't do it, consider the potential consequences of getting caught doing it and ask yourself a couple of questions. Is finding, sharing and trading pornography for free really worth the potential embarrassment, expense and blemish on your permanent legal record that getting caught would create?

You could accidentally download and/or redistribute child pornography or other illegal material. When you view content on Pink Visual's sites, you can rest assured that the content is compliant with 18 USC 2257, a section of federal U.S. law that requires producers of adult content to obtain and store documents which affirm that all models and performers were of legal age at the time of production. When you obtain sexually explicit content through peer-to-peer systems, from torrents or sites that allow for user uploaded content, you have no such assurance. It is very important to understand that if you download child pornography, even if you had no intent of doing so, then you are in possession of illegal material, and ignorance of the law or 'mistake of age' are not likely to be accepted as an excuse by law enforcement, or as a legal defense by the court.

Cyber security concerns. As has been observed by tech experts and law enforcement alike, peer-to-peer systems are frequently targeted for abuse by hackers, phishers and other forms of cyber thieves. Hackers have already created exploits specifically for use on p2p systems many times, and in several instances they have used the appeal of adult content to encourage the spread of their worms, viruses and other exploits. The ongoing popularity of p2p systems virtually guarantees that hackers will continue to make exploiting the p2p environment a high priority for future attacks, and you can bet that they will continue to use the lure of free pornographic content to hook additional victims.

You could accidentally compromise your own online privacy. Peer-to-Peer networks also have been abused by hackers and because of this these systems potentially expose your computer and files to millions of other users on the network . Your private files from personal finance documents to your adult videos could be accessible by others. In addition, despite the fact that you are online does not mean that you are not identifiable. The legal process allow for your personal information to be discovered through legal investigations associated with such P2P activities such as copyright infringement and child pornography. Thousands of end-users are sued on a weekly basis for copyright infringement.

While p2p, tube sites and torrents all have their benefits as technological forms, Pink Visual urges you to make use of them with great caution. Be sure to keep your anti-virus software up to date, and follow the same common sense rules that you would where your email is concerned; if you wouldn't open an attachment to an email that was sent to you by someone you don't know, why would you download a file that had been uploaded by a stranger?
