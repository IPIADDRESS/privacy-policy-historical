What Personal Information Do We Collect Through This Website?

We collect three types of personal information through this Website:  the personal information you knowingly choose to provide or post when you use this Website; personal information collected from your browser or using standard Web technologies, such as cookies; and the personal information others may provide about you when they post User Generated Content on this Website.

Information You Provide. We collect the personal information you knowingly provide (using your mouse and keyboard) when you use this Website. The information we collect depends on how you use this Website. For example, if you apply to host a party, we collect the information you provide as part of the online application process. If you post photographs, comments or other User Generated Content on a party page, we collect that information also.  All postings must comply with the requirements for User Generated Content as set forth in our Terms of Service. 

Information Others Post on This Website.  The User Generated Content others post on this Website may contain your personal information.  For example, if you attend a party, someone may post photographs of you and others that were taken at the party.  All postings must comply with the requirements for User Generated Content as set forth in our Terms of Service. 

Information Sent to Us by Your Web Browser.  We collect information that is sent to us automatically by your Web browser.  This information typically includes your IP address, the identity of your Internet service provider, the name and version of your operating system, the name and version of your browser, the date and time of your visit, and the pages you visit.  Please check your browser if you want to learn what information your browser sends or how to change your settings.  Generally, we do not link the information provided by your browser to information that identifies you by name.  You should know, however, that when you access this Website by clicking on a link in an email or other electronic communication you receive from us, your browser tells us that someone has clicked on a particular link in a communication addressed to you.  We collect that information to improve the effectiveness of our marketing efforts.

More About IP Addresses.  An “IP address” is a unique number that is automatically assigned to your computer when you connect to the Internet.  It is used to identify your computer’s “location” in cyberspace, so that the information you request can be delivered to you.  If you use a dial-up connection or a connection that assigns dynamic IP addresses, your computer will be assigned a new IP Address each time you connect to the Internet.  If, however, your computer is permanently connected to the Internet using a static IP address, the IP address assigned to your computer will generally be the same each time you use your computer.

IP addresses do not include your name, email address or other information that identifies you personally, but in some cases, they can be used to identify you.  For example, if you have created a user identity, for example, by joining House Party, we may link your IP address to information that identifies you personally.  In addition, if, for example, we suspect fraud or a threat to the security of this Website, we may share our server logs – which contain visitors’ IP addresses -- with the appropriate investigative authorities, who could use that information to trace and identify individuals.  Because IP addresses can be used, under certain circumstances, to trace and identify particular individuals, House Party treats them as personal information.

Cookies and Similar Technologies.  We use "cookies" and other Web technologies to collect information and support certain features of this Website.  For example, we may use these technologies to

      • collect information about the ways visitors use this Website – which pages they visit, which links they use, and how long they stay on each page;

      • to manage and determine the effectiveness of our advertising and marketing campaigns;

      • to support the features and functionality of this Website — for example, to save you the trouble of reentering information already in our database or to prompt the settings you established on previous visits to this Websites; and

      • to personalize your experience when you use this Website.

Generally, the information we collect using these Web technologies does not identify you personally.  If, however, you have created a user identity, for example, by joining House Party or applying to host a party, we may link the information we collect using these Web technologies to other information that identifies you personally.

If you do not wish to receive cookies, you may set your browser to reject cookies or to alert you when a cookie is placed on your computer.  You may also delete our cookies as soon as you leave this Website.  Although you are not required to accept our cookies when you visit this Website, if you set your browser to reject cookies, you will not be able to use all of the features and functionality of this Website.

How Does House Party Share My Personal Information?

Service Providers.  We share personal information with companies and organizations that perform services on our behalf, for example, companies that provide support services to us (such as email management services and data storage services).  These third parties are required by contract to use the personal information we share with them only to perform services on our behalf and to treat your personal information as strictly confidential.

Unless you “opted-out” on the application, the Sponsor may also add the contact information on your application to its mailing lists and marketing databases and contact you with information and offers the Sponsor believes will be of interest to you.  If you are selected to host one of our events, the Sponsor will use your contact information to contact you regarding the event (before, during and after the event).  Please be aware that if you communicate directly with the Sponsor or visit the Sponsor’s Web site, any information you provide to the Sponsor will be governed by the Sponsor’s privacy policies.

If you sign up to attend one of our events as a guest, we may share your contact information with the host and the Sponsor, who may use your information to contact you about the event.  Unless you “opted-out” during the registration process, the Sponsor may also add your contact information to its mailing lists and contact you with information and offers the Sponsor believes will be of interest to you.  If you communicate directly with the Sponsor or visit the Sponsor’s Web site, any information you provide to the Sponsor will be governed by the Sponsor’s privacy policies.

Our Sponsors have full access to the party pages and national page for their events and may use the User Generated Content posted on those pages to advertise and promote their products, services and brands.  Please see below for more information about the ways User Generated Content is shared and distributed.

Other Marketing Partners.  From time to time, we may wish to share your personal information with carefully selected marketing partners so that they can contact you with information and offers we and they believe will be of interest to you.  We will not do so, however, unless you have given us your express (“opt-in”) consent.

User Generated Content.  HouseParty.com is all about sharing User Generated Content with visitors to this Website and with the general public.  As used in this Privacy Policy, “User Generated Content” means all of the text, photographs, images, illustrations, graphics, sounds, video and audio-video clips, and other content you post on or through this Website, except your Feedback and any part of your Account Information that is not included in your Public Profile.  User Generated Content includes anything you post on a party page and the content of any emails or other communications you send through this Website, such as invitations to a party or follow-up communications.  Please be aware that User Generated Content may be forwarded by email or to other Web sites, such as Facebook.com, using features and functionality available through this Website.  For example, our national party pages may be displayed on other Web sites, on cell phones and other mobile devices, and in other media using the RSS feed available through this Website.  All User Generated Content must comply with our Terms of Service, and only Registered Users may post User Generated Content..

You post User Generated Content at your own risk. You should have no expectation of privacy with respect to the User Generated Content you or others post.  You should not post any User Generated Content you do not wish to make permanently available to the general public, and you must take special care to make sure your postings do not violate the privacy or other rights of others.  If, at any time, you wish us to take down User Generated Content that contains your personal information, please contact us by email at privacy@houseparty.com and we will work with you to remove the User Generated Content from this Website.  Please be aware, however, that if the User Generated Content has already been distributed to other Websites or published in other media (for example, via an RSS Feed), we will not be able to recapture it and delete it from third-party Websites or other media.

Your Account Information and Public Profile.  When you register on this Website, you will be required to provide certain personal information to us.  This information becomes part of your House Party account information (your “Account Information”) and some of this information becomes part of your “Public Profile.” Your Account Information includes your personal details (such as your name, contact information, shipping address, screen name and password), your photograph, and your contact preferences.  Your Account Information is password protected.  Your Public Profile is the information that others will see if they click on your screen name or User Generated Content you have posted.  Your Public Profile includes your screen name, age, gender and photograph (unless you choose to use an icon instead of a photograph). Your Public Profile also tells people how many photos, videos, and blog posts you have posted.

Business Transfers.  Your personal information may be transferred to a company that has acquired the stock or assets of House Party or one of our businesses, for example, as the result of a sale, merger, reorganization or liquidation.  If such a transfer occurs, the acquiring company’s use of your personal information will still be subject to this Privacy Policy and the privacy preferences you have expressed to us.

Compliance With Laws and Protection of Our Rights and the Rights of Others.  We may disclose personal information when we, in good faith, believe disclosure is appropriate to comply with the law, a court order or a subpoena.  We may also disclose personal information to prevent or investigate a possible crime, such as fraud or identity theft; to enforce or apply our online Terms of Service or other agreements; or to protect our own rights or property or the rights, property or safety of our users or others.

House Party currently uses the following procedures in the event that you submit information via the Website for age and/or indentify verification purposes in connection with your registration for certain activities or events, such as product demonstrations, parties, and sampling, educational and informational activities (collectively “Activities”) for which House Party has implemented age and/or identification verification procedures.   Anyone wishing to register for such Activities will need to submit to House Party’s current age and/or identity verification procedures.   It is important to note that submitting to the verification process is completely voluntary.  Individuals who choose not to submit to the verification process will not be allowed to register or attend such Activities.
