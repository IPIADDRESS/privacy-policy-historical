This website is maintained and hosted by the Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs on behalf of the Family Assistance Office. In accordance with the Guidelines for Federal and ACT Government World Wide Websites issued by the Office of the Privacy Commissioner, the Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs provides the following privacy statements.

Site Visit Data

This is a cookie-free site operated by the Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs.

Whenever you visit this website, your visit and the following information is recorded for statistical purposes:

  • your server address
  • your top level domain name (for example .com, .gov, .au, .uk etc)
  • the date and time of your visit to the site
  • the pages you accessed and documents downloaded
  • the previous site you have visited and
  • the type of browser you are using

This information does not identify you personally and you remain anonymous. This information assists with the continual improvement of this site.

No attempt will be made to identify users or their browsing activities except in the unlikely event of an investigation of abuse or a security incident. The data will not be disclosed to any external agencies except in the case where a law enforcement agency may exercise a warrant to inspect the department's logs.

External sites that are linked to or from the Department's Internet site are not under our control and you are advised to review their privacy statement.

Collection of Personal Information

Individuals who manage the Family Assistance Office site are subject to the Commonwealth Privacy Act 1988 and, Social Security (Administration) Ac t1999 and A New Tax System (Family Assistance)(Administration) Act 1999.

The Privacy Act 1988 governs the way in which Commonwealth Government departments and agencies collect, use and disclose personal information. More information about the Privacy Act can be obtained from the Privacy Commissioner's website.

The Social Security and Family Assistance Law contains confidentiality provisions which governs the way in which protected information (which may include personal information) is collected, used and disclosed.

Your personal information including email address will only be used for the purpose for which you have provided it. Your personal information will not be added to a mailing list, be used for any other purpose or disclosed to any individual, agency or organisation, unless required by law, or you provide consent.

When completing the site's feedback forms, we request your name, email address and telephone or facsimile number. These details are only used to respond to your message and will not be used for any other purpose and will not be disclosed to any individual, agency or organisation, unless required by law, or you provide consent.

Protected information will only be used in accordance with the confidentiality provisions contained in the social security and family assistance Law.

Security of Information

Protected information is protected under the confidentiality provisions contained within the social security and family assistance law. These laws prohibit any person from accessing, using or disclosing any personal information in the possession of the Family Assistance Office except when performing their duties in accordance with these laws or in specific situations permitted by the law.

Protected information will not be made available to any person, agency or organisation unless the law permits or your permission is given. Penalties apply to individuals who fail to observe these provisions.

Before you consider transmitting any confidential information over the Internet, you should contact us to seek our agreement to the application of the 'confidential' classification to the information you wish to provide. When the Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs agrees to treat your personal information as 'confidential' it will receive the appropriate level of security.

While the Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs endeavours to provide a secure Internet environment, please note that the privacy of your personal information cannot be guaranteed if you choose to transmit that information over the Internet.

If you do not wish to contact the Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs via the Internet, there are alternative ways to obtain and provide information. Please contact the Department of Families, Housing, Community Services and Indigenous Affairs by telephone or post if you have concerns about the security of your email message. When you telephone the department, you can request access to FaHCSIA facsimile numbers.
