 Privacy Statement

First Degree Fitness is committed to protecting your privacy and developing technology that gives you the most powerful and safe online experience. This Statement of Privacy applies to the First Degree Fitness Web site and governs data collection and usage. By using the First Degree Fitness website, you consent to the data practices described in this statement.

Collection of your Personal Information

First Degree Fitness collects personally identifiable information, such as your e-mail address, name, home or work address or telephone number. First Degree Fitness also collects anonymous demographic information, which is not unique to you, such as your ZIP code, age, gender, preferences, interests and favorites.

There is also information about your computer hardware and software that is automatically collected by First Degree Fitness. This information can include: your IP address, browser type, domain names, access times and referring Web site addresses. This information is used by First Degree Fitness for the operation of the service, to maintain quality of the service, and to provide general statistics regarding use of the First Degree Fitness Web site.

Please keep in mind that if you directly disclose personally identifiable information or personally sensitive data through First Degree Fitness public message boards, this information may be collected and used by others. Note: First Degree Fitness does not read any of your private online communications.

First Degree Fitness encourages you to review the privacy statements of Web sites you choose to link to from First Degree Fitness so that you can understand how those Web sites collect, use and share your information. First Degree Fitness is not responsible for the privacy statements or other content on Web sites outside of the First Degree Fitness and First Degree Fitness family of Web sites.

Use of your Personal Information

First Degree Fitness collects and uses your personal information to operate the First Degree Fitness Web site and deliver the services you have requested. First Degree Fitness also uses your personally identifiable information to inform you of other products or services available from First Degree Fitness and its affiliates. First Degree Fitness may also contact you via surveys to conduct research about your opinion of current services or of potential new services that may be offered.

First Degree Fitness does not sell, rent or lease its customer lists to third parties. First Degree Fitness may, from time to time, contact you on behalf of external business partners about a particular offering that may be of interest to you. In those cases, your unique personally identifiable information (e-mail, name, address, telephone number) is not transferred to the third party. In addition, First Degree Fitness may share data with trusted partners to help us perform statistical analysis, send you email or postal mail, provide customer support, or arrange for deliveries. All such third parties are prohibited from using your personal information except to provide these services to First Degree Fitness, and they are required to maintain the confidentiality of your information.

First Degree Fitness does not use or disclose sensitive personal information, such as race, religion, or political affiliations, without your explicit consent.

First Degree Fitness keeps track of the Web sites and pages our customers visit within First Degree Fitness, in order to determine what First Degree Fitness services are the most popular. This data is used to deliver customized content and advertising within First Degree Fitness to customers whose behavior indicates that they are interested in a particular subject area.

First Degree Fitness Web sites will disclose your personal information, without notice, only if required to do so by law or in the good faith belief that such action is necessary to: (a) conform to the edicts of the law or comply with legal process served on First Degree Fitness or the site; (b) protect and defend the rights or property of First Degree Fitness; and, (c) act under exigent circumstances to protect the personal safety of users of First Degree Fitness, or the public.

Use of Cookies

The First Degree Fitness Web site use "cookies" to help you personalize your online experience. A cookie is a text file that is placed on your hard disk by a Web page server. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. Cookies are uniquely assigned to you, and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie to you.

One of the primary purposes of cookies is to provide a convenience feature to save you time. The purpose of a cookie is to tell the Web server that you have returned to a specific page. For example, if you personalize First Degree Fitness pages, or register with First Degree Fitness site or services, a cookie helps First Degree Fitness to recall your specific information on subsequent visits. This simplifies the process of recording your personal information, such as billing addresses, shipping addresses, and so on. When you return to the same First Degree Fitness Web site, the information you previously provided can be retrieved, so you can easily use the First Degree Fitness features that you customized.

You have the ability to accept or decline cookies. Most Web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer. If you choose to decline cookies, you may not be able to fully experience the interactive features of the First Degree Fitness services or Web sites you visit.

Security of your Personal Information

First Degree Fitness secures your personal information from unauthorized access, use or disclosure. First Degree Fitness secures the personally identifiable information you provide on computer servers in a controlled, secure environment, protected from unauthorized access, use or disclosure. When personal information (such as a credit card number) is transmitted to other Web sites, it is protected through the use of encryption, such as the Secure Socket Layer (SSL) protocol.
