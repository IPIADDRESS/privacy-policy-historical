Collection of Information

Fig collects non-personally identifiable information from you regarding your usage of our Site (“Non-Personally Identifiable Information”). Non-Personally Identifiable Information is information that alone cannot be used to identify or contact you. Non-Personally Identifiable Information which we store in our log files may include, without limitation, Site pages viewed, sites visited before coming to our Site, browser type, operating system, organization name, presentation and videos viewed, time spent viewing pages of our Site or using certain features of our Site, demographic data such as server locations, clickstream data, cookies existing on your computer, search criteria used and results, date and time of access or visits to our Site, frequency of visits to our Site, connection speed, location services and other information which does not specifically identify you. We may combine this automatically collected log information with other information we collect about you. We do this to improve services we offer you.

We and our service providers collect this information using various technologies such as: first and third party cookies, beacons, session variables, log files, browser plug-ins and add-ons, and JavaScript. These technologies are used in analyzing trends, administering the Site, tracking users’ movements around the Site and to gather demographic information about our user base as a whole. We may receive reports based on the use of these technologies by such service providers on an individual as well as aggregated basis.

We use cookies for authentication, session management and personalization of user experience. Users can control the use of cookies at the individual browser level. If you reject cookies, you may still use our Site, but your ability to use some features or areas of our Site may be limited.

This Privacy Policy does not cover the collection of information by cookies or other methods by third parties, such as our business partners. Any information submitted by you to a third party will be controlled by that third party’s privacy policy. We do not control how these third parties collect information or by what means such third parties may use their own cookies to collect information about you. We encourage you to review the privacy policy of any company before submitting your personal information.

We may partner with a third party to manage our advertising on other sites. If you wish to not have this information used for the purpose of serving you interest-based ads, you may contact us via our contact form on help.fig.co to withdraw or change your consent to use of your information for the purposes of interest-based advertising. Please note this does not opt you out of being served ads. You will continue to receive generic ads.

Fig also collects information from you when you create an account, send us an email through the Site, or make an investment or contribution through the Site, which may enable us to identify you personally (“Personally Identifiable Information”), including information you provide in forms or upload to the Site, as well as information which may also be contained on server logs, such as your Internet protocol (“IP”) address.

Security

We use reasonable efforts to secure access to Personally Identifiable Information in our possession, but will not be liable for any failure to safeguard such information, whether inadvertent or otherwise. Personally Identifiable Information is considered highly confidential. We maintain administrative, technical, and physical safeguards for the protection of personal information. With respect to administrative safeguards, access to Personally Identifiable Information is limited to Fig personnel and authorized third parties who have a legitimate need to know. We conduct employment background checks, and we regularly train our personnel on security protocols. With respect to technical safeguards, we utilize up-to-date firewall protection and high-quality antivirus and anti-malware software. We perform an ongoing risk assessment and adjust protocols. We protect the security of your information during transmission by using Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) software, which encrypts information you input. Wherever appropriate, we obfuscate and/or encrypt information in our systems and/or during information transfer. We also employ session timeouts. With respect to physical safeguards, we store all sensitive financial information is stored in a highly secure data center, with physical safeguards such as security guards, access badges, and a video monitoring system.

Personally Identifiable Information is also stored with third parties who have informed us that they also use reasonable efforts to secure access to Personally Identifiable Information, but we will not be liable for the failure of any third party to safeguard Personally Identifiable Information, whether inadvertent or otherwise. We utilize third party service providers who may store on their servers social security numbers, addresses, financial information, electronic signatures, and payment information. For your security, we display only the last four digits of your social security number or tax ID number and bank account information when confirming an investment or contribution.

It is important for you to protect against unauthorized access to your password and to your computer. Be sure to log off when finished using a shared computer.
