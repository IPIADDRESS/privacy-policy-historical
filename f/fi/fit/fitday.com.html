Fitday.com Privacy Statement
Last Updated: October 10, 2000 6:28 pm


At Fitday.com, personal privacy is of the utmost importance to us. This page outlines our privacy policy, and gives you contact information if you have any other questions.

Information Collected
Fitday.com collects personal information about you when you sign up for a Fitday account. This information includes your age, weight, sex, and level of activity. Once you sign up for an account and sign into Fitday.com, you are not anonymous. Fitday also stores the information you enter about foods eaten, activities, goals, and all other information collected in the online forms. Fitday logs information such as the IP address that you access the server with, and what pages you requested.

Fitday WILL NOT sell or rent any of the personally identifiable information that you enter. Fitday may use this information to target specific advertisements or to suggest items to you. Fitday will reveal any information needed to comply with any applicable laws or legal proceedings.

Public Journals
Fitday.com gives you an option to let others on the web see your fitness journal. When you first create your account, this feature is off and your journal is password protected. If you choose to make your journal public by pressing the "Make My Journal Public" button in the home tab, anyone with access to the web can see your fitness journal but not edit it. When your journal is displayed, your username will be shown, but FitDay will not show your email address.

You can stop your journal from being public at any time by pressing the "Remove My Public Journal" button in the home tab and your journal will return to being password protected.

Cookies
If you choose for Fitday.com to save your login name and password, Fitday.com places a cookie on your machine. This cookie is used to find the correct login name and password. We do not use this cookie to track you in any way. If you do not want a cookie on your system, simple do not check the "save password" checkbox when logging in and none will be saved.

FitDay also uses a temporary cookie to show that you haved logged in. This is used so you do not have to log into every page. This cookie is destroyed by your browser after every session. Please understand that FitDay uses cookies only to enhance your experience and never uses cookies to violate your privacy.

Removing your account
You may request to have you Fitday account removed at any time by sending an email to remove@fitday.com. Fitday will attempt to remove your account promptly, but we do not guarantee that your account will be removed in any specific amount of time. After your account is removed, it will not be accessible by anyone through the web, but your information may remain in Fitday archives that Fitday maintains.

Fitday reserves the right to terminate your membership if Fitday learns that you are abusing your membership or not adhering to the terms of service.

Security
Your account is protected through a username and password. It is your responsibility to protect this information. Fitday.com is not a secure server, and none of the information is encrypted.

Changes to this Privacy Statement
Fitday changes this privacy statement to reflect any changes in our policy. The statement will have a date of last change posted at the top, to alert you to any changes.

Contacting Us.
Feel free to contact Fitday with any comments, question, or suggestions. Send any email to privacy@fitday.com

You can find more information in our terms of service.
