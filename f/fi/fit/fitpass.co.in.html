5. Cookies

5.1 We may place text files in the browser files of your computer system. A cookie itself does not contain personal information and it will only enable us to relate your use of the Company’s Electronic Media and/or Services and your behaviour on the Electronic Media to information that you have specifically and knowingly provided. The only personal information that a cookie can contain is the information you yourself supply to the cookie. A cookie can’t read data off your hard disk or read cookie files created by other websites.

5.2 We use cookies to track User traffic patterns on the Electronic Media and their use of the Services. We do not use cookies to track your use of third party websites, applications or software. Please also note that we do not control use of cookies by any third party.

5.3 We may place both permanent and temporary cookies. A permanent cookie will remain on your web browser until its expiry date, unless deleted prior to such expiry date. However, most cookies are “session cookies” meaning that they automatically get deleted from your computer system at the end of the session. You can refuse cookies by turning them off in your browser or alternatively you can also set your browser to warn you before accepting any cookies. Therefore, you are always free to decline our cookies if your browser permits, however:

(i) we use certain encrypted cookies to authenticate the User on each page after the User logs on to the Electronic Media or accesses any Services. You will not be entitled to log in to the members’ area of the Electronic Media without accepting such cookies; and

(ii) accepting the encrypted or the log-in cookies will only enable you to access the members’ area of the Electronic Media or the Services.

Please note that some other features and services (such as creating customised searches for vehicles) may still not function properly or conveniently on your computer system if the other cookies placed by us on your hard drive are disabled.
