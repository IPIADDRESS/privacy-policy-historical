Fyber GmbH Privacy Policy

This is the privacy policy of Fyber GmbH, Johannisstr. 20, 10117 Berlin, (heretoafter referred to as "Fyber"). It applies to all Fyber websites and services.

This privacy policy is intended for all users of Fyber websites and services ("user"). The Fyber services relate primarily to advertising from third parties ("advertising partners") on websites operated by other third parties ("publishers"). Thus users are predominantly advertisers, publishers and other interested parties or their employees. Among the users are also users of services on the Internet provided by publishers ("publisher-user") as well as other private users who are in direct contact with us.

All personal information that the user provides in the course of all business relations that Fyber collects through use of the websites and services and the user voluntarily submits will of course be handled by Fyber in regards to applicable data protection legal provisions.

Fyber saves and uses data to prevent and combat fraud and abuse on Fyber pages and to offer services adapted to our users. The details are laid out in this privacy policy.

In the event that the user is forwarded – for instance via a link or an advertisement – to another website not operated by Fyber, the user will be informed by Fyber that this document does not apply to website concerned. This privacy policy does not apply to the websites of the publishers. Fyber is not responsible for the way the operator of the other website handles data. In particular, Fyber is not responsible for the fact that this privacy policy does not apply on websites not operated by Fyber. Fyber recommends that users always carefully read the privacy policy of third policy websites.

I. Which personal information does Fyber collect, process and store?

Fyber collects, processes and stores that personal information that Fyber collects from the user or the user voluntarily submits (for instance in forms or elsewhere on websites or for services operated by Fyber) in electronic or any other form of communication.

To register to use the websites or services from Fyber the users needs to submit certain personal information. This includes among other things names, position in the company and email address.

The following describes which information is stored by Fyber:

Usage data:

When a user makes use of websites or services operated by Fyber, the latter automatically saves the domain name and/or IP address of the computer used to contact Fyber; the date as well as the length of the visit at websites and services operated by Fyber for instance through cookies; as well as pages accessed by the user on Fyber operated websites and services as well as the browser used.

Mobile Use of Fyber Services:

Users can also access content and services from Fyber on mobile devices (such as smartphones or other mobile devices that use the Apple iOS). Fyber additionally stores the device's identification number (i.e. UDID or IMEI) that accesses the content as well as the standard technical information such as the operating system version and the device type to enable the user to access the content or service.

Email Address:

Fyber stores the email addresses of its users if they are submitted. The user can either submit their email address voluntarily via contact form through the websites or services of Fyber or submit it in the course of direct communication with Fyber.

Information resulting from communication with Fyber:

When the user submits a ticket to Fyber customer support, Fyber saves the history of communication with the user as well as the information submitted by the user in the course of the communication (for example the email address), to answer their inquiry as best we can. Fyber does not share this information with third parties.

Other, voluntarily submitted information about the user:

Fyber always strives to improve its offerings and services as well as to adapt them to its users. Therefore the user can submit additional information to Fyber as part of the use of Fyber offers and services or in the course of direct communication with Fyber such as name, address, age, gender, interests and, if applicable, payment data (if the user processes a payment via Fyber). The user can also allow Fyber to record their usage behavior on partner websites.

Usage data of users of offers from publishers (publisher-user):

The publisher who works with Fyber shares information about publisher-user's usage behavior with Fyber. Fyber needs the publisher-user's data so that the user can receive benefits for offers from the publisher. In doing so, the publisher assigns each user a personal ("UserID") connected with the usage data. The UserID can only be read out by the publisher. Fyber cannot directly associate the usage data shared by the publishers via the UserID with the user. The identity of the publisher-user can only be determined by Fyber if the publisher-user itself initiates such a request.

Therefore, the information transmitted to Fyber by the publishers is not related to one individual unless the publisher-user disclosed its identity thus waiving the protection of his private information (for instance accessing the publisher's content with a user name connected to the real name or entering the email address on the publisher's website). In this case, but not solely, Fyber is entitled to the above-mentioned rights to use the personal information.

Publisher-users should check the privacy policy of the publisher's website in regard to the publisher's handling of personal information.

II. How does Fyber use personal information?

Usage data from users and publisher-users:

Optimization of the service

Fyber collects, processes, and stores users' personal information, in so far as Fyber sees legitimate business purposes for it. In other words: Fyber collects, processes, and stores the user data to better understand users of their websites and services and thus offer them better services. Fyber can, for instance, not only use the information stored for direct processing of the agreement concluded with the user (i.e. use of services, creation of invoices, use of customer support), but we can also use it to identify malfunctions and/or misuse of Fyber websites and services.

Prevention and combat of abuse

Therefore, Fyber logs and stores information on the use of Fyber websites and services such as activity on the publisher's website and regard to customers' advertisements or services. These include the monitoring of behavior patterns and checking for manipulations that can be useful in detecting misuse or improper activity by the user or user-publisher. Fyber uses special software, for example, to detect the use of illicit scripts and identify the IP address as well as other data in regard to the behavior of users and publisher-users. If Fyber determines that a user or user-publisher violates its contractual obligations, for instance by using illicit scripts, Fyber explicitly reserves the right to bring the user's or user-publisher's data together with other data stored by Fyber on this person to prove the violation. Fyber will then possibly take additional measures against the users or publisher-users concerned to restore normal operation.

Billing of services

Fyber is entitled to share user data with the service provider responsible for payment processing insofar as this is required for billing. Fyber is also entitled to provide user data to third parties authorized to collect outstanding balances, insofar as this information is required or useful to collect the balance. Fyber will inform the user of the name of the third party. If Fyber cooperates with third parties for these services, Fyber will obligate them to observe applicable data protection laws and guarantee sufficient data protection.

Market research

Fyber is entitled to use the information stored by and from the user or publisher-user to evaluate demography, interests and user behavior. This occurs anonymously. Fyber is also entitled to analyze information stored about the user or publisher-user in an anonymous, statistical and aggregate form. If and to the extent to which Fyber uses data to create user profiles, Fyber will never share the profiles with third parties without the consent of the person concerned and will immediately cease the use of the user profile if said objects to it.

Personalized offers tailored to the user.

Fyber may allow third parties to contact the user for advertising purposes. As long as the user does not object to the use of its user data for this purpose, advertising can be sent that matches his or her profile and interests (based on the information submitted to Fyber as well as the actions within Fyber websites and services. This information cannot be attributed to the user, unless the user voluntarily made this information known on a website operated by or on behalf of Fyber.

Email Address:

Fyber can use the email addresses acquired through an offer from Fyber or received directly from the user, to send the user newsletters and information on Fyber offers. The user can of course withdraw this consent in writing at any time (for example via email) at no charge, apart from costs incurred for transmission of the withdrawal (at the basic rates). The user will have the opportunity in every information and newsletter sent by Fyber to unsubscribe or send a cancellation notice.

Miscellaneous, voluntarily submitted information:

If the user voluntarily shares personal information on request by Fyber, then Fyber will use this information with his or her consent so that the user receives personalized special offers.

Glossary

The most important terms are explained below to help understand the privacy policy:

What is personal information?

Personal information is information that can be attributed to an identified or identifiable user. This information can be used to contact or identify the user outside of the website and the services of Fyber. This information includes, for instance, the user's name, email address, telephone number, and mailing address or credit card details.

What is user data?

User data is information that Fyber collects, processes and stores when the website is used. User data include in particular the features necessary to identify the user (such as the domain name and/or IP address of the computer used to contact Fyber, which can be used to determine the location of the computer), information about the start and end of the respective use of the Fyber websites and services as well as information about the pages accessed.

What are IP addresses?

IP addresses serve to identify devices that are connected to the Internet. Every computer connected to the Internet can be attributed to a specific IP address or combination of numbers.

What are UDID and IMEI?

The UDID and IMEI serve as identification of mobile devices. The iPhone manufacturer Apple Inc. uses the so-called UDID, whereas the IMEI is used for other mobile devices. The codes are stored in the devices and cannot be modified by the user.

What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small text file that websites and services operated by Fyber - transfer to the cookie file of the user's browser so that the website and services can identify you the next time you visit. A cookie typically contains the name of the domain from which the cookie has come, the "life span" of the cookie, and a value, usually a randomly generated unique number. With the help of cookies, Fyber is able to recognize the user. This can help facilitate the use of Fyber websites and services. However, cookies per se cannot be used to identify the user. Of course, Fyber websites and services can also be used without cookies if the browser is set to decline cookies. Cookies already placed on the computer can be manually deleted by the user at any time. Fyber warns the user that use of its websites and services without cookies may be limited.
