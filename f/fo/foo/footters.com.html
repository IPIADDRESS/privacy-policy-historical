What type of information does Footters collect?

We live in a world driven by data. We believe that our Footters Services result in significant improvements if we use the right type of information in the right way. But rest assured that we care about your data: if we don’t need to know something we won’t ask for that information about you or use it.

We collect various kinds of information that you provide to us as well as information we obtain from your use of the Footters Services. We call this information “Personal Information”, because it’s information relating to you and that can therefore identify you.

See some examples, to clarify. Personal Information includes:

  1. contact data: your name, your e-mail address, telephone number, residence address;
  2. demographic data: your gender and date of birth;
  3. certain data about your online activity, such as:
    1. technical information, including your browser type, service provider, IP address, operating system and webpages visited;
    2. information about what you’ve searched for and looked at while using the Footters Services;
    3. metadata, which means information related to items you made available through the Footters Services, such as the date, time or location that a shared photograph or video was taken or posted;
  4. data we collect from your using certain features of Footters Services, such as when you post content, reviews or comments, or when you use plug-ins like “Me gusta”, “Golazo”, “Paradón”, “Fair Play”, “Jugadón” o “Vaya regate”, or when you share content with the “Share” button; and
  5. information about your location and your mobile device, when you download and use the Footters app and have consented to location services. Most mobile devices allow you to turn off location services.

How we share and disclose personal information?

Footters is not in the business of sharing personal information with third parties. However, we will share it to be able to provide services and run Footters business. Here we tell you when and why we will share Personal Information with third parties:

  1. To perform services. We may disclose Personal Information to third parties in order to perform services requested or functions initiated by users, such as when you use the Share button.
  2. With third party service providers performing services on our behalf, such as hosting and data analyses.
  3. For legal purposes, to enforce our rights, protect our property or protect the rights, property or safety of others, or as needed to support external auditing, compliance and corporate governance functions. We will disclose Personal Information as we deem necessary to respond to a subpoena, regulation, binding order of a data protection agency, legal process, governmental request or other legal or regulatory process. We may also share Personal Information as required to pursue available remedies or limit damages we may sustain.
  4. In aggregated form. We may share information with third parties, in an aggregated form – that is, in a statistical or summary form such that it won’t be possible to identify you from that aggregated data.
  5. Promotional communications. At times we may send you promotional offers on behalf of third parties. In these cases, we will only share information in aggregated form to those third parties.
  6. Business transfers and corporate changes. We may transfer Personal Information in connection with a merger, sale, acquisition, transfer or change of ownership or control, of the whole Footters business or of some of its units or assets. We will notify you before your information is transferred or becomes subject to a different Privacy Information.

With your consent. Other than as set out above, we will ask for your consent before sharing personal information with third parties.

Cookies policy

A cookie is a file downloaded on your device when accessing certain webpages or applications. Cookies contain information about your device, such as the User ID, the Operating System, user settings, browsing history and activities you conduct while using Footters Services. Cookies themselves do not allow Footters to identify you, but may be linked to Personal Information about you to allow Footters to recognize you every time that you access our page or application helping us improve the services we give you by for example: storing your password, tracking your navigation habits and customizing your user experience, analyzing technical and navigational information about the use of Footters Services.

If you want to learn more about Cookies and how they work, visit www.allaboutcookies.org
