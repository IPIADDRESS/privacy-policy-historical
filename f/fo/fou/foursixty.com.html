privacy policy

Foursixty Inc. (“Foursixty”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is committed to the protection of personal information. As part of this commitment, we have set out this Privacy Policy to describe the manner in which we treat the personal information that we maintain on behalf of our customers (“Customers”), as well as the personal information we collect, such as through our website at  https://foursixty.com/ (“Website”).

Collection and Use of Information

Information we maintain on behalf of our Customers

Foursixty provides social commerce and social content management services for brands, bloggers, and advertising agencies (“Customers”), including an API and related website services that enable Customers to create shoppable Instagram galleries on their websites (“Sites”), and to secure rights to use ephotos and videos that have been posted on Instagram (together, the “Services”).

We collect and maintain the following information on behalf of our Customers for the purposes of providing the Services:

·     Individuals who visit a Customer’s Instagram gallery (end users):

o   We collect log data (Internet Protocol address, cookies, date/timestamp, clicks) and cookie data to track if a session has interacted with a Foursixty post. We use this data for the purpose of tracking and analyzing which digital assets an end-user interacted with in order to show conversion data to Customers, analyzing the use of the Services by end users in order to improve the marketing, analytics and functionality of the Services.

 

·      Individuals who post content on Instagram that may be collected and used by Customer (social media users):

o   We collect social media user name as well as public uploads and posts from social media sites (including publicly posted photographs and videos and associated Instagram URL, date/time of posting and metadata and caption associated with the posts). We use this data to contact social media users in order to request rights to use photos posted on Instagram.

We also collect and use the above data to provide our Customer with reports in aggregate form regarding the usage of their Site so that they can better understand the engagement of their end users who access and interact with their Site.

We collect and use the first and last name and email address of authorized employees and independent contractors of our Customers in order to provide and administer accounts necessary to access and use the Services.

We rely on our Customers to ensure that they have obtained all authority in accordance with applicable privacy laws for us to provide the Services on their behalf.

Website Information and Analytics

In general, you can visit our Website without indicating who you are or submitting any personal information. However, we collect and/or maintain the IP (Internet protocol) addresses of all visitors to the Website, and other related information such as page requests, browser type, operating system and average time spent on the Website. We use this information to help us understand our Website’ activity and to monitor and improve the Websites and our Services.

Cookies & Similar Technologies: The Website uses a technology called “cookies”. A cookie is data that the Website can send to your browser, which may then be stored on your hard drive so that you can be recognized when you return. We use cookies on pages on the Website where you are prompted to log in or that are customizable. If you registered an account on the Website, these cookies may: (i) let us know who you are and identify your browser as one that has visited the Website before; and (ii) may be necessary to access your account information (stored on our computers) in order to deliver products and personalized services. We also use cookies to provide us and our service providers with information about your use of the Website that we will use to personalize the Website or other content in accordance with your interests and preferences and improve your user experience. You may set your web browser to notify you when you receive a cookie or to not accept certain cookies. However, if you decide not to accept cookies from the Website, you may not be able to take advantage of all of the features of the Website.

Tracer Tags & Web Beacons: The Website may also use a technology called “tracer tags” or “Web Beacons”. This technology allows us to understand which pages you visit on the Website. These tracer tags are used to help us optimize and tailor the Website for you and other future visitors to the Website.

Analytics: We use third-party service providers to help us provide certain features on the Website and analyze our visitors' preferences, such as Google Analytics. These third parties may use cookies and similar technologies described above to collect and analyze information about use of our Websites. Information we collect about the use of the Website may include the pages most read, areas visited, time spent, search terms and other engagement data on the Website. We use this information in order to better understand and improve our Website and Services, including the user experience and convenience of the Website. You can learn more about Google Analytics here: http://www.google.com/policies/privacy/partners/

Online Interest-Based Advertising

We work with third-parties such as ad networks and other advertising companies that use their own tracking technologies (including cookies, tracer tags, web beacons and pixel tags) on the Website in order to provide you with tailored advertisements on our behalf across the Internet (including on social media websites). These companies may collect information about your activity across your different devices on our Website and third-party websites (such as web pages you visit and your interaction with our advertising) and use this information to deliver ads that are more relevant and tailored to you on our Website and third party websites. This information may also be used to evaluate our online advertising campaigns  

We adhere to the Digital Advertising Alliance of Canada’s (DAAC) Self-Regulatory Principles for Online Behavioural Advertising. To learn more about this interest-based advertising practice and to understand your options, including how you can opt-out of receiving interest-based ads from participating third-party advertising companies, please  visit the Digital Advertising Alliance website at http://www.aboutads.info/choices/ or the Digital Advertising Alliance of Canada website at www.youradchoices.ca/choices.

To successfully opt-out, you must have cookies enabled in your web browser. Please see your browser’s instructions for information on cookies and how to enable them. Your opt-out only applies to the web browser you use so you must opt-out of each web browser on each device that you use. Once you opt out, if you delete your browser’s saved cookies, you may need to opt-out again.

Please note that even if you opt-out interest-based advertising by a third party, these tracking technologies may still collect data for other purposes including analytics and you will still see ads from us, but the ads will not be targeted based on behavioural information about you and may therefore be less relevant to you and your interests.
