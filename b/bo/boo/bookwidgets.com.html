5. Cookies

Cookies are small pieces of data that a website asks your browser to store on your computer or other device. This enables a site or service provider’s systems to recognize your browser and capture and remember certain information. Most browsers support cookies, but users can set their browsers to decline them and can delete them whenever they like.

We use the following cookies:

  • Necessary cookies: These cookies are essential to visit and use our website, or to use certain parts or functionalities of it. For instance, these cookies allow you to log in securely, and to switch between different pages of the site without having to login again on every page. They are required to make sure your personal information on the Platform is only accessible by you.
  • Functional cookies: Functional cookies are cookies that make using our Website more user-friendly or personalized. For instance, these cookies remember your preferences, or keep track of whether you’ve already taken a survey, so we don’t keep asking you the same questions over and over.
  • Improvement cookies: We use these cookies to gather information about how our Website is being used, with the goal to improve its functionalities. For instance, there are cookies that track which blog posts are most popular, or how many unique visitors access a particular tutorial.

Most internet browsers are initially set up to automatically accept cookies. Please note that you can change the settings to block cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent to your computer or device. There are a number of ways to manage cookies. Please consult your browser instructions or help screen to learn more about how to adjust or modify your browser settings.

If you disable the cookies that we use, this may impact your experience of our Platform, for example you may not be able to visit certain areas of the Platform.
