Information We Collect From You and How We Collect It

Information We Collect Directly
When you create your account, you will be asked for your first name, last name, user ID and a password. We may also request additional optional information about you such as date of birth, email address, other contact information, or nickname for example. We may also ask you for information relating to your preferences however, such information is optional. We will only require information that is reasonably necessary for your successful use of the Site.

Information We Collect Passively
We may also passively collect information on site usage patterns or through content that members post on our message boards or by comments. For example, we may collect certain non-personal information from all visitors to our site, such as IP address, the date and time of visit, time spent on the site, the type of browser used (e.g., Firefox, Internet Explorer), the type of operating system used (e.g., Windows XP or Mac OS), the ISP from which the visitor receives Internet access, and aggregate information regarding what pages users of the site access or visit. This information may be combined with the personal information we collect from users.
Bonzi uses Cookies to track the your session on the Site or to make your use of the Site more convenient by storing certain pieces of data.  A Cookie is a small piece of information sent by a web server to store on a web browser so it can later be read back from that browser. You can easily delete Cookies from your computer by following instructions provided in the Help guide for your browser. If you choose to disable Cookies, the Site may not work properly during use.

Content Posted to or Sent Through Site
Although Users on our Site may be allowed by their Organization to communicate only with teammates in a "closed" environment (only with their teammates), it's important to note that by their very nature email accounts, message boards, and other interactive / social networking services disclose information, including personal information you've entered to others on your team. We do not review any content before it is posted and cannot guarantee that we will find and remove inappropriate content that has been posted to our Site. We look to each team and its members to use the Site appropriately and in accordance with our Terms of Use.

Tracking Activities on the Site
When you are logged into your account, we may track your activities within the Site. For example, we track the pages visited, the length of time spent logged in, the information submitted about preferences and comments posted on the team's dashboards. This information is associated with your Bonzi username and may be combined with the personally identifiable information we collect from you.

We may partner with third parties to provide content and/or to display targeted advertisements. We will not share your personally identifiable information with these third parties.
