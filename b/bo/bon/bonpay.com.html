The UK and EU AML regime is set out in the following legislation and regulations:
UK Regulations:

 

  • The Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA), as amended by the: Serious Organised Crime and Police Act 2005 (SOCPA); and the Proceeds of Crime Act (Amendment) Regulations 2007;
  • The Terrorism Act 2000, as amended by the: The Anti-Terrorism, Crime & Security Act 2001; and the Terrorism Act (Amendment) Regulations 2007;
  • The Terrorism Act 2006;
  • The Money Laundering Regulations 2007;
  • The Criminal Finances Act 2017; and
  • The Joint Money Laundering Steering Group (JMLSG) Guidance for the UK Financial Sector on the prevention of money laundering/combating terrorist financing.
Other applicable regulations of national governments and multinational bodies in relation to financial sanctions;
European Union Directive on the prevention of the use of the financial system for the purposes of money laundering or terrorist financing; and
Any other national or international law or regulation applicable to BONPAY’s operations.
GOALS OF THE POLICY
The purposes of this Policy as follows:

 

  • To prevent BONPAY from being used for financial crime so as to comply with all applicable legal requirements; and
  • To ensure that the most appropriate action is taken by BONPAY to mitigate the risks associated with financial crime.
This Policy outlines the legal requirements related to financial crime which BONPAY remains in compliance with, as well as internal measures which are established by BONPAY to ensure it complies with these legal requirements. The Policy sets the parameters for BONPAY in relation to the AML, CTF and sanctions framework.
SCOPE AND APPLICATION
The BONPAY Policy is designed to prevent money laundering by meeting the UK and international AML legislation obligations including the need to have adequate systems and controls in place to mitigate the risk of the firm being used to facilitate financial crime. This Policy sets out the minimum standards to be complied with and includes:

 

  • Establishing and maintaining risk-based customer due diligence, identification, verification and know your customer (KYC) procedures;
  • The maintenance of appropriate records for the minimum prescribed periods;
  • Establishing and maintaining a Risk Based Approach (RBA) towards assessing and managing the money laundering and terrorist financing risks to the company.
The Policy applies to all employees, all functions, all units in BONPAY.
The aim of BONPAY is not only to comply with relevant legal requirements, but also to mitigate and reduce the potential risk to BONPAY of our customers using our products, services and delivery channels to launder the proceeds of illegal activity, fund terrorist activity or conduct prohibited financial sanctions activity (these offences denote “financial crime”).

BONPAY does not tolerate any breach of the requirements defined herein.
RISK-BASED APPROACH
BONPAY, in line with the international requirements, has adopted a risk-based approach to combating money laundering and terrorist financing. By adopting a risk-based approach, BONPAY is able to ensure that measures to prevent or mitigate money laundering and terrorist financing are commensurate with the identified risks. This will allow resources to be allocated in the most efficient ways. The principle is that resources should be directed in accordance with priorities so that the greatest risks receive the highest attention.
A risk-based approach includes:

 

  • Risk Assessment;
  • Risk Monitoring;
  • Managing and Mitigation the risk;
  • Monitoring and improving the effectiveness of control.
