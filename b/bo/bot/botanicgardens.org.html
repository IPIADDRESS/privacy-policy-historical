privacy policy

Collection and Usage of Personal Information
Denver Botanic Gardens (the Gardens), operators of www.botanicgardens.org, recognizes the importance of protecting the privacy of the users of this Web site. Except when specifically and knowingly provided by you, the Web site user, the Gardens collects no personally identifying information about individuals.

As a method of tracking the use of this Web site, www.botanicgardens.org may utilize the Web technology known as “cookies,” which enables the Gardens to relate a visitor’s use of the Gardens information to any information that the visitor may have specifically and knowingly provided to the Gardens. Any such voluntarily provided identifying information, if applicable, may be used by the Gardens for editorial, marketing or promotional purposes. Such identifying information, if applicable, will not be transferred to any other party unless otherwise stated at the time of collection.

The Gardens only collects any type of personally identifying information to the extent that it serves our legitimate business purposes, and the Gardens will maintain appropriate safeguards to ensure the security, integrity and privacy of the information.

Changes to this Privacy Policy
We reserve the right to make changes to this policy at our sole discretion. Such revisions may arise in response to changes in the law, policy, our site, or other factors. We encourage you to periodically visit this page to review our most current policy.

garden spotlight

Children's Secret Path at York Street

Especially for kids: Get your hands dirty in the soil station!

It's a fact: The alligator drum and giant amadinda are musical instruments made from plants.

Botanical interest: Children are encouraged to interact with plants, including smelling fragrant chives (Allium schoenoprasum), touching lamb’s ear (Stachys byzantina) and soft artemisia (Artemisia schmidtiana ‘Silver Mound’).
