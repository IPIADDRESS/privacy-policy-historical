B. Your Right to Opt Out

You have the right to “opt-out” of certain of our uses of your Personal Information.  Here are some ways to do it:

1.  When you sign up for the Service, you may have the opportunity to set certain privacy, permissions and account options. You may adjust these settings at any time by following the instructions on your computer or device or by navigating to the settings, account or privacy features on your device or the platform you are using.  Please be aware that if you limit our access to certain information, you may not be able to use all of the features and functionality of the Service.

2.  You may opt-out of receiving emails from Bandsintown Group at any time by clicking the op-out or unsubscribe link within the email or by sending an email to privacy_group@bandsintown.com identifying the Service and detailing your request.

3.  You may opt-out of having your Personal Information shared with other companies or individuals at any time by sending an email to privacy_group@bandsintown.com .  Please identify the Service and tell us your preference.  For example, you can ask that your Personal Information not be shared only for promotional purposes or, in some cases, you can request that it not be shared for any purpose.  Please be aware that if you limit our ability to share certain information you may not be able to use all of the features and functionality of the Service.   

4.  You may opt out of receiving SMS correspondence or Premium SMS services by texting “STOP” to the short code provided for the Service.

5.  To opt out from the collection and use of information to serve targeted advertising by advertisers and/or third party networks, see “Third Party Advertisers” below.  You can also set your browser to permit you to reject certain cookies.  For information regarding other locally shared objects, see “Cookies and Other Locally Shared Objects” below.  Please understand that rejecting certain cookies and locally shared objects may limit your ability to use all of the features and functionality of the Service.  It may also delete your privacy and account settings.

 Please understand that if you opt-out of receiving promotional e-mails or SMS from Bandsintown Group, we may still contact you by e-mail or SMS in connection with your account with us or to provide you with important information about Bandsintown Group or the Service. Also, a request to have us stop sharing your Personal Information with other companies or individuals will only apply as of the date of the request, and Bandsintown Group will not be responsible for any communications that you may receive from companies or individuals that received your Personal Information prior to such request. In these cases, please contact that company or individual directly.  Further, if you download a third party game or other application on to your computer or device through the Service, the game or application may continue to send you emails or other messages even after you have cancelled the Service.

Cookies and Other Locally Shared Objects

We try to provide you with services, products and offers customized to your interests. In order to do that, we may use “cookies” and “locally shared objects” to keep, and sometimes track, information about you. Cookies are small pieces of data that are sent to your computer or device from a server and stored on your computer’s or device’s hard drive or memory. Locally shared objects are also files that are stored on your computer or device using a media player that is installed on your computer or devices (such as a Flash player) or using HTML5 (or similar computer language), which is the language that your computer or device uses to communicate with our servers.  Cookies and locally shared objects do not gather any Personal Information about you; they simply track where you travel on the Service and what you look at. (Cookies and locally shared objects may, however, store your login name, which allows you access to our online forums and other features and services.) In doing so, they may enable Bandsintown Group to relate your use of the Service to Personal Information that has already been submitted. Many other service providers use cookies and locally shared objects for very similar purposes.

We use cookies and locally shared objects to access your account information (stored on our computers) in order to deliver better and more personalized services; estimate our audience size for our advertisers; assist in processing items in your shopping basket; track the number of entries in our promotions, sweepstakes and contests; and identify which areas of the Service you have visited. We may also match information obtained from cookies and locally shared objects with data provided by you or collected by third parties. We analyze this data both in the aggregate and in connection with individual users. This information lets us give you the services you want that are connected with your interests. We also may provide the analysis of our users (as a group, of course) to prospective business partners, advertisers and other third parties. You may occasionally get cookies and “locally shared objects” from our advertisers.  These cookies are sent from third-party computers and are subject to such third parties’ policies.

Most web browsers can be set to inform you when a cookie has been sent to you and provide you with the opportunity to refuse that cookie. Similarly, most media players that enable locally shared objects can be set to manage them.  For example, Adobe’s Flash player allows you to manage locally shared objects (also known as “Flash cookies”) through its Flash Player Settings Manager.  You may be able to control the use of HTML5 locally shared objects by reducing the amount of space you allow for them through your Web Browser’s settings.  Refusing a cookie or locally shared object will generally not interfere with your use of our web sites. However, refusal may, in some cases, preclude you from using some or all of the features related to the Service.

Clear GIFs

We may use “clear GIFs” (aka “Web beacons” or “pixel tags”) or similar technologies to help track usage of the Service.  Clear GIFs allow Bandsintown Group and our service providers and business partners to track page viewing counts and patterns, which we use, for example, for future service development and to measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns.  We may also use clear GIFs within the context of certain emails to determine the effectiveness of the mailing.  A clear GIF may enable us to relate your viewing or receipt of a message to other information about you, including your Personal Information.

IP Address and Clickstream Data

Our server automatically collects data about your server’s Internet address when you visit us. This information, known as an Internet Protocol address, or IP Address, is a number that’s automatically assigned to your computer by your Internet service provider.  Similarly, when you access the Service via a mobile device, our server automatically collects data about the unique device identifier assigned to your mobile device (“UDID”). When you request pages from the Service, our servers may log your IP Address or UDID. Our server may also record the referring page that linked you to us (e.g., another web or mobile site or a search engine); the pages you visit on the Service; the web or mobile site you visit after the Service; the ads you see and/or click on; other information about the type of browser, computer, device, platform, related software and settings you are using; any search terms you have entered on the Service or a referral site; and other usage activity and data logged by our servers. We use this information for internal system administration, to help diagnose problems with our server, to report aggregate information to our advertisers, and to administer the Service. Such information may also be used to gather broad demographic information, such as country of origin and Internet Service Provider. We may also link this information with your Personal Information. Our servers cannot automatically record your name or e-mail address, but in some cases they may receive such information from Third Party Platforms and third party application or providers, if you are a registered member of the platform or service.
