We do not require that you provide, nor do we collect, any Personal Information if you are just browsing the Website. These include the ability to: post expert content, opinions or other content; participation in sweepstakes or contests subscriptions and e-mail newsletters; and participation in message boards.

We collect Personal Information when you provide it to us. From time to time, we may also collect other sensitive information such as your job title, gender, username and password or other demographic information. We may also receive information about you from other sources and add it to the information you provided to us. If you choose to voluntarily disclose certain information on any public areas of the Website or though submission of any material or content, this Privacy Policy will not cover such disclosure of information. We urge you to exercise caution when submitting content or disclosing any information on public message boards or discussion forums.

Bestcovery may also monitor, on an anonymous aggregate basis, patterns of use of the Website or in connection with e-mails that it sends out, including which links are clicked on in our e-mail communications, so that we may use such information to better personalize the content of the Website and our mailings for our users. All such information will be protected in accordance with our Privacy Policy. We may also automatically collect your Internet Protocol address for analysis or to help diagnose problems or for system administration. Bestcovery reserves the right to request any additional information necessary to establish and maintain your account for use of the Website.

3. BESTCOVERY'S PRACTICE REGARDING COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGY.
Your Internet browser has a feature called cookies, which stores small amounts of data on your computer about your visit to the Website. You do not need to have cookies turned on to visit Bestcovery, although active participation in certain areas of the Website may require cookies. Cookies alone tell us nothing about who you are unless you specifically give us personally identifiable information, in which case we use the information to enhance services we provide to you in the ways described below. By adjusting your settings on your browser, you may elect not to allow cookies on your computer.

We use cookies and tracking technologies in a variety of ways, including but not limited to: Keep count of number of times you visit the Website or our advertisers' or partners' sites; collect aggregate data (data collected in mass) that is anonymous, statistical information on the Website and advertisement usage for analysis and reporting purposes; track behaviors on the Website and to determine popularity of features and functions; implement interest-based advertising, save your username or password for easy login when you visit our Website; and displaying the most appropriate advertising or content. We use technologies that allow us to track general user-traffic patterns and we also automatically receive and record information from our servers and from your browser, including your IP address, the time of your visit to pages on our Website, and information about pages you visited. Unless you have registered for one of our products or services, the information we gather through the use of tracking technologies cannot be matched with any personally identifiable information about you.

We allow other companies that are advertisers on the Website and/or our newsletters to use tracking technology on your computer. We also use third party advertising companies to serve ads on the Website. We do not provide Personal Information to these third parties as part of this relationship. These tracking technologies tell those advertisers nothing about who you are unless you visit the web sites of these companies and advertisers, either directly or by clicking ads on our Website and then providing them with personally identifiable information about yourself,. Cookies, 1x1 or other size pixels, .gifs or web beacons, or similar tracking technologies may be utilized or placed by third parties on your computer to measure and improve the effectiveness of ads or similar purposes. These companies may use anonymous information about your visits to our Website and other web sites, but will not collect any information which can personally identify you or can be linked to you (unless if you explicitly give this type of information to them). This information can include: date/time of ad shown, the ad that was displayed, cookies, the IP address, your inferred and expressed interests, including transactional information and product interests we derive from your visits to the Website or other web sites. The recipients of this information use it to develop and deliver targeted advertising on our Website and on the web sites of third parties. This information can also be used for online preference marketing purposes. Cookies or other tracking technologies placed on your computer by third parties by or through the Website, if any, are not within Bestcovery’s control and are not subject to the Bestcovery terms of use or Privacy Policy. If you have any questions concerning third-party cookies, please contact the third party placing such cookies to find out what information is collected and how it is used. BESTCOVERY ASSUMES NO RESPONSIBILITY OR LIABILITY FOR ANY THIRD-PARTY COOKIES OR TRACKING AND DATA GATHERING TECHNOLOGIES.

If you want to prevent a third-party advertiser from collecting data, currently you may either visit each ad network's web site individually and opt out or visit the NAI gateway opt-out site to opt-out of all network advertising cookies. Note that if you opt out, you will still receive advertising. Also, if you opt out and later delete your cookies, use a different browser, or buy a new computer, you will need to renew your opt-out choice. Click here for the NAI gateway opt-out site.
