Protecting your privacy is among out top concerns, and we believe that you should have control over what information is available about you online and how this information is used by third parties. This Privacy Policy covers our business, how our sites operate, and how we collect, store, use, and ultimately protect your privacy in connection with your use of Bigsight.org (http://Bigsight.org) and a variety of applications we own and operate on Bigsight.

By visiting this site, you voluntarily consent to the terms outlined below. If you do not agree to these terms, please do not use our company sites.

Our Business: An editor-managed, public-facing people directory

We own, operate, and manage a public-facing people directory at http://Bigsight.org. The goal of this site is to help users like you manage their online identity and share their work through a public-facing webpage hosted on our site. An individual’s personal information such as school, company, location, and affiliations are then linked to other pages on the site. Many of these pages appear high in search engine rankings.

One key difference in our business relative to other people directories is that certain elements of our site are managed by an editor or team of editors. This difference is critical to how we approach and deal with privacy and personally identifiable information. There are four key differences:

User-Managed Profile Network

Editor-Managed, Public Directory of Profiles

  • User has 100% editorial control of information

  • User content must submit to few restrictions

  • User identity must be verified

  • Site owners cannot build third party profiles

  • User shares some editorial control with site editors

  • User content must adhere to form and design standards

  • Verification is at the discretion of site editors

  • Site editors may build third-party profiles

The Information we Collect

When you visit Bigsight you provide us with two types of information: personal information you knowingly choose to disclose that is collected by us and Site use information collected by us as you interact with our Sites.

When you register with Bigsight, you provide us with certain personal information, such as your name, your email address, your telephone number, your address, your gender, schools attended and any other personal or preference information that you provide to us.

When you enter Bigsight, we collect your browser type and IP address. This information is gathered for all Bigsight visitors. In addition, we store certain information from your browser using "cookies." A cookie is a piece of data stored on the user's computer tied to information about the user. We use session ID cookies to confirm that users are logged in. These cookies terminate once the user closes the browser. By default, we use a persistent cookie that stores your login ID (but not your password) to make it easier for you to login when you come back to Bigsight. You can remove or block this cookie using the settings in your browser if you want to disable this convenience feature.

When you use Bigsight, you may set up your personal profile, send messages, perform searches and queries, form groups, set up events, browse for jobs, and transmit information through various channels. We collect this information so that we can provide you the service and offer personalized features. In most cases, we retain it so that, for instance, you can return to view prior messages you have sent or easily see your friend list. When you update information, we usually keep a backup copy of the prior version for a reasonable period of time to enable reversion to the prior version of that information.

You post User Content (as defined in the Bigsight Terms of Use) on the Site at your own risk. We do not restrict access to these data. You understand and acknowledge that, even after removal, copies of User Content may remain viewable in cached and archived pages or if other Users have copied or stored your User Content.

Any improper collection or misuse of information provided on Bigsight is a violation of the Bigsight Terms of Service and should be reported to privacy@Bigsight.org .

If you choose to use our invitation service to tell a friend about our site, we will ask you for information needed to send the invitation, such as your friend's email address. We will automatically send your friend a one-time email or instant message inviting him or her to visit the site. Bigsight stores this information to send this one-time invitation, to register a friend connection if your invitation is accepted, and to track the success of our referral program. Your friend may contact us at info@Bigsight.org to request that we remove this information from our database.

Bigsight may also collect information about you from other sources, such as newspapers, blogs, instant messaging services, and other users of the Bigsight service through the operation of the service (e.g., photo tags) in order to provide you with more useful information and a more personalized experience. We may also use these data to further enrich your profile.

No one under age 13 may provide any personal information to or on Bigsight. In the event that we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under age 13 without verification of parental consent, we will delete that information as quickly as possible. Information Available to Public Search Engines

Each profile is created to be open and publicly available on the Internet; as such, information submitted to us through the site quickly becomes part of a much larger public database of web pages. Because of this, profiles will likely appear in public search engines. These search engines often take "pictures" of each page and record past versions of them as "cached" copies of the site. This means that it is possible that search engines might present out-dated data for a profile; furthermore, should you elect to have your profile deleted at any time, we cannot guarantee that it will be deleted from third party databases. As such, we cannot be held liable for this cached information.

Limited Editorial and Content Control on Public Profiles

We operate our site as a public directory; as such, we aim to ensure each profile adheres to standards of form and design that we set and can adjust at any time. These profiles are thus maintained by a team of Company-hired editors. While the editors aim to give as much content control as possible to the users, they retain the right to modify and adjust any profile at any time to adhere to our standards. Should you disagree with a specific edit, please contact editor@Bigsight.org to discuss this matter; if your request is not resolved and you remain displeased, you can have your profile deleted (delete@Bigsight.org).

Third Party Advertising

At the moment, we do not serve advertisements on Bigsight; however, we plan to do so in the future. Advertisements that appear on Bigsight will be delivered (or "served") directly to users by third party advertisers. They automatically receive your IP address when this happens. These third party advertisers may also download cookies to your computer, or use other technologies such as JavaScript and "web beacons" (also known as "1x1 gifs") to measure the effectiveness of their ads and to personalize advertising content. Doing this allows the advertising network to recognize your computer each time they send you an advertisement in order to measure the effectiveness of their ads and to personalize advertising content. In this way, they may compile information about where individuals using your computer or browser saw their advertisements and determine which advertisements are clicked. Bigsight does not have access to or control of the cookies that may be placed by the third party advertisers. Third party advertisers have no access to your contact information stored on Bigsight unless you choose to share it with them.
