If you have questions or concerns regarding this statement, you should first contact Bills.com Customer Care at customercare@bills.com or customercare@theeasyloansite.com. In order to operate the Bills.com services and to provide to you the valuable services that you request through these websites, Bills.com, LLC. ("Bills.com" or "we") must ask you to provide us with information about yourself.

The information we collect may be from the following sources:

  • On www.bills.com only - Information, including your email address, when you ask a question in the “Ask Bill” section of the website (so that we can respond and post our answer to your question).

Log Files

As is true of most Web sites, we gather certain information automatically and store it in log files. This information includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, internet service provider (ISP), referring/exit pages, operating system, date/time stamp, and clickstream data. We use this information, which does not identify individual users, to analyze trends, to administer the site, to track users’ movements around the site and to gather demographic information about our user base as a whole. We do not link this automatically-collected data to personally identifiable information. IP addresses are tied to personally identifiable information to help us remember your information during your visit to the Bills.com Web sites. We use an internal program that uses log files to track non-personally identifiable information about visitors (what pages were visited, for how long and in what order) to our site in the aggregate to assist us in analyzing user behavior and to improve the layout of the website to make it easier to use.

Tracking and Web Beacons

We utilize session variables to 1) help us manage our online advertising efforts by tracking where consumers come from and 2) remember what forms a consumer has already completed so that they won’t need to complete the same form twice. The session variables are attached to each user who begins to complete one of our forms (session variables are not attached to a site visitor who does not initiate a form) and let us know whether or not a consumer has completed a form on our site; these completions or non-completions are then attributed to our various advertising campaigns, which then tell us whether or not these campaigns are successful. The variables are stored along with a consumer’s personally identifiable information, and are used solely for the purposes outlined above. The variables expire when either, the customer leaves the site, or after twenty minutes, whichever comes first. We allow some of our business partners to use cookies and clear GIFs on this site, allowing them to track a successful completion of a form when a user visits this site. The information collected through this technology is not personally identifiable. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of Web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user’s computer hard drive, clear gifs are embedded invisibly on Web pages and are about the size of the period at the end of this sentence.

Cookies and IP Addresses

To serve you faster and with better quality, we use "cookies" technology. "Cookies" are small files of data that reside on your computer and allow us to recognize you as a Bills.com customer if you return to the Bills.com site using the same computer and browser. We send a "session cookie" to your computer if and when you enter personal information on the Bills.com site. These cookies allow us to recognize you if you visit multiple pages in our site during the same session, so that you don't need to re-enter your personal information multiple times. Once you log out or close your browser, these session cookies expire and no longer have any effect. Cookies are not tied to personally identifiable information. Some of our business partners (e.g., advertisers) use cookies on our site. We have no access to or control over these cookies. See “Third Party Links.”
