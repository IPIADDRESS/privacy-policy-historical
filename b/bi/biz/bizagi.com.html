This Privacy Statement lets you know how Bizagi collects and uses your personal information, how you can control its use, and describes our practices regarding information collected from the different Bizagi sites that link or refer to this Privacy Statement (such as our websites, computer or mobile software applications, social media pages and HTML-formatted e-mail messages), as well as through offline sales and marketing activities (collectively, the "Channels").

The Channels may provide links to or the ability to connect with non-Bizagi websites, services, social networks or applications. Clicking on those links or enabling those connections may allow the third party to collect or share information about you. Those third-party websites or services are beyond our control. We encourage you to check the privacy policies and terms of use of any non-Bizagi website or service before providing your Personal Information to them.

If you have questions or complaints regarding Bizagi’s Privacy Statement or associated practices, please contact us by emailing at privacy@bizagi.com.

This Privacy Statement covers the information practices, including how Bizagi collects, uses, shares and secures the personal information you provide, of websites that link to this Privacy Statement (collectively referred to as “Bizagi’s Websites”).

When expressing an interest in obtaining additional information about the Services, in registering to use Bizagi’s Websites or other Services, or in registering for an event, Bizagi requires you to provide your personal contact information, such as name, company name, address, phone number, and email address (“Contact Information”). Contact Information, Billing Information, Applicant Information, Optional Information and any other information you submit to Bizagi to or through the Services are referred to collectively as “Data.”

As you navigate Bizagi’s Web sites, Bizagi may also collect information through the use of commonly-used information-gathering tools, such as Cookies, Web Beacons and Other Technologies (“Website Tools”). Website Tools information includes standard information from your Web browser (such as browser type and browser language), your Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, your device used to connect to the website, and the actions you take on Bizagi’s Websites (such as the Webpages viewed and the links clicked).

From time to time, Bizagi and our third-party service providers may collect both Personal Information and Other Information from a variety of sources that fall into three categories: 1. Direct Interactions: From your use of and interaction with us through the Channels and other activities such as account creation, submission of registrations and forms, or sales inquiries and transactions; 2. Publicly Available Data / Data from Third Parties: Data from Automated Interactions on non-Bizagi Websites, or other data you may have made publicly available, such as social media posts, or data provided by third-party sources, such as marketing opt-in lists, or data aggregators; and, 3. Automated Interactions: From the use of technologies such as electronic communication protocols, cookies, embedded URLs or pixels, or widgets, buttons and tools.

The information that Bizagi collects, either directly or indirectly, may be combined to help us improve its overall accuracy and completeness and to help us better tailor our interactions with you.

Bizagi may collect information about the training classes you take. Bizagi may collect your name, your role and classes you complete. Bizagi may also collect test scores from these classes. We may collect this information from you or your employer.

4. Cookies, Web Beacons, and Other Technologies

Bizagi uses commonly-used information-gathering tools, such as cookies and Web beacons, to collect information as you navigate the Bizagi Websites (“Website Tools”). As described more fully below, our partners and Bizagi use these cookies or similar technologies to analyze trends, administer Websites and Services, track users’ movements around our Websites and Services, serve targeted advertisements and gather demographic information about our user base as a whole. This section describes the types of Website Tools Information used on the Bizagi Web sites and Services, and how this information may be used.

4.1. Cookies: A cookie is a small file placed on your computer’s hard drive (“Cookies”). It enables Bizagi’s Websites to identify your computer and collect information about how you browse Bizagi’s Websites. Cookies allow Bizagi’s Websites to: 1. store your preferences to present content, options or functions that are specific to you; and, 2. enable us to see information like how many people use the website and what pages they tend to visit.

4.1.1. Bizagi may use cookies to analyze our web traffic using an analytics package; aggregated usage data helps us improve the website structure, design, content, and functions; identify whether you are signed in to Bizagi’s Websites; a cookie allows us to check whether you are signed in to the site; test content on our website.
Please be aware that Cookies do not provide us with access to your computer or any information about you, other than that which you choose to share with us.

4.1.2. Managing Cookie Preferences: You can use your web browser’s cookie settings to determine how the Bizagi Websites use cookies. If you do not want Bizagi’s Websites to store cookies on your computer or device, you should set your web browser to refuse cookies. However, please note that doing this may affect how Bizagi’s Websites function. Some pages and services may become unavailable to you. Unless you have changed your browser to refuse cookies, Bizagi’s Websites will issue cookies when you visit them.

4.1.2.1 Managing information about you when you fill in a form or provide your details on Bizagi’s Websites, you will see one or more tickboxes allowing you to:

  • Opt-in to receive marketing communications from us by email, telephone, text message or post.
  • Opt-in to receive marketing communications from our third-party partners by email, telephone, text message or post.
  • If you have agreed that Bizagi can use your information for marketing purposes, you can change your mind quickly by managing your preferences here.

4.1.3. Flash Cookies: Bizagi may use Flash cookies, also known as "local shared objects," on our electronic Channels that employ Flash technology. Flash cookies are used to remember the site's settings to personalize the look and feel of the site. Like regular cookies, Flash cookies are represented as small files on your computer. One method of preventing Flash cookies from being placed is to adjust your preferences in the Macromedia website privacy settings panel at macromedia.com.

4.2. Embedded URLs: Bizagi may use a tracking technique that employs embedded URLs to allow the use of the electronic Channels without cookies. Embedded URLs allow limited information to follow you as you navigate the electronic Channels, but are not associated with Data and are not used beyond the session.

4.3. Embedded Pixels and Similar Technologies: On the electronic Channels, Bizagi and its service providers may use embedded pixel technologies to identify unique user visits (as opposed to aggregate hits) and for advertising purposes. Also, embedded pixels or other technologies may be used in e-mails and our online display advertising to provide information on when the e-mail or ad was opened to track marketing campaign responsiveness; information collected using these technologies may be associated with the recipient's e-mail address.

4.4. Widgets, Buttons, and Tools: Our electronic Channels may include widgets, which are interactive mini-programs that run on our site to provide specific services from another company (e.g., links to bookmarked sites), along with buttons or other tools that link to other companies' services (e.g., a "Like" button or third-party map). The widget, button or tool may collect and automatically send Data, such as your e-mail address, or other information (such as your browser information, or IP address), to a third party. Cookies may also be set or used by the widgets, buttons or tools to enable them to function properly or for other purposes, which may include advertising. Information collected or used by a widget, button or tool, including cookie settings and preferences, is governed by the privacy policy of the company that created it.

4.5. Physical Location: Bizagi may collect and share the physical location of your device for purposes consistent with this Privacy Statement, such as to provide you with personalized location-based services or content. In some instances, you may be permitted to allow or deny such use of your device's location, but if you choose to deny such use, we may not be able to provide you with the applicable personalized services or content.

4.6. Log Files, IP Addresses, URLs and Other Data: As is true of most websites, Bizagi gathers specific information automatically to analyze trends in the aggregate and administer Bizagi’s Websites and Services. This information may include your Internet Protocol (IP) address (or the proxy server you use to access the World Wide Web), device and application identification numbers, your location, your browser type, your Internet service provider and/or mobile carrier, the pages and files you viewed, your searches, your operating system and system configuration information, and date/time stamps associated with your usage. Due to Internet communications standards, when you visit or use Bizagi’s Websites and Services, Bizagi automatically receives the URL of the website from which you came and the website to which you go when you leave Bizagi’s Websites. This information is used to analyze overall trends, to help us improve Bizagi’s Websites and Services, to track and aggregate non-personal information, and to provide Bizagi’s Websites and Services. For example, Bizagi uses IP addresses to monitor the regions from which Customers and Visitors navigate the Bizagi’s Websites. Bizagi also collects IP addresses from Customers when they log into the Services as part of the Bizagi’s “Identity Confirmation” and “IP Range Restrictions” security features.

4.7. Social Media Features and Single Sign-on: Bizagi’s Websites may use social media features, such as the LinkedIn “like” button (“Social Media Features”). These features may collect your IP address and which page you are visiting on the Bizagi Websites, and may set a cookie to enable the feature to function properly. You may be given the option by such Social Media Features to post information about your activities on Bizagi’s Websites to a profile page of yours that is provided by a third-party social media network to share with others within your network. Social Media Features are either hosted by a third party or hosted directly on Bizagi’s Websites. Your interactions with these features are governed by the privacy policy of the company providing the relevant Social Media Features. Bizagi also allows you to log in to certain of Bizagi’s Websites and Services using sign-in services such as Linkedin, Microsoft and Google. These services will authenticate your identity and provide you the option to share certain personal information with us such as your name and email address to pre-populate our sign-up form.

4.8. Do Not Track: Currently, various browsers — including Internet Explorer, Firefox, and Safari — offer a “do not track” or “DNT” option that relies on a technology known as a DNT header, which sends a signal to websites’ visited by the user about the user's browser DNT preference setting. Bizagi does not currently commit to responding to browsers' DNT signals concerning the Bizagi Websites, in part, because no common industry standard for DNT has been adopted by industry groups, technology companies or regulators, including no consistent standard of interpreting user intent. Bizagi takes privacy and meaningful choice seriously and will make efforts to continue to monitor developments around DNT browser technology and the implementation of a standard.
