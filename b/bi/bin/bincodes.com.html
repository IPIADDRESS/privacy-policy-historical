BIN Codes

Bank Identification Numbers (BIN) & Issuer identification number (IIN) Search

Bank Identification Numbers ("BIN") or Issuer identification number ("IIN") is the first six digits of a bank card number or payment cards number and it is part of ISO/IEC 7812. It is commonly used in credit cards and debit cards, stored-value cards, gift cards and other similar cards.

BIN is beneficial to identify a card's brand, issuing institution or bank, country of issuance, card type and category of cards. This information is very useful in commercial business for fraud prevention, especially in online store.

An ISO/IEC 7812 number is up to 16 digits in length and used the following format

A BBBBB CCCCCCCCCC

where A BBBBB denote the Bank Identification Numbers ("BIN") or Issuer identification number ("IIN")

  • A specify the Major Industry Identifier (MII).
  • BBBBB specify the issuing institution or bank.
  • CCCCCCCCCC specify the individual account identifier.
