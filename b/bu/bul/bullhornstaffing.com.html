Bullhorn Safe Harbor Privacy Policy Statement

Bullhorn is committed to maintain data privacy rights for its customers and partners . In addition to our web privacy policy, Bullhorn self-certifies with the U.S. Department of Commerce's EU Safe Harbor Framework which concerns the transfer of data from the European Union to the United States. Bullhorn acknowledges and adheres to the privacy principles set forth in the Safe Harbor Framework in respect to all data transferred from the EU to the U.S. Additional information regarding Safe Harbor can be viewed at the Department of Commerce's site, http://www.export.gov/safeharbor/.

Bullhorn complies with the U.S. - E.U. Safe Harbor Framework as set forth by the U.S. Department of Commerce regarding the collection, use and retention of personal information from European Union member countries. Bullhorn Inc. has certified that it adheres to the Safe Harbor Privacy Principles of notice, choice, onward transfer, security, data integrity, access, and enforcement. To learn more about the Safe Harbor program, and to view Bullhorn's certification, please visit http://www.export.gov/safeharbor/.

The Department of Commerce's Safe Harbor Framework provides a set of principles that support the EU Data Directive of data protection. The following principles are part of Bullhorn's privacy protection process and are used in the collection and handling of data from the European Union.

  • Notice - Bullhorn will disclose the type of information being collected, its purpose, and any third party with whom data may be shared.
  • Choice - Bullhorn will offer the opportunity of Customers to opt-out of any transfer of data to third parties or use of collected data for purposes other than originally stated for and authorized unless permitted by Customer.
  • Onward Transfer - Any disclosure of information by Bullhorn to third parties will make the Notice and Choice principles applicable to the transfer unless permitted by Customer.
  • Transfer of data to parties acting as agents of Bullhorn will only be done if the third party itself meets the requirements of the Safe Harbor Framework or has been deemed adequate by the EU.
  • Security - Bullhorn takes reasonable precautions in its protection of data and any misuse, loss, destruction, alteration, and unauthorized access or disclosure.
  • Access - Bullhorn will facilitate access through Customer to personal information to individuals and allow them to correct, amend, or delete information where it is inaccurate unless the cost of such access is prohibitive in relation to the risk of privacy or where the rights of another individual would be violated.
  • Data Integrity - Bullhorn takes reasonable steps to provide that any personal information it collects is relevant for the purpose it is being used.
  • Enforcement - Bullhorn will attempt to reasonably resolve any privacy concerns directly with Customers. In the event a resolution cannot be made, Customers may engage Bullhorn's independent recourse mechanism, (TRUSTe).
