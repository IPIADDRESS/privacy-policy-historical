Radio One stockholders give permission for a reverse stock split

The board now has approval to do a reverse split of anywhere from 1-for-2 all the way to 1-for-50, at its discretion. Radio One stock (“ROIA”) has lived for much of the year around or below the level of $1 a share, and just two months ago it was able to tell the NASDAQ exchange that it had regained compliance of its $1-a-share minimum trade rule. Today – Radio One stock is around $3.80 a share. Other actions just announced from last week’s annual shareholders meeting in Greenbelt, MD: New directors were elected, including Terry Jones and Brian McNeill as Class A (outside) directors, and Cathy Hughes, Alfred Liggins, Geoffrey Armstrong, Ronald Blaylock and Doyle Mitchell as Class B shareholders. Ernst & Young will be the firm’s outside public accountant.
