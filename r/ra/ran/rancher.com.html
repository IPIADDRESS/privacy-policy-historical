Information Collection and Use

In general, you can visit rancher.com without telling us who you are or revealing any information about yourself. However, there may be instances where we collect personal information if you wish additional information or want to participate in various activities on our site. Some of those activities might include: requesting support, contacting us, forum participation, on-line meet-ups, requesting demonstrations, free trainings, pricing requests, downloading eBooks, or requesting information about partnering with Rancher. In general, we will collect the following information: name, company, job title, email address, phone number, country, state/region. We will use your information to correspond with you and for our general commercial purposes including to offer our own, our affiliates’ or third-party products and services that we think you may find of interest. You can elect not to receive emails from us by “unsubscribing” to an email following the instructions in the email you receive. We may also collect statistical information about Site visitors including their IP address. We use this aggregated non-personal information for research and commercial purposes.

Children’s Privacy

This Site is not designed nor intended to be attractive to use by children under the age of 13 (“Minors”). We do not knowingly collect information from minors. If you are under the age of 13, please do not submit any information to us.

Cookies and Do Not Track Signals

We use cookies to manage and improve our Site and to collect aggregate information about site usage by all of our users. Information about your computer hardware and software may be automatically collected by the Company. This information can include: your IP address, browser type, domain names, access times and referring website addresses. This information is used for the operation of the service, to maintain quality of the service, and to provide general statistics regarding use of the Company website. A cookie is a text file that our Site transfers to your computer’s hard drive for record-keeping purposes and to enhance the quality of your visit to our Site. The cookie assigns a random, unique number to your computer. We may use cookies that expire when you close your browser (session cookies) and cookies that remain on your browser for an extended period of time (persistent cookies).

We do not use technology that recognizes a “do-not-track” signal from your web browser. We may permit third parties to place cookies through the Site to provide us with better insights into the use of the Site or user demographics or to provide relevant advertising to you. These third parties may collect information about a consumer’s online activities over time and across different websites when he or she uses our website. These third parties may be affiliated companies, government entities, opposing parties, or acquiring companies. You can set your browser to refuse cookies, but doing so will limit system performance and may even cause certain features of the Site to malfunction or not to work at all.

Links

This website contains links to other sites. Please be aware that we are not responsible for the content or privacy practices of such other sites. We encourage our users to be aware when they leave our site and to read the privacy statements of any other site that collects personally identifiable information.

External Data Storage Sites

We may store your data on servers provided by third party hosting vendors with whom we have contracted.

Class Action Waiver

Disclosing Information

We may reveal your personal information to our affiliated companies for their commercial use. We may also reveal your information to service providers, government entities, opposing parties and acquiring companies. We may disclose your information to unaffiliated third parties: (1) if you request or authorize it; (2) to help complete a transaction for you; (3) to comply with the law, applicable regulations, governmental and quasi-governmental requests, court orders or subpoenas, to enforce our agreements, or to protect our rights, property or safety or the rights, property or safety of our users or others; (4) if the disclosure is done as part of a purchase, transfer or sale of services, assets, or stock; (5) if the disclosure is to our agents, outside vendors or service providers to perform functions on our behalf; or (6) as otherwise described in this Privacy Policy.
