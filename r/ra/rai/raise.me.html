Privacy Policy

Raise Labs, Inc. (together with raise.me, “Raise”) is committed to providing a safe, secure user experience. By choosing to create a Raise Profile you are agreeing to the terms of this Privacy Policy. This Privacy Policy sets forth the online data collection and usage policies and practices that apply to the use of the Raise website (our “Services”). Your use of our Services is also governed by our Terms of Use.


Users participate in Raise voluntarily. Raise is not responsible for the privacy policies or practices of other websites to which you choose to link from Raise.

What Information is Collected

Information collected may include, among other things, ‘Personal Information’ (which is information that can be used to identify or contact a specific individual, such as your name, phone number, address, and email address), account information (such as a password or other information that helps us confirm that it is you accessing your account) and demographic or other information (such as your school, gender, age or birthday, and other information about your interests and preferences). Any information combined with Personal Information will be treated as Personal Information.


You may have the opportunity to create a profile, which may include Personal Information, photographs, information about your academic and work history, your interests and activities, your use of the Services and other information (“Profile”).


The Services may provide you the opportunity to participate and post content publicly, through interactive features and through other communication functionality (“Community Forums”). You may choose through such features or otherwise, to submit or post reviews, problems, suggestions, ideas, solutions, questions, answers, comments, testimonials, resources, or other materials (“User Content”). Your User Content may also be posted in your profile. You may edit or remove your profile information, but you may not be able to remove answers or postings or other uploaded material. Note that anything you post to a community forum may be public. If you choose to voluntarily disclose Personal Information on a Community Forum, that information will be considered public information and the protections of this Privacy Policy will not apply.


In some instances, a User that wins a scholarship award may be required to provide additional information, such as proof of identity (which may include a driver’s license, passport, voting card or similar government issued identification), proof of academic and work history (which may include high school transcripts, standardized test scores, or references from teachers or counselors), or proof of financial need (which may include completing a FAFSA or CSS profile, and providing other family income documentation), in order to claim the award.


If a User does provide such information, he or she is thereby consenting to the use of that information in accordance with the policies and practices described in this Privacy Policy. The User maintains full rights and ownership of personal data. All information entered by a user is voluntary and at his or her discretion.

How Information is collected

Raise may collect data when a user initially logs onto Raise.me and opens an account (“Registration”), creates a Profile, completes a scholarship challenge, or during the use of other Services on the site.


Personal Information is mostly gathered directly from Users through online Registration forms. Only the User can modify his or her own, Raise Profile once it has been created. In some cases, with the User’s permission, the User’s school, school district, teachers, or parents may provide additional information about the User.

Usage of Personal Information by Raise

Raise uses personal information to provide information and Services that a User has requested; to service a User's account; to improve Raise's Services; to develop and/or inform Users of additional products or services we think will be of interest and benefit to Users; to answer questions Users may have; and to inform winners of scholarships awards. A user may deactivate their account at any time and their information will be removed from Raise’s servers or made anonymous.


Each User is able to edit and update their information provided in his or her Raise Profile or account settings. Raise may, on occasion, email our users to send notifications about new features on the site. Each User shall be allowed opt-out or back into to such email messaging.

Personal Information Usage by Third Parties

Raise will not knowingly sell, share, rent or otherwise transfer your information other than in accordance with the terms set forth in this Privacy Policy, or as otherwise provided by law.


We disclose information if legally required to do so, if requested to do so by a governmental entity or if we believe in good faith that such action is necessary to: (a) conform to legal requirements or comply with legal process; (b) protect our rights or property of our affiliated companies; (c) prevent a crime or protect national security; or (d) protect the personal safety of users or the public.


We do not disclose Users' Personal Information, combined personal and demographic information or information about use of Raise.com to Third Parties, except as set forth below:

If you are selected as a winner of a scholarship on Raise sponsored by a third party, such as a non-profit or Corporation, your profile information may be shared with a third-party scholarship administrator to select scholarship recipients. Cookies

We use cookie technology within Raise.me. We use cookies for remembering User names and passwords and for tracking click streams. Cookies cannot obtain personally identifiable information such as e-mail addresses, or any other information stored on our Users’ hard drives. Cookies are pieces of data normally stored by your browser, and you can quite easily prevent cookie data from being sent by adjusting your browser settings; however Raise.me may not function properly if Cookies are not enabled. One of the primary purposes of cookies is to provide a convenience feature to save time. The purpose of a cookie is to tell the web server that you have returned to a specific page. For example, if you personalize Raise pages, or register for services, a cookie helps us to recall your specific information. Because of our use of cookies, we can deliver faster and more accurate results and a more personalized site experience.

No data transmission over the Internet or any wireless network can be guaranteed to be secure. As a result, while we take sophisticated measures to protect your personal information, we cannot ensure or guarantee the security of any information you send to us and you do so at your own risk.
