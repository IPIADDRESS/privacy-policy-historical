Information Collection & Uses

Types of Users We have two different types of users of the site and it’s services. The type of user you are will determine the collection and use policies of your information. The first type is a client. A client is a RegOnline customer that is using the services we provide to create a registration form for an event. They are a company or an individual using the software and services from RegOnline to run and manage an event. The other type of user is an attendee. An attendee is a person signing up for an event through a registration page created by our services and software by a client. The types of information collected and the use of that information is detailed below.

Attendee information collected by RegOnline All attendee data that is collected and maintained through our service is solely owned by the client, and we have no rights to sell or use the information provided. The client, not RegOnline, is responsible for what information is collected and required on the registration forms. We will provide and maintain the secure storage of all information collected from the attendee and will not sell, use or rent the information collected to any third party.

Client information collected by RegOnline Clients purchasing service through RegOnline will be required to provide certain personally identifiable information. This will include personal information (such as name, email, phone, and shipping address) and financial information (such as credit number and expiration date). This information from clients is collected when a client signs up for a live demo, opens a new account, or calls and starts a new account over the phone.

The information collected is used for billing purposes and to send orders to the client or other involved third parties. If we have trouble relaying the order or the client cannot fill an order, the information provided will be used to contact the client. RegOnline does not share, rent, or sell this information to any third parties.

Attendee information collected by client’s registration pages Attendee information that is collected by RegOnline’s service is the sole property of the client. The client will decide what information will be required and collected as well as how they will use the information. RegOnline is responsible for safe and secure storage of all information collected but will not have any rights to sell or use the information provided.

Surveys From time-to-time we may provide you the opportunity to participate in contests or surveys on our site. If you participate, we will request certain personally identifiable information (such as demographic information, e-mail, phone, and address) from you. Participation in these surveys are completely voluntary and you therefore have a choice whether or not to disclose this information.

We use the information gathered in the survey to better personalize our service, improve the ease-of-use of our website, or to get customer feedback on changes or improvements that we have made. These surveys are conducted in-house. We will not share the personally identifiable information provided through your survey with any other third party unless we give you prior notice and choice.

Tell-A-Friend If you choose to use our referral service to tell a friend about our site, we will ask you for your friend’s name and email address. We will automatically send your friend a one-time email inviting him or her to visit the site with a link to our site. RegOnline does not store this information. Cookies & Site Stats

A cookie is a small text file that is stored on a user's computer for record-keeping purposes. We use cookies on this site, however we do not link the information stored in cookies to any personally identifiable information you submit while on our site. We only use session ID cookies to make it easier for you to navigate our site. A session ID cookie expires when you close your browser.

We also use Google Site Stats. This is a tracking feature offered by Google that works for Google’s ad subscribers. To measure performance, Google uses small strings of text (known as a cookie) that is placed on your computer when you click on ads. Cookies typically remain active on your computer for about 30 days. If you visit certain pages of the RegOnline’s website during that period, Google and the RegOnline will be able to tell that you saw the ad delivered by Google. We only use this type of cookie to see how effective our advertising is on Google’s website and will not link any information stored in the cookies to any personally identifiable information you submit while on our website.

Some of our business partners (e.g., advertisers, service providers, tracking utility companies) use cookies on our site. We have no access to or control over these cookies.

This privacy statement covers the use of cookies by this site only and does not cover the use of cookies by any third parties.
