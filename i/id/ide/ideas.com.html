Once any changes that We make to this Privacy Policy become effective, Your continued use of the Site will be subject to such revised terms.   2.

Information We Collect

  • Registration
    If You register to buy or sell ideas using ideas.com, We collect and store information that specifically identifies You or Your account ("Personally Identifiable Information"). Personally Identifiable Information includes information that You provide when registering for the Site, which currently includes Your User name, password, first name, last name, corporation name (if applicable), state and country of residence, email address, and other information as may be requested from You and that You choose to provide when You register. We also collect and store ideas, submissions or other content that You choose to provide to the Site or to other parties through the Site, including information You place on the Site and Your responses to contests, pulls, and promotions. We may also collect and track information related to Your interaction with our customer service, emails and letters that You send us, complaints, reports of abusive conduct, and information related to Your interaction other parties, including other members’ or users’ comments about You. We may, in the future, include other optional requests for information from registered users, to help us tailor the Site for and deliver personalized information to these users.We may also store IP addresses, login times, pages visited and referrer logs as part of Your account information to help us identify You and to protect our Site against abuse.

  • The Use of Cookies
    The "help" portion of the toolbar on most browsers will tell You how to prevent Your browser from accepting new cookies, how to have the browser notify You when You receive a new cookie, or how to disable cookies altogether. However, You will not be able to participate in our Site if Your browser does not accept cookies. Because Your information is required to ensure proper credit to any ideas You submit, ideas cannot be submitted with cookies disabled. If You are not a registered User, You may view some portions of the Site without having cookies turned on, but You will not have access to other portions of the Site (for example, portions that allow You to post information and communicate with other parties) without accepting cookies.

While we will use commercially reasonable efforts to protect Your Personally Identifiable Information, we cannot promise and You should not expect that Your information will remain at all times private.

    We may send You email communications including information that We think may be of interest to You. You may opt-out of receiving these promotional email communications, either when You register, or at any time thereafter by sending a message to Unsubscribe@ideas.com. We use non-identifying and aggregated information to better design our Web site and to share with advertisers. For example, We may tell an advertiser that X number of individuals visited a certain area on our Web site, but We would not disclose anything that could be used to identify those individuals. If We use or share with third parties the Personally Identifiable Information provided to us online in ways unrelated to the ones described above, We will provide You an opportunity to opt-out of such uses.

   
  4.

Our Commitment To Data Security

To prevent unauthorized access, maintain data accuracy, and ensure the correct use of information, We will use commercially reasonable methods to keep Personally Identifiable Information securely in our files and systems. Please note that with current technology, We cannot guarantee the security of Your data as it travels over the Internet. We work to protect the security of Your information during transmission by using Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) software, which encrypts information You input. It is important for You to protect against unauthorized access to Your password and to Your computer. Be sure to sign off when finished using a shared computer. If You give access to Your User Name and password to another party, You may be held responsible for such party’s actions using Your account and You may lose protection for Your Personally Identifiable Information. If You believe that Your password is no longer secure, You may change Your password in the “My Profile” section of the Web site after you login.

   
  5.

Our Commitment To Children's Privacy

Protecting the privacy of children is especially important. For that reason, We do not collect or maintain information at our Web site from those We actually know are under 13, and no part of our Web site is structured to attract anyone under 13. To register and/or submit an idea to ideas.com a user must be at least 18 years of age.

   
  6.

Terminating Your Membership

To terminate Your membership, send email to Service@ideas.com. There may be a delay in processing Your termination request and in updating our records, and this may result in a delay in terminating any active use of Your Personally Identifiable Information that is permitted under this Privacy Policy. In the event that Your membership terminates, We may continue to store Your Personally Identifiable Information for our own internal business records. We may at our discretion delete Your Personally Identifiable Information at any time after termination, in which event, Your Personally Identifiable Information cannot be recovered. You may continue to receive email communications until You have requested to be removed from our email lists by following the procedure herein.

  2. In addition, You may also be able to modify information found in the My Idea Quests and the My Targeted and Public Ideas pages.

  3. For other requests, please email Service@ideas.com

This Site provides users the opportunity to opt-out of receiving promotional email communications from us by contacting Unsubscribe@ideas.com.
