Case's Ladder/Intermix Network Privacy Policy

The Intermix Network of websites (the "Network") provides diversionary entertainment, topical news, information, editorial content and on-line shopping to millions of users and subscribers on a daily basis. We recognize the importance of safeguarding the on-line privacy of our users. The Network recognizes the importance of safeguarding the on-line privacy of its users. The information collected from users of, and subscribers to, the various sites comprising the Network will be used in accordance with the Privacy Policy set forth below. However, this Privacy Policy does not cover web sites or web pages that you may access or link to that are outside the Network.

Collection of Information

Information that is Automatically Collected. When a user accesses a Network site, our web server automatically collects and stores the user's domain name, the date and time of access to the site and the Internet address of the web site that linked you to our site.

Information Voluntarily Provided. We collect and store information that is voluntarily provided when a user signs up for a site membership, purchases a product, responds to a survey, signs up for a promotion or to receive or send electronic newsletters, greetings and other emails or content such as weekly jokes or enters a contest sponsored by a Network site. When a membership or a product is purchased, the user provides: name, mailing address, email address, telephone number and credit card number and credit card expiration date in order to complete the transaction. If a user enters a contest or signs up for a promotion, the user provides: name and email address. At various times, users may be asked to voluntarily provide demographic information such as address, age range, gender, occupation, interests or hobbies. Also, in connection with signing up for, or subscribing to, services or features offered across the Network, a user may be requested to provide optional background information in order to identify users and their interests.

Use of Cookies and Storage of Information. We use cookies to collect and store information. Cookies are small files that the user's web browser places on the user's hard drive for record keeping purposes. Cookies permit us to identify the user and to recall from the Network's database the user's email address and other information that the user has voluntarily provided. Third-party advertising companies that may contract with the Network sites also use cookies. For more information about third-party advertising companies and removal of their cookies, see below - Uses of Information.

Uses of Information

We use the information that we collect from users in a variety of ways, including to process membership or purchase transactions, to improve the content of the Network sites to address user preferences and to attract new users, to track user trends and patterns on the Internet so that users may be informed about products and services of interest and, in some instances, to properly credit third parties that have referred users to the site. Stored information is also used to pre-populate information fields for purchases, contests or promotions.

Unless a user elects not to receive offers and promotions from sites within the Network or from third-parties, the Network shares individual information with other reputable organizations including its affiliates, its advertisers, its marketing partners and third-party advertising companies that are interested in marketing to the Network users. Demographic information is usually reported in aggregate to third parties who are interested in knowing what type of audience the Network attracts. We use this information to ensure that ads, offers and promotions are relevant and of value to users. Our advertisers and marketing partners who contract directly with the Netork sites are required to confirm that they will comply with the Network's Privacy Policy with respect to information collected through ads displayed on any Network site. Third-party advertising companies use cookies to measure and improve the effectiveness of their clients' ads. To do this, advertisers may use anonymous information about a user's visit to a Network site (as well as other websites) including what banner ad was shown, the date and time it was shown, the cookie and the IP address. This information may also be used for online preference marketing purposes. Visit Network Advertising Initiative's ("NAI") gateway opt-out site www.networkadvertising.org to review the privacy policies of third-party advertising companies and/or opt-out of third-party advertising cookies.

Users always have the ability through their account page to update, change or delete information that we have collected via the Network. Users may also unsubscribe from any email list, opt-out of receiving third party ads or prevent the disclosure of your information to any third party by sending the Network an email at support@intermix.com. Please be aware that it may take up to 48 hours for your change to take effect.

In the event that Intermix Media merges with another company or transfers or sells substantially all of its assets to a third party, all information stored on the Network server would be included in the merger, transfer or sale. Also, if required by law, information may be disclosed to a third party.

Intermix Media reserves the right to change its privacy policy at any time.

Protection of Information

To prevent unauthorized access, maintain data accuracy and ensure the correct use of personal information, we use appropriate industry standard procedures to safeguard the confidentiality of personal information including the use of firewall, encryption, token authentication, application proxies, monitoring technology and adaptive analysis of network traffic to track abuse of the Network and its data. All transmissions of information are through secure computer servers that that also run authentication and Secure Sockets Layer ("SSL") 128-bit software data encryption software to encrypt all personal information including your name, address and credit card number so that it cannot be read as the information travels over the Internet. Access to registered users' accounts is password protected.

Children's Privacy Statement

In general, we do not direct our activities or market products that are appropriate for children under the age of 13. It is our policy to refrain from knowingly collecting personally identifiable information from children 12 or under. If future site activity is directed at children 12 or under, it is our policy to include parental notice and/or consent features consistent with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act.

Questions about Privacy

If you have any questions about privacy, please email us at support@intermix.com.
