Flexential has certified to the Department of Commerce that it adheres to the Privacy Shield Principles for both of the Privacy Shield Frameworks, and is subject to the investigatory and enforcement powers of the U.S. Federal Trade Commission. To learn more about the Privacy Shield Program, and to view our certification, please visit https://www.privacyshield.gov/.

Flexential’s accountability for personal data we receive under each of the Privacy Shield Frameworks and subsequently transfer to a third party is described in the applicable Privacy Shield Principles. In particular, Flexential remains responsible and liable under the applicable Privacy Shield Principles if third-party agents that we engage to process personal data on our behalf to do so in a manner inconsistent with those Principles, unless Flexential proves that we are not responsible for the event giving rise to the damage.

Flexential commits to resolve complaints about collection or use of your Personal Information. Individuals in the European Union or Switzerland with inquiries or complaints regarding our Privacy Shield policy should first contact Flexential via email at privacy@flexential.com or via mail at 8809 Lenox Pointe Drive, Suite G, Charlotte, NC 28273 Attn: Privacy & Security Officer. For any Privacy Shield complaints that cannot be resolved with Flexential directly, Flexential has committed to refer unresolved Privacy Shield complaints to the International Centre for Dispute Resolution/American Arbitration Association, an alternative dispute resolution provider located in the United States. As further explained in the Privacy Shield Principles for each of the Privacy Shield Frameworks, a binding arbitration option will also be made available to you in order to address residual complaints not resolved by any other means.

Definitions

“Anonymous Information” means information that does not personally identify an individual, including aggregate demographic information.

“Cookies” means small files that are stored by your web browser to help a particular system recognize you and the pages you visited in a website. Flexential’s Website uses cookies to store your account information between sessions and to maintain information regarding how you navigate our website. Flexential does not collect any Personal Information from cookies. Most browsers automatically accept cookies but you can set your browser to refuse cookies, though you may not be able to use all the features of this website. Flexential may use third party cookies (i.e. a cookie planted by a third party such as Google).

“Customer” means an entity which receives services from Flexential pursuant to a written agreement.

“Information” means, collectively, Personal Information, Anonymous Information and Sensitive Information.

“Partner” means an entity which participates in the Flexential Partner Network Program pursuant to a written agreement(s).

“Partner Network Program” means one or more partner programs operated by Flexential allowing Partners to, among other things: refer prospective customers to Flexential and receive a referral fee or an on-going commission fee; maintain end-user relationships on Participating Partner contracts; or manage billing functionality for Customers.

“Personal Information” means information that can identify a person as a specific individual, such as name, phone number, email address, Social Security number or credit card number. Personal Information includes the information provided by Customers or Partners which may be necessary for Flexential to provide the Flexential services. Personal Information includes Personal Information provided by Partners.

“Sensitive Information” means personal information that reveals a natural person’s race; ethnic origin; political, philosophical or religious beliefs; trade union membership; information specifying a person’s sex life; or that concerns a natural person’s health or medical condition.
