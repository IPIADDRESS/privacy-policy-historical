What other types of information may be collected via this website?

We also may collect and store non-personal information that is generated automatically as you navigate through our website.  For example, to help make the site more responsive to the needs of users, we may employ a standard feature of browser software, called a “cookie,” to assign each user a unique, random number that resides on a user's computer.  The cookie does not personally identify the user; it merely identifies the computer with which the user accesses the website.  Unless you voluntarily identify yourself (through registration, for example), the website will not be able to identify you, even if a cookie is assigned to the computer.  The only personal information a cookie can contain is information supplied by a user. 

 

Cookies simplify and improve navigation and personalize your website experience by allowing a website server that hosts a site to recognize your browser when you visit certain web pages.  Also, by showing how and when visitors use the website, cookies help us track user trends and patterns.  They also prevent you from having to re-enter your preferences on certain areas of the site where you have entered preference information before.  We also use cookies to make sure you don't see the same ad repeatedly during a single visit to our website.  Our advertisers and third parties that provide products and/or services through our website may also assign their own cookies, a process we don't control.  The help function on most web browsers contains information on how to set your browser to notify you before accepting a cookie or to disable cookies entirely.  However, if you set your web browser not to accept cookies, you may be unable to take advantage of the personalized features enjoyed by other users of our website.

 

In addition, our web server may automatically collect limited information about your computer configuration when you visit our website, including the type of browser software you use, the operating system you’re running, the resolution of your computer monitor, the website that referred you, and your IP address.  We may use this information to identify your computer so that data (such as the web pages you want to view) can be transmitted to you.  We also may use this information for systems administration and trouble shooting purposes.

To help us keep track of how many users are accessing particular pages within our website and seeing particular ads, we also may place a small file, called a web beacon, on some of our pages.  These web beacons allow us to track the number of users who view particular pages and particular ads on those pages, so as to report ad performance to the applicable advertiser.  Third party advertisers that post banner ads or other forms of advertisements and/or links on our website may use their own web beacons to collect information.  Insight does not have control over web beacons utilized by third party advertisers.
