Because of the financial nature of our business, our Web sites are not designed to appeal to children under the age of 13. Therefore, we don't knowingly attempt to solicit or receive any information from children.

HOW AND WHY WE USE WEB TECHNOLOGIES TO MANAGE OUR WEB SITE.

Here is how and why we use some common Web technologies to help manage our Web site:

  • Cookies. A "cookie" is a small piece of information that our Web site may provide to your browser while you are at our site. The Intuit.com Web site supplies your browser with cookies that contain a unique identifier used to better understand Web site usage in the aggregate so that we know what areas of our site users prefer, based on the number of visits to those areas. We also use cookies to verify your identity and provide you access to areas of our Web site that are limited to registered users. You may receive additional cookies when you request a service through this site. A description of how these additional cookies are used is provided in the section Web Site Usage Data.

    If you are an Intuit Public Sector Solutions customer, you will receive a cookie when responding to a URL from a promotional communication. These cookies are used to determine response rates to our communications. You may choose not to accept these cookies and will still have full access to site functionality.

    If you simply want to browse, you do not have to accept cookies from our site, although choosing not to accept cookies could limit your ability to use certain areas of the Web site. Should you decide, however, that you would like to register for a service and you have modified your browser settings not to accept cookies, you will need to re-set your browser to accept the cookies that we send. Most browsers are defaulted to accept and maintain cookies.

    Advertising networks that serve ads on this Web site may assign different cookies to you. Those cookies may be used to track your involvement with the advertising on our site. You may choose not to accept these cookies. Intuit does not control those parties. You should review their privacy policies to learn more about what, why and how they collect and use private information. If you have questions about our ad serving networks, please contact us at https://privacy.intuit.com.

  • Web Beacons. Web beacons (also known as single-pixel or clear GIFs) are graphical images that we may use to set and access cookies as described in the section Cookies. Web beacons allow us to determine which pages and features of our site are accessed by our users, as described in the Web Site Usage Data section below. In this way we can determine what areas of our site are most useful to you. Web beacons are also used when we send you e-mail, as described in the section E-Mail Messaging.

  • Web Site Usage Data. Our Web site tracks usage data, including, for example, your IP address, your browser type and version, which pages you view, which page, if any, linked you to our site, and which link, if any, you follow off of our site. We use this data in the aggregate to better understand Web site activity to improve our site offerings. We may also use this data to provide you a more personalized Web site experience, assistance with technical support questions, and to send you special offers, product and service updates, or other promotional materials that are relevant and tailored to your interests. If you do not wish to receive these offers or promotions, simply indicate your contact preferences during the registration process or by visiting https://privacy.intuit.com. We do not share your information with outside companies for their promotional use. We do not track URLs that you type into your browser, nor do we track you across the Internet once you leave our sites.
