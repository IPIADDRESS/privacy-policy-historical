  • The 'drinkable books' that are saving lives

    The BBC recently reported on 'drinkable books' which contain filter paper and instructions on how and why water should be filtered. Once read, the pages can be torn out and used to filter dirty water into drinkable water.

Privacy Policy

IWA is committed to protecting the privacy of its members and registered users of the site. The following statement is valid for the IWA group sites: IWAHQ and IWA Publishing.

Registering for promotional information on the site: When registering on the site for email alerts we ask you for one or some of the following information depending which service you are registering for: Title, Name, Email address, Organization, Job title, Address, User name, Password. This information is used so that we can provide specific services and information to our members and website users and help us develop new products to best serve the water community as a whole.

IWA does not, under any circumstances, sell, trade or hire any personal details submitted via the IWA website to any 3rd party. Only subscribed IWA members can view and amend their membership details. Only subscribed IWA members who are enrolled members of a Specialist Group can view other members of the same group contact details. If you do not want your contact details to be accessible to other IWA members via the website, please contact water@iwahq.org.uk to have your details removed from the website. No contact details for members of IWA and registered users of the IWA website are in the public domain.

You can unsubscribe from any service you have registered for on the IWA website at any time by using the links on the website, or by emailing water@iwahq.org.uk. You can unsubscribe from any service you have registered for on the IWA Publishing website at any time by using the links on the website, or by emailing publications@iwap.co.uk.

In accordance with the requirements of the Data Protection Act 1998 the information you provide is kept secure. IWA has implemented appropriate physical, electronic and managerial security procedures in order to protect personal information from loss, misuse, alteration or destruction. IWA regularly reviews security and encryption technologies and will strive to protect information to the fullest extent possible. However, IWA cannot warrant the security of any information you transmit before it reaches us.

Any information submitted in the purchase of books on the Turpin website is subject to the privacy policy of Turpin Limited. Further details can be found by contacting Turpin. From time to time it may be necessary for us to review and revise our privacy policy. We reserve the right to change our privacy policy at any time.
