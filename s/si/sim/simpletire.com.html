Federal Excise Tax (FET) is collected on many consumable products sold in the United States, such as alcohol, gasoline and tires. While FET on tires has been based on a tire's physical weight for years, since January 1, 2005 the method for calculating Federal Excise Tax has been based on the tire's maximum rated load carrying capacity.

 
Regardless of the tire's physical weight, tires with a maximum load carrying capacity under 3,500 pounds (Load Index <124) will not be charged FET, where tires with a maximum load carrying capacity above 3,500 pounds (Load Index >124) will. The FET formula is based on the amount of the tire's maximum rated load capacity if it exceeds 3,500 pounds and is assessed on radial tires at a rate of 9.45 cents for every 10 pounds of tire load capacity above 3,500 pounds (bias ply tires are assessed at a rate of 4.725 cents).

While there was a time when the Federal Government charged excise tax on all tires, the number of tires with FET has been dramatically reduced. Only a handful of consumer-oriented passenger and light truck tires are assigned an FET under the new formula.
 
Excise tax amounts can range from pennies to several dollars per tire and will appear as "Additional Fees and Taxes" before the order is processed online.
