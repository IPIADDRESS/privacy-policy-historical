    You should read this Privacy Policy in its entirety before submitting any Personal Information so you know how we collect and share your Personal Information.

    Unless otherwise defined in this Privacy Policy, capitalized terms used in this Privacy Policy have the same meanings as in our (i) Mighty AI – Spare5 Terms of Service and Use Agreement and (ii) Mighty AI – Customer Terms of Service. By “Services” in this Privacy Policy, we mean “Services” as they are defined in the Mighty AI Customer Terms and Conditions if you are a Customer (as defined therein) or the “Spare5 Services” as they are defined in the Mighty AI – Spare5 Terms of Service and Use Agreement if you are a Service Provider (as defined therein).

    1. The Services

    By using our Services, you agree to our collection, storage, use and disclosure of your Personal Information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy, you may not use our Services.

    2. Information Collection

    2.1 Information We Collect From You

    We collect Personal Information such as your name, image or likeness, phone number, email address and home postal address, and other information, such as your credit card, PayPal or bank account number and related information.

    Some of the times that we may collect your Personal Information include when you are:

    • Creating or updating your Account
    • Sending us an email message or other communication
    • Providing us with your contact information to receive marketing or promotional materials
    • Authorizing the disclosure of your Personal Information to us via third party authentication systems such as Facebook, LinkedIn, and others
    • Using the App to perform Tasks, including communications with third parties

    We may also collect other information that you choose to provide. This includes items such as your zip code (on its own), individual preferences related to your Account and demographic information.

    2.2 Other Ways We Collect Information

    • Images. We collect images, videos and related image/recording data (e.g., pedestrians, cars, license plates and street signs) taken by from autonomous vehicles sent to us by Customers who would like to use our Services. We also obtain and other images and recordings from a Service Provider to enhance our Services.
    • Site and App Log Data. The Site and App automatically collect information from the computer or mobile device used to access the Site or use the App, such as the date and time you accessed the Site or App, your IP address, the URL you accessed and the referring URL, the operating system you are using and any content you post to the Site. This information is stored and may be disclosed to others and permanently archived for future use.
    • Cookies. As is commonplace on the Internet, we use cookies, a small piece of code that enables Web servers to “identify” visitors, by associating your browser and computer with your use of the Site each time you initiate a session on our websites. The information you provide while using our Services may be stored in these cookies or may be stored on our servers, depending on the information. You may take steps to block or remove cookies from your own computer at any time, but the site may not function properly if you do.
    • Pixel tags. From time to time we may use “pixel tags,” also known as “web beacons,” which are small graphic files that allow us to monitor the use of our Sites, emails and other communications that we send to you. A pixel tag can collect information such as the Internet Protocol (“IP”) address of the computer that downloaded the page or opened the email on which the tag appears; the URL of the page on which the pixel tag appears; the time the page containing the pixel tag was viewed; the browser type and language; the device type; geographic location; and the identification number of any cookie on the computer previously placed by that server. When corresponding with you via HTML capable email, we or our third party-service providers may use “format sensing” technology, which allows pixel tags to let us know whether you received and opened our email.
    • Third-party web components and Advertising. From time to time we may use third-party web components to display advertising, maps or rich text editors. These third-party advertising partners may set tracking tools (e.g., cookies) to collect information regarding your activities (e.g., your IP address, page(s) visited, time of day). We may also share this information with third-Party advertising partners. These advertising partners may use this information (and similar information collected from other websites) for purposes of delivering targeted advertisements to you when you visit non-Mighty AI related websites within their networks. This practice is commonly referred to as “interest-based advertising” or “online behavioral advertising.” You may block or restrict the placement of cookies on your computer or remove them from your browser by adjusting your web browser’s preferences.
    • Location-Based Information. For Service Providers, when you use our App, we may collect and store information about your location by converting your IP address into a rough geo-location or by accessing your mobile device’s GPS coordinates or coarse location if you enable location services on your device. We may also use location information in connection with our Fives to verify your compliance with our Mighty AI – Spare5 Terms of Service and Use Agreement.
    • Do Not Track (“DNT”) is a privacy preference that users can set in certain web browsers. DNT is a way for users to inform websites and services that they do not want certain information about their webpage visits collected over time and across websites or online services. Mighty AI does not recognize or respond to browser-initiated DNT signals. For information about “do-not-track”, visit allaboutdnt.org.

    3.5 Improve Services: We may use your Personal Information to identify when you’ve logged into the Services, to tell us how you interact with the Services, to track and measure the performance of our Services, to monitor how many visitors use or view our Services, to monitor the effectiveness of our advertising, specifications of the device you use to access the Services and its related components, the pages on our Services you’ve browsed, and other statistics.

This information is used for a variety of functions, including the measurement of visitors’ interest in and use of various portions or features of the Sites. Anonymized or aggregated information is not Personal Information, and we may use such information in a number of ways, including research, internal analysis, analytics, training, machine learning and any other legally permissible purposes.
