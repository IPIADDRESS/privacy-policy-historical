Information Collected Through Secure Browser

When an End User installs and uses Secure Browser, certain information is collected from such End User, and this section describes how we collect, use and share such information. Universally Unique Identifier – When you install the Secure Browser, a universally unique identifier (“UUID”) is created that identifies that particular installation of the Secure Browser software and the identification of the computer where it is installed. This information does not identify a particular person. Safer uses this information for updates, debugging, reporting and system maintenance.

Search Queries – The Secure Browser includes search functionality from a Search Provider selected by Safer. When you enter a search query using the search function included in the Secure Browser, your query is transmitted to the Search Provider. The Search Provider’s use of the search query, any results and other information collected, used or disclosed associated with the search is governed by their privacy policy. Safer does not maintain a record of you search queries, although the Secure Browser installed on your computer may maintain a history of your recent search activity to improve your browsing experience. You may delete this locally-stored search history, or prevent such local storage by enabling the private browsing mode, at any time through the user controls contained within the Secure Browser. Any time you use the Secure Browser to access a Search Provider, such as by sending a search query, the Secure Browser may send the Search Provider standard, limited information including some or all of the following: your Internet Protocol address, browser type, browser language, and the date and time of your request. Our current Search Providers include, but are not limited to the following, and their privacy policies may be found at the URLs listed below:

Yahoo! (https://policies.yahoo.com/us/en/yahoo/privacy/products/search/index.htm)

Search Auto-Suggest – The Search Provider may also provide functionality whereby search queries are automatically suggested by the Search Provider when you enter a search query. These recommendations are generated based upon the internal anonymous query database of maintained by the Search Provider. Additionally, if you have a separate account with the Search Provider, to which you are logged-in when transmitting a search query, the Search Provider may take into consideration non-personally identifying information about you in its database when generating search query recommendations. This information is not stored by Safer.

Bookmarks – The Secure Browser allows users to bookmark web pages for quick reference and access. Secure Browser may record this information and when End-Users interact with these bookmarks. This information does not contain personally identifying information and is not disclosed by Safer to third parties.

Usage Reporting – The Secure Browser automatically transmits information to us on how End-User interact with our software. We use this information in aggregate form to improve our internal operations and software. End-Users may opt out of this transmission of information by adjusting their preferences within the Secure Browser, except for information regarding the version of the Secure Browser in use and the browser extensions that are enabled. The browser version and extension information does not contain personally identifying information and is used by us to understand the versions of the Secure Browser that are in use and to provide critical software updates.

Geolocation Data – The Secure Browser allows users to share their geolocation information with third-party websites and services. Users may share geolocation by enabling this feature, which is provided by Google. Use of your geolocation information will be governed by the applicable terms of service and privacy policies of Google and the third party websites and services with whom you choose to share this information and users are encouraged to review such terms and polices before sharing their geolocation information. Safer does not collect or store End-User geolocation information.

Third Party Sign On – The Secure Browser may provide the option for you to sign onto one or more third party services (such as Facebook or Twitter). To use this feature, you will need to provide personal information, such as you user name and password, in order to link and sign onto the third-party service. This information is not be transmitted to us and your use of this feature is at your option.
