USE OF COOKIES

A cookie is a text file that a website transfers to your computer's hard drive for record-keeping purposes and to enhance the quality of your visit to the website.  Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer.

Like other major websites, we may use cookie technology to provide us with information concerning your navigation to various areas on our site and to help us analyze the traffic on our site.  We do not use cookies to read any information on your computer.  Most browsers are initially set to accept cookies but can be configured to refuse cookies or warn you before a cookie is placed on your computer.  Please consult the documentation for your particular browser for details.

Web beacons, also known as clear gif technology or action tags, may be used to assist in delivering cookies on our site.  This technology tells us how many visitors clicked on key elements (such as links or graphics) on a particular web page.  We do not use this technology to access your personal information, but we do use it to compile aggregated statistics about usage of our website.  We may share aggregated site statistics with marketing partners but do not allow unaffiliated companies to place clear gifs on our sites.
