What personal information do we collect?

We do not collect or retain personally identifiable information of users. In other words, no information which we collect is aimed or used to identify the user or enables us to identify the user.  However, we collect and use the IP address that an Internet service provider allocated to your computer and that you use to access the Internet. In certain cases and given the legal authorization in the proper procedure, the IP address, together with other details, may lead to revealing the person who used the computer, for legal proceeding or other purposes as the law may authorize. However, we do not connect the IP address with any information that can reasonably identify you and we do not make any efforts to know your identity. Therefore, unless you provide us specific identifying details, for example, when you contact us, the personal information that we collect will remain non-identifying.

What non personally identifying information do we collect?

We collect and retain some non-personally identifiable information (non-PII) such as the country from which you access the Internet (based on the country of residence of the Internet service provider that allocated the IP address to you), the type of browser that you use and its version, the Software installation date, the operating system that you use, your default search provider, status of the Software installation, unique user and affiliate ID generated by the Software, the source URL of the Webpage you have been visited, date and timestamp of marking the text. This information enables us to provide you the service, and to constantly improve it by understanding your online behavior. We do not use it to reveal your identity.

Why do we collect this information?

We collect user’s online behavior information in order to optimize and improve our services and the user online experience as well as targeting potential new users. This information is statistically processed, for aggregated analytics, and enables us to better understand user’s preferences and interest.

How do we collect information?

When you install the software and use our Services, we may collect and retain the stated information by using several technical modules. Such modules may include using your system’s reports to our servers as part of the installation process or as part of the advertisements display, or by storing a cookie and pixel tag on your computer to help us better understand your online behavior and preferences. A cookie is a small piece of data sent from a website and stored in a user’s web browser while a user is browsing a website. This cookie shall be stored until you delete it from your computer. At all times, you may choose not to accept cookies from us or delete existing cookies, by accessing your browser preferences. However, some services or options provided by the Software or Services may be dependent on the use of cookies and not be available otherwise. We may also use raw logs which include some non-personally identifiable information about you as stated above (and is not allowing us to personally identify you in any manner). Such information is available in its form for less than 24 hours, before it becomes aggregative. Raw logs are plain text files containing information about visitors and their activity on certain websites.
