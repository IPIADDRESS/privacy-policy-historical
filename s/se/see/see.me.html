This started as a small idea,
that grew...

A few years ago I was having dinner in Brooklyn with Jason Goodman, founder of the creative space 3rd Ward. We were talking about the experience of struggling artists. It was something we’d both experienced firsthand, and we wanted to take a new approach to supporting the creative dialogue we are so passionate about.

We worked with hundreds of artists in our neighborhood and knew there were millions more around the world with similar needs and ambitions.

That night I drew up the idea of a dynamic online community that gives creatives of all types access to tools to promote their work while connecting them with real-world opportunities and financial support.

That dream became a plan and, after two years of late nights and bad coffee, that plan became reality in See.Me.

We Built See.Me To Foster a Creative Community.

Our mission has always been simple: we support creatives. From years working on outsider artistic projects in the streets, warehouses and forgotten places of New York City, I deeply appreciate the need for support, recognition and community.

More than anything, I understand the need for artists to have ongoing financial support.

With that, we’ve built a visual social network that allows creatives of all types to have a permanent home on the web. And now we are releasing a feature that allows creatives, like you, to earn money directly through your See.Me profile.

The See.Me Community is built on generosity.

If you choose, you can accept contributions through your See.Me profile so that anyone, anywhere in the world can support your creative work with tips. Think of it like the old system of patronage, but instead of one wealthy family supporting a handful of artists, millions of viewers can contribute ongoing financial support to a wide and varied community.

You can easily add rewards to encourage contributions and your supporters will receive monthly updates as you add new work and come up with new ideas. There are no limitations on how much you can make, and you can accept contributions for as long as you like.

It’s an honor to serve the hundreds of thousands of creatives who use our site, and we can’t wait to see the creative ways you will use these tools.

Sincerely,

William Etundi Jr.
Founder & CEO of See.Me

p.s. To check-out the first users accepting contributions, visit Pete Eckert, an accomplished photographer from Sacramento, Anya Sapozhnikova a Brooklyn-based circus performer, and Aaron Taylor Kuffner, who is accepting support for a fully robotic Gamelan Orchestra.
