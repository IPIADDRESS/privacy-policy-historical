Cookies
A cookie is a small amount of data that often includes an anonymous unique identifier, which is sent to your browser from a Web site's computer and stored on your computer's hard drive. Each Web site can send its own cookie to your browser if your browser's preferences allow it, but (to protect your privacy) your browser only permits a Web site to access the cookies it has already sent to you, not the cookies sent to you by other sites.

Segway uses cookies to store your user ID on your computer, allowing personalization of our Web site. The cookie is placed on your computer only when you log into our Web site and is removed when you log out.

You can configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is set. (Each browser is different, so check the "Help" menu of your browser to learn how to change your cookie preferences.) You may reject all cookies sent to you without experiencing any loss of Web site functionality.

Log Files
We use IP addresses to analyze trends, administer the site, track user’s movement, and gather broad demographic information for aggregate use. IP addresses are not linked to personally identifiable information. These log files contain statistical data used to analyze traffic patterns, specifically the user's Internet Protocol (IP) address, the date and time of the visit, the file requested by the user, the size of the file requested by the user, the status of the request (e.g., whether the request was successfully served by our Web server software), and the referring URL that generated the request. This data is not tied in with any personally identifiable information and is used in aggregate form only. We will not share demographic information, aggregated or otherwise, with anyone.
