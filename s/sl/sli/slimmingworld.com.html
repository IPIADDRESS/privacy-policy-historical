Slimming World Privacy Policy

Miles Bramwell USA, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company, d/b/a Slimming World (“Slimming World”, “us”, “we,” or “our”), has created this Privacy Policy (“Policy”) in order to demonstrate our firm commitment to the privacy of your personal information. The following discloses our information gathering and dissemination practices if you visit our website (“Website”) or if you use one or more of our products or services, including your use of our various mobile applications (“Apps”), including the Slimming World Magazine (“Magazine”) either via our Magazine mobile application (“Magazine App”) or via the browser-based edition on our Website, or our blog (“Blog”) (collectively, our “Online Products and Services”).

This Policy tells you, among other things, what information we gather from you, how we may use or disclose that information, and our efforts to protect it. Please read this Policy carefully and make sure you fully understand and agree with it before you access our Online Products and Services.

What Types of Information Do We Collect?

For purposes of this Policy:

  • “Slimming World” means Slimming World and all other wholly owned groups;

  • “Partner” means a business that Slimming World works with to maintain or improve our services, or perform services on our behalf;

  • “Third Party” means a business that is outside the Slimming World family and that is not a Partner.

In connection with your use of our Online Products and Services, we may collect the following information:

  • The email addresses of users who communicate with us via our Online Products and Services or via email;

  • Information volunteered or submitted by the user, such as:

    • “Personally Identifiable Information” (“PII”) including information you provide when you register with our Online Products and Services that can be used to identify you, such as your name, address, date of birth, mobile phone number or email address. While your IP address may not be considered PII on its own, when it is combined with certain other identifiers, it may become PII. More precise location positioning, or location information combined with other data, may make it possible to pinpoint an exact location, which in some instances could be considered PII;

    • “Non-PII”, which is information that cannot be used on its own to identify you personally. It may also be linked to your mobile device, which may enable us to recognize your device Some examples of Non-PII include:

      • data that you enter into or through using our Online Products and Services such as age, gender, duration of physical activities and other types of information that you provide using our Online Products and Services. You can refuse to supply this type of data, but it may prevent you from engaging in certain Online Product and Service-related content;

      • what websites you have visited and information about your visit, including what time of day you visited those websites;

      • for the Website, other Non-PII we collect may include your IP address and the type of browser you are using (e.g., Internet Explorer, Firefox, Safari, etc.).

    • “Account Identifiers,” such as your username, password, or credit card information;

    • “Account preferences,” such as how often you wish to receive email notifications and which information you choose to share with the community;

    • “Health Information”, such as your weight and height, diseases you have been diagnosed with, symptoms, moods and other health-related information; or

    • “Protected Health Information” or “PHI,” which is a subset of Health Information that contains information that identifies or can be reasonably used to identify you. PHI collected and stored by us may not be protected under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (“HIPAA”).

  • “Device Information,” such as the type and model, software version, manufacturer, operating system (e.g. iOS or Android), carrier name, mobile browser (e.g. Chrome, Safari), and identifiers assigned to your device, such as its iOS Identifier for Advertising (IDFA), Android Advertising ID (AAID), information your browser or operating system automatically provides to us, such as your IP address, your location information, such as the approximate geographic area provided by your Internet service provider (ISP) or location positioning information provided by the location services and GPS features of your mobile device or website when location services have been enabled;

  • Information about your communications with us, such as information regarding any contact you may have with our chat or support teams.

In some cases, we have arrangements with Third Parties to collect and store the foregoing information for us, e.g., to provide services in connection with hosting or the operation of our Apps, or to facilitate reporting of analytics regarding our Apps (such as with Google Analytics or WebTrends).

Although the Non-PII information that we share, including the Non-PII that is not linked to your mobile device, does not identify you personally, there is a risk that Third Parties who receive such information from us may be able to re-identify you through other information they gather.

When you register for our Online Products and Services that require payment we ask for certain PII, Non-PII, Health Information and PHI to create your account, including, but not limited to: first name, last name, e-mail address, postal address, telephone number, applicable billing information (e.g. credit card number and expiration date), current weight, current height, mood, health details, and food preferences. We use this information to personalize your experience within the members’ area and to send you a copy of our new member book.

We may also obtain access to your photo library. We obtain access to your photo library for purposes of building a profile in and uploading photos to our community. Your device will prompt you to ask for your permission for our Apps to access your photo library. If you agree, our Apps may access the photos in your photo library until you manually revoke permission for us to access your photo library or you delete our Apps from your mobile device. If you revoke Slimming World’s access to your photo library, it will prevent our Apps from accessing the photos on your device but it does not affect any photo stored in your online profile.

We may also collect Health Information about you through health apps on your mobile device, such as Google Fit or Apple HealthKit, or activity trackers, such as Fitbit, in order to provide a more seamless weight loss experience for you (“Health Data”). The types of Health Data that we could collect include:

  • Information about your physical traits, such as height, weight and body measurements;

  • Details about your athletic or recreational activities, such as type of activity, exercise frequency, and performance goals;

  • Data about athletic performance, such as time spent doing an activity, speed, distance, and heart rate; and

Like your photo library above, you will be asked for permission to allow our Apps to access Health Data from other apps and activity trackers and we will continue to collect Health Data until you revoke your permission or delete our Apps from your mobile device. If at any time you deny or remove your permission for us to gather Health Data on your mobile device, each time you attempt to use a service on our Apps that uses Health Data, you will be prompted to provide your permission. Revoking permission to collect Health Data does not remove Health Data from our database that we have already collected.

If you connect with us through social media, we may collect account or profile information about you.

Our Online Products and Services may also offer you the opportunity to chat with one of our representatives should you have any questions about Slimming World. Any information that you provide to us during a chat, including any PHI that you disclose, is stored in our database and a copy of the chat may be posted within your member administration area. Any information we provide to you during a chat is not meant to be medical advice. You should consult with your physician or another medical professional before using Slimming World Online Products and Services and follow your physician’s treatment advice. We will not use any information you provide to us during a chat for any marketing purposes. We or our Partners may use the chat to improve our customer service or to enhance our chat features.

Cookies

We and our Partners may collect Non-PII from you through technologies such as “cookies” and “pixel tags” (which are also called clear GIFs, web beacons, or pixels). “Cookies” are small data files that are stored by your web browser when you interact with a website. When you visit a web page, the cookie sends back Non-PII. “Pixel tags” are small graphic images (usually invisible) that can be embedded in content and ads on a webpage that track usage of our Apps and effectiveness of communication. We may also use web beacons in connection with emails sent to users, to let us know whether and when the email was opened. No personal information is transmitted through web beacons.

We and/or our Partners may utilize persistent cookies in connection with our Online Products and Services, which are cookies that remain within your mobile device over a period of time. In addition to our creating and placing a persistent cookie on your mobile device, we have arrangements with third party vendors which may also create and place persistent cookies on your mobile device as you use our Online Products and Services. These Third Parties collect and store information to provide services to us.

Cookies are usually stored in your browser which gives you the ability to manage them (though browsers for mobile devices may not offer this possibility). You can opt-out of the information that is collected about you through cookies. If you refuse cookies by turning them off in your browser, you will not be able to log-in to the site and therefore you will not be able to use Slimming World Online Products and Services. Your mobile device may offer settings to limit or disable mobile apps from gathering certain information. You should check the settings for the app and your device. For more information, you may review the FTC’s guide to opting out of online tracking for computers and mobile devices here.

Google Analytics cookies. Google Analytics uses first-party cookies to track visitor interactions and collect information about how visitors use our Online Products and Services. Google Analytics collects information anonymously. It reports website trends without identifying individual visitors. We then use the information to compile reports and to help us improve our Apps and website. You can opt out of Google Analytics – for more information on opting out of being tracked by Google Analytics across all websites you use, visit this Google page.
