What We Collect

When you register to become a member of Slide, you can create an anonymous account, or you can give us your email address and create a registered account. If you create a registered account, we collect your email address and the user name you create. We may also offer you the opportunity to submit other personally identifiable information (such as gender, age, occupation, hobbies, interests, zip code, etc.), which would then also be covered by this Privacy Policy.

In addition to personally identifiable information, you may submit other content ("Content"), such as blogs (web logs), titles in your media library, photos and other media, each of which you present through technology available at Slide ("Slide Shows"). It is entirely up to you whether you want to submit this additional information, and whether you would like to make it or your Slide Shows available to others. To protect your privacy, the Service allows you to mark your Content as “Private”, in which event it can only be seen by you and people with whom the unique URL of your Content has been shared. However, Slide has no responsibility for how people you allow to view your Content (or the public if you make any information available to the public) use that material, or to whom they may forward it. Moreover, if you "embed" a Slide Show on another web page, it will be visible to the public in accordance with the policies of that web page, regardless of whether you have restricted access to that Slide Show on Slide.

When you invite new members into your network or send messages through the Slide service, we collect and maintain on secure servers the information associated with those messages, including email addresses and content.

Finally, in order to provide the best possible service and to allow us to make certain internal reports, we collect aggregate information about the use of the Service, including information about users accessing the Slide site or viewing Slide shows on other sites, such as internet protocol addresses, browser type, browser language, referring / exit pages and URLs, platform type, number of clicks, domain names, landing pages, pages viewed and the order of those pages, the amount of time spent on particular pages, and the date and time.
