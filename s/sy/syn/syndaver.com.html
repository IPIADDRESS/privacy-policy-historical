Privacy Policy

General

This Privacy Policy (Policy) sets forth the SynDaver™ Labs (SYNDAVER, we, or us) policies regarding the collection and use of information relating to the SYNDAVER web site located at www.syndaver.com (Site). This Policy addresses our privacy practices relating to the use of our Site.

Personal Information

Personally Identifiable Information (Personal Information) is information that can be used to identify or contact you, such as your name, email address, telephone number or similar information. SYNDAVER only collects Personal Information that you choose to share with us, such as when (1) contacting us via the Site (by clicking on the “Contact Us” link at the top of each web page), (2) submitting your resume online in response to a job posting, (3) registering online for an event, course, or promotion, or, (4) upon registration for access to those areas of our Site intended only for medical industry professionals.

Registered Users

If you are an industry professional, to register, you must provide us with certain Personal Information, including your name, email address, postal mail address, title and industry affiliation. We will use this Personal Information to review and verify your registration information. We may use your Personal Information to send periodic marketing or promotional notices to you or notify you of any changes to this Policy. You can always opt-out of receiving these communications from us by following the “opt-out” procedures described below.

Uses

Any information you provide to SYNDAVER must be true, accurate, current, and non-misleading for the purpose for which you are providing it. And, if you are a registered user you must have the authority to provide the information submitted to and through the Site, and understand and agree that we may from time to time verify such authority.In particular we may need to contact you directly to verify the authenticity of business transactions.

If your Personal Information is submitted through the Site when applying for a position with SYNDAVER, your Personal Information will be used solely for the purpose of accepting and evaluating you for the position. We may retain your Personal Information for a period of time, but only for the purpose of considering your application for current or future positions at SYNDAVER. This information may be shared within SYNDAVER as well as with third party service providers contracted by us to collect and maintain candidate submissions for job postings.

If you contact SYNDAVER via the Site, we will use the Personal Information contained in your email to respond to your specific inquiry or comments in the email. In some cases we may also add you to our general email list, but will never transfer this Personal Information, including your email address to a third party. You can always opt-out of receiving any communications from us by following the “opt-out” procedures described below.

Tracking

The Site automatically tracks certain information about your behavior while visiting the Site. The information gathered includes only the URL from which you accessed this Site, and which URL you visit next. We collect and store this aggregate information in order to measure the Site’s performance and improve the Site’s design and functionality. If you are a registered user, we may use your Usage Data without your Personal Information, in order to measure the Site’s performance and improve the Site’s design and functionality.

Cookies

At this time, SYNDAVER does not permanently store “cookies” on your PC and does not employ “cookies” to transmit personal information. SYNDAVER may use “session cookies,” which consist of information stored in the browser cache and passed back to the server whenever a request for a new page on the Site is made. This session cookie is not typically saved or written to disk, and the information contained in the session cookie will only be valid for that particular session. The session cookie is discarded when the browser is closed. Most web browsers automatically accept session cookies, and most may also be configured to provide notice of when a session cookie is about to be placed on the browser. No information is saved in these session cookies other than an identification number associated with the particular session.
