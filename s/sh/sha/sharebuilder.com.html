Privacy Policy

Effective as of December 1, 1999

Netstock Direct Corporation, Netstock Investment Corporation, and their divisions and subsidiaries are concerned about preventing unauthorized disclosure of customer information. We also want you to know what personal information we collect and how we use it. The following privacy policy discusses these matters. If after reading it you have questions about our policy, please e-mail your questions to Customer Care.

We may from time to time amend our Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our business and services and our policies relating to privacy of customer information. Amendments will become effective at the time they are posted on our web sites. We will not provide notice of changes in our Privacy Policy.

This Privacy Policy uses the following terms:

"Contact Information" Your name, address, telephone number, fax number, e-mail address, and similar information that is useful for contacting you.
"Personal Nonpublic Information" Personally identifiable information about you and transactions in your account that is not generally available to the public, including your social security number, account numbers, and information about your assets, accounts, transactions, tax status, and similar matters.

1. Information Collected.
  a. In order to open accounts and provide financial services to you, we will ask you to provide Contact Information and Personal Nonpublic Information. As we provide financial services to you, we will also record and retain Personal Nonpublic Information about transactions and positions in your accounts.

b. Our Kidstock web site only provides information to visitors, and no Contact Information or Personal Nonpublic Information is collected through the web site.

c. We collect information about usage of our web sites (for instance, the number of visitors that click on advertisements and links displayed upon our site), but we do not link such usage information to Contact Information or Personal Nonpublic Information for any particular customer or visitor to our web sites.

d. If we do not accept your application to open an account or provide services, we will not retain any personal information that you have supplied during the registration process, other than as may be required for regulatory purposes.

e. If you send us any personal correspondence, such as e-mail messages or letters, we will answer your questions appropriately. We will maintain a record of the correspondence.

2. Use of Personal Information; Restrictions on Use.
  a. We will use Contact Information to communicate with you about your accounts with us and to obtain your views on the quality of our services. We and our affiliated companies may also use Contact Information from time to time to bring to your attention new products and services that we are offering, and to offer to you other promotional opportunities. If you do not wish to receive communications about new products and services, you may "opt out" by updating your Personal Profile.

b. We will use Contact Information and Personal Nonpublic Information about you for the purpose of servicing your accounts with us and for other internal purposes. We may provide Contact Information and Personal Nonpublic Information to third party vendors that help us to provide services to you. In such cases, we will have agreements with third parties pursuant to which they will agree to maintain the confidentiality of your personal information, and to use it only for the purpose of helping us to serve you.

c. We may from time to time share Contact Information with unaffiliated third parties, so that they can bring to your attention goods and services that they offer. Lists of Contact Information about our customers may be sorted by criterion that involves Personal Nonpublic Information. Third parties receiving Contact Information will not be allowed to pass along Contact Information to others. If you do not wish to receive marketing communications from third parties, you may "opt out" by updating your Personal Profile.

d. Where we have joined with a third party financial institution to offer a service such as Sharebuilder to the financial institution's customers, we may share with the financial institution Personal Nonpublic Information about such customers.

e. It may be necessary for us to share information about you with unaffiliated third parties in order to provide services to you and to facilitate transactions for your benefit. For instance, we may need to communicate information about you and your accounts with transfer agents retained by companies to manage the companies' direct investment programs. We do not control and are not responsible for the actions of such third parties, or for the manner in which they deal with information about you.

f. In addition to the circumstances described above, we may disclose Contact Information and Personal Nonpublic Information to unaffiliated third parties when, in our good faith judgment, one of the following circumstances exist:
  (1) You have given your consent (whether orally, in writing, or via e-mail);

(2) Where necessary for security purposes, to protect against fraud or unauthorized transactions, to resolve customer disputes or inquiries, or to respond to requests from persons who are acting in a fiduciary or representative capacity for you, or who hold a legal or beneficial interest in your account;

(3) To perform necessary credit checks or collect or report on debts owed to us;

(4) To protect our rights or property;

(5) Upon reasonable request by a mutual fund or other company in which you have chosen to invest;

(6) As required by applicable law, legal process, or our regulators; or

(7) In connection with other legitimate business purposes not involving direct marketing activities.

g. If you close your account with us, we will retain information about you and your account in an "inactive" status. We will not use Contact Information or Personal Nonpublic Information about inactive customers for any purpose other than as required in order to carry out our responsibilities with respect to the inactive account.

3. Contests and Promotions.
  We may on our own or in conjunction with other companies offer contests, sweepstakes, and promotions. You may be asked to supply certain personal information to participate in these promotional events, and this information may be used by the sponsoring parties for marketing purposes. Even if you have previously elected not to receive information or mailings about us or third parties, you may still receive information through your participation in these promotions. Rules and guidelines for each promotion will be clearly posted, as will notification about how the information gathered may be used. You should not participate in these special promotions if you do not want to receive marketing information about us or other sponsoring parties.

4. Our Use of Cookies.
  We use cookies to track usage of our web sites and to personalize web site pages for individual customers. A "cookie" is a small text file placed on your hard drive by our web page server. By configuring your preferences or options in your browser, you can determine if and how a cookie will be accepted. Data on web site usage that we collect using cookies is not tied to individuals, and is only used in aggregate form.

5. Links to Other Sites.
  While you are browsing our web sites, you might be directed to web sites operated by third parties. These other web sites may send their own cookies to you and collect data or solicit personal information. We do not control and are not responsible for what third parties do on their separate web sites, or how they handle information about you.

6. Maintenance of Accurate Information.
  We try out best to insure that the personal information we maintain about you is accurate, current, and complete. We will respond to your requests to correct inaccurate information in a timely manner. If you ever find that your personal or account information is incomplete, inaccurate or not current, you should update your Personal Profile.

7. Security.
  We use industry standard means such as secure socket layers to safeguard the confidentiality of your personal and financial information. We update and test our technology periodically to improve the protection of our information about you and to insure the integrity of such information.
