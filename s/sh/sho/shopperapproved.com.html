Privacy Policy and Cookie Policy

Shopper Approved is in the business of collecting and displaying customer ratings and reviews. We understand that your privacy is important to you, and that you care about how your information is used and shared online. We respect and value the privacy of everyone who visits Our Site and will only collect and use information in ways that are useful to you and in a manner consistent with your rights and Our obligations under the law.

This Policy applies to Our use of any and all data collected by us in relation to your use of Our Site. Please read this Privacy Policy carefully and ensure that you understand it. Your acceptance of Our Privacy Policy is deemed to occur upon your first use of Our Site if you are a Non-European resident. If you are a European resident, you will be specifically asked to accept our privacy and cookie policy when you (a) sign up for our services as a client, or (b) use our services to leave a rating or review as one of our client’s customers. If you do not accept and agree with this Privacy Policy, we ask that you please do not use Our Site.

"Cookie"

means a small text file placed on your computer or device by Our Site when you visit certain parts of Our Site and/or when you use certain features of Our Site. Details of the Cookies used by Our Site are set out in section 12, below

"Our Site"

means this website, http://www.shopperapproved.com, or forms that are displayed on Our client’s websites in order to collect or display ratings and reviews.

For Shopper Approved Client’s Customers leaving a rating or review or other feedback:

  • First and Last Name (although we only post your first name and the first letter of your last name with your review. If you don’t want to use your name, you can use an alias or web name).
  • Contact information such as email address (if receiving a follow up survey via email), or a telephone number (if receiving a follow up survey via text message).
  • IP address (automatically collected)
  • Web browser type and version (automatically collected)
  • Operating system (automatically collected)
  • A list of URLs starting with a referring site, your activity on Our Site, and the site you exit to (automatically collected)

If you would like to have your review removed from one of Our clients' social media portals, you will need to contact the company directly and have them remove it from the specific social media platforms. Posts to social media will contain: The reviewer's display name as left on our platform, the overall rating, and the written review.
  • Any other information you choose to share publicly on the Shopper Approved survey.

If you are under the age of 16, we ask that you please to not sign up for our services as a client, or leave any type of rating or review as one of our client’s customers.

Under the GDPR, you have:

  1. the right to request access to, deletion of or correction of, your personal data held by Us;

Section 14 - Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

  1. In the event that We use personal data for the purposes of automated decision-making and those decisions have a legal (or similarly significant effect) on you, you have the right to challenge to such decisions under GDPR, requesting human intervention, expressing their own point of view, and obtaining an explanation of the decision from Us.

  2. The right described in section 14(A) does not apply in the following circumstances:

    1. The decision is necessary for the entry into, or performance of, a contract between the you and Us;
    2. The decision is authorised by law; or
    3. You have given your explicit consent.

  3. Where We use your personal data for profiling purposes, the following shall apply:

    1. Clear information explaining the profiling will be provided, including its significance and the likely consequences;
    2. Appropriate mathematical or statistical procedures will be used;
    3. Technical and organisational measures necessary to minimise the risk of errors and to enable such errors to be easily corrected shall be implemented; and
    4. All personal data processed for profiling purposes shall be secured in order to prevent discriminatory effects arising out of profiling.
