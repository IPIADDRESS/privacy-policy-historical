SHIFT.COM Privacy Policy

Last Revised: November 30. 2012

Welcome to Shift.com, a website operated by GraphEffect, Inc. d/b/a SHIFT (“SHIFT”, “We” or “Us”) (the “Website“), and the home of the SHIFT collaboration platform that connects people across and within organizations to help them work more efficiently (the “SHIFT Service”).

This Privacy Policy governs your interactions with the Website and the SHIFT Service and any additional services provided by SHIFT made available through the Website (collectively, the “Services“). The Services include applications that are integrated with and accessed from the SHIFT Service and published by SHIFT (the “SHIFT Integrated Applications“), for example, our GraphEffect media execution platform application. This Privacy Policy does not govern your interactions with third party applications that integrate with the SHIFT Service (“Third Party Integrated Applications” or “TPIA“). Please see the section below on TPIAs for more information.

Capitalized words in this Privacy Policy that are not defined here have the meaning given to them in the SHIFT Terms of Service made available atwww.shift.com/tos.

A Note to Users Outside of the United States. Your Personal Information may be processed in the country in which it was collected and in other countries, including the United States, where laws regarding processing of Personal Information may be less stringent than the laws in your country. By using the Services, you are authorizing the transfer of your Personal Information in this way.

Types of Information. “Personal Information” means data that allows someone to identify or contact you, including, for example, your name, address, telephone number, e-mail address, as well as any other non-public information about you that is associated with or linked to any of the foregoing information. “Anonymous Information” means data that is not associated with or linked to your Personal Information; Anonymous Information does not, by itself, permit the identification of individual persons. We collect Personal Information and Anonymous Information, as described below.

Information We Collect.

Information You Provide. We collect Personal Information when you knowingly provide it to us, including in the following ways:

We may collect Personal Information from you, such as your first and last name, company information, e-mail and password when you create an account to use the SHIFT Service (“Account”);

When using the Services, you will submit Personal Information, for example, the individuals you invite to join a team, files you upload into a team area or postings you make to a public area of the Website or Services;

We will collect information about the Integrated Applications you access through the Services as provided below in the section entitled Information Collected via Technology. We may collect Personal Information through a TPIA, with the understanding that a publisher of a TPIA is contractually required to disclose what Personal Information it is disclosing to us in the its TPIA privacy policy. Please see the section below on TPIAs for more information about how we interact with TPIAs;

If you engage in any purchases through the Services, we will collect all information necessary to complete the transaction, including your name, credit card information, billing information and shipping information (collectively “Billing Information”);

We collect feedback on products and services if you provide it;

We collect the information you provide when you participate in one of our surveys, and

If you contact us regarding support for the Services, we may collect other types of Personal Information that you provide to us voluntarily, such as your operating system and location.

Information Collected via Technology.

To make our Services more useful to you, our servers (which may be hosted by a third party service provider) collect information from you automatically, including your browser type, operating system and Internet Protocol (IP) address (a number that is automatically assigned to your computer when you use the Internet, which may vary from session to session);

We also automatically collect information about the Integrated Applications you access through the Services, including information about how, when and for how long, and

We also use cookies and URL information to gather and store information regarding the date and time of your visit and the information for which you searched and which you viewed. “Cookies” are small pieces of information that a website sends to your computer’s browser while you are viewing a web site. We may use both session Cookies (which expire once you close your web browser) and persistent Cookies (which stay on your computer until you delete them) to provide you with a more personal and interactive experience on our Website. Persistent Cookies can be removed by following Internet browser help file directions. You can learn more about cookies by visiting www.allaboutcookies.com.

Information Collected from You About Others. If you decide to invite a third party to create an Account or join a Team, we will collect your and the third party’s names and e-mail addresses in order to send an e-mail and follow up with the third party.

Information Collected from Social Networking Sites. If you log on to the Service with your login credentials from a social network site (“SNS”) (e.g., through Facebook Connect), we may receive Personal Information about you from such SNS in accordance with its terms of use and privacy policy and your privacy settings on such SNS. For example, if your Facebook privacy settings allow, Facebook may share your friend list with us, in order to help us establish your Account and find other Service users that you know. We may add this information to the information we have already collected from you via the Service.

Third Party Integrated Applications.

Framing TPIA Content. One of the many features of the SHIFT Service is that other applications can integrate with the SHIFT Service and the power of collaboration is felt exponentially. When you view a TPIA through the SHIFT Service you do so through a “frame”. The TPIA pages and content you see through the frame are in fact sitting on the servers controlled by the TPIA publisher, not us. So, unless you copy and paste (or, if the TPIA allows, otherwise import) the content into the Shift Service, we will not have access to, or store, the TPIA content viewed through the frame. Similarly, unless you expressly authorize us, we do not provide your Personal Information to any TPIA publisher.

Authentication. We have created interfaces that allow you to connect with a TPIA, and authorize us to access and display your TPIA account. In order to provide this authorization, you do not provide us with your TPIA user name or password, but you will log-in to the TPIA directly through our Service. Once authorized by you, the TPIA provides us a token that allows the TPIA to recognize us when we ask, on your behalf, for access to your account information or to post information. To be clear, we cannot, and will not, access your TPIA account with this token unless you expressly authorize us to do so in each case.

TPIA Privacy. We are bound by this SHIFT privacy policy and the publisher of the TPIA is bound by its own privacy policy. Each publisher of a TPIA is contractually required by SHIFT to host and make available to you a statement of privacy practices that discloses its information collection, use and sharing practices (a “TPIA Privacy Statement”), including what Personal Information, if any, it provides to us. The TPIA Privacy Statement, and not the SHIFT privacy policy, governs the third party’s use of your personal informational. We are not bound by any TPIA Privacy Policy.

Supplemental Privacy Provisions. Specific Services, including SHIFT Integrated Applications, may have information collection, use and sharing disclosures that are specific to the Service (“Supplemental Privacy Provisions”). Any Supplemental Privacy Provisions will either be listed in this Agreement or will be presented to you for your acceptance when you sign up to use the Service. If any provision in this Privacy Policy is inconsistent with any provision in the Supplemental Privacy Provisions, the Supplemental Privacy Provisions will control, but only for such Service.
