Privacy Policy

Principles

We have a few fundamental principles about privacy and the information we collect:

  • We don't ask you for personal information unless we truly need it.
  • We don't share your personal information with anyone except to comply with the law, develop our products, or protect our rights.
  • You retain copyright and any other rights you already hold in Content which you submit, post or display through SameID.net.

Below is our privacy policy which incorporates these goals.

Introduction

SameID.net operates web-based services. It is SameID.net's policy to respect your privacy regarding any information we may collect while operating our websites. This privacy policy describes the choices available to you regarding our use of the personal information we collect and how you can access and update this information.

If you have questions or concerns regarding this Policy, contact admin@SameID.net.

Website Visitors

Like most website operators, SameID.net collects non-personally-identifying information of the sort that web browsers and servers typically make available, such as the browser type, language preference, referring site, and the date and time of each visitor request. SameID.net's purpose in collecting non-personally identifying information is to better understand how SameID.net’s visitors use its website. From time to time, SameID.net may release non-personally-identifying information in the aggregate, e.g., by publishing a report on trends in the usage of its website.

SameID.net also uses third party tracking-utility partners who use log files to collect potentially personally-identifying information like Internet Protocol (IP) addresses. SameID.net does not use such information to identify its visitors, however, and does not disclose such information, other than under the same circumstances that it uses and discloses personally-identifying information, as described below.

Gathering of Personally-Identifying Information

Certain visitors to SameID.net's websites choose to interact with SameID.net in ways that require SameID.net to gather personally-identifying information. The amount and type of information that SameID.net gathers depends on the nature of the interaction. For example, we ask visitors who sign up as a user at SameID.net to provide an email address. We also provide them the opportunity to provide additional information such as name and phone number. Those who engage in transactions with SameID.net – by purchasing a account plan at SameID.net, for example – are asked to provide additional information, including as necessary the personal and financial information required to process those transactions. In each case, SameID.net collects such information only insofar as is necessary or appropriate to fulfill the purpose of the visitor’s interaction with SameID.net. SameID.net does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below. And visitors can always refuse to supply personally-identifying information, with the caveat that it may prevent them from engaging in certain website-related activities.

Cookies

A cookie is a string of information that a website stores on a visitor's computer, and that the visitor's browser provides to the website each time the visitor returns. SameID.net uses cookies to help SameID.net identify and track visitors, their usage of SameID.net website, and their website access preferences. We do not link the information we store in cookies to any personally identifiable information you submit while on our site. SameID.net visitors who do not wish to have cookies placed on their computers should set their browsers to refuse cookies before using SameID.net’s websites, with the drawback that certain features of SameID.net’s websites may not function properly without the aid of cookies.

The use of cookies by our partners is not covered by our privacy statement. We do not have access or control over these cookies. Our partners use session ID cookies to to help us track the effectiveness of our websites and generate visitor traffic.

Clear Gifs

We use clear gifs in our HTML-based emails to let us know which emails have been opened by recipients. This allows us to gauge the effectiveness of certain communications and the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.
