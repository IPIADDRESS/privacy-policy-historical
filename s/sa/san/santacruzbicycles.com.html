6.              Cookies.  The Site may use cookies.  A “cookie” is a small piece of data generated by a website and saved in your internet browser.  It remembers information you provided as well as analytics about how you used the website, and can help to simplify your use of the website.  There are “session” cookies and “persistent” cookies.  Session cookies are automatically deleted from your hard drive at the end of your browser session.  As compared to a session cookie, a persistent cookie may remain on your browser following your session on the Site.  You can reject cookies by setting your browser to notify you when one is received so you can decide whether to accept or reject it.  Persistent cookies can also be removed by following the directions on your internet browser.  Cookies may be generated from third party websites, and cookies may be used for third-party retargeting and remarketing functions.  Users can view the Site without accepting cookies, and users can also opt-out of cookie-based tracking using an opt-out feature on the Site.  However, rejecting or removing cookies and opting out of cookie-based tracking may negatively impact use of the Site.

HubSpot may use cookies and other tools to track user data.  Users will have an opportunity to opt-out of HubSpot tracking by using an opt-out feature on the Site. When a user opts-out of HubSpot tracking, HubSpot installs an opt-out cookie valid for two years.  We encourage users to visit the HubSpot website to learn more about the cookies and other tools used by HubSpot and to review HubSpot’s privacy policies.
