Our Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy sets out how we collect, store and use information about you when you use our website (https://sonniss.com), and where we obtain or collect information about you. We believe you should always know what data we collect from you and how we use it.

Your privacy is very important to us and for that reason, you must agree to the terms of this Policy before using our website.

HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

We only use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • Contacting you in response to a specific order, query or feedback.
  • General administrative and business purposes
  • Monitoring Site performance;
  • Improving our Site and services to you;
  • Internal administration; and
  • Other purposes that are in accordance with your instructions.


COOKIES AND ADVERTISING

We use third party analytics services that use cookies to record general site usage information – like page visits and the way users move between pages on the site. This general site analytics information is not matched to your user account. Other cookies are used to enhance your use of the site, such as remembering that you are logged in and what’s in your shopping cart. You are able to minimise or block cookies using the privacy settings in your internet browser, but this may restrict your use of our site.

This website may contain links to other sites. Neither Sonniss, not its directors or board members are responsible for the privacy practices of linked sites, or the content. Underlined words and phrases are click-through links or hyperlinks to pages and websites.

SECURITY

This Site has security measures in place to protect the loss or misuse of, or alteration or unauthorised access to, information under our control by using appropriate technical and organisational measures such as storing your information on secure servers, encrypting transfers of data and only granting access to your information where necessary.

INTERNATIONAL TRANSFER OF INFORMATION

Sonniss Limited is based in England, UK, and maintains servers in the US and Europe for sending emails, website hosting, newsletter services, storing and delivering files, this means that any information collected by us may sometimes be held outside of the European Economic Area. By voluntarily providing information to us when using this website, you are consenting to the collection and use of your personal information by us. When we do so, we will ensure appropriate safeguards are in place.

INFORMATION COLLECTION

The only reason we collection personal information about you is to provide you with a better experience whilst browsing and buying sound libraries from our store. We only collect personal information about you that we consider necessary for this purpose and to achieve this goal. You are under no obligation to provide us with any information unless you want to make a purchase. Some information is collected automatically which is not purchase specific – those details can be found below.

When you visit this website, we automatically record certain information in relation to your visit such as:

  • Your IP or proxy server IP;
  • Basic domain information;
  • Your Internet service provider
  • The date and time of your visit to the website;
  • The length of your session;
  • The pages which you have accessed;
  • The number of times you access our site within any month;
  • The website which referred you to our website; and
  • The operating system which your computer uses.

 

When purchasing a sound library from our store we will also collect customer specific details such as: Email Address, Full Name, Billing Address, Location, Telephone number, VAT number and Business name. This information is collected to fulfil your order. No credit card details are ever stored.

HOW LONG DO WE KEEP THE INFORMATION FOR?

We keep all records for a minimum of 10 years as required to do so under European law, taking into account any and all legal obligations we have (e.g. to maintain records for tax purposes), any other legal basis we have for using your information (e.g. your consent, performance of a contract with you or our legitimate interests as a business).
