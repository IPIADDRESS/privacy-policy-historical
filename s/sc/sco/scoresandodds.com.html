HOW INFORMATION MAY BE COLLECTED

Cookies

ScoresAndOdds.com uses standard technology called "cookies," which are small text files that are transferred to your computer when you visit ScoresAndOdds.com if you allow your browser to accept cookies. Cookies automatically identify your web browser to ScoresAndOdds.com whenever you visit ScoresAndOdds.com, and may make navigating and using ScoresAndOdds.com easier for you. Also, by tracking how and when you use ScoresAndOdds.com, cookies help us determine which areas are popular and which are not. Many improvements and updates to ScoresAndOdds.com are based on data obtained from cookies. Additionally, accepting cookies may allow you to, among other things, personalize your experience on ScoresAndOdds.com. Cookies may also allow ScoresAndOdds.com to present to you advertising that may be of interest to you as well as limit how often you see certain ads that may detract from your user experience on ScoresAndOdds.com. If you do not want to allow information to be collected through the use of cookies, you can generally opt out of providing this information by setting your browser to reject cookies. However, please be aware that some areas of the ScoresAndOdds.com may not be able to provide complete functionality if you do not enable cookies.

Web Beacons

ScoresAndOdds.com pages may contain electronic images (generally, single-pixel ".gif" images) called "web beacons." These web beacons allow us to monitor and collect certain anonymous information about the viewer of the web page, such as the type of browser being used, the IP address of the computer that the web beacon is sent to and the time the web beacon was viewed. ScoresAndOdds.com uses web beacons for purposes that include, without limitation, the following:

  • Counting unique users (actually, unique web browsers), visits and page views.
  • Monitoring traffic and conversion patterns through our various product and service offerings on ScoresAndOdds.com.
  • Personalizing your experience when you visit ScoresAndOdds.com, including the advertising and content you see.
  • Determining whether or not e-mail messages were opened, links were clicked or notifications/offers were acted upon.
