Lesson 1: Understanding the Use of your IP Address Every computer online, including yours, has an IP address. In fact, it is not possible to use the Internet without one. An IP address is a unique number used by computers to identify one another. It consists of four groups of numbers separated by decimal points and may look something like this: 195.58.64.1. IP addresses may be "dynamic" or "fixed," and depending on the type of IP address you have, it may or may not be possible to identify your individual computer. If an IP address is dynamic, it is automatically assigned to your computer from a pool of available numbers when you log on to the Internet or your local network. This means that your computer's IP address will be different each time you use the Internet, and can only be used to identify the network (e.g. your company) or Internet Service Provider (e.g. AOL or CompuServe) that owns the pool of addresses from which your address came. If an IP address is fixed, you or a network administrator would have had to manually enter an IP address into your computer on when it was first set up to access the Internet. In this case your IP address remains the same each time you log on to the Internet or your local network and may be used to identify your individual computer. SchoolCity.com does collect your IP address when you visit our site. We use this information to administer our website and help diagnose problems with our server. Your IP address is also used to gather broad demographic information (e.g. how many users are connecting from commercial (.com) locations versus government (.gov) locations). We do not attempt to link your IP address information to any of your personally identifiable information.

Lesson 3: Choosing your Cookies Cookies in the offline world are tasty treats, but you wouldn't necessarily want to accept one from someone you didn't know. Online cookies are similar in some ways. On the Internet, cookies are pieces of information that a web site transfers to your computer's hard drive. These cookies then send information about your on-line activities back to a web site. Cookies are used by most major web sites and can be viewed as a great help in web surfing or as an invasion of privacy. Most browsers (e.g. Internet Explorer or Netscape) are initially set up to accept cookies. By changing your browser's preferences, you can have it refuse all cookies, accept some cookies and refuse others, or ask for your approval before accepting a cookie. While refusing all cookies will help to protect your privacy, most major web sites either will not function properly or will be considerably slower if you refuse cookies. For example, without cookies, you may not be able to set personalized news preferences or complete online shopping transactions. At SchoolCity.com, we use cookies to deliver content specific to your interests. Cookies also help us to determine how you use our site and to make improvements to our site. These cookies do not contain or track any personal information regarding the user. We use an outside advertising company to display ads on our site. These ads may also contain cookies. The cookies you receive through these banner ads are collected by an advertising company, and SchoolCity.com does not have access to or control over this information. The use of advertising cookies sent by third-party servers is standard in the Internet industry.
